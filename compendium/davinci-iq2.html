# DaVinci IQ2

Overview

The DaVinci IQ2 is a portable, battery-powered dry herb vaporizer manufactured by DaVinci (DaVinci Tech). Released as the successor to the original DaVinci IQ, the IQ2 is a session-style conduction vaporizer known for its compact, pocket-friendly form factor and stealth design. It is marketed as a dual-use device (dry herb and dosage pod compatible) and features precision temperature control, an adjustable airflow dial, and smartphone app integration. The device was positioned by DaVinci as a premium portable targeting connoisseurs who prioritize flavor, discretion, and control.

DaVinci was active within the vaporizer community and engaged directly with users through its official account, including discussions about trade-in/upgrade programs for original IQ owners looking to move to the IQ2.

Community Reception

Community opinion on the DaVinci IQ2 was notably divided. The device had both vocal advocates and detractors, and this split was evident in purchase advice threads where prospective buyers often reported encountering "a lot of negative reviews and feedback" during their research.

Pros cited by the community:

  • Stealth and pocketability: The IQ2 was widely praised for its truly pocket-friendly size. One user in an illegal jurisdiction noted that the DaVinci was "a fairly small device" and commended its discretion, though it still required its own pocket. The original IQ was described by one community member as "so stealthy!"
  • Large bowl for a pocketable device: The chamber capacity was a significant advantage, with community members noting it could hold roughly three times the material of a DynaVap bowl — making it suitable for users who wanted larger loads in a portable form factor.
  • Adjustable airflow dial: The five-position air dial on the bottom was highlighted as a key feature, particularly for users who preferred mouth-to-lung (MTL) draw styles. One knowledgeable community member specifically recommended the IQ2 for MTL users, noting the adjustable air dial provided meaningful draw restriction options.
  • Smart Path temperature stepping: The automatic temperature ramping was appreciated as a "load and go" feature, allowing sessions without needing to manually adjust temperature.
  • All-in-one simplicity: Compared to setups requiring multiple components (e.g., a DynaVap with an induction heater), the IQ2 was praised as a single-piece solution.

Cons and criticisms:

  • Session conduction limitations: Experienced users in the community cautioned that session conduction vaporizers like the IQ2 might not satisfy users accustomed to on-demand convection devices. One member warned that session conduction portables are "good for beginners" but may not "pass a reach test after a honeymoon" for more experienced users.
  • Speed: Users seeking quick, on-demand hits found the IQ2 lacking. One user who owned both a DynaVap and an IQ2 specifically lamented that "the DaVinci just means" a longer process when stealth and speed were priorities — describing it as a session device that didn't suit quick one-hit use cases.
  • Mixed reputation: The device attracted enough negative feedback that prospective buyers frequently mentioned encountering critical reviews before purchasing, leading them to consider alternatives like the Crafty+ or Mighty.

The IQ2 appeared to be best regarded as a solid choice for users who specifically wanted a pocketable, session-style conduction vaporizer with a larger bowl and draw restriction options — particularly those transitioning from smoking who preferred MTL technique. However, the community generally steered experienced on-demand users toward other options.

Resale value: Used IQ2 units were commonly listed in the community marketplace at €115–€140 in Europe and around $140 in the US (with WPA), suggesting moderate depreciation from original retail pricing.

Tips & Techniques

Loading

  • The IQ2's chamber is relatively large for a pocket vaporizer. Community discussions suggest it accommodates approximately 0.5g of finely ground material.
  • The device comes with a flavor chamber and ceramic zirconia pearl, both of which influence airflow and flavor extraction. Users experimented with pearl placement and flavor chamber configurations for different session characteristics.

Draw Technique

  • The IQ2 was specifically recommended for mouth-to-lung (MTL) vaping. The adjustable airflow dial on the bottom of the device features five restriction levels. For MTL users transitioning from smoking, a more restricted airflow setting (higher restriction) helps replicate the draw resistance of a spliff or cigarette.
  • One experienced community member described the IQ2 as ideal for users who "take a big pull and fill the mouth with vapor, then take it back into the lungs," as opposed to direct-lung inhale styles common with convection devices.

Smart Path Usage

  • The Smart Path feature provides automatic temperature stepping over the course of a session. For users who want a hands-free experience, this was described as a "load and go" feature — no need to manually adjust temperatures during a session.

Temperature Guide

The IQ2 offers precision temperature control across its full range. While specific community-agreed "sweet spots" were not extensively detailed in the available discussions, general conduction session vaporizer guidance applies:

Maintenance & Cleaning

While detailed cleaning procedures were not extensively discussed in the available community threads, some relevant points emerged:

  • Pearl and threading care: One community member reported that the thread holding the ceramic pearl broke during a deep cleaning session. This was significant enough to require a warranty replacement (RMA). Users were advised to handle the pearl and its threading carefully, particularly during thorough cleanings.
  • General maintenance: As a conduction vaporizer, the IQ2's chamber requires regular cleaning to prevent residue buildup. Standard approaches for conduction chambers (isopropyl alcohol on cotton swabs, cleaning of the mouthpiece and flavor chamber) apply.
  • Flavor chamber: The flavor chamber is a removable component that benefits from regular cleaning to maintain vapor quality. The device was sometimes sold with double sets of flavor chambers, pearls, and mouthpieces, facilitating rotation during cleaning.

Common Issues & Fixes

Pearl Threading Breakage

One community member reported the thread that holds the zirconia pearl breaking during deep cleaning. DaVinci honored the warranty replacement, though the RMA process took over a month to complete. Fix: Handle the pearl assembly gently during cleaning; contact DaVinci support for warranty service if breakage occurs.

RMA and Warranty Experience

Community experiences with DaVinci's warranty service were mixed. The company was praised for honoring replacements (including for older original IQ units), but turnaround times were noted as slow in some cases. DaVinci representatives were active in the community and responsive to complaints, sometimes offering to personally remedy issues raised in forum discussions.

Session Length and Speed Frustrations

Users seeking on-demand performance found the IQ2's session-style heating to be a fundamental limitation. This is not a defect but a characteristic of the device's conduction design. Users needing quick, one-hit capability were consistently directed toward on-demand alternatives.

Accessories & Modifications

Water Pipe Adapter (WPA)

A WPA was available for the IQ2, allowing connection to glass water pieces. Units were sometimes sold bundled with the WPA, and it appeared in community marketplace listings as a valued accessory.

Leather Glove / Case

Third-party leather gloves (protective cases) were available from makers such as Zyyde (South Africa), providing additional protection and a tactile upgrade for the device's aluminum body.

Flavor Chamber and Pearl

The IQ2 shipped with a flavor chamber and zirconia pearl as standard. Having doubles of these components (as noted in some marketplace listings) was considered useful for rotation and uninterrupted use during cleaning.

Dosage Pods

As a dual-use vaporizer, the IQ2 was compatible with dosage pods for pre-loaded convenience, though community discussion of pod usage was limited in the available threads.

Comparisons

DaVinci IQ2 vs. Crafty+ / Mighty (Storz & Bickel)

The IQ2 was most frequently compared against Storz & Bickel's Crafty+ and Mighty. One community member who personally favored the IQ2 directed users to read both device threads before deciding. Another member suggested skipping the Crafty+ entirely in favor of the Mighty, noting the Crafty had "really poor battery life" and didn't seem "as well made as the Mighty." The IQ2's advantage in this comparison was its smaller form factor and more adjustable airflow, while the Crafty+/Mighty were generally regarded as having superior vapor quality in the broader community.

DaVinci IQ2 vs. DynaVap

Users who owned both devices noted they served very different use cases. The DynaVap excelled in quick, on-demand extraction but had a much smaller bowl and required a heat source (torch or induction heater). The IQ2 offered a larger bowl ("three times as much" material), session-style simplicity, and adjustable draw restriction — making it the better choice for extended sessions and MTL technique. However, one user specifically noted the DynaVap was faster and more discreet for quick use in public.

DaVinci IQ2 vs. Grasshopper

One user who owned both a DynaVap and an IQ2 expressed that neither adequately replaced the Grasshopper's combination of extreme stealth, fast heat-up, and powerful on-demand hits. The IQ2, while compact, was described as too slow and session-oriented to match the Grasshopper's use case. Community members in that discussion recommended the Tinymight as a better Grasshopper replacement than the IQ2.

DaVinci IQ2 vs. On-Demand Convection Portables (Tinymight, Firewood, Tetra P80)

The community widely agreed that session conduction vaporizers like the IQ2 served a fundamentally different niche than on-demand convection portables. Experienced users with collections of convection devices were generally steered away from session conduction portables, with one member noting such devices might not sustain long-term interest for advanced users. The IQ2 was more commonly recommended for users transitioning from smoking, beginners, or those specifically seeking a simple session experience with draw restriction.

DaVinci IQ (Original) vs. IQ2

DaVinci actively discussed a trade-in/upgrade program with the community for original IQ owners. Community members estimated the used street value of a working original IQ at $120–$150 and suggested trade-in credits should reflect this range. The IQ2 added the adjustable airflow dial, Smart Path improvements, and dual-use capability as upgrades over the original. One community member noted that the original IQ "works flawlessly" and expressed hesitation about trading in, particularly as convection vaping was "dominating" their lineup at the time.
