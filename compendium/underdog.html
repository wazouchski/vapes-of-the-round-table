# Underdog

Overview

The Underdog is a desktop log-style dry herb vaporizer manufactured by a small artisan operation based in the United States. It is one of the pioneering log vaporizers, earning a reputation within the vaporizer community as a "tried and true" option that helped define the log vaporizer category. The Underdog uses convection heating, drawing hot air through a small load of ground herb packed into a glass or stainless steel stem. It is powered by a plug-in AC power supply with a variable voltage controller (VVPS), allowing the user to dial in precise temperatures. Each unit is handcrafted from a variety of hardwoods, making every Underdog unique.

The Underdog lineup has expanded over the years to include several models, including the standard SC3, the SC3+ (an enhanced version with reportedly improved heat recovery), the Alpha (known for its larger bowl and harder-hitting performance), the Chico (a compact variant), and the Micro Dog (the smallest form factor). The device is designed to be left on continuously as a "leave it on the coffee table" vaporizer, ready for on-demand use at any time.

Community Reception

The Underdog was widely regarded within the vaporizer community as one of the best log vaporizers available, frequently mentioned alongside the E-Nano, Heat Island (HI), and Woodscents as the top-tier options in the category. Community members consistently praised the Underdog for its efficiency with small loads, excellent vapor quality, and the artisan craftsmanship of its wooden bodies.

Pros as discussed by the community:

  • Exceptional vapor quality through convection heating
  • Outstanding efficiency — very small amounts of herb required per stem load
  • The Alpha model was specifically noted for having "the biggest bowl and probably biggest hit" among log vaporizers
  • The entire Underdog lineup was described as "really good" by experienced owners
  • Excellent customer service from the manufacturer, which was highlighted in contrast to some competitors
  • Beautiful, handcrafted wood bodies in many exotic species
  • Discreet appearance — can be left on a coffee table without drawing attention
  • Can be left powered on indefinitely for true on-demand use
  • Strong accessory ecosystem including various stem types and GonG adapters
  • Included accessories were praised; one community member noted preferring the Underdog over the E-Nano partly due to the included accessories rather than a fundamental performance difference
  • Promotional sales (such as 4/20 sales) were notable — at one point, a 4/20 sale included both a full-size log and a mini log

Cons as discussed by the community:

  • The standard SC3 model was noted as needing "a little time between hits" for heat recovery, meaning rapid back-to-back draws could result in diminishing vapor production
  • Requires a wall outlet, limiting portability
  • The VVPS lacks a digital temperature display, requiring users to learn their preferred dial settings through experience
  • Must remain plugged in during use (no battery option)

The community widely agreed that choosing between the Underdog and competing log vapes was largely a matter of personal preference and specific feature priorities, with one member summarizing that among the top logs, "it doesn't seem like you can go wrong."

Tips & Techniques

The community developed several best practices for getting the most out of the Underdog:

  • Loading: Pack stems lightly. The small bowl size is by design — log vaporizers are optimized for micro-dosing efficiency. A pinch of finely ground herb is sufficient for a full stem.
  • Leave it on: The Underdog is designed to be left powered on continuously. Many community members kept theirs on 24/7, treating it as an always-ready, on-demand device sitting on a desk or coffee table.
  • Heat recovery with SC3 vs. SC3+: The standard SC3 requires a brief pause between draws to allow the heater to recover. The SC3+ was reported to offer somewhat better heat recovery, making it more suitable for users who prefer rapid successive draws. The Alpha model, with its larger heater core and bowl, was noted for delivering the biggest hits.
  • Water pipe use: Use GonG (glass-on-glass) adapters to connect the Underdog to water pipes. Both 14mm and 18mm adapters were available. One community member noted that most vapes with male 18mm connections may require an additional adapter depending on the specific water pipe configuration.
  • Draw technique: Slow, steady draws are generally recommended for convection log vaporizers to allow hot air to fully pass through the herb load without overwhelming the heater's recovery capacity.
  • VVPS dial settings: Since there is no digital temperature readout, users learned their preferred settings through experimentation. Optimal dial position can vary between individual units and power supplies.

Temperature Guide

The Underdog uses a variable voltage power supply (VVPS) without a digital temperature display, so temperature is controlled by dial position rather than a specific degree setting. Community guidance was therefore expressed in terms of dial positions and observable vapor behavior rather than exact temperatures.

General guidelines based on community experience:

  • Lower settings — Emphasize flavor and lighter vapor; ideal for terpene-rich, tasty draws. Suitable for users who prefer a more gradual extraction.
  • Mid-range settings — The community sweet spot for most users; balances flavor with solid vapor production and efficient extraction.
  • Higher settings — Maximize vapor density and extraction speed; recommended for use through water pipes where the additional cooling mitigates any harshness.

Because each Underdog unit and VVPS combination can vary slightly, the community strongly encouraged new owners to start at lower dial settings and gradually increase until finding their personal sweet spot. Keeping a mental or written note of preferred dial positions was a common recommendation.

Note: The community did not converge on specific Fahrenheit/Celsius values for the Underdog due to the analog nature of its temperature control. Users interested in precise temperature measurement sometimes used external thermometers, but this was not standard practice.

Maintenance & Cleaning

  • Stems: Glass stems can be soaked in isopropyl alcohol (ISO) to dissolve accumulated residue. This was the standard cleaning method recommended throughout the community.
  • Heater core: The internal heater should be kept free of debris. A dry cotton swab or pipe cleaner can be used to gently clean the heater bore.
  • Wood body: The wooden exterior should not be exposed to alcohol or water. A dry or slightly damp cloth is sufficient for external cleaning.
  • GonG adapters: Same ISO soaking protocol as stems. Reclaim (condensed oil) in GonG adapters and stems was noted as usable — a practice common across the log vaporizer community.
  • ABV (Already Been Vaped): The spent herb in the stem can be tapped out easily. Keeping stems clean ensures optimal airflow and vapor quality.

Community members who traded or sold Underdog-compatible glass accessories noted the expectation that pieces would be ISO-washed to "perfection" before changing hands.

Accessories & Modifications

The Underdog ecosystem included a robust selection of accessories:

  • Glass direct-draw stems — The primary delivery method; various lengths available.
  • GonG (Glass-on-Glass) adapters — Available in 14mm and 18mm sizes for connecting to water pipes. "Long GonG" variants were also produced for added reach.
  • Stainless steel stems — A more durable alternative to glass.
  • Compatible glass bubblers — Community members used and traded custom and commercial glass bubblers specifically sized for Underdog stems. American-made artisan bubblers — including hammer bubblers and sherlock-style pieces — were popular among owners.
  • Water pipes — Any 14mm or 18mm female-jointed water pipe could be used with the appropriate GonG adapter.
  • VVPS (Variable Voltage Power Supply) — Essential for operation and temperature control; replacements were available from the manufacturer and third-party sources.
  • Mini Log / Micro Dog — Not strictly an accessory, but many community members collected multiple Underdogs in different wood species and sizes. Bundles of multiple units were occasionally seen on the secondary market.

Comparisons

The Underdog was most frequently compared to other log vaporizers and desktop convection units within the community:

Underdog vs. E-Nano

Both are highly regarded plug-in log vaporizers with convection heating. Community members found them to be closely matched in core performance, with preferences often coming down to ancillary factors. One member noted preferring the Underdog based on included accessories rather than a fundamental usage difference. However, the E-Nano was noted by some community members as having less reliable customer service experiences compared to the Underdog's manufacturer, who was praised for excellent support.

Underdog vs. Woodscents

The Woodscents was frequently compared favorably to the Underdog, with both earning strong recommendations. Key differences noted by the community:

  • Heat recovery: The Woodscents was reported to have slightly faster heat recovery than the standard Underdog SC3 model.
  • Dynavap tip compatibility: The Woodscents offered native compatibility with Dynavap tips, enabling the use of various Dynavap-ecosystem stems and accessories. This was seen as a significant advantage, though community members cautioned that Dynavap tip inner diameters could vary between production runs, occasionally causing fitment issues.
  • Bowl size: The Underdog Alpha was noted as having a larger bowl and harder hit than the Woodscents.
  • Customer service: Both manufacturers were praised for excellent customer support.

Underdog vs. Heat Island (HI)

The Heat Island was mentioned alongside the Underdog as a peer in the log vaporizer category. Community members recommended reading up on the differences between all four major logs (Underdog, E-Nano, HI, Woodscents) before making a purchase decision, suggesting that all were competitive options.

Underdog vs. Butane Vaporizers (e.g., Hydrobrick Maxx)

For users torn between a log vaporizer and a butane brick-style vaporizer, the community noted a key distinction: logs require wall power and are designed for stationary home use, while butane vaporizers offer portability and freedom from electrical outlets. Both were considered excellent choices for on-demand use and water pipe compatibility, with the recommendation to choose based on lifestyle and power source preference.
