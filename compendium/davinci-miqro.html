# DaVinci Miqro

Overview

The DaVinci Miqro (stylized as "MIQRO") is an ultra-compact portable dry herb vaporizer manufactured by DaVinci, a California-based company known for its line of portable conduction vaporizers. Released in 2018, the Miqro was designed as a smaller, more pocketable counterpart to the DaVinci IQ, targeting users who prioritized discretion and portability above all else. It uses a full conduction heating method and is powered by a single removable 18350 battery, making it one of the smallest removable-battery vaporizers on the market at the time of its release.

The device was offered in several color options including black and grey, and was available in both a base model and an "Explorer Edition" that included additional accessories. Community members frequently described the Miqro as "very stealthy" with a "fast heat up," positioning it squarely as a pocket-friendly device for use on the go rather than a home session vaporizer.

Community Reception

The DaVinci Miqro occupied a niche but somewhat polarizing position within the vaporizer community. Its reception can be characterized as mixed-to-lukewarm, with users acknowledging its strengths in portability and stealth while frequently finding it insufficient as a primary vaporizer.

Pros cited by the community:

  • Exceptional portability and stealth: The Miqro's ultra-compact size was consistently praised. Community members highlighted its pocketability as its standout feature, noting it was purpose-built for discreet on-the-go use.
  • Fast heat-up time: The conduction oven was noted for reaching vaping temperature quickly.
  • Removable 18350 battery: The ability to carry spare batteries was appreciated, as the small battery capacity meant limited session life from a single cell.
  • Build quality: Hinges and construction were generally regarded as solid, with one community member noting the "hinges are great" even after extended use.
  • Included accessories: The Explorer Edition was seen as a well-thought-out package, particularly the carry can/battery case and the dual stir tools.

Cons and reservations:

  • Limited battery life: The single 18350 battery was a frequent point of criticism. While the removable battery design mitigated this somewhat, users noted the need for spare batteries.
  • Small chamber: While the small oven was part of the portability design, it limited the device's appeal for heavier users or longer sessions.
  • Better suited to portability than performance: One community member who sold their Miqro noted plainly, "I use most of my vapes at home and this is actually made to be portable," acknowledging it did not compete with home-use devices on vapor quality or output. The same user stated they "didn't really love it."
  • Conduction heating limitations: As a full conduction device, the Miqro required proper grinding and packing technique to perform well. Community members noted that a medium-to-coarse grind worked best.
  • Paint/finish wear: At least one user reported minor paint wear on the bottom of the unit over time.

The Miqro appeared frequently in the community's marketplace sections, with multiple users offering devices for sale or giveaway — often lightly used — suggesting a pattern of users trying the device and moving on. Resale prices in the community ranged from $50 to $85, while the device was also given away free on at least two occasions. The carry can/case was a sufficiently valued accessory that at least one community member sought to purchase one separately.

Tips & Techniques

While the available community discussion threads were primarily marketplace-oriented rather than technique-focused, several practical insights can be gleaned from community posts:

Grinding

  • A medium-to-coarse grind was recommended for the Miqro. One experienced community member specifically noted that this grind consistency "works well in conduction vapes such as this," and recommended the SWll grinder for achieving it. A grind that is too fine may restrict airflow in the small conduction oven.

Packing

  • As a conduction vaporizer, the Miqro benefits from a firm but not overly tight pack to ensure even heat distribution through the material while maintaining adequate airflow.
  • The device includes an adjustable "pearl" or oven spacer that allows users to reduce the chamber capacity for smaller loads, which can help with efficiency when vaping less material.

Stirring

  • The inclusion of two stir tools (one stored inside the unit, one in the battery holder sleeve) suggests that mid-session stirring was considered important by the manufacturer and community alike for achieving even extraction from the conduction oven.

Battery Management

  • Carrying a spare 18350 battery was strongly advisable for any extended outings. The Explorer Edition's battery storage case facilitated this. Users recommended keeping batteries fresh, with one giveaway listing noting "no real use on either battery" as a selling point.

Temperature Guide

The DaVinci Miqro offers precision temperature control and DaVinci's "Smart Path" modes via the companion app. While specific community-agreed sweet spots for the Miqro were not extensively documented in the available threads, general conduction vaporizer temperature guidance applies:

300°F–340°F (149°C–171°C)Light flavor, minimal visible vaporBest for tasting terpene profiles; very light effects

The Miqro's Smart Path modes allow the device to automatically ramp through a temperature range over the course of a session, which some users preferred over setting a static temperature.

Maintenance & Cleaning

The DaVinci Miqro shipped with a branded cleaning kit, indicating the manufacturer recognized the importance of regular maintenance for this conduction device.

Routine Cleaning

  • Conduction oven: The oven will develop staining with regular use — described by one community member as "usual stains of used Conduction Heater." Regular cleaning with isopropyl alcohol (90% or higher recommended) and the included cleaning tools helps maintain performance and flavor.
  • Mouthpieces: Both the flat and extended mouthpieces should be soaked periodically in isopropyl alcohol to remove resin buildup.
  • Stir tool / pick: The included stir tools double as cleaning implements for the oven.
  • Vapor path: Pipe cleaners or cotton swabs dipped in isopropyl alcohol can be used to clean the vapor path.

General Maintenance

  • Battery contacts: Keep clean to ensure consistent power delivery from the 18350 cell.
  • Hinge mechanism: The flip-top design should be kept free of debris to maintain smooth operation.

Accessories & Modifications

Included / Official Accessories

  • Flat mouthpiece: Lower profile for maximum stealth.
  • Extended mouthpiece: Provides a slightly cooler vapor path.
  • Carry can / battery storage case: A cylindrical case for storing the Miqro and spare battery. This was a popular accessory — at least one community member sought to purchase one separately.
  • Protective glove/cover: A silicone or similar sleeve to protect the device's finish.
  • Stir tools: Two included, stored in different locations on the device and battery holder for convenience.
  • DaVinci cleaning kit: Official cleaning supplies.

Third-Party Accessories

  • SWll grinder: Recommended by a community member for producing the medium-to-coarse grind ideal for conduction vaporizers like the Miqro.
  • 18350 batteries: Spare batteries from reputable manufacturers were commonly recommended given the limited battery life.

Community discussion of aftermarket modifications or water pipe adapter (WPA) compatibility for the Miqro was limited in the available threads, reflecting the device's positioning as a simple, self-contained portable unit rather than a modifiable platform.

Comparisons

Direct comparison data from community discussions was limited in the available threads, but the Miqro's place in the market can be contextualized:

  • DaVinci Miqro vs. DaVinci IQ: The Miqro was designed as a smaller, more affordable sibling to the IQ. It trades battery life (18350 vs. 18650), chamber size, and session length for dramatically improved portability and stealth.
  • Conduction portable category: As a full conduction vaporizer, the Miqro competed with other compact conduction devices. The community generally viewed pure conduction portables as suitable for convenience-focused users rather than those prioritizing vapor quality or efficiency.
  • Home use suitability: One community member explicitly noted selling their Miqro to fund a VapBong (a high-end desktop unit), underscoring the gap between the Miqro's portable-focused design and the expectations of users who primarily vape at home.

The Miqro was most appreciated by users who specifically needed an ultra-compact, discreet device and were willing to accept the tradeoffs inherent in its diminutive size. For users whose primary use case was at home or who demanded longer sessions and larger chambers, the community generally suggested looking at larger devices.
