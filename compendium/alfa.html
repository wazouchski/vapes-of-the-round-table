# Alfa (Goboof)

Overview

The Alfa is a compact portable dry herb vaporizer manufactured by Goboof, a company based in Dublin, Ireland. The device was first announced and discussed in the vaporizer community in late 2014, with sample units being distributed to reviewers around September–October of that year. The Alfa was notable for being one of the few vaporizers designed and manufactured in Europe at the time of its release, which was a significant point of appeal for European consumers concerned about the build quality of Chinese-manufactured alternatives.

The Alfa uses a conduction heating method and is powered by an internal rechargeable battery. It was designed as a stealthy, highly portable unit with a minimalist form factor—nothing protrudes from the body, and the mouthpiece is flush with the device. The unit features preset temperature settings rather than full variable temperature control. It was positioned as a competitor to the Pax and other compact session-style portables, retailing at a price point around €140 at launch, which placed it competitively against offerings like the Flowermate V5.0S (approximately €130 in European shops at the time).

Community Reception

The Alfa generated considerable interest within the vaporizer community upon its announcement, with one community member describing it as "the most interesting unit this year" due to its small size, competitive price, and European manufacture. The device's packaging and artwork were also praised for their aesthetic appeal.

Pros identified by the community:

  • Compact and stealthy design: The flush mouthpiece and small body made it one of the most discreet portables available. It was repeatedly described as a "perfect little stealth vape." Nothing protrudes from the device, making it easy to carry and use inconspicuously.
  • European manufacture: The fact that it was built in Dublin, Ireland was a meaningful selling point for community members who expressed concerns about the quality control and material safety of Chinese-manufactured vaporizers. As one member noted, "I'd rather pay 10€ more and have a unit that was built here in Europe."
  • Ease of use: The device was described as fully automatic in operation, requiring no technique or learning curve—a contrast to manual devices like the Magic-Flight Launch Box (MFLB).
  • Vapor quality: Despite being a conduction vaporizer, the Alfa was noted to produce good-tasting vapor, with at least one experienced community member stating it tasted better than the Pax.
  • Extraction speed and vapor production: The Alfa was praised for rapid extraction and solid vapor production relative to its size.

Cons identified by the community:

  • Temperature presets too high/limited: This was the most significant and frequently discussed criticism. The lowest preset of 190°C (374°F) was considered too high by many community members who preferred low-temperature vaping in the 160–170°C (320–338°F) range. The temperature preset scheme was compared unfavorably to the Flowermate V5.0S, which shared a similar structure and similar practical limitations—specifically, the mid setting (210°C) was seen as largely redundant because most of the active compounds were already extracted at the low setting, leaving little vapor production at the middle temperature.
  • High temperature setting near combustion threshold: The 230°C (446°F) high setting was criticized for being dangerously close to the combustion threshold for dry herb, leaving very little safety margin. While some speculated this setting was intended for concentrates (oil and wax), this was of limited value to European users who generally did not have access to legal concentrates.
  • Bottom-loading chamber door: The hinged door mechanism for loading the chamber was described as "not always easy to close," suggesting a minor but recurring usability frustration.
  • Small bowl size: At approximately 0.2 g maximum, the chamber was modest. While this suited some users who preferred micro-dosing or solo sessions, it limited the device for group use or users with higher tolerance.

By 2018, the Alfa had settled into a niche as a well-regarded stealth portable. Community members were observed giving away units in good condition, suggesting it had been largely superseded by newer devices but was still considered a functional and worthwhile vaporizer.

Tips & Techniques

Community discussion yielded the following practical advice for Alfa users:

  • Grind fine and pack moderately: As a conduction vaporizer, the Alfa benefits from a fine to medium-fine grind to maximize surface contact with the heated chamber walls. Packing should be firm but not overly tight—the small chamber rewards consistent, even loads.
  • Start on the low setting and step up: Despite the criticism that 190°C was too high as a "low" option, community members advised beginning sessions on the lowest setting to extract the most flavorful terpenes first, then stepping up to the 210°C setting to finish the bowl. Skipping directly from low to high was sometimes recommended, as the mid setting was found to produce relatively little additional vapor after a thorough low-temp session.
  • Stir between temperature steps: Given the conduction heating, stirring the chamber contents between temperature increases was implied as good practice for even extraction, consistent with general conduction vaporizer technique.
  • Close the door carefully: The bottom-loading door mechanism required a bit of practice to close securely. Users should ensure it is fully latched before use to maintain proper airflow and prevent herb from falling out.

Temperature Guide

The Alfa offers three preset temperature levels:

SettingTemperatureRecommended Use
Low190°C / 374°FBest starting point for flavor-oriented sessions. Extracts lighter terpenes and produces smooth, tasty vapor. Community members who preferred lower temperatures (160–170°C) found this setting already somewhat aggressive, but it was functional for a flavor-first approach.
Mid210°C / 410°FA moderate extraction temperature. In practice, community members noted that after a full session on the low setting, stepping to mid produced limited additional vapor. Most useful if the low setting session was cut short.
High230°C / 446°FMaximum extraction. Produces the densest vapor and most thorough extraction. The community cautioned that this setting is very close to the combustion threshold and recommended careful use—particularly monitoring draw speed and avoiding overly dry material. Potentially intended for use with concentrates.

Community sweet spot: The general consensus was that the low setting (190°C) was where most flavorful and enjoyable sessions occurred, with a step up to the high setting (230°C) to finish extraction. The mid setting was widely considered the least useful of the three.

Note: Several community members expressed a strong preference for temperatures below 190°C (particularly in the 160–180°C / 320–356°F range) and considered the Alfa's temperature floor a meaningful drawback. Users who prefer very low-temperature vaping should be aware that this device cannot accommodate that preference.

Maintenance & Cleaning

While detailed cleaning protocols were not extensively documented in the community threads for the Alfa specifically, general conduction vaporizer maintenance practices apply:

  • Chamber cleaning: Brush out ABV (Already Been Vaped) material after each session while the unit is still slightly warm. Use the included brush or a small stiff-bristled brush.
  • Mouthpiece and vapor path: Periodically clean the mouthpiece and any screens with isopropyl alcohol (90% or higher recommended). Allow all parts to dry completely before reassembly and use.
  • Chamber deep clean: Soak a cotton swab in isopropyl alcohol and wipe the interior of the chamber to remove residue buildup. Avoid excessive liquid that could seep into electronics.
  • Bottom door: Pay attention to the loading door mechanism; residue buildup around the hinge could exacerbate the already-noted difficulty in closing it securely.

Common Issues & Fixes

IssueDetails & Solutions
Bottom door difficult to closeThis was a design characteristic noted by early reviewers. Ensure the chamber is not overpacked, which can prevent the door from seating properly. With use, the mechanism may loosen slightly and become easier to operate.
Minimal vapor on mid setting after low-temp sessionThis was widely reported and is inherent to the preset temperature scheme rather than a defect. The community recommended skipping from low directly to high for finishing a bowl.
Concerns about combustion on high settingThe 230°C setting was noted to be near the combustion threshold. Use drier material cautiously at this setting, maintain a steady draw, and avoid leaving the unit sitting at temperature without drawing. If material shows signs of charring or produces harsh, acrid taste, discontinue use at the high setting.
Charging cable lossAt least one community member noted difficulty keeping track of the charging cable, suggesting it may have used a less common connector. Users were advised to keep the cable with the carrying case.

Accessories & Modifications

The Alfa was sold with a carrying case and USB charging cable as standard accessories. Community discussion did not reveal a significant aftermarket accessory ecosystem for the device. No water pipe adapter (WPA) was documented as being available or commonly used with the Alfa.

Given the device's positioning as a simple, self-contained stealth vaporizer, the lack of extensive modification options was consistent with its design philosophy. Users seeking extensive customization or water pipe compatibility were generally directed toward other devices.

Comparisons

The Alfa was most frequently compared to other compact portable vaporizers of its era:

Alfa vs. Pax (1st Generation)

The Pax was the Alfa's most direct competitor in terms of form factor and target audience. One experienced community member who received an early review unit stated that the Alfa had "all the qualities of a Pax, but better—much better." Specific advantages cited included better vapor flavor than the Pax and a similarly compact, discreet design. Both devices use conduction heating and are session-style portables.

Alfa vs. Flowermate V5.0S

The two devices shared a nearly identical three-preset temperature scheme (the Flowermate V5.0S also offered settings at approximately 188°C, 193°C, and 230°C depending on the version). The community noted that both suffered from the same practical limitation: the mid setting was largely redundant after a full low-temp session. The Alfa was approximately €10 more expensive than the Flowermate in European markets, but community members considered the European manufacture worth the premium. Concerns about Chinese manufacturing quality were frequently cited in favor of the Alfa.

Alfa vs. Magic-Flight Launch Box (MFLB)

The Alfa was described as slightly larger than the MFLB but superior in vapor production and extraction speed. The MFLB was credited with slightly better flavor, attributed to its on-demand convection/conduction hybrid heating. However, the Alfa's fully automatic operation was seen as a significant advantage over the MFLB's manual technique-dependent design, making the Alfa far more accessible to new users.

Alfa vs. Lotus

The Lotus, a butane-powered convection vaporizer, was acknowledged as capable of producing more vapor with better flavor than the Alfa. However, community members considered the comparison somewhat unfair, as the Lotus was not truly portable in the same sense—it required a butane torch and was typically used at home on a water pipe (bubbler). For true portable, discreet use, the Alfa was the clear winner.
