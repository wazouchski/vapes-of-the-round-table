# E-nano

Overview

The E-nano (often stylized as "E-Nano" or "e-nano") is a desktop log-style dry herb vaporizer manufactured by EpicVape, a small American artisan company. First released around 2013–2014, the device belongs to a lineage of log vaporizers that traces its conceptual roots back to the Purple Days, one of the earliest and most celebrated log vapes in the community. The E-nano uses predominantly convection heating, where hot air is drawn through a small load of herb packed into a glass or metal-screened stem that inserts into the top of a compact wooden log body. It is powered by a standard AC wall outlet and features a variable-temperature dial for heat adjustment.

The E-nano earned a devoted following within the vaporizer community as one of the most efficient desktop vaporizers available, capable of producing satisfying sessions from remarkably small amounts of herb — often cited as low as 0.025–0.03 grams per load. Its compact form factor (roughly the size of a coffee mug), artisan wood construction, and "leave it on all day" operational philosophy made it a staple recommendation for home users seeking maximum efficiency.

Community Reception

The E-nano was widely regarded within the community as one of the premier log vaporizers, frequently mentioned alongside other log-style units such as the Purple Days, the Underdog, and the Woodscents. Community members consistently praised several key attributes:

Pros:

  • Exceptional efficiency: The E-nano's tiny bowl size was frequently highlighted as one of its greatest strengths. Loads as small as 0.025–0.03g could produce fully satisfying sessions, making it among the most material-efficient vaporizers ever discussed. Community members noted it was comparable in efficiency to the famed Purple Days lineage.
  • Simplicity and reliability: With no batteries, no electronics to fail, and a single analog dial for temperature control, the E-nano was praised as a "set it and forget it" device. Many users reported leaving it plugged in and powered on throughout the day for on-demand use.
  • Build quality and aesthetics: As a handcrafted wood product, each unit was unique. The community appreciated the artisan craftsmanship and variety of wood species available.
  • Vapor quality: Convection-dominant heating through glass stems was noted for producing clean, flavorful vapor.
  • Water pipe compatibility: The GonG stem options made the E-nano a natural companion for water filtration setups, which many community members preferred.
  • Durability: Even units with cosmetic damage such as cracks in the wood body were reported to continue performing without issue. One community member selling a unit noted a crack down the back side that "hasn't ever gotten any worse and still performs the same."

Cons:

  • Customer service concerns: A notable number of community members reported poor customer service experiences with EpicVape. This was cited as a distinguishing negative compared to competitors like Underdog and Woodscents, whose manufacturers were praised for responsive and attentive support. One community member specifically noted that "a fair amount of people have poor CS experiences" with EpicVape, describing this as "very much unlike the other former two manufacturers."
  • Desktop-only limitation: As an AC-powered device, portability was its principal weakness. Some users explored inverter solutions for travel (see Accessories & Modifications), but the unit was fundamentally a home-use device.
  • Learning curve with the analog dial: Without digital temperature readout, users had to learn their own unit's "sweet spots" by experimentation, as no two units' dials corresponded exactly.
  • Glass stem fragility: The borosilicate glass stems, while excellent for vapor purity, could break if dropped.
  • Cord vulnerability: At least one community member reported their E-nano's power cord being damaged by a pet chewing on it, exposing the copper wire, though the unit continued to function after the cord was repaired with electrical tape.

The E-nano was frequently recommended to new vapers, including those transitioning from smoking. One experienced community member specifically highlighted it as a favored log vape alongside the Woodscents when advising a new user looking to quit smoking, positioning it as an excellent home-use complement to portable devices. Used units were actively sought after in classified sections, with members posting wanted-to-buy listings specifying interest in particular wood types and accessory bundles — indicating sustained demand on the secondary market.

Tips & Techniques

Loading

  • Use very small amounts. The E-nano's stems are designed for micro-loads. Community members consistently reported best results with approximately 0.025–0.05g of finely ground herb. Overpacking reduces airflow and can impair convection efficiency.
  • Grind consistency matters. A medium-fine grind was generally recommended to allow good airflow while maintaining sufficient surface area for convection extraction. Too fine a grind can pull material through screens or clog airflow.
  • Screen placement: Stems came in both fixed-screen (glass screen built into the stem) and adjustable-screen (metal screen) variants. The adjustable screen allows the user to control bowl depth, which affects the distance between the herb and the heater — a key variable in temperature tuning.
  • Stem insertion depth: Pushing the stem deeper brings herb closer to the heater for more intense extraction. New users were encouraged to experiment with insertion depth alongside dial position.

Drawing Technique

  • Slow, steady draws are the standard recommendation for convection log vaporizers. Drawing too fast cools the air passing through the herb; drawing too slowly can risk overheating the load. A moderate, controlled sip-like draw was the community's consensus technique.
  • Breathing control was described by some members as part of the learning curve that makes the device rewarding to master — analogous to the breath control required by the Magic-Flight Launch Box, but in a different context.

Orientation and Water Filtration

  • Avoid running the E-nano in an upward vertical position. When the unit is pointed upward, herb can fall off the basket screen and onto the heater screen, which is undesirable.
  • Sidecar-style bubblers are strongly recommended for water filtration. These allow the E-nano to remain in a horizontal or slightly angled position while still connecting to a water piece. The Matrix Sidecar (available from DHgate in both 14mm and 18mm joint sizes) was a popular specific recommendation.

Operational Philosophy

  • Many experienced users left the E-nano powered on all day at a moderate dial setting, treating it as an always-ready on-demand vaporizer. The low power draw (~24 watts) made this practical and economical.
  • For truly on-demand use, a higher dial setting with shorter draws allowed single-hit extraction from a loaded stem. For a more session-like experience, a lower setting with multiple draws was preferred.

Maintenance & Cleaning

Stems

  • Glass stems should be soaked in isopropyl alcohol (ISO) periodically to remove resin buildup. Many community members reclaimed this resin ("stem honey" or "stem milk") by simmering the stems in whole milk or cream, producing an infused beverage from the collected condensate.
  • Metal screens in adjustable stems can be replaced when clogged or distorted. Keeping screens clean ensures proper airflow and consistent performance.
  • Stem cleaning every 1–2 weeks for regular users was a common recommendation, though frequency depended on usage intensity.

Heater and Log Body

  • The heater core inside the log body generally requires minimal maintenance. Occasional inspection of the heater well to ensure no debris has fallen inside was recommended. A dry cotton swab or pipe cleaner can be used to remove any debris — avoid introducing liquids into the heater area.
  • The wood body can be wiped down with a dry or slightly damp cloth. Avoid using solvents on the wood exterior to protect the finish. Some users applied food-safe wood oils or waxes periodically to maintain appearance.

Accessories & Modifications

Stems and GonG Adapters

The E-nano's versatility was significantly expanded through its stem ecosystem:

  • Standard glass stems — used for direct-draw vaporization, typically with a built-in glass screen or an adjustable metal screen. Spare stems are recommended since glass can break.
  • 14mm GonG adapters — glass-on-glass adapters allowing the E-nano to connect directly to 14mm female water pipe joints.
  • 18mm GonG adapters — same concept for 18mm water pipe joints.
  • Long stems — extended glass stems with metal screens for cooler direct-draw vapor.
  • DIY GonG adapters — some community members cut existing stems to create custom adapters. Techniques for cutting thick borosilicate glass tubes were extensively discussed (see Common Issues & Fixes).
  • E-pik — EpicVape's loading and stirring tool, included with some packages.

Third-Party and Artisan Accessories

  • JoDa Glassworks — a third-party glassblower who produced custom decorative stems for the E-nano, including colored and glitter-infused designs. These were traded and sold among community members.
  • Delta 3D Studios — this third-party accessory maker produced organizational items for the E-nano ecosystem, including glow-in-the-dark honeycomb organizers for storing stems and accessories. Well-regarded in the community.
  • Silicone grip sleeves — added to glass stems for improved handling comfort and heat insulation, as well as some drop protection.

Water Pipe Integration

The E-nano was widely used with water pipes of various sizes. Sidecar-style bubblers allowed the log to remain at a proper angle while connecting to a water piece. The Matrix Sidecar bubbler (available from DHgate in both 14mm and 18mm) was the most frequently recommended option. The GonG stems allowed the log to sit directly atop a water pipe, creating a compact and efficient setup that was one of the most popular configurations in the community.

Portable Power Solutions

For users who wished to take the E-nano on trips, a DC-AC inverter rated at 100+ watts was confirmed to work. Community members noted that the choice between lithium-ion and lead-acid battery packs depended on the balance between weight, cost, and desired hours of use.

Storage

  • The included muslin bag can be used for storage and transport of the unit when not in use.

E-nano vs. Purple Days / Purple Days Derivatives

The E-nano was frequently discussed in the context of the Purple Days lineage, as log vaporizers share a common conceptual origin. Both were praised for extreme efficiency with loads of approximately 0.025–0.03g. Community members generally considered them comparable in extraction capability, with the E-nano offering the advantage of variable temperature control versus the Purple Days' fixed-temperature design.

E-nano vs. Magic-Flight Launch Box (MFLB)

This comparison arose frequently because both devices were celebrated for efficiency and small loads:

  • Efficiency: Community members were divided but several reported comparable efficiency between the two. However, the MFLB tended to leave more extractable material behind, which could be further vaped in a desktop unit.
  • Extraction completeness: Desktop units like the E-nano, with their higher available temperatures and consistent AC power, were noted to provide more thorough extraction.
  • Use case: The MFLB served as a portable complement, while the E-nano excelled at home. The community widely agreed that the ideal setup combined both: a portable for on-the-go use and a log vape for home sessions.

E-nano vs. Heat Island (HI)

The Heat Island entered community conversation as an alternative log vaporizer. Community members sometimes cross-shopped or upgraded between log vaporizer brands when the HI came to market.

E-nano vs. Session Portables (Arizer Solo/Air, VapCap M)

When advising new users — particularly those quitting smoking — community members sometimes recommended the E-nano alongside session portables like the Arizer Solo or VapCap M. The E-nano served the home-use role while the portable covered on-the-go needs. The combination was described as the "perfect combination" by multiple community members.
