# E-Nano

Overview

The E-Nano is a log-style desktop dry herb vaporizer manufactured by Epic Vape (also referred to as EpicVape). It is an American-made convection vaporizer that operates on 120V AC power directly, distinguishing it from most other log vaporizers that run on 12V DC through an external power supply. The unit was designed by the maker known in the community as "ACE OF VAPE," who stated that the goal was "to blend the efficiency, beauty and ease of use of a log style vape with the power and benefits of 120v ac."

The E-Nano features a compact wooden body housing a ceramic heating element, with vapor delivered through glass stems or glass-on-glass (GonG) adapters. It is a convection-dominant vaporizer, heating herb primarily through hot air drawn through the small chamber. The unit includes a built-in variable power supply (VPS) with a numbered dial for temperature control, a feature that set it apart from competing log vaporizers at the time of release.

The E-Nano was widely regarded within the vaporizer community as one of the most efficient vaporizers available, capable of delivering effective sessions from remarkably small amounts of material — typically 0.05–0.1 grams per load.

Community Reception

The E-Nano was very highly regarded within the vaporizer community, consistently recommended as a top-tier desktop vaporizer, particularly for users prioritizing efficiency, flavor, and ease of use. It was frequently cited alongside the Underdog and the Heat Island (HI) as one of the premier log vaporizers available.

Pros commonly cited by the community:

  • Exceptional efficiency — The tiny bowl size was seen as a major advantage, allowing users to achieve strong effects from minimal material. Community members frequently praised the E-Nano's ability to produce effective sessions from a single small load.
  • Built-in temperature control — The integrated VPS with a numbered dial was appreciated for its simplicity, eliminating the need for a separate variable voltage power supply required by competing log vapes like the Underdog.
  • Fast heat-up time — Running on 120V AC gave the E-Nano a noticeably faster warm-up compared to 12V log vaporizers, which could take 15–30 minutes.
  • No external power supply — The direct AC power design meant one less piece of hardware on the desk, contributing to the unit's clean, unobtrusive aesthetic.
  • Always-on readiness — Like other log vaporizers, the E-Nano could be safely left on 24/7, making it essentially an on-demand vaporizer despite being a desktop unit. Community members valued this for casual, coffee-table use.
  • Excellent water pipe performance — Through GonG adapters, the E-Nano was widely praised for its ability to "milk" water pipes effectively, producing dense, visible vapor through glass.
  • Concentrate capability — Users reported success using concentrates sandwiched between herb or placed in organic cotton/hemp fiber within the stems.
  • Beautiful craftsmanship — The hand-selected premium wood bodies were frequently praised for their aesthetics.
  • Discreet appearance — Multiple community members noted it looked like a decorative object that could sit unobtrusively on a coffee table.

Cons and criticisms:

  • Small bowl size — While efficiency-minded users loved this, some users accustomed to larger-chamber vaporizers found the tiny bowl limiting, particularly those seeking "knockout hits." The community acknowledged a learning curve in finding the "Goldilocks zone" for packing.
  • Tethered to the wall — As a corded desktop unit, it lacked portability. Some sellers on the secondhand market cited this as their reason for moving on.
  • Glass fragility — Stems and GonG adapters were prone to breaking, and replacement glass was an ongoing cost for some users. Fixed all-glass screen versions were noted as particularly fragile.
  • Hit size not comparable to top-tier desktops — While capable of producing dense hits, the community generally acknowledged the E-Nano could not match the raw cloud production of units like the VapeXhale Cloud EVO, though one experienced user noted it was "not far off."
  • Temperature dial variation — The numbered dial settings varied from unit to unit, meaning specific number recommendations were not universal. Each user needed to calibrate their own unit.

One community member who owned both the E-Nano and Cloud EVO described using the E-Nano as beneficial for tolerance management, noting that "reasonably sized log vape hits help keep your tolerance down" compared to always pursuing maximum-density hits from more powerful units.

Tips & Techniques

Bowl Packing

The E-Nano's small chamber demanded precise packing technique. The community developed several specific approaches:

  • Screen positioning for larger loads: One detailed technique involved flipping the stainless steel screen so it formed an upward-facing "basket" shape, then pushing the screen approximately 1 inch from the top of the GonG. This maximized the distance between the herb and the heater while creating a defined loading area. Users were advised not to fill past the top walls of the screen basket.
  • Adjustable screen for dose control: The adjustable stainless steel screens allowed users to push the screen deeper or shallower to control bowl size. This was considered a significant advantage over fixed glass screens, enabling everything from micro-doses to the maximum the chamber could hold.
  • Do not overpack: Community members emphasized that overpacking reduced performance. The small chamber worked best with a light, not-too-tight pack that allowed air to flow through the material.

Draw Technique

  • A slow to moderate draw speed was generally recommended, though this was a point of some variation among users. One user reported that harder draws seemed to produce better hits, but the broader community consensus favored a controlled, steady draw that allowed the convection heating to fully engage with the material.
  • The draw technique interacted significantly with temperature settings — higher temperatures were more forgiving of faster draws, while lower temperatures rewarded patience.

Always-On Use

  • The E-Nano was designed to be left on continuously, making it functionally on-demand. Community members commonly left their units powered on at their preferred temperature setting throughout the day, ready for immediate use.

Concentrate Use

  • Concentrates could be used by sandwiching them between layers of dry herb, or by placing them in organic cotton or hemp fiber inside the stem. One experienced community member specifically mentioned using "concentrates in cotton or hemp" in the E-Nano.

Reclaim Collection

  • When used with a GonG adapter and water pipe, organic cotton placed in the water pipe adapter (WPA) served double duty: it kept the water pipe cleaner and collected reclaimable condensate that users reported was potent and reusable.
  • An Arizer dome screen placed in an 18mm GonG also helped keep downstream glass clean.

Temperature Guide

The E-Nano's temperature dial uses arbitrary numbered settings rather than precise degree readouts, and the community consistently noted that each unit varies slightly, so specific number recommendations should be treated as starting points rather than absolutes.

Dial Setting (approximate)EffectNotes
5.0–6.0Low temperature / flavor-focusedBest for tasting terpenes; lighter vapor production; suitable for those who prefer cooler, more flavorful draws
6.5Medium / all-around sweet spotFrequently cited starting point; good balance of flavor and vapor density. One detailed community technique specifically recommended setting the dial to 6.5 for consistent results
7.0–7.5Higher temperature / dense vaporDenser clouds, more thorough extraction; recommended for use through water pipes where cooling compensates for the higher temperature
8.0+Maximum extractionRisk of approaching combustion temperatures; generally used only by experienced users seeking the densest possible hits

Note: The community consistently advised new users to start at a moderate setting and incrementally increase until finding their preferred balance. The dial numbers themselves "don't really mean anything" universally — as one owner of multiple vaporizers noted, "each unit is going to vary a bit, and it really only takes a session or two to figure out the optimal temperature."

For water pipe use, slightly higher temperatures were generally recommended to compensate for vapor cooling through water filtration.

Maintenance & Cleaning

Glass Stems and GonG Adapters

  • ISO soak: The standard cleaning method involved soaking glass pieces in isopropyl alcohol (ISO) or ethyl alcohol for approximately 5 minutes, followed by soap, rinse, and drying. Community members described this as quick and effective — "easy peasy."
  • Stainless steel screens: Adjustable basket screens could be removed, cleaned with a brief application of a torch (one to two seconds), and replaced. This was faster and more thorough than trying to clean screens in place.
  • Fixed glass screens: These were harder to clean and more fragile than the adjustable stainless steel versions. Material tended to migrate through the glass screen toward the end of sessions, which was cited as another reason to prefer adjustable screens.

Stem Honey/Reclaim

  • Over time, condensed vapor (commonly called "honey" or "reclaim") accumulated inside stems and GonG adapters. This could be collected during cleaning and was considered a potent byproduct.

Body

  • The wooden body required minimal maintenance. The metal heating core could develop some patina over time — one seller described the metal core as having "aged a bit" — but this did not affect function.

Common Issues & Fixes

Broken Glass Stems

The most commonly reported issue was glass breakage. Stems and GonG adapters were fragile and could break from accidental drops or mishandling. The community recommended:

  • Keeping spare stems on hand (many users purchased multiple stems and GonGs upfront)
  • Using silicone grips/sleeves on glass pieces to improve grip and provide some drop protection
  • Checking the secondhand market and community classifieds for replacement glass

Inconsistent Hits

Users who struggled with hit consistency were typically advised to:

Adjust screen position (ensure proper distance between herb and heater)

Check pack density (avoid overpacking)

Experiment with draw speed

Fine-tune the temperature dial in small increments

Cosmetic Cracks in Wood

At least one community member reported a crack in the wooden body that was purely cosmetic and did not affect function. The manufacturer's warranty covered such issues, and the warranty was confirmed to be transferable to new owners.

Temperature Dial Calibration

Since each unit's dial corresponded to slightly different actual temperatures, new users sometimes struggled to find their ideal setting. The community consistently advised patience: "it really only takes a session or two to figure out the optimal temperature."

Accessories & Modifications

Glass Stems

Two primary types were available:

Adjustable (stainless steel basket screen): Community favorite. Allowed dose size customization, easier cleaning, and the ability to torch-clean the screen. Recommended for most users.

Fixed (all-glass honeycomb screen): More fragile, harder to clean, and less versatile. Material could pass through the glass screen late in sessions. Some users preferred the all-glass vapor path, but the adjustable version was more widely recommended.

Both standard-length and long stems were available.

GonG Adapters (Glass-on-Glass)

  • Available in 14mm and 18mm sizes for water pipe compatibility
  • Both fixed glass screen and adjustable screen versions were offered
  • The community generally recommended 18mm as the more versatile base size, as it was easy to adapt 18mm glass to accept 14mm accessories using an adapter, but not the reverse
  • A 14mm to 18mm low-profile adapter was a commonly purchased accessory

Water Pipes and Bubblers

The E-Nano was extensively used with water filtration. Popular community-recommended pairings included:

  • D020 bubbler (from DHgate/Sunshinestore): A compact, affordable, and widely praised pairing. Frequently mentioned as an excellent complement to the E-Nano.
  • Matrix Sidecar (DHgate clones of Mobius design): Another popular choice, available in multiple sizes. Offered more diffusion than the D020.
  • Claisen adapter: Recommended by community members for creating additional airflow paths when using water pipes. Lab glass versions were available cheaply on eBay.

Community guidance on selecting glass:

  • Consider whether you want high or minimal diffusion
  • Smaller-volume pieces generally preserved flavor better with the E-Nano's small loads
  • An Arizer dome screen or organic cotton in the WPA joint helped keep water pipes cleaner

Other Accessories

  • E-Pick tool: A stirring/cleaning tool included with new units
  • Aromatherapy cup: An accessory for diffusing essential oils, available in multiple versions
  • Display stand: A wooden stand for upright storage
  • Walnut power supply cover: An aesthetic upgrade to match wooden bodies
  • Walnut storage box: For organized storage of the unit and accessories
  • Custom glass by Joda: Community glass blower who produced custom stems, GonG adapters, steamrollers, and carbed GonGs specifically designed for the E-Nano. These were highly valued in the secondhand market.
  • Silicone grips: Color-coded silicone sleeves for glass stems and GonGs, providing grip and identification

Community members who owned both generally considered them comparable in vapor quality. One member admitted to "liking the Underdog a bit better than the E-Nano but that's based on the included accessories more than one being better at use." The E-Nano's faster heat-up and lack of external power supply were its primary advantages; the Underdog's broader accessory ecosystem and artisan character were its strengths.

E-Nano vs. Life Saber (LSV)

The LSV (by 7th Floor) was a larger, non-log convection vaporizer sometimes compared to log vapes.

  • The LSV offered larger load sizes and a slight edge in first-hit flavor
  • The E-Nano had advantages in efficiency, temperature control (built-in VPS), size/footprint, energy consumption, and ease of use
  • One experienced community member who owned both an LSV and multiple logs concluded that "every category I cared about, the log had a slight advantage" — the LSV's advantages were "bigger, more power hungry, and hotter"
  • The LSV's temperature dial was unmarked but users learned to reference it by "clock position" (e.g., "the 2 o'clock position")

E-Nano vs. Heat Island (HI) / Herborizer Ti

Key technical differences noted by the community:

  • The E-Nano runs on 110V AC; the HI runs on DC power
  • The E-Nano has a built-in VPS; the HI does not
  • E-Nano stems have a lip that prevents the heater from contacting material; HI stems do not
  • HI stems were only available in 14mm; E-Nano offered both 14mm and 18mm
  • The E-Nano was slightly larger than the HI
  • Glass compatibility: E-Nano stems were slightly smaller in diameter and did not fit the HI or Underdog

For water pipe use specifically, one community member noted: "If your priority is milking and running through glass, the nano is a beast."

E-Nano vs. VapeXhale Cloud EVO

  • The Cloud EVO produced larger, denser hits and was considered a more powerful unit overall
  • The E-Nano was significantly less expensive and more efficient with material
  • One user who owned both described them as complementary: the EVO for maximum extraction and the E-Nano for more measured, tolerance-friendly sessions
  • A community member described the E-Nano's vapor production through glass as "not up to EVO size IMO but not far off"
  • Both were recommended as excellent primary home vaporizers; some community members used them side by side as their two main desktop units
  • The community sometimes suggested the E-Nano as a budget-friendly alternative for users who couldn't afford the EVO, with "many would say it's similar to Cloud EVO"

E-Nano vs. Woodscents

The Woodscents (by Ed's TnT) was mentioned alongside the E-Nano as a comparable log vaporizer, particularly for users interested in concentrate use with log-style units. Both were referenced as capable of handling concentrates in cotton or hemp fiber. However, detailed head-to-head comparisons were less common in the available community discussions.
