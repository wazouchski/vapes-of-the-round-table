# Storz & Bickel Mighty

Overview

The Storz & Bickel Mighty is a portable dry herb vaporizer manufactured by Storz & Bickel, a German company best known for the Volcano desktop vaporizer. Released in late 2014, the Mighty quickly became one of the most widely discussed and recommended portable vaporizers in the enthusiast community. It employs a hybrid heating system combining both conduction and convection elements, powered by dual integrated lithium-ion batteries. The Mighty was one of the first portable vaporizers to achieve medical device certification in some jurisdictions, a fact frequently cited in community discussions regarding material safety and build quality.

The Mighty is the larger sibling to the Storz & Bickel Crafty, sharing the same heating element and oven design but featuring dual batteries (versus the Crafty's single cell), an integrated LED display for precise temperature control, and the ability to be used while charging (passthrough charging). It was widely regarded within the community as the benchmark portable vaporizer for much of its production life, with one community member summarizing a common sentiment: the Crafty and Mighty are "peerless" among battery-operated portable vaporizers.

Community Reception

The Mighty was one of the most consistently praised portable vaporizers in community discussions spanning from its 2014 launch through the early 2020s. It was commonly described as "the top dog for portables" and was frequently the default recommendation for users seeking a reliable, easy-to-use battery-powered portable, particularly for those transitioning from combustion or upgrading from entry-level devices.

Strengths

  • Ease of use: The community widely agreed that the Mighty's greatest selling point was its simplicity. Turn it on, set a temperature, wait for heat-up, and draw. Multiple members described it as the most user-friendly portable vaporizer available, requiring virtually no technique to produce satisfying results.
  • Vapor production: The Mighty was praised for consistent, dense vapor production across its temperature range. One community member noted it was "more than capable of assuming the role of a home vaporiser" despite being a portable unit.
  • Low draw resistance: The Mighty was noted for having reasonably low draw resistance compared to many competing portables, making it comfortable for extended sessions.
  • Passthrough charging: The ability to use the device while plugged in was considered a significant advantage over the Crafty and many other portables. Community members highlighted this as one of the key reasons to choose the Mighty over its smaller sibling, effectively giving it desktop-like utility at home.
  • Dual battery life: Significantly longer battery life than the single-cell Crafty, making it more practical for extended use away from power.
  • Onboard temperature control: The integrated display and buttons were preferred by many over the Crafty's app-dependent temperature control.
  • Medical certification: Frequently cited by health-conscious users as a differentiator, providing some reassurance about material safety.

Criticisms

  • Vapor dryness: Some experienced users found the Mighty's vapor dry and potentially irritating compared to convection-dominant or glass-pathed vaporizers. One community member described it as "quite dry and irritating," and this sentiment was echoed by others who had moved on to pure convection designs.
  • Flavor retention in plastics: A recurring criticism involved the thermoplastic components of the vapor path. Community members noted that the plastic appeared to retain residual AVB (already been vaped) flavors over time, potentially diminishing the taste experience of fresh loads. One member observed that while the Mighty initially tasted excellent, flavor degradation became noticeable with extended use if cleaning was not rigorous.
  • Bowl cleaning difficulty: When used without dosing capsules, the hybrid bowl (with its stainless steel chamber) was considered by some to be among the harder portable oven designs to keep clean. One experienced member noted the bowl was actually harder to clean than the cooling unit, contrary to common assumptions.
  • Build quality concerns (early units): Early adopters expressed concerns about the durability of the plastic tabs at the top of the unit where the cooling unit connects. The junction point between the two halves of the outer shell was identified as a potential weak spot, particularly if the cooling unit was repeatedly overtorqued during attachment. The display screen was also noted as appearing fragile and easy to damage, though widespread screen failures were not commonly reported.
  • Session-style vapor waste: As a session vaporizer, the Mighty continues to cook material between draws. Community discussion acknowledged that some vapor is inevitably lost during pauses in a session, a trade-off inherent to the design category.
  • Non-replaceable batteries: The integrated batteries were a persistent concern. Once battery life degraded (typically after 1–3 years of regular use), users faced either expensive manufacturer replacement or out-of-warranty situations where Storz & Bickel offered only a discounted replacement unit rather than a simple battery swap.
  • Size: While some members were pleasantly surprised that the Mighty fit in jeans pockets alongside a phone, others considered it large for a portable, especially compared to devices like the Pax or DynaVap.
  • Price: At approximately $350–$400 USD at retail, the Mighty was consistently one of the most expensive portable vaporizers on the market, which gave many prospective buyers pause.

Overall Standing

Despite its criticisms, the Mighty maintained a reputation as the "safe choice" for portable vaporization throughout its production run. It was not universally considered the best-tasting or most efficient portable — enthusiasts of pure convection devices, on-demand vaporizers, and glass-pathed units often preferred alternatives — but it was almost universally acknowledged as the most reliable and easiest to recommend, particularly for new users or those prioritizing consistency and simplicity.

Tips & Techniques

Loading the Chamber

  • Grind: A medium to medium-fine grind was the most commonly recommended consistency. Overly fine grinds could restrict airflow and pull material through the screen.
  • Packing density: The community generally advised a loose to lightly tamped pack. Overpacking was discouraged as it could increase draw resistance and reduce convection efficiency.
  • Dosing capsules for smaller loads: For users who did not want to fill the full chamber, dosing capsules were frequently recommended. They reduced the effective chamber size, kept the oven cleaner, allowed for pre-loading multiple sessions, and were reported by some to improve flavor consistency.
  • Liquid pad trick: For partial loads without capsules, the included liquid pad could be placed on top of a smaller amount of material to keep it in place and improve heat distribution.

Drawing Technique

  • Slow, steady draws: The community favored long, slow, and steady draws rather than hard, fast pulls. The hybrid heating system responded well to moderate airflow, and aggressive drawing could cool the heater and reduce vapor density.
  • Taking hits while powering down: One technique discussed was to turn the unit off after a session but continue taking draws as the temperature dropped through the vaping range, capturing residual vapor that would otherwise be wasted.

Session Management

  • Temperature stepping: A widely practiced technique was to start at a lower temperature for flavor-focused early draws, then progressively increase temperature throughout the session to extract remaining active compounds. This approach was described by multiple members as their standard usage pattern.
  • Passthrough use at home: Many community members used the Mighty plugged in as a quasi-desktop unit, preserving battery cycles and ensuring uninterrupted sessions.

Efficiency Considerations

  • The Mighty is a session vaporizer, meaning material is heated continuously while the unit is on. Community members who prioritized efficiency and only took occasional hits were sometimes advised to consider on-demand alternatives, as vapor is inevitably lost between draws during active sessions.

Temperature Guide

The Mighty's 40°C–210°C (104°F–410°F) range with single-degree precision gave users granular control. Community-discussed temperature preferences included:

Temperature RangeEffect Profile
148°C–170°C (298°F–338°F)Flavor-forward, light effects. One member reported getting psychoactive effects at as low as 148°C on the Mighty. Best for tasting terpene profiles.
170°C–190°C (338°F–374°F)Balanced flavor and effect. A common starting range for sessions.
190°C–200°C (374°F–392°F)Fuller extraction, denser vapor. The community frequently used this range as the primary session temperature. 195°C (383°F) was specifically mentioned by one member as producing "amazing taste" when used with dosing capsules.
200°C–210°C (392°F–410°F)Maximum extraction. Vapor becomes thicker and hotter. Some community members expressed concern about approaching combustion thresholds at the upper end, though debate existed about whether the Mighty's hybrid heating method made this less of a concern than with pure conduction devices. One member noted that 210°C on the Mighty did not taste "combusted" in the way 210°C on the Arizer Solo did, attributing this to the convection component of the heating system.

Temperature Stepping Sweet Spot (Community Consensus)

A typical community-recommended session progression:

Start at 175°C–185°C (347°F–365°F) for the first several draws (flavor phase)

Step up to 190°C–200°C (374°F–392°F) for mid-session extraction

Finish at 205°C–210°C (401°F–410°F) to fully extract remaining compounds

Users who saved their AVB for edibles often stopped at lower maximum temperatures (around 190°C–200°C / 374°F–392°F) to leave residual compounds in the spent material.

Maintenance & Cleaning

Cooling Unit

The cooling unit was the component requiring the most frequent cleaning attention. The community found it straightforward to maintain:

  • Disassemble the cooling unit into its component parts (top cap, base, O-rings, screen).
  • Soak in isopropyl alcohol (90%+ concentration recommended) for 15–30 minutes, or longer for heavily soiled units.
  • Rinse thoroughly with warm water after soaking and allow to dry completely before reassembly.
  • Reclaim: Some users collected the honey-colored resin that accumulated in the cooling unit, as it contained condensed active compounds. This was sometimes repurposed for edibles or applied to material for enhanced sessions.
  • Frequency: The community generally recommended cleaning the cooling unit every 5–10 sessions, or whenever draw resistance noticeably increased.

Oven / Chamber

  • Without dosing capsules, the stainless steel chamber was described as relatively difficult to keep clean among portables. Residue could build up on the chamber walls and around the screen.
  • Brush the chamber after each session while still warm (the included brush was adequate).
  • Isopropyl alcohol on a cotton swab for periodic deeper cleaning.
  • Community members noted that using dosing capsules dramatically reduced oven cleaning requirements, as most residue was contained within the capsule.

Screens

  • Replace screens periodically as they become clogged or discolored.
  • Soaking in isopropyl alcohol could extend screen life.

Plastic Flavor Retention

  • Community members who were sensitive to flavor noted that thorough and frequent cleaning of all plastic vapor path components was essential. Some felt that no amount of cleaning could fully eliminate the retained flavors in thermoplastic parts over time.

Common Issues & Fixes

Battery Degradation

Problem: After 1–3 years of regular use, battery capacity noticeably declined.

Manufacturer solution: Storz & Bickel offered battery replacement services during the warranty period. Post-warranty, they were reported to offer only discounted replacement units (e.g., €248 plus return of the old unit), rather than battery-only replacements. This was a significant source of frustration in the community.

Community solution: A community member offered custom-assembled battery packs for user-serviceable replacement. The Mighty requires two battery packs (versus one for the Crafty). These aftermarket packs were offered at approximately $70 USD for a pair plus shipping. Battery replacement required opening the device and soldering or using spot-welded connections. The feasibility of user-replaceable battery sleds was discussed but deemed impractical given the space constraints inside the unit.

Error Codes (Early Units)

Problem: Some early units displayed error codes such as "Err 04" on first use.

Fix: Community members reported that powering the unit off (holding the power button), waiting a few minutes, and restarting typically resolved the issue. Persistent error codes warranted warranty service.

Cooling Unit Connection Point Fragility

Problem: The plastic tabs at the top of the unit where the cooling unit locks into place were identified as a potential weak point. Repeatedly twisting the cooling unit too far, or attaching it backwards, could stress and eventually crack this area.

Fix: Handle the cooling unit gently during attachment and removal. Avoid over-rotation. This issue was more commonly reported on the Crafty but was flagged as a concern for the Mighty as well. Storz & Bickel reportedly covered such damage under warranty in at least some cases.

Display Screen Fragility

Problem: The LED display was noted as appearing vulnerable to damage from drops or pressure.

Fix: No field repair available; exercise care in handling and transport. A protective case was recommended for travel.

Dry / Irritating Vapor

Problem: Some users found the vapor uncomfortably dry, particularly at higher temperatures.

Fix: Using a water pipe adapter (WPA) to run the vapor through water filtration was the most commonly recommended solution. Drawing more slowly could also reduce harshness.

Accessories & Modifications

Dosing Capsules

The Storz & Bickel dosing capsule system was one of the most frequently recommended accessories. Benefits included:

  • Pre-loading multiple sessions for convenience
  • Keeping the oven significantly cleaner
  • Potentially improved flavor consistency (by preventing direct contact between material and chamber walls)
  • Easy post-session disposal

A capsule magazine was also available for carrying multiple pre-loaded capsules.

Water Pipe Adapters (WPA)

The Mighty did not ship with native water pipe compatibility, but aftermarket WPA solutions were widely available and frequently recommended. Adapters replaced the standard cooling unit mouthpiece and allowed connection to 14mm or 18mm water pieces. Community members specifically mentioned adapters marketed as "Easy Flow" water tool adapters for the Crafty/Mighty. Using the Mighty through water was praised for:

  • Cooling and moisturizing vapor
  • Reducing throat irritation
  • Enhancing the overall experience, especially at higher temperatures

One community member noted that the Mighty and Crafty did not pair with water "as easily" as some other portables (such as the Arizer Air with a GonG adapter or the Grasshopper), but that available adapters had adequately resolved this limitation.

Stand / Charging Dock

Various third-party stands were available to keep the Mighty upright during charging and between sessions.

Custom Battery Packs

As discussed in the Common Issues section, aftermarket battery packs were produced by community members for user-serviceable battery replacement, extending the useful life of units beyond manufacturer support.

Comparisons

Mighty vs. Crafty

The most common comparison in the community. Both devices share the same heater and oven design, producing essentially identical vapor quality. Key differences:

FeatureMightyCrafty
BatteryDual cells; longer battery lifeSingle cell; shorter life
Temperature ControlOnboard LED display + buttonsSmartphone app required
Passthrough ChargingYes (can be used while plugged in)No
SizeLargerSmaller, more pocket-friendly
Car ChargingYesNot standard

Community consensus generally favored the Mighty for home use and users who valued independence from a smartphone app, while the Crafty was preferred for situations requiring maximum portability. Battery reliability concerns were more acute with the Crafty due to its single-cell design.

Mighty vs. Arizer Solo / Solo 2

The Solo and Solo 2 were frequently mentioned as alternatives at a lower price point. The Solo featured a glass stem vapor path (preferred by flavor purists), replaceable batteries (in the Air model), and simpler construction. However, the Mighty was generally considered to produce denser vapor with less technique required and to offer a more versatile feature set. For medical users specifically, both the Solo 2 and Mighty were considered top contenders, with the choice often coming down to preference for glass vapor path (Solo 2) versus overall convenience and vapor density (Mighty).

Mighty vs. Arizer Air

The Air's primary advantage was user-replaceable 18650 batteries, making it attractive for camping and off-grid use where charging was unavailable. The Mighty was favored for overall vapor quality and ease of use but lacked battery swappability. One community member who owned both ranked the Mighty first but described the Air as "very very good."

Mighty vs. Plenty

The Plenty, also by Storz & Bickel, was the company's dedicated home-use portable. Community members who compared the two generally concluded that the Mighty "hits just as hard in a small little package," making the Plenty's larger form factor difficult to justify unless vaping with large groups. The Mighty's ability to serve as both a portable and a home unit (especially when plugged in) led many to recommend it over the Plenty.

Mighty vs. Desktop Vaporizers

The Mighty was frequently praised as competitive with desktop units for home use, though community members who owned high-end desktops (Volcano, FlowerPot, VapeXhale EVO, log vapes) generally considered those to deliver harder-hitting, more satisfying vapor. The Mighty was often recommended as a complement to a desktop setup for portable use, or as a versatile single-vaporizer solution for users who could not justify owning both categories. Users seeking the hardest possible hits were typically directed toward desktop solutions such as the Silver Surfer, FlowerPot, or log vapes.

Mighty vs. On-Demand Convection Portables

By the early 2020s, pure convection on-demand portables such as the TinyMight, Tetra P80, and Firewood 7 were increasingly mentioned as alternatives that offered superior flavor and extraction speed. Community members who had transitioned to these devices sometimes described them as "kicking the Mighty out of the park." However, these devices typically required more technique and were produced by smaller manufacturers with less established customer support, leading to longer wait times and availability concerns. The Mighty retained its reputation as the safer, more reliable choice despite not being the cutting edge in vapor quality.

Mighty vs. DynaVap VapCap

For users prioritizing material safety and an all-metal/glass vapor path, the VapCap was sometimes recommended as an alternative. The VapCap used no electronics or plastic in the vapor path, while the Mighty's medically certified thermoplastic construction, though generally considered safe, did not satisfy all health-conscious users. The two devices occupied very different use categories — the VapCap being a butane-heated, on-demand, micro-dosing tool versus the Mighty's electronic, session-based, set-and-forget approach.
