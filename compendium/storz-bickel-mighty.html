# Storz & Bickel Mighty

Overview

The Storz & Bickel Mighty is a portable dry herb vaporizer manufactured by the German company Storz & Bickel, the same firm responsible for the Volcano desktop vaporizer. Originally released around 2014, the Mighty quickly became one of the most widely discussed and recommended portable vaporizers in the community. It utilizes a hybrid heating system combining both conduction and convection elements, powered by a dual lithium-ion battery arrangement. The Mighty was widely regarded as a benchmark portable vaporizer — often described as a "beast" and a "ripper" by community members — though its relatively large size for a portable unit was a frequent point of discussion.

The Mighty occupies an interesting niche: while technically portable, its bulk led some users to consider it more of a "transportable" device rather than a truly pocketable one. One community member noted that the device was purchased as a gift for a relative who "did not like the size," having "only been used a handful of times" before being resold. Despite this, the Mighty's vapor quality, reliability, and overall performance earned it a near-legendary reputation within the vaporizer community.

Community Reception

The Mighty was one of the most consistently praised portable vaporizers in community discussions. It was frequently recommended as a top-tier option for users seeking powerful vapor production, reliable performance, and good flavor.

Pros as identified by the community:

  • Exceptional vapor quality: The hybrid heating system was regarded as producing dense, flavorful vapor. One member described the Mighty as "a beast and a ripper," a sentiment echoed across many discussions.
  • Powerful performance: The device was frequently recommended for users wanting to "get really lifted," with vapor production that rivaled many desktop units.
  • Group-friendly: The Mighty was considered one of the better portable vaporizers for sharing in group sessions, due to its large chamber, consistent output, and session-style heating.
  • Reliability: Storz & Bickel's build quality and the included 2-year warranty were frequently cited as strong points.
  • Session-style heating: The device excels at extended session use, making it ideal for users who prefer to sip on vapor over several minutes.

Cons as identified by the community:

  • Size: The Mighty's bulk was its most commonly cited drawback. It was described as "huge" relative to other portables. Multiple community members noted that its size limited true on-the-go portability.
  • Cleaning can be tedious: One community member described the Mighty as "kind of a pain in the ass to clean," particularly the Cooling Unit assembly.
  • Material efficiency concerns: At least one member referred to the Mighty as a "herb hog," suggesting it may use more material per session than some more efficient alternatives, particularly for solo use.
  • Non-removable batteries: Unlike competitors such as the Arizer Air or ArGo, the Mighty does not feature user-replaceable batteries, which was a concern for longevity.
  • Water pipe pairing was not seamless out of the box: While possible, the Mighty did not natively pair with water tools as easily as some competitors, though third-party adapters eventually addressed this.

The community also noted that the Mighty sat at a premium price point. Used units with accessories were listed at prices around $255–$450 on the community marketplace, often bundled with water tool accessories and dosing capsules.

Tips & Techniques

While the specific thread data available is primarily comparative and marketplace-oriented, several usage insights emerged from community discussions:

  • Dosing Capsules: Storz & Bickel dosing capsules (also referred to as "dosing pods") were popular among Mighty users. These small metal capsules pre-loaded with herb allowed for quick, clean loading and reduced chamber cleanup. Community members actively sought out these capsules, with at least one member posting specifically to acquire "just one or two" pods — a testament to their perceived value.
  • Packing the Chamber: For solo sessions where full efficiency is desired, community wisdom suggested using the dosing capsules or partially filling the chamber rather than packing it full, reducing the "herb hog" reputation the device sometimes carried.
  • Session Pacing: As a session-style vaporizer, the Mighty is designed for extended draw sessions rather than single large hits. Users who preferred on-demand, one-hit style usage sometimes found the Mighty less suited to their needs, though it could still deliver powerful individual draws, particularly at higher temperatures.

Temperature Guide

The Mighty offers a wide temperature range of 40°C–210°C (104°F–410°F). While the available thread data does not provide granular temperature recommendations, the following general guidelines reflect widely held community knowledge about the Mighty:

Temperature RangeEffect Profile
170°C–180°C (338°F–356°F)Flavor-forward, light vapor; ideal for tasting terpenes and for light sessions
180°C–195°C (356°F–383°F)Balanced flavor and vapor density; commonly used "sweet spot" range for daily sessions
195°C–210°C (383°F–410°F)Maximum extraction; thick clouds, strongest effects; may sacrifice some flavor nuance

Many users reported stepping up temperature throughout a session — starting low for flavor and gradually increasing to fully extract the material.

Maintenance & Cleaning

Cleaning was one of the more commonly discussed maintenance concerns with the Mighty. The Cooling Unit — the top section containing the mouthpiece and a series of cooling fins — was identified as the primary area requiring regular attention.

  • Cooling Unit Disassembly: The Cooling Unit can be fully disassembled for cleaning. Regular cleaning is important as resin buildup in the cooling fins can restrict airflow and impact flavor.
  • Isopropyl Alcohol (ISO) Soaking: The plastic components of the Cooling Unit can be soaked in isopropyl alcohol to dissolve resin, though community members exercised caution regarding prolonged soaking of plastic parts.
  • O-Ring Maintenance: Replacement O-rings should be kept on hand, as cleaning solvents and regular wear can degrade them over time.
  • Chamber Brush-Out: The herb chamber should be brushed out regularly between sessions using the included brush.
  • Dosing Capsules Reduce Cleaning Frequency: Using S&B dosing capsules was widely recommended as a way to keep the chamber cleaner for longer, reducing the frequency of deep cleans.

One community member bluntly noted the Mighty was "kind of a pain in the ass to clean," suggesting that while the device performs exceptionally, users should expect regular maintenance as part of ownership.

Common Issues & Fixes

Based on community discussion patterns, the following issues were noted:

  • Battery Degradation Over Time: As with any device using non-removable lithium-ion batteries, long-term battery degradation was a concern. Storz & Bickel's warranty and repair service was the primary recourse for battery-related issues.
  • Size and Portability Complaints: While not a defect, the Mighty's size was a recurring point of friction for users seeking a truly pocketable device. This was often positioned as a known trade-off rather than a fixable issue — users seeking smaller form factors were frequently directed to the Crafty (described as the Mighty's "baby brother") or other portables.
  • Cooling Unit Clogging: Resin accumulation in the Cooling Unit could restrict airflow if not cleaned regularly. Regular maintenance (see above) was the recommended fix.

Accessories & Modifications

Water Pipe Adapters (WPAs)

One of the more active topics around the Mighty was pairing it with water filtration. While the Mighty does not natively include a WPA, the community identified several solutions:

  • Third-Party Easy Flow Water Tool Adapters: Adapters such as the "Crafty/Mighty Easy Flow Water Tool Adapter" (available from retailers like Vapefiend) were discussed as effective solutions for connecting the Mighty to standard glass water pieces. One community member noted that these adapters could potentially double as a glass mouthpiece when not used with water.
  • Hydratubes: The Mighty was sometimes paired with hydratubes (small, self-contained water filtration attachments). At least one marketplace listing bundled a Mighty with a hydratube and separate water tool attachment, indicating this was a popular accessory combination.
  • Pinnacle Watertool (PNWT): While more commonly associated with other portables, the PNWT and similar small bubblers were mentioned in the broader context of pairing portables with water filtration.

One community member noted that the Mighty and Crafty "don't pair with water as easily as the Grasshopper or Air," but acknowledged that third-party adapters had largely resolved this limitation.

Dosing Capsules

Storz & Bickel dosing capsules (also called "dosing pods") were among the most sought-after accessories. These allow pre-loading of herb for quick, clean session changes and reduced chamber maintenance. Community demand for these pods was high enough that individual members posted seeking to buy or trade for even one or two capsules.

Bundled and Aftermarket Accessories

Standard Mighty accessory kits typically included replacement screens, a filling tool, the brush, and the standard mouthpiece assembly. Aftermarket options expanded the ecosystem significantly with glass mouthpieces, WPAs, and third-party Cooling Unit replacements.

Comparisons

Mighty vs. Crafty

The Crafty was frequently described as the Mighty's "baby brother." One community member noted: "The mighty is a beast and a ripper. However Crafty is his baby brother and it meets most of your requirements at a lower price." The primary differences centered on battery life (the Mighty's dual batteries providing significantly longer sessions than the Crafty's single battery), size (the Crafty being more compact), and price (the Crafty being more affordable). The Crafty also featured Bluetooth app control, which the original Mighty lacked. For users prioritizing portability over battery life, the Crafty was often recommended as an alternative.

Mighty vs. Arizer ArGo

This comparison highlighted fundamentally different design philosophies. One community member described the contrast succinctly: "Argo is a sipper while the Mighty is a ripper." The ArGo was praised for its compact, truly pocketable form factor, replaceable batteries, and efficiency for solo use. The Mighty was favored for group sessions, vapor density, and raw performance. The ArGo was considered a poor choice for group use, while the Mighty was seen as less than ideal for discreet, on-the-go use. Users seeking both qualities sometimes acquired both devices for different use cases.

Mighty vs. Arizer Air / Air II

One community member stated they would "take [the Air II] over a Mighty," citing easy loading, high efficiency, easy cleaning, and replaceable batteries as advantages. However, the Air II was described as "not terribly portable without a case." The Mighty was acknowledged as superior for single-user sessions where maximum effect was desired, but the Arizer devices were favored by users who prioritized efficiency, ease of cleaning, and battery replaceability.

Mighty vs. Grasshopper

In the context of water pipe pairing, the Grasshopper was noted as being easier to connect to glass than the Mighty. One community member stated that the Grasshopper was their "personal favorite portable vape" but acknowledged that the Mighty/Crafty represented a step up in overall vapor quality, noting: "Just doesn't get better than that unless you step up to a Mighty/Crafty."

Mighty vs. DaVinci IQ

At least one community member was specifically seeking to upgrade from the DaVinci IQ, listing the Mighty among their top candidates. The thread context suggests the IQ was seen as adequate for portable and group use but that the Mighty represented a meaningful upgrade in vapor quality, flavor, and smoothness.
