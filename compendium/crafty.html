# Crafty+

Overview

The Crafty+ is a portable dry herb vaporizer manufactured by Storz & Bickel, the German company also known for producing the Volcano desktop vaporizer and the Mighty/Mighty+ portable units. The Crafty+ is an updated version of the original Crafty, featuring improvements to battery life, build quality, and connectivity. It utilizes a hybrid heating system combining conduction and convection elements, powered by an internal rechargeable battery. The device is controlled in part through a companion smartphone app, which allows for precise temperature adjustment and usage tracking, including cumulative operating hours.

The Crafty+ occupies a specific niche in the Storz & Bickel lineup as the more compact, pocket-friendly sibling of the Mighty+. It was released as an iterative improvement over the original Crafty, with a later revision adding USB-C charging to replace the earlier micro-USB port. Community discussion distinguished between the pre-USB-C and USB-C versions of the Crafty+, with the USB-C model generally considered the more desirable revision.

Community Reception

The Crafty+ was widely regarded within the community as producing excellent vapor quality, inheriting the same hybrid heating platform that earned the Mighty its strong reputation. It was frequently referenced as a benchmark for portable vapor quality, with one community member noting that "one bowl can usually get several seasoned stoners high" — a comment made in the context of both Crafty and Mighty devices being used to test extraction efficiency.

Pros identified by the community:

  • Outstanding vapor quality and extraction efficiency
  • Compact, pocketable size compared to the Mighty
  • Precise temperature control via app
  • Well-built with high-quality German manufacturing
  • Effective for both THC and CBD flower extraction

Cons identified by the community:

  • Battery life was the most frequently cited drawback. One long-term user described the battery life as "insufficient," particularly for heavy or medical use throughout a full day. This was the primary motivator for users considering upgrades to the Mighty+ or other devices with larger batteries.
  • Reliance on the smartphone app for full temperature control was seen as a limitation by some
  • Price point, while justified by quality, placed it in premium territory — used units with accessories were observed selling in the EU market at approximately €80–90 per unit

Community members who sold their Crafty+ units reported typical usage in the range of 45–60+ operating hours before resale, suggesting moderate longevity, though battery degradation over time remained a concern.

Tips & Techniques

Loading

  • Full chamber packing: For maximum extraction, the community generally recommended a full, evenly ground load with a medium-fine grind. The hybrid heating system benefits from consistent material contact with the oven walls (conduction element) while allowing airflow through the material (convection element).
  • CBD flower usage: One community member who was a CBD shop owner and chronic pain patient used the Crafty+ extensively for CBD-dominant strains (below 0.3% THC). For medical users seeking maximum cannabinoid extraction, the community emphasized the importance of thorough extraction — vaping until the material is well-spent — as every available cannabinoid matters during pain episodes.
  • Dosing capsules: These stainless steel capsules allow pre-loading and keep the chamber cleaner, reducing maintenance frequency.

Draw Technique

  • Slow, steady draws are generally recommended to allow the hybrid heater to maintain temperature and maximize convection contribution.

Efficiency Considerations

  • Community discussion noted that desktop vaporizers like the E-Nano could achieve comparable or superior extraction with less material. Users switching between a Crafty+ and a desktop unit observed that they could "use less weed overall for the same result" with an efficient desktop log vaporizer, suggesting the Crafty+ — while highly capable for a portable — may not match the absolute efficiency of top-tier desktop units.

Temperature Guide

The Crafty+ allows precise temperature selection via its companion app. While the thread data does not include extensively detailed temperature recommendations specific to this model, general community wisdom for hybrid Storz & Bickel devices applied:

Temperature RangeEffect Profile
170°C–180°C (338°F–356°F)Flavor-forward, lighter effects; good for terpene-rich early draws
185°C–195°C (365°F–383°F)Balanced extraction; good vapor production with flavor retention
200°C–210°C (392°F–410°F)Maximum extraction; dense clouds; best for thorough cannabinoid extraction; recommended for medical/pain use

For CBD-dominant flower, where the goal is comprehensive cannabinoid and terpene extraction rather than psychoactive effect, higher temperatures near the top of the range were implicitly favored by community members focused on pain management.

Maintenance & Cleaning

The Crafty+ shares its cooling unit design with the Mighty, and maintenance practices were discussed in community threads:

  • Cooling unit: The most maintenance-intensive component. Regular disassembly and cleaning of the cooling unit is essential to maintain airflow and vapor quality. Isopropyl alcohol (ISO) soaking is the standard cleaning method.
  • Air intake: One community member specifically noted cleaning the air intake and removing accumulated dust as part of thorough device maintenance before resale, indicating this is an area that requires periodic attention.
  • Chamber: Regular brushing after sessions; periodic ISO cleaning of the oven with a cotton swab.
  • Screens: Replacement screens should be swapped when airflow becomes restricted despite cleaning.

Compared to some competitors, the Crafty+ was noted as requiring moderate maintenance — more involved than the Arizer Solo II (which was praised for having "almost zero maintenance"), but manageable with regular attention.

Common Issues & Fixes

Insufficient Battery Life

This was the dominant complaint. The Crafty+ battery was frequently described as inadequate for heavy users, particularly those with medical needs requiring multiple sessions throughout the day.

Workarounds discussed:

  • Carrying a portable USB-C power bank for mid-day charging (USB-C revision only)
  • Upgrading to the Mighty+ for its significantly larger battery
  • Using the Crafty+ as a secondary portable while relying on a desktop unit at home

Pre-USB-C vs. USB-C Revision

Community members distinguished between the two hardware revisions. The pre-USB-C model used micro-USB charging, which was slower and less convenient. When selling or purchasing used units, the presence or absence of USB-C was noted as a distinguishing factor affecting value.

No Effects from Vaping

In a broader troubleshooting discussion, the Crafty (alongside the Mighty) was recommended as a diagnostic tool — if a user experienced no effects from cannabis, testing with a single bowl from a Crafty or Mighty was suggested as a reliable way to determine whether the issue was the device, the material, or the user's physiology, given the known extraction efficiency of these devices.

Accessories & Modifications

Water Pipe Adapters (WPA) — "Muzzmods"

A notable community modification involved creating glass mouthpiece adapters for the Crafty+ cooling unit, colloquially known as "muzzmods." These modifications used a 14mm female to 18mm male glass adapter attached to the cooling unit, allowing the Crafty+ to be connected to standard water pipes for cooled, filtered vapor.

Key details from community discussion:

  • The modification required a Crafty/Mighty cooling unit (~$20 at the time) and a 14mm F / 18mm M glass adapter
  • Glass adapters were sourced from various suppliers, with community members seeking affordable options to keep total cost reasonable
  • One active community member produced and sold these custom adapters, noting materials and labor costs that put finished units in the $40+ range

Dosing Capsules

Standard Storz & Bickel dosing capsules fit the Crafty+ and were widely used for convenience and cleanliness.

Third-Party Accessories

  • Replacement cooling units
  • Replacement screens and seal rings
  • Various carrying cases

Comparisons

Crafty+ vs. Mighty / Mighty+

The most common comparison in community discussion. The two devices share the same hybrid heating platform and produce comparable vapor quality. The critical differentiator is battery life — the Mighty/Mighty+ features a significantly larger battery (dual cells vs. the Crafty+'s single cell), making it the preferred choice for heavy users and those with medical needs requiring extended daily use. One long-term Crafty+ user explicitly cited battery insufficiency as their primary reason for considering the Mighty+ as an upgrade. The trade-off is size: the Crafty+ is notably more compact and pocket-friendly.

Crafty+ vs. Arizer Solo II

The Solo II was recommended by community members as an alternative for users prioritizing battery life and low maintenance. Specific advantages cited for the Solo II included:

  • "Huge battery"
  • "Consistent results"
  • "Big clouds"
  • "Almost zero maintenance (cleaning, just debowl the stems)"
  • Inexpensive replacement stems and WPAs available on secondary markets

The Solo II relies more heavily on convection heating compared to the Crafty+'s hybrid approach, resulting in somewhat different vapor characteristics.

Crafty+ vs. Desktop Vaporizers (E-Nano)

Community members who owned both the Crafty+ and desktop log vaporizers like the E-Nano noted that the desktop units could achieve equivalent or superior extraction using less material. For home use, particularly for heavy consumers or medical patients, a desktop vaporizer was often recommended as the primary unit, with the Crafty+ serving as the portable complement.

Crafty+ vs. DynaVap Vapcap

The butane-powered Vapcap was suggested as an alternative for users seeking extreme efficiency and independence from battery life concerns. Community members noted the Vapcap's very small chamber size and exceptional material efficiency, though the Crafty+ user who was offered this suggestion expressed skepticism about lower-priced vaporizers based on past negative experiences with budget devices (MFLB, VapBong, HotBox).
