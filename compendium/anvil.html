# Anvil by Vestratto

Overview

The Anvil is a flame-powered portable dry herb vaporizer designed and manufactured by Vestratto, a company based in North America. First announced in late 2021 and shipping to early adopters by late December 2021, the Anvil is a butane torch-heated device that draws clear design lineage from the DynaVap VapCap family while introducing significant engineering refinements intended to address common criticisms of that platform.

The device employs a hybrid conduction/convection heating method. Its defining engineering feature is a tri-layer construction: an outer and inner skin of stainless steel sandwiching a copper core. This copper-stainless sandwich design is intended to distribute heat more evenly across the herb chamber than single-material alternatives. The Anvil uses a temperature clicker mechanism—similar in concept to the DynaVap's click—but Vestratto designed the clicker to measure bowl temperature rather than cap temperature, a distinction the community regarded as a meaningful improvement in temperature accuracy and consistency.

The device was marketed with the tagline "1hit 1derful," reflecting its optimization for full extraction of a loaded bowl in a single extended draw, a technique the community commonly referred to as "riding the line."

Community Reception

The vaporizer community's reception of the Anvil was broadly enthusiastic, particularly among users who had found the DynaVap platform inconsistent or technique-dependent. Several key themes emerged from community discussion:

Praised aspects:

  • One-hit extraction capability: The Anvil's ability to fully extract a bowl in a single long draw (approximately 90 seconds) was widely praised. Community members noted that the device was specifically engineered for this purpose and required minimal user skill to achieve it, unlike competing devices where full extraction in one hit demanded advanced technique.
  • Build quality and engineering: The tri-layer copper/stainless construction was regarded as a well-executed engineering solution. One community member noted that the Anvil had "twice the mass" of a DynaVap with "no cutaways or heat dispersing fins," contributing to its heat retention capabilities.
  • Superior airflow: Multiple community members highlighted the Anvil's airflow design as a significant improvement. The adjustable airflow passes directly through the herb, which members attributed to better vapor quality and more consistent extraction.
  • Swappable chambers: The ability to pre-load multiple chambers and swap them quickly was considered a standout feature, particularly for portable and outdoor use.
  • Clicker accuracy: The fact that the clicker measures actual bowl temperature rather than cap temperature was seen as a fundamental design improvement that reduced user error.
  • Cool-down safety: Community members noted that the cool-down click occurs almost immediately after removing the chamber from heat, making it essentially impossible to accidentally reheat before the device has properly cooled. This was viewed as a meaningful safety advantage.
  • Flavor on first hit: Users who prioritized terpene-forward flavor found the single-heat-cycle design appealing, as it concentrated all flavor into one draw rather than diminishing over multiple heat cycles.

Criticisms and concerns:

  • Perceived fragility: Some community members described the Anvil as feeling more "fragile" compared to more robust competitors, despite the all-metal construction. One member who compared it to the Dani Fusion described the Anvil as "more engineered" but less tank-like in hand feel.
  • Price point: At $325–$345 for a full kit, the Anvil represented a significant investment for a butane-powered device. Vestratto was noted for not running sales, which some members appreciated (as it removed temptation) while others found it a barrier to entry.
  • Flavor trade-offs: At least one self-described "flavour chaser" in the community indicated that the Anvil was not their preferred device for pure flavor, suggesting that while extraction efficiency was excellent, some dedicated flavor-focused devices may have had an edge in that specific dimension.
  • Learning curve (modest): While considerably easier to master than a DynaVap according to community consensus, the Anvil still required users to learn its torch technique and airflow adjustment for optimal results.

Overall, the community regarded the Anvil as one of the most significant innovations in the flame-powered vaporizer category, with many members describing it as a clear step forward from the DynaVap in terms of engineering sophistication and extraction consistency.

Tips & Techniques

"Riding the Line" — Single-Hit Full Extraction

The Anvil was explicitly designed for a technique the community called "riding the line": fully extracting an entire bowl in one continuous draw. The general approach:

Load the chamber — Use either the full-size or half-size bowl. A moderately packed chamber (not overly tight) allows airflow through the herb, which is critical to the Anvil's convective extraction.

Heat with a torch — Apply heat evenly around the body of the Anvil, rotating the device to ensure the copper core distributes heat uniformly to the bowl.

Listen for the click — The temperature clicker indicates when the bowl has reached vaporization temperature. Because the clicker measures bowl temperature directly, the signal is more reliable than cap-based indicators on other devices.

Draw slowly and steadily — After the click, take a long, controlled draw lasting up to approximately 90 seconds. The Anvil's thermal mass sustains vaporization temperature throughout this extended draw.

Use the adjustable airflow — Dial in airflow restriction to personal preference. More restriction typically produces denser vapor; more open airflow can improve flavor clarity.

Water Pipe Use

Many community members preferred running the Anvil through water, using the 14mm WPA accessory. Users seeking portable water filtration for outdoor activities (hiking, camping) explored pairing the WPA with compact 14mm-compatible pieces.

Chamber Pre-Loading

A widely recommended technique was to pre-load multiple chambers before a session or outing. The magnetic chamber remover in the utility tube facilitated quick swaps, turning what might otherwise be a cumbersome reload process into a few-second operation.

Heating After Cool-Down Click

The Anvil's cool-down click occurs almost immediately upon removing the device from heat. Community members noted this as both a safety feature and a practical timing aid—users should wait for the cool-down click before beginning a new heat cycle to avoid overheating and potential combustion.

Temperature Guide

The Anvil does not feature electronic temperature control; temperature is managed through torch technique and the audible click indicator. The community's guidance focused on technique rather than precise temperature targets:

ApproachTechniqueExpected Result
Flavor-forwardHeat gently, stop at or slightly before the clickLower extraction temperature; terpene-rich, lighter vapor
Full extraction (recommended)Heat to the click, then draw steadily for ~90 secondsComplete extraction in a single draw; robust vapor with full cannabinoid profile
Maximum cloud productionHeat slightly past the click (with caution)Dense, thick clouds; reduced flavor; increased combustion risk

The community generally advised new users to respect the click and resist the temptation to heat past it, as the Anvil's substantial thermal mass means that residual heat continues to rise even after the torch is removed. The margin between full extraction and combustion is narrower than on lighter-mass devices.

Note: Precise temperature ranges in degrees were not commonly discussed in the community for this device, as the click mechanism serves as the primary temperature reference point. Community members generally trusted the clicker rather than attempting to target specific Celsius/Fahrenheit values.

Maintenance & Cleaning

While detailed cleaning protocols were not extensively documented in the available community discussions, general maintenance principles for the Anvil follow those common to stainless steel and copper vaporizers:

  • Herb chambers: Remove and clean regularly. Isopropyl alcohol (ISO) is the standard cleaning agent for stainless steel components. Soak chambers periodically and use pipe cleaners or cotton swabs to remove residue.
  • Airpath: Ensure the adjustable airflow mechanism remains free of debris. Buildup in the air path can restrict flow and reduce vapor quality.
  • Copper core (external): The copper layer is sandwiched between stainless steel skins and does not require direct maintenance, but the external stainless steel can be wiped down to maintain appearance.
  • Clicker mechanism: Keep the clicker area clean to ensure reliable temperature indication. Debris accumulation could potentially affect click timing.
  • Utility tube: The magnetic chamber remover and tube should be kept clean for hygienic storage of loaded chambers.

One community member mentioned having cleaned their device within days of receipt after approximately 5 heat cycles, suggesting that even minimal use leaves enough residue to warrant cleaning before storage.

Accessories & Modifications

Vestratto offered a range of official accessories for the Anvil:

  • Half-size chamber — For smaller loads (~50% of the full-size bowl capacity). Useful for solo sessions or microdosing.
  • Full-size chamber — Included with the base kit; holds slightly more than a standard DynaVap bowl.
  • XL Mouthpiece — An extended mouthpiece option for improved cooling and comfort.
  • 14mm Water Pipe Adapter (WPA) — Allows the Anvil to be used with standard 14mm water pieces. Community members particularly valued this for through-water use, and at least one member sought a durable 14mm bowl stem for portable outdoor use with the WPA.
  • Utility Tube — A storage tube with a built-in magnetic chamber remover. Functions as both a carrying case and a tool.

Community Modifications

  • Portable water pipe setups: Members explored compact 14mm-compatible glass and wood pieces for use with the Anvil WPA during hiking and camping.
  • Pre-loaded chamber kits: Some users assembled sets of pre-loaded chambers in utility tubes for extended outings—effectively creating a "magazine" system for rapid reloading.

Available Kits

At launch, the Anvil was available in configurations including:

  • Founder's Edition — An early-release edition with distinct finishing, sought after by collectors.
  • User's Kit — The standard kit, available in colorways such as "Shift Blue" and "Silver Mist," typically including the Anvil body, one full-size chamber, and a mouthpiece. Chambers, utility tubes, and the XL mouthpiece could be added à la carte.

Comparisons

Anvil vs. DynaVap VapCap

This was the most commonly drawn comparison in the community, given the devices' shared flame-powered, click-indicating design philosophy. Community consensus strongly favored the Anvil in most categories:

One community member succinctly noted: "They are 2 different vapes," suggesting that despite surface similarities, the user experience diverges significantly. Another reported that their DynaVap had been "in a drawer for a year" after acquiring an Anvil, citing inconsistent extraction and declining flavor across multiple heat cycles as the primary reasons for the switch.

The observation that DynaVap eventually introduced a copper cap option was noted with some amusement in the community, though members pointed out that adding copper to the cap alone would not replicate the Anvil's overall thermal design, mass, or airflow characteristics.

Anvil vs. Dani Fusion

A smaller but notable comparison discussion emerged between the Anvil and the Dani Fusion (also referred to as the Dani BFG):

  • The Anvil was described as "more engineered," producing impressive clouds with minimal effort, particularly on first use.
  • The Dani Fusion was characterized as "more robust, like a tank," with a build quality that inspired more confidence in durability.
  • In terms of extraction capability, community members who owned both found no dramatic difference in performance, with the primary distinctions being taste (with some preferring the Fusion's flavor profile) and timing (the Anvil required less counting after the click).
  • Community opinion was divided on whether owning both was worthwhile, with some members viewing them as sufficiently different to justify both, while others found the overlap too significant.
  • One flavor-focused community member explicitly noted that they stopped considering the Anvil because their priorities aligned more closely with the Dani Fusion's characteristics.
