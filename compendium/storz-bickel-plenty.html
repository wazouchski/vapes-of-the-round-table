# Storz & Bickel Plenty

Overview

The Storz & Bickel Plenty is a corded handheld (sometimes described as "semi-desktop") dry herb vaporizer manufactured by the German company Storz & Bickel, known primarily for the Volcano balloon vaporizer. The Plenty was designed as a more affordable, direct-draw alternative to the Volcano, featuring a distinctive power-tool-like form factor with a pistol grip, a large stainless steel cooling coil, and a generous herb chamber. It utilizes a hybrid convection/conduction heating system and is powered by mains electricity via a cord, placing it in the uncommon category of "corded handheld" vaporizers — not quite desktop, not quite portable.

The Plenty was released in the early 2010s and was produced for many years before being eventually discontinued. It earned a reputation within the vaporizer community as a high-capacity session vaporizer well-suited for heavy users and group sessions, though its efficiency with small loads was frequently debated.

Community Reception

The vaporizer community regarded the Plenty as a capable and well-built device, though opinion was somewhat divided on its overall value proposition relative to other options in its price range.

Pros frequently cited:

  • Large chamber capacity: The Plenty was widely recognized as one of the highest-capacity vaporizers available, easily accommodating loads exceeding one gram. This made it a popular recommendation for heavy users. As one community member noted when advising a user who consumed multiple grams daily, "The Plenty holds a lot." It was frequently recommended alongside the Volcano for users seeking high-capacity solutions.
  • Storz & Bickel build quality: The "premium" reputation of Storz & Bickel was acknowledged by the community, and the Plenty benefited from this brand cachet. One member candidly admitted being "drawn to the mystique of Storz and Bickel's 'premium' reputation."
  • Vapor quality: When loaded generously and used at appropriate temperatures, the Plenty was praised for producing thick, satisfying vapor.
  • Reliability: The unit was considered durable and built for heavy, sustained use. Community members expressed confidence in its ability to operate continuously for extended periods.
  • Unique form factor: Its corded handheld design appealed to users who wanted something more ergonomic or personal than a traditional tabletop unit.

Cons frequently cited:

  • Efficiency concerns with small loads: The large chamber, while a benefit for heavy users, was widely seen as a drawback for those with lighter consumption habits. Users who vaped the equivalent of one or two small bowls per day found it excessive.
  • Dry, hot vapor: Multiple community members reported that the Plenty produced vapor that could be drying and irritating to the throat. One member who was a singer reported that using the Plenty "nonstop almost daily" caused their voice to become "really raspy/cracking, in a way that feels much worse than smoking." Other community members confirmed hearing similar complaints about throat dryness with the Plenty.
  • Difficulty pairing with water filtration: Unlike many desktop vaporizers, the Plenty was described as "not ideal for pairing with water," though community members confirmed it could be adapted with some effort.
  • Size and bulk: Compared to log vaporizers and other corded handhelds, the Plenty was considered quite large. One community member stated plainly that "the LSV and Plenty are just too big."
  • Price: At its retail price point, some community members felt the Plenty was outperformed by less expensive alternatives. Several members suggested that units like the E-Nano or Underdog offered better value.
  • Analog temperature control: The lack of a digital display or precise temperature readout was considered a limitation by some users who preferred more exact control.

Despite being discontinued, the Plenty retained a loyal following. One community member expressed that they "kinda regret getting rid of the Plenty" after parting with it, suggesting it had a lasting impression even among users with large collections. Used units were sought after on the secondhand market, with community members looking to purchase them for around $100.

Tips & Techniques

The community developed several key techniques for getting the best performance from the Plenty:

Loading

  • For heavy users: Pack the chamber fully — the Plenty was designed to handle large loads and performs best when given sufficient material. Loads of one gram or more were common among heavy users.
  • For lighter users: While the Plenty can technically be used with smaller loads, the community generally acknowledged that its efficiency suffered compared to vaporizers with smaller chambers. Some users employed the included liquid pad or a concentrate pad to reduce effective chamber volume when using smaller amounts.

Drawing Technique

  • Small sips vs. large rips: Community members recommended taking several smaller "sips" of vapor rather than attempting massive single draws. As one experienced member advised, "Several small 'sips' of vapor will get you just as medicated as one giant rip would, but without as much risk of coughing and/or irritation." This was especially important for users who experienced throat dryness.
  • The Plenty benefits from a slow, steady draw rather than hard, fast inhalation.

Temperature Management

  • Start low: For flavor and throat comfort, the community consistently recommended starting at lower temperatures and gradually increasing.
  • Avoid maximum temperature for extended sessions: Running the Plenty at its highest settings for prolonged periods was noted as a primary cause of throat irritation and dryness.

Hydration

  • Community members strongly recommended staying well-hydrated when using the Plenty, as the dry vapor characteristic of the device was consistently mentioned. One experienced member emphasized: "Drink lots of water too, I can't emphasize that enough."

Whip Adaptation

While the Plenty is not a traditional whip-style vaporizer, community members noted that "you could always add an actual whip to it," expanding its versatility. This was considered a useful modification for those who preferred a more relaxed draw style or wanted to connect the device to water filtration.

Temperature Guide

The Plenty uses an analog dial rather than a precise digital display, so community temperature recommendations were often discussed in approximate terms or by reference to the dial position:

340°F–370°F (170°C–188°C)Balanced flavor and effectGood starting point for most sessions
370°F–391°F (188°C–199°C)Fuller extraction, thicker cloudsCommunity-recommended upper range for daily use. One experienced member noted that going "too much past temp 391" significantly increased throat irritation and recommended water filtration above this point.
391°F–395°F (199°C–202°C)Maximum extractionProduces the densest clouds but with noticeably harsher vapor; recommended only with water filtration or for finishing bowls

Community sweet spot: Approximately 370°F–391°F (188°C–199°C) appeared to be the most commonly recommended range for daily use, balancing vapor production with comfort. The community widely agreed that reducing temperature was the first and most effective step for addressing throat irritation.

Maintenance & Cleaning

The Plenty's stainless steel cooling coil is the primary component requiring regular maintenance. Key cleaning considerations discussed by the community include:

  • Cooling coil: The long coiled vapor path collects residue over time and should be cleaned regularly. Isopropyl alcohol soaking is effective for the metal components.
  • Screens and chamber: Screens should be replaced or cleaned periodically. The chamber itself can be brushed out after sessions.
  • Mouthpiece: Regular cleaning to prevent buildup that can affect flavor and airflow.
  • Reclaim: The cooling coil accumulates significant amounts of condensed resin, which some community members collected and repurposed.

The community noted that the Plenty's many metal parts are generally durable and tolerant of alcohol-based cleaning, making maintenance relatively straightforward if performed regularly.

Common Issues & Fixes

Dry / Harsh Vapor

Problem: The most commonly reported issue with the Plenty was dry, irritating vapor that could cause throat discomfort, coughing, and voice problems.

Solutions:

Lower the temperature — this was the single most recommended fix.

Take smaller, gentler draws rather than large rips.

Add water filtration by rigging a whip to a bubbler (see Accessories section).

Stay well-hydrated during sessions.

Use an extended mouthpiece or additional cooling.

Inefficiency with Small Loads

Problem: The large chamber was poorly suited for users with low consumption, leading to perceived waste of material.

Solutions:

Use the included liquid pad to reduce effective chamber size.

Consider a different vaporizer for solo, low-consumption use (the community frequently recommended log vaporizers like the E-Nano for this purpose).

Limited Water Filtration Compatibility

Problem: The Plenty's mouthpiece design did not lend itself naturally to water pipe connections.

Solutions:

Adapt a whip setup for connection to a bubbler.

Use silicone adapters (community members referenced adapters compatible with Storz & Bickel mouthpieces in 14mm and 18mm sizes).

DIY solutions using silicone tubing.

Accessories & Modifications

Water Pipe Adapters (WPA)

The Plenty was not designed with native water pipe compatibility, but the community found several workarounds:

  • Silicone adapters: Community members referenced silicone adapters (such as those made by community artisans) that could fit between the Plenty's mouthpiece and standard 14mm or 18mm water pipe joints. These were reported to work "very very well" and produce "major cloudage."
  • Glass mouthpiece adapters: Glass mouthpieces designed for Storz & Bickel's portable units (Crafty/Mighty) were sometimes adapted for use with the Plenty's cooling unit connection.
  • DIY whip setups: Running silicone or food-grade tubing from the Plenty's mouthpiece to a small bubbler was a commonly suggested modification for adding moisture to the vapor path.

Other Accessories

  • Liquid pads: Included with the Plenty, these could be used for concentrates or to reduce effective chamber size for smaller dry herb loads.
  • Replacement screens: Standard maintenance item; the community recommended keeping spares on hand.
  • Extended mouthpieces or cooling stems: Some users experimented with additional cooling elements to reduce vapor temperature before inhalation.

Comparisons

The Plenty was frequently compared to other vaporizers in community discussions:

Plenty vs. Volcano (Storz & Bickel)

Both from the same manufacturer and sharing similar heating technology, the Volcano was considered the more versatile and proven unit with its balloon delivery system. The Volcano was noted for its ability to hold "several grams easy" and could "run 24/7 with no issue." The Plenty offered a more affordable entry point into Storz & Bickel's lineup and a direct-draw experience, but lacked the Volcano's balloon system and its established track record for heavy, continuous use.

Plenty vs. Life Saber (LSV) by 7th Floor

This was a common comparison in the corded handheld category. Community consensus leaned toward the LSV for most users, particularly those who valued:

  • Water pipe compatibility (the LSV excelled through a bubbler, with one member calling it their "favorite through a bubbler")
  • An all-glass vapor path
  • More versatile draw characteristics ("hits ranging from subtle and tasty to billowing clouds")
  • Lower price point

One member recommended the LSV "over the Plenty" outright, while noting both were "just too big" compared to smaller alternatives. The Plenty's advantage was its larger chamber for heavy users and its Storz & Bickel build quality.

Plenty vs. Log Vaporizers (E-Nano, Underdog)

For users with moderate or low consumption, the community frequently steered people toward log vaporizers instead of the Plenty. The E-Nano in particular was described as "a vastly superior vaporizer as well as being much more efficient" by one member, at a significantly lower price point (approximately $150 less). Log vaporizers excelled at small-load efficiency — the Plenty's principal weakness — while offering comparable or superior vapor quality. The Plenty's advantage remained its large chamber capacity for heavy users.

Plenty vs. Silver Surfer (SSV) by 7th Floor

The SSV was occasionally mentioned as a whip-based alternative offering similar functionality in a more traditional desktop form factor, with some users preferring the SSV's whip delivery system over the Plenty's direct-draw cooling coil design.

This article is based on community discussions spanning approximately 2012–2022, drawn from a popular vaporizer enthusiast forum. The Storz & Bickel Plenty has since been discontinued, though it remains available on the secondhand market and retains a following among heavy-use enthusiasts.
