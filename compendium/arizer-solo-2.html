# Arizer Solo 2

Overview

The Arizer Solo 2 is a portable dry herb vaporizer manufactured by Arizer, a Canadian company based in Waterloo, Ontario. Released in 2017 as the successor to the original Arizer Solo, the Solo 2 is a session-style conduction/convection hybrid vaporizer powered by an internal rechargeable lithium-ion battery. It features Arizer's signature glass stem system, in which herb is packed into a borosilicate glass tube that inserts into a stainless steel oven atop the unit. The Solo 2 quickly became one of the most widely recommended entry-level and mid-range portable vaporizers in the vaporizer community, valued for its simplicity, build quality, flavor performance, and long battery life.

The device charges via a 12V DC barrel-style charger and features an OLED digital display with precise single-degree temperature control. It retains the cylindrical form factor of the original Solo but with a more refined design and significantly improved heat-up time and battery capacity.

Community Reception

The Arizer Solo 2 was widely regarded in the vaporizer community as one of the best values in the portable vaporizer market. It was frequently recommended to newcomers seeking a reliable, easy-to-use device that prioritized flavor and simplicity. Community members praised the following attributes:

Pros:

  • Flavor quality: The all-glass airpath through the stem was appreciated for delivering clean, flavorful vapor, particularly at lower and mid-range temperatures. The Solo 2 was often cited as a strong flavor performer in its price class.
  • Battery life: With approximately 3 hours of use per charge, the Solo 2 was noted for having one of the longest battery lives among portable vaporizers.
  • Build quality and reliability: The simple design with few moving parts contributed to the device's reputation for durability and longevity. Many community members reported years of consistent use.
  • Ease of use: The straightforward glass stem loading system and digital display made the Solo 2 approachable for beginners.
  • Glass stem system: The interchangeable glass stems were seen as a key advantage, allowing easy cleaning and replacement. The stem-as-bowl design meant the oven itself stayed relatively clean.
  • Session experience: At higher temperatures (around 220°C/428°F), the device was noted to "hit strong," satisfying users seeking more substantial effects.

Cons:

  • Draw resistance: The Solo 2 was consistently noted for having a somewhat restricted airflow. Multiple community members described the draw as requiring "long, slow" inhales rather than quick, open pulls. One community member who owned a Solo 2, Mighty+, and Airvape Legacy Pro noted that none of these devices were "really open" in terms of airflow, and sought alternatives with more open draws.
  • Airpath cleanliness concerns: One knowledgeable community member pointed out that the Solo 2's airpath was "not 100% clean," noting that air could enter through the 12V charger port in addition to the designated inlet air holes. While the member acknowledged the device still tasted acceptable, they rated its flavor below that of pure convection devices with fully isolated airpaths.
  • Harshness at higher temperatures: At least one long-time Arizer user reported that the Solo 2's vapor could be harsh, particularly for those with sensitive lungs or throats. This user, who had previously used an Arizer Air as a daily driver for years, found the Solo 2 too irritating and ultimately sold it.
  • Session-only usage: As a session vaporizer, the Solo 2 lacked on-demand capability, which some users found limiting compared to convection on-demand devices.
  • Portability: While technically portable, the cylindrical body combined with the protruding glass stem made the Solo 2 less pocket-friendly than some competitors. Community members developed creative stealth solutions (such as fitting the stem inside a fast-food cup) to address this.

The device held its value reasonably well on the secondhand market in the community's classifieds section, with used units typically listed between $99–$125 USD depending on condition and included accessories. Brand new units with extras occasionally appeared at $200–$220 USD/CAD.

One Reddit user's enthusiastic post about switching from smoking to the Solo 2 garnered over 2,100 upvotes in the r/vaporents community, underscoring the device's role as a gateway vaporizer for many users transitioning away from combustion.

Tips & Techniques

  • Grind and pack: A medium grind works well. Pack the herb into the glass stem loosely — do not tamp it down tightly, as this increases draw resistance (which is already somewhat restricted on the Solo 2).
  • Stem insertion: Insert the loaded glass stem into the heated oven gently with a slight twist. The o-ring seal should provide a snug fit.
  • Slow, steady draws: Due to the Solo 2's draw resistance, the community widely recommended long, slow sipping draws rather than quick pulls. This technique allows the heater to keep up with airflow and produces the best vapor quality.
  • Temperature stepping: Many users advocated starting sessions at a lower temperature (around 170–185°C / 338–365°F) and gradually stepping up in increments of 5–10°C over the course of the session. This approach extracts lighter terpenes and flavor compounds first before moving to full extraction at higher temperatures.
  • Stealth use: A well-known community tip involved inserting the Solo 2 with its stem into a fast-food beverage cup with a straw hole in the lid. The Planetvape stealth adapter was also referenced as a purpose-built solution for discreet use. One viral Reddit post demonstrating this technique received nearly 1,900 upvotes.
  • Dry burn cycles: Before first use, run several dry heating cycles (without herb) at maximum temperature to burn off any manufacturing residues. One seller mentioned running "a few dry cycles" on their unit before use.
  • Stem as storage: Pre-pack multiple stems with herb and cap them with silicone stem caps for convenient on-the-go use. Sellers and community members frequently mentioned stem caps as a useful accessory.

Community members who described themselves as "flavor chasers" tended to spend more time in the lower ranges, while those prioritizing efficiency and effect worked toward the upper limit. The maximum setting of 220°C (428°F) was frequently referenced as the endpoint for full extraction sessions.

Maintenance & Cleaning

The Solo 2's glass stem system significantly simplifies cleaning, as most residue accumulates on the glass rather than inside the device itself.

  • Glass stems: Soak in isopropyl alcohol (90% or higher recommended) for 15–30 minutes, then rinse thoroughly with warm water. For heavier buildup, use a pipe cleaner or cotton swab while soaking. Some community members recommended saving the reclaim from stems, as the honey-colored residue ("stem milk") can be reclaimed by simmering stems in milk or a fat-containing liquid.
  • Oven: Periodically brush out the stainless steel oven with the included stirring tool or a small brush after sessions. Run an occasional dry burn cycle at maximum temperature to burn off residue.
  • Screens: Replace the small screens in the glass stems as needed, or clean with isopropyl alcohol.
  • Body: Wipe down the exterior with a damp cloth. Avoid getting liquid inside the charging port.
  • Cleaning agents: Isopropyl alcohol for glass parts; powdered brewery wash (PBW) was mentioned by at least one community member as an effective cleaning agent, particularly for water pipe accessories used alongside the Solo 2.

Common Issues & Fixes

  • Draw resistance too high: Ensure herb is not packed too tightly in the stem. A looser pack allows for better airflow. Cleaning screens and stems of buildup also helps.
  • Harsh vapor: Use the device through a water pipe adapter (WPA) for smoother hits, or stay at lower temperatures. At least one long-time user found the dry vapor too harsh at higher temps and considered water filtration essential.
  • Airpath not fully isolated: Community members noted that air can enter through the 12V charger port, meaning the airpath is not completely sealed from the device's internals. While this does not appear to pose a health risk, flavor purists considered this a drawback. No user-level fix was identified; this is a design characteristic.
  • Glass stem breakage: Glass stems are inherently fragile. The community recommended keeping spares on hand. Aftermarket stems with plastic/silicone tips were noted as slightly more durable for daily carry.
  • Battery degradation over time: As with all lithium-ion battery devices, capacity diminishes with extensive use. The battery is not user-replaceable, though some technically inclined users have performed battery swaps.

Accessories & Modifications

The Solo 2 benefited from a robust ecosystem of both OEM and aftermarket accessories:

  • Glass Stems: Available in multiple lengths (standard ~90mm long stem, ~70mm short stem, and ~60mm variants). Stems with plastic/silicone tips were available for bite comfort and added durability.
  • Water Pipe Adapters (WPA/GonG): Available in both 14mm and 18mm sizes. WPAs were among the most commonly recommended accessories, allowing the Solo 2 to be used with bubblers and water pipes for smoother vapor. Community members frequently sold their Solo 2 units bundled with WPA adapters, reflecting how common this pairing was.
  • Bubblers: Small 14mm bubblers (such as the Mobius Ion Matrix style) were popular companion pieces, providing portable water filtration.
  • Stem Caps: Silicone caps for sealing pre-packed stems, enabling convenient portable use.
  • Carry Cases/Pouches: The Solo 2 ships with a carry pouch; aftermarket cases with stem storage compartments were also available.
  • Stealth Adapters: Purpose-built adapters (such as the Planetvape stealth adapter) allowed the Solo 2 to be used discreetly through a beverage cup.
  • 420EDC Bundles: A small Maine-based retailer called 420EDC specialized in outfitting Arizer second-generation portables (Solo 2, Air 2, ArGo) with curated accessory bundles, offering customers the ability to build custom packages. The proprietor was active in the community and provided pre- and post-purchase support.
  • Aroma Dish/Pot: Included with the device for aromatherapy use; largely unused by the herb vaporization community.

Comparisons

The Solo 2 was frequently compared to other devices in the community, both within and outside the Arizer lineup:

vs. Arizer Air / Air MAX

The original Arizer Air shared the same glass stem system and general design philosophy but used a replaceable 18650 battery and had a smaller heater with longer heat-up times. Community members coming from the Air found the Solo 2 to be a meaningful upgrade in heat-up speed and battery life. When the Air MAX was released, users debated whether to opt for the newer Air MAX or grab a deal on the Solo 2. One community member advised that if choosing between the two, users should either get "the most new device" from Arizer or consider stepping up to a device with a fully isolated airpath.

vs. Arizer ArGo

The ArGo used the same glass stem system in a more compact, pocketable form factor with a shorter stem that retracted into the body. The Solo 2 offered better battery life and somewhat better vapor production, while the ArGo won on portability. All three second-generation Arizer portables (Solo 2, Air 2, ArGo) were served by the same accessory ecosystem.

vs. Storz & Bickel Mighty / Mighty+

The Mighty (and Mighty+) was frequently mentioned alongside the Solo 2 in comparison threads. Both were session vaporizers, but the Mighty was generally considered to produce denser vapor with slightly more open airflow. One community member who owned both a Solo 2 and a Mighty described neither as having truly "open" airflow but noted each had its own appeal. The Mighty commanded a significantly higher price point.

vs. TinyMight

For users seeking more open airflow and on-demand capability, the TinyMight was a commonly recommended upgrade path from the Solo 2. Community members described the TinyMight as having "much better airflow than all those portables" (referencing the Solo 2, Mighty, and Airvape Legacy Pro). One member noted that "pure convection on demand typically has open airflow" and suggested coupling the TinyMight with an 18mm WPA and aftermarket glass cooling stems for the best experience. The TinyMight occupied a different price tier and use case (on-demand convection vs. session hybrid), making it more of a complement than a direct competitor.

vs. Other Pure Convection Devices

Community members with advanced collections noted that the Solo 2's flavor, while good for its class, did not reach the level of pure convection devices such as the Tubo, Milaana 3 (Mi3), Splinter, Healthy Rips devices, Tetra P80, Firewood 7/8, or Tafee Bowle. These devices were cited as having both cleaner airpaths and superior taste, though typically at higher price points and with steeper learning curves.
