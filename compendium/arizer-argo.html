# Arizer ArGo

Overview

The Arizer ArGo (stylized as "ArGo," short for Arizer Go) is a compact portable dry herb vaporizer manufactured by Arizer, a Canadian company based in Waterloo, Ontario. Released in 2017, the ArGo was designed as Arizer's most pocket-friendly portable, building on the company's established glass stem system used in the Air and Solo lines. It utilizes a predominantly conduction heating method with some convective properties generated by drawing air through the heated glass stem. The unit is powered by a single user-replaceable 18650 lithium-ion battery, a feature that became one of its most frequently cited advantages in community discussions.

The ArGo was positioned as the "go anywhere" vaporizer in Arizer's lineup, distinguished from its siblings by a unique retractable stem design that allows the glass mouthpiece to sit fully enclosed within the body of the unit, making it significantly more pocket-friendly than the Air or Solo models.

Community Reception

The vaporizer community generally regarded the Arizer ArGo as a reliable, well-built portable vaporizer that excelled in a specific niche: discreet, pocketable, solo-use sessions. It earned a loyal following among users who valued its combination of a replaceable battery, glass vapor path, compact form factor, and ease of cleaning.

Pros

  • Discretion and portability: The ArGo's retractable stem design was widely praised. Community members frequently cited it as one of the most pocketable battery-powered portables available, with the stem tucking entirely inside the unit. One community member noted they used it to "sneak in a session on the train" during commutes.
  • Replaceable 18650 battery: This was consistently identified as one of the ArGo's most important features. Users could carry spare batteries for extended use away from a charger, a significant advantage over competitors with sealed batteries.
  • Ease of cleaning: The glass stem system was considered one of the easiest to maintain in the portable category. The glass stems could simply be soaked in isopropyl alcohol, and the oven itself required minimal maintenance.
  • Glass vapor path: Prized for clean, untainted flavor, a hallmark of the Arizer design philosophy.
  • Pre-loadable stems: Users could pre-pack multiple stems in advance and carry them capped with silicone stem caps, effectively creating a dosing capsule system.
  • Build quality and reliability: Arizer's reputation for solid construction and longevity was frequently mentioned. One seller noted their unit still had "plastic on the screen" after years of ownership, indicating the build held up well over time.

Cons

  • Session-style extraction: As a predominantly conduction vaporizer, the ArGo was not capable of quick, on-demand extraction. The herb continues to cook even when the user is not drawing, which some community members considered less efficient. One experienced member specifically noted: "it's not really efficient with herb because of the conduction heating, meaning it will cook the herb even when you're not toking."
  • Not ideal for groups: The ArGo was frequently described as a "sipper" rather than a "ripper." One community member put it memorably: "Argo is a sipper while the Mighty is a ripper. The Argo isn't really a good group vape." Its small bowl and gentle extraction made it best suited for solo use.
  • Vapor production: Compared to more powerful portables, the ArGo produced modest clouds, which was a trade-off for its compact size.
  • Heat-up time: While not unusually slow, the ArGo was not the fastest to reach temperature compared to some competitors.

Community members who sought on-demand extraction or more aggressive performance often moved on from the ArGo to convection-based alternatives, while those who valued its simplicity, portability, and replaceable battery tended to remain devoted users. As one long-time owner noted even in 2023: "With all the vapes I have, I still like to load up a few stems and enjoy the Argo."

Tips & Techniques

Loading

  • Grind consistency: A medium grind works well. Unlike the Pax, which benefits from a fine grind and tight pack, the ArGo performs better with a slightly looser pack that allows some airflow through the glass stem.
  • Pre-load stems: One of the ArGo's greatest practical advantages is the ability to pre-pack multiple stems, cap them with the included silicone stem caps, and carry them ready to go. This was a widely used technique among community members who took the ArGo out for the day.
  • Fill level: Pack the stem gently — not too tight. The herb should be lightly tamped, allowing air to pass through for the convective component of heating to contribute.

Drawing Technique

  • Slow, steady draws: The ArGo rewards a slow, gentle sipping technique. Aggressive draws cool the heater and reduce vapor production. The community frequently characterized it as a "sipper" for this reason.
  • Stem rotation: While the ArGo does not generally require stirring mid-session, some users reported slightly rotating the stem during use to promote more even extraction, a technique borrowed from the Air/Solo line.

Efficiency Maximization

  • Step-up temperature approach: Start at a lower temperature for flavor-forward hits and gradually increase temperature throughout the session to fully extract the material (see Temperature Guide below).
  • Session pacing: Because conduction heating continues between draws, try to maintain a steady pace rather than leaving the unit idle at temperature for extended periods.

Temperature Guide

The ArGo's digital temperature control allows precise degree-by-degree adjustment across its full range, which community members appreciated for dialing in specific effects.

Temperature RangeEffectNotes

175°C–195°C (347°F–383°F)Balanced flavor and effectCommonly used middle ground; good vapor production with retained flavor

210°C–220°C (410°F–428°F)Maximum extractionUsed to finish a bowl completely; vapor will be warm and wispy at this stage if stepping up

Community "Sweet Spot": While individual preferences varied, the 185°C–200°C (365°F–392°F) range was frequently referenced as the everyday sweet spot balancing flavor, effect, and vapor density.

Stepped Sessions: A commonly recommended technique was to start around 170°C (338°F) and increase by 5–10°C every few draws, finishing at 200–210°C (392–410°F). This approach maximized both the flavor experience and total extraction from a single bowl.

Maintenance & Cleaning

The ArGo's glass stem system was widely regarded as one of the easiest to clean among portable vaporizers.

Glass Stems

  • Soak in isopropyl alcohol (ISO): Drop used stems into a container of 90%+ isopropyl alcohol and allow them to soak. Residue dissolves readily from glass. Rinse thoroughly with warm water afterward.
  • Stem milk (reclaim): A popular community technique: simmer used stems in whole milk or cream (the fat absorbs the active compounds). This reclaims residual material for an edible effect while simultaneously cleaning the stems.
  • Frequency: Clean stems when airflow restriction becomes noticeable or visual buildup is apparent. Frequency depends on usage, but a weekly ISO soak was common for regular users.

Screens

  • Replace the small screens in the stems periodically, or soak them alongside the stems in ISO. Spare screens were included with the unit, and replacements were inexpensive.

Oven

  • The oven itself stays relatively clean because the herb is contained within the glass stem rather than sitting directly in the heater chamber. A quick brush with the included stir tool or a small brush after sessions is usually sufficient.
  • Periodically inspect the oven for any fallen plant material and brush it clean.

Exterior

  • Wipe down the exterior with a slightly damp cloth. Avoid getting liquid into the electronics or charging port.

Common Issues & Fixes

Odor When Off

Like most conduction portables, the ArGo can emit some residual smell from the oven and stems, especially when warm. Community members recommended:

  • Always cap stems with the silicone stem caps when not in use.
  • Keep the unit in its included travel case/pouch for additional odor containment.
  • Third-party smell-proof containers (e.g., Jyarz) were mentioned as accessories paired with the ArGo by some sellers.

Battery Life Concerns

While the replaceable battery was a major advantage, the ArGo runs on a single 18650 cell, which can be limiting for heavy users in a single outing. The solution most community members adopted was simply carrying spare charged 18650 batteries, which is the primary benefit of the removable battery design.

Stem Fragility

Glass stems can break if dropped. Community members recommended:

  • Carrying stems in the stem caps provided.
  • Purchasing spare stems or aftermarket options for backup.
  • Being mindful when the stem is extended during use outdoors.

Session Vape Limitations

Some users purchasing the ArGo hoping for quick on-demand extraction were disappointed. The community widely agreed that the ArGo is firmly a session vaporizer and should be understood as such before purchase. Users seeking on-demand performance were redirected to other options (see Comparisons below).

Desire for Heater Update

By 2023, some community members expressed a wish for Arizer to update the ArGo's heater technology, similar to the improvements seen in the Air Max. One member specifically noted they "would like to see a heater update like the Air Max got."

Accessories & Modifications

Stems

  • Standard ArGo stems: The proprietary shorter glass stems designed to retract into the body. Multiple stems were included and additional stems were available from Arizer.
  • Long mouthpiece: A longer stem option was available, offering cooler vapor at the expense of the ArGo's compact profile.
  • Third-party stems: Aftermarket stems from various artisans were discussed, though the ArGo's proprietary stem size limited options compared to the Air/Solo line.

Water Pipe Adapter (WPA)

  • 18mm WPA: An 18mm water pipe adapter was available for the ArGo, allowing it to be used through a water piece for cooled, filtered vapor. WPAs were commonly listed as part of ArGo setups in the community marketplace. This was a popular accessory for home use.

Stem Caps

  • Silicone stem caps were included with the unit and served the dual purpose of keeping pre-loaded stems sealed and minimizing odor. Additional caps were available for purchase.

Carrying Solutions

  • The included travel case/pouch was appreciated for basic portability.
  • Some users paired the ArGo with smell-proof storage containers (e.g., Jyarz brand) for maximum discretion.

Batteries

  • Standard 18650 lithium-ion batteries. Community members recommended carrying at least one spare for extended outings. High-quality cells from reputable manufacturers were advised.

Comparisons

The ArGo was frequently compared to several other portable vaporizers in community discussions:

ArGo vs. Arizer Air / Air 2

The ArGo shares Arizer's glass stem heating system with the Air line but in a more compact, pocket-friendly body. The Air 2 offered improved heating technology and digital temperature control over the original Air (which used preset temperature levels). Community members generally agreed the Air 2 was the better performer of the two Airs, but the ArGo's retractable stem made it significantly more portable. One member summarized: the Air is "not terribly portable without a case," but since "the ArGo is so similar... well you get the point." The trade-off was that the ArGo's shorter stems may produce slightly warmer vapor compared to the Air's longer stems.

ArGo vs. Storz & Bickel Mighty

This was a frequent comparison, though community members consistently noted these were fundamentally different vaporizers. The Mighty was described as significantly larger, more powerful, and better for group use — "a beast and a ripper." The ArGo was preferred for discretion, portability, replaceable battery, ease of cleaning, and solo use. One community member framed it as: "Two entirely different vapes. Mighty is huge compared to ArGo." For users prioritizing pocketability and simplicity, the ArGo won; for vapor production and group sessions, the Mighty was the clear choice. The Crafty was sometimes mentioned as a middle ground.

ArGo vs. Pax 3

Users migrating from the Pax 3 appreciated the ArGo's smaller bowl size, much easier cleaning process, replaceable battery, and lack of the persistent odor associated with the Pax. However, the Pax 3 was acknowledged as having a superior stealth profile (no protruding stem) and slightly better manipulation without looking at it. One community member who had owned both a Pax 3, ArGo, and Fury-style vape suggested the Pax 3 still held its own for stealth and battery life.

ArGo vs. Fury 2 / Fury Edge / Fierce

The Fury-style vaporizers were frequently mentioned as alternatives. The Fierce was noted as offering a removable battery like the ArGo but in a larger body. The Fury Edge was recommended by some community members as "a great upgrade from Arizer portables." However, concerns about off-gassing and quality control with early Fierce units were noted. The ArGo's glass vapor path and Arizer's reliability record gave it an edge for users prioritizing clean flavor and long-term durability.

ArGo vs. On-Demand Vaporizers

Users seeking to replace the ArGo with something offering on-demand extraction were directed toward options such as the Firefly 2+, Mistvape Imp, Milaana, TinyMight, and P80-based setups. The community acknowledged that the ArGo occupied a fundamentally different category as a session conduction vaporizer and was not suitable for users who wanted quick, on-demand hits.

ArGo vs. Xvape Starry

When compared to the Starry (a conduction vaporizer with removable battery from a Chinese manufacturer), community members gave the edge to the ArGo on performance. One experienced member noted: "Starry is a conduction vape and I bet it sucks compared to the ArGo." The Xvape Fog was suggested as a more competitive alternative from the budget segment.
