# Arizer ArGo

Overview

The Arizer ArGo (stylized as "ArGo," a portmanteau of "Arizer" and "Go") is a compact portable dry herb vaporizer manufactured by Arizer, a Canadian company based in Waterloo, Ontario. Released in late 2017/early 2018, the ArGo was designed as Arizer's most pocket-friendly portable unit, building on the company's well-established line of glass-stem-based vaporizers that includes the Solo, Solo 2, Air, and Air 2.

The ArGo uses a hybrid heating method that leans predominantly toward conduction, with some convection contribution from air drawn through the glass stem. It is powered by a single user-replaceable 18650 lithium-ion battery and features digital temperature control via an OLED display and on-board buttons. The device's defining design feature is its retractable glass stem, which tucks entirely inside the unit's body for protected transport — a significant departure from the exposed-stem design of other Arizer portables.

The ArGo is classified as a session-style vaporizer, meaning it is designed to be used over a period of several minutes per bowl rather than delivering vapor on demand in one or two draws.

The ArGo uses Arizer's signature glass-stem loading system: the herb is packed into the end of a borosilicate glass tube, which is then inserted into the oven. This design keeps the herb in a glass-isolated air path, a feature the community valued for flavor purity.

Community Reception

The vaporizer community generally regarded the Arizer ArGo as a solid, reliable portable vaporizer whose chief virtue was its exceptionally compact and discreet form factor among glass-stemmed devices. It occupied a unique niche: no other vaporizer at the time offered Arizer's signature glass air path in such a truly pocketable package.

Pros frequently cited by the community:

  • Ultra-portable size and discreteness. The retractable stem design was widely praised. Multiple community members noted that its small size made it ideal for walking, commuting, and situations where discretion was essential. One member described using it regularly during train commutes.
  • Replaceable 18650 battery. This was considered a "huge advantage" over competitors with sealed batteries, such as the Healthy Rips Fury 2. Users could carry spare batteries for extended use away from a charger.
  • Glass air path and excellent flavor. Community members consistently praised the flavor quality, which was described as "great." The all-glass stem design was appreciated by users who prioritized clean vapor taste.
  • Easy to clean. The glass stems are simple to maintain, and the loading system keeps the oven itself relatively clean. Multiple members listed ease of cleaning as a notable strength.
  • Pre-loadable stems. Users could load multiple glass stems in advance and carry them capped with included stem caps, enabling quick bowl changes on the go — effectively a built-in dosing capsule system.
  • Arizer build quality and reliability. The community widely regarded Arizer as a reputable manufacturer, and the ArGo benefited from this trust. One community member noted the device was still in "excellent condition" with "plastic still on the screen" after extended ownership.

Cons and criticisms frequently cited:

  • Session-style extraction is slow. This was the most consistent criticism. One prospective buyer expressed concern after reading it took up to 30 minutes to finish a bowl, though experienced owners clarified that 5–10 minute sessions were more realistic with the configurable timer. Still, community members acknowledged it was not suitable for users seeking fast, on-demand extraction. As one member put it: "once you go on-demand, it's hard to go back."
  • Conduction-dominant heating reduces efficiency. Because the heater is active during the entire session, herb continues to cook even when the user is not drawing. One community member explicitly noted that "it will cook the herb even when you're not toking," making it less efficient than on-demand convection devices.
  • Modest vapor production. The ArGo was characterized as a "sipper" rather than a "ripper." Community members noted it was not well-suited for group sessions due to its small bowl and gentle vapor output. One member stated plainly: "the Argo isn't really a good group vape."
  • Not the most powerful Arizer. While sharing the same general heating technology as the Air 2 and Solo 2, the ArGo's smaller form factor was perceived to limit its power output somewhat. Community wordplay summarized the sentiment: "It's not the mightiest vape for sure but it's craftier than most."
  • Price relative to competitors. At its original retail price, some community members felt the ArGo was expensive compared to alternatives like the Healthy Rips Fury 2 or XMAX V3 Pro, particularly given its session-only nature. Over time, significant sales brought the price down, making it more competitive.

Overall, the community regarded the ArGo as an excellent choice for solo users who prioritized discretion, flavor, and the convenience of replaceable batteries and pre-loadable glass stems, but recommended users with higher extraction speed or group-use needs look elsewhere.

Tips & Techniques

Loading

The ArGo uses Arizer's stem-loading system. Herb is packed directly into the open end of the short glass aroma tube. The community recommended:

  • Grind consistency: A medium grind generally works well. Unlike the Pax, which benefits from a fine grind and tight pack, the ArGo's hybrid heating with airflow through the stem works effectively with a moderate grind that allows some air to pass through.
  • Pre-load multiple stems: One of the ArGo's key advantages is the ability to pre-load several glass stems, cap them with the included silicone stem caps, and carry them ready to use. This was widely recommended for on-the-go use, effectively turning the stems into portable dosing capsules.
  • Pack level: Avoid overpacking. The small stem holds a modest amount of herb, and airflow restriction from overpacking can reduce vapor production.

Drawing Technique

As a session vaporizer, the ArGo rewards slow, steady draws rather than hard pulls. The "sipping" characterization used by the community reflects the optimal technique: gentle, extended inhales that allow heated air to pass evenly through the herb in the glass stem.

Session Management

  • Use the built-in session timer settings (5 or 10 minutes) to manage sessions. A single stem can typically be fully extracted within one or two timed sessions.
  • Users who found extraction too slow could increase temperature incrementally across a session — starting lower for flavor and stepping up for more complete extraction.

Carry Solutions

  • The included travel pouch was noted as functional. Community members also explored smell-proof cases (brands like Cloudten and Pelican were mentioned in adjacent discussions) for carrying the ArGo and accessories discreetly.
  • The retractable stem design means the unit can be pocketed without concern for stem breakage — a significant practical advantage over the Air and Solo models.

Temperature Guide

The ArGo offers precise digital temperature control from 50°C to 220°C (122°F to 428°F) in single-degree increments. While specific community-agreed sweet spots were not extensively documented in the available discussions, general Arizer portable guidance applies:

Temperature RangeEffectNotes
170°C–185°C (338°F–365°F)Flavor-focused, light effectsBest for tasting terpenes; wispy vapor. One community member noted the value of very low temp settings for "milking flavors."
185°C–200°C (365°F–392°F)Balanced flavor and vapor productionA good middle ground for most sessions.
200°C–220°C (392°F–428°F)Maximum extraction, thicker vaporHeavier effects, less flavor. Useful for finishing a bowl or for users seeking stronger sessions.

Temperature stepping — starting a session at a lower temperature and gradually increasing — was an implied technique within the community for getting the most out of each stem load.

Maintenance & Cleaning

One of the ArGo's most-praised attributes was its ease of maintenance, largely owing to the glass stem design.

Glass Stems

  • Soak in isopropyl alcohol (ISO). The glass stems can be submerged in high-concentration isopropyl alcohol to dissolve resin buildup. This is the standard cleaning method recommended across the Arizer portable lineup.
  • Reclaim from stems. The honey-colored resin that accumulates inside the stems over time can be reclaimed by soaking stems in ISO and evaporating the solution, or by pushing it through with a cotton swab. This is a well-known practice within the community for all Arizer glass-stem vaporizers.
  • Replace screens as needed. The small screens at the bottom of the stems can be replaced when they become clogged or discolored. Spare screens are included with the unit and are widely available.

Oven Chamber

  • Because the herb sits inside the glass stem rather than directly in the oven, the heating chamber itself stays relatively clean. Occasional cleaning with a dry brush or ISO-dampened cotton swab is sufficient.

General

  • The exterior can be wiped down with a damp cloth. The OLED screen area should be kept dry.
  • Community consensus was that the ArGo is among the easiest portable vaporizers to maintain, especially compared to devices like the Pax 3, which was criticized for its more involved cleaning process.

Common Issues & Fixes

Based on community discussions, the ArGo was regarded as a reliable device without widespread reports of major defects. However, several common user concerns emerged:

Slow Extraction

  • Issue: New users accustomed to on-demand vaporizers or combustion found the session-style extraction frustratingly slow.
  • Fix: Increase temperature, use the 5-minute session timer, and draw slowly and steadily. Setting the temperature to 200°C+ (392°F+) will accelerate extraction, though at the cost of flavor. Users who consistently found session-style vaping too slow were advised by the community to consider on-demand alternatives.

Battery Life Expectations

  • Issue: While the replaceable battery was a strength, individual session battery life was finite.
  • Fix: Carry one or more spare 18650 batteries. The community considered this a key advantage of the ArGo's design. Use quality, high-capacity 18650 cells from reputable brands.

Herb Efficiency Concerns

  • Issue: As a conduction-dominant session vape, the ArGo continues heating the herb between draws, leading to some waste.
  • Fix: Take consistent, regular draws throughout the session rather than letting the device sit idle while heated. Use the session timer to avoid unnecessarily long heating periods.

Desire for Heater Update

  • At least one community member expressed a wish for Arizer to update the ArGo's heater to match improvements seen in the Air Max, suggesting the heating performance could benefit from modernization. As of the available discussion data, no updated ArGo model had been released.

Accessories & Modifications

Glass Stems

  • The ArGo uses proprietary short glass aroma tubes that are shorter than those used by the Air and Solo models.
  • Aftermarket and accessory stems were available in various styles, including curved stems (e.g., "3D curved blue stem"), extended-length stems, and colored glass options. Community members and marketplace listings referenced pink, blue, and XL glass stems.
  • Stems could also be sourced from third-party glass artists.

Water Pipe Adapter (WPA)

  • An 18mm WPA (water pipe adapter) was available for the ArGo, allowing it to be used with water pieces for cooled, filtered vapor. WPA use was referenced in community marketplace listings and was a popular accessory across the Arizer portable line.

Stem Caps

  • Silicone stem caps (included with the unit) were essential for pre-loading stems and preventing herb from falling out during transport. They also helped contain odor to some degree.

Carrying Cases

  • The included travel pouch was adequate. Users seeking enhanced smell-proofing explored third-party options such as Cloudten cases, Pelican cases, and Jyarz containers for herb storage.

Third-Party Accessories

  • Community member delta3dstudios (referenced in related discussions) produced 3D-printed accessories for various vaporizers, including end caps and organizational tools that some ArGo users found useful.

Comparisons

The ArGo was frequently compared to other portable vaporizers in community discussions. Below are the most substantive comparisons:

Community members noted that the Fury 2 was an excellent out-and-about device but was limited by its sealed battery and shorter battery life. The ArGo's replaceable battery was described as "a huge advantage." Both were considered solid choices, with the decision often coming down to budget and battery priorities. One community member who owned both a Fury and a Solo 2 (which shares heating technology with the ArGo) preferred the Arizer's approach overall.

ArGo vs. Arizer Air / Air 2

As part of the same product family, the ArGo was frequently compared to its siblings. The Air 2 offered the same digital temperature control and reportedly slightly improved heating technology over the original Air. The primary differentiator was form factor: the ArGo's retractable stem made it significantly more pocket-friendly, while the Air/Air 2's longer stems provided a longer air path and potentially cooler vapor.

Community members who already owned the Air 2 and Solo 2 and were considering the ArGo were generally doing so specifically for the smaller size. One member noted: "I already have the Solo 2 and the Air 2. I do enjoy them... but I'm liking the small size of the Argo."

The performance was considered very similar across the current-generation Arizer portables, with one member noting: "The Arizers' new generation portables all work with the same heating technique."

ArGo vs. Storz & Bickel Mighty

This comparison highlighted how different the two devices were despite both being popular session portables. A community member summarized it memorably: "Argo is a sipper while the Mighty is a ripper." The Mighty was recommended for group use and users seeking heavy vapor production, while the ArGo was favored for solo, discreet, on-the-go sessions. The Mighty was noted as harder to clean and much larger. One Air 2 owner (the Air 2 being very similar to the ArGo) actually preferred it over the Mighty for efficiency and ease of maintenance, calling the Mighty a "herb hog."

ArGo vs. XMAX V3 Pro

This comparison pitted the ArGo against a newer-generation budget portable. The V3 Pro was described as "more to the convection side" and capable of on-demand use, which several community members considered a meaningful advantage. One experienced member recommended the V3 Pro and noted it could be used with Fury 2 WPA accessories and glass stems for an "Arizer-style" experience. The ArGo's advantage remained its replaceable battery and all-glass loading system.

ArGo vs. Haze Square

An early comparison thread examined these two then-new devices. The Haze Square offered four large bowls, faster heat-up time, on-demand heating capability, and a companion app, but was larger, heavier, and had shorter battery life. The ArGo won on battery life (replaceable and long-lasting), compactness, on-board controls, and likely ease of maintenance. One owner of both devices called the Square "a no-brainer" for its performance, but acknowledged the ArGo's significant advantages in portability and battery management.

ArGo vs. Pax 3

Several users came to the ArGo as a Pax 3 replacement or alternative. The Pax 3 was praised for its stealth factor and solid construction but criticized for its large bowl, involved cleaning process, magnetic charger, and smell. The ArGo addressed most of these complaints with its smaller bowl, easy-to-clean glass stems, and standard USB charging, though it lacked the Pax's sleek, stem-free design and haptic feedback.

ArGo vs. On-Demand Convection Portables

Users seeking to replace the ArGo with something faster were directed toward on-demand convection devices such as the TinyMight, Tetra P80/X, Milaana, Mistvape Imp, and Firefly 2+. The community acknowledged that these represented a fundamentally different vaping experience — faster extraction and better efficiency per hit — but at the cost of the ArGo's simplicity, reliability, and ultra-compact form factor. One former ArGo owner who purchased it at launch noted it "got put away in about a month" once they returned to on-demand convection devices.
