# Da Buddha (DBV)

Overview

The Da Buddha (commonly abbreviated as DBV) is a desktop whip-style dry herb vaporizer manufactured by 7th Floor, a company based in Colorado Springs, Colorado. The DBV is a convection-dominant vaporizer that uses a ceramic heating element to heat air, which is then drawn through ground herb held in a glass wand (also called a "whip" assembly). It operates on AC mains power and is designed for home/stationary use.

The Da Buddha has been one of the most widely discussed and recommended entry-level desktop vaporizers within the vaporizer community, valued for its simplicity, reliability, and strong vapor production at a relatively affordable price point. It shares much of its core design DNA with 7th Floor's higher-end model, the Silver Surfer Vaporizer (SSV), but at a lower price point and with a simpler aesthetic. Production of the DBV has involved partial assembly in China, though the community generally considered the quality to be on par with earlier units that were manufactured entirely in the United States.

Community Reception

The Da Buddha was widely regarded within the community as an excellent value desktop vaporizer and a strong recommendation for users new to vaporizing. Its combination of simplicity, reliability, and effective vapor production earned it a loyal following over many years of discussion.

Pros identified by the community:

  • Excellent value for money — frequently cited as one of the best desktop vaporizers in its price range.
  • Simple, intuitive operation — a single dial controls temperature with no digital interface to learn.
  • Strong vapor production — capable of delivering thick, satisfying hits, especially when paired with aftermarket modifications such as the DDave Mod.
  • Reliable and durable — community members reported using their DBVs regularly for years without significant issues. The ceramic heating element was considered very long-lasting.
  • Clean vapor path — the glass-on-glass design meant vapor primarily contacts glass and ceramic, which was important to users concerned about off-gassing from plastics or metals.
  • Shares parts with the SSV — standard heater covers (HC), tubing, and other 7th Floor accessories are cross-compatible, giving users a wide ecosystem of glass options.

Cons noted by the community:

  • Analog temperature control — the unmarked dial requires users to learn their preferred settings by feel and experience rather than precise temperature readout.
  • Whip-only out of the box — no bag/balloon filling capability without modifications.
  • Partial Chinese assembly — some users expressed concern about overseas manufacturing, though the community generally concluded this did not meaningfully affect quality or vapor path cleanliness.
  • Efficiency concerns at stock configuration — several users noted that the stock wand allowed herb to sit relatively far from the heat source, which could reduce extraction efficiency. This was widely addressed by the DDave Mod (see Accessories & Modifications below).

One community member purchased a DBV from a local headshop for $200 (slightly above typical online pricing at the time) and noted that the lack of shipping costs and inclusion of accessories like the aroma top and heat cover helped offset the premium. The community generally advised purchasing directly from 7th Floor or authorized retailers for the best price.

Tips & Techniques

Community members developed and refined a number of techniques over the years for getting the best performance from the Da Buddha:

  • Draw speed matters: Because the DBV is primarily convection-driven, the speed at which you draw air through the whip directly affects vapor production and temperature at the herb. A slow, steady draw is generally recommended to allow the air to heat fully before passing through the herb. Drawing too quickly can cool the air and reduce vapor output.
  • Dial position sweet spot: While the dial is unmarked, most community members reported finding their preferred setting somewhere around the 12 o'clock to 2 o'clock position for typical use. New users were advised to start low and gradually increase until visible vapor was produced, to avoid combustion.
  • Pack the wand lightly: A loosely packed wand allows for better airflow and more even extraction. Overpacking restricts airflow and can lead to uneven heating or require excessively high temperatures.
  • Stir between draws: Periodically removing the wand and gently stirring the herb was recommended to ensure even extraction across the load.
  • Let it warm up: Allowing the unit to reach stable operating temperature (2–3 minutes after turning on) before the first draw yields more consistent results.
  • Use with a water pipe for cooling: Many users found the DBV's vapor, especially at higher temperatures, benefited significantly from water filtration for smoothness and comfort.

Temperature Guide

Because the Da Buddha uses an unmarked analog dial rather than a digital temperature display, precise temperature guidance is limited. The community developed a general framework based on dial position:

Dial Position (approx.)EffectDescription
~10–11 o'clockLow / FlavorWispy vapor; best flavor extraction; lighter effects. Suitable for tasting terpenes.
~12–1 o'clockMedium / BalancedModerate visible vapor; good balance of flavor and effect. Often cited as a good starting point.
~1–2 o'clockMedium-High / Full ExtractionThick, visible vapor clouds; fuller body effects; good efficiency. Community-agreed "sweet spot" for many users.
~2–3 o'clockHighVery thick vapor; maximum extraction; harsher on the throat without water filtration. Risk of combustion increases significantly.
>3 o'clockDanger ZoneHigh risk of combustion; not generally recommended for normal use.

Note: Dial positions are approximate and can vary between individual units due to manufacturing tolerances in the analog potentiometer. Users were strongly advised to learn their own unit's behavior incrementally. The community emphasized that ambient room temperature, draw speed, and how tightly the wand is packed all influence effective temperature at the herb.

Maintenance & Cleaning

The Da Buddha's relatively simple design makes maintenance straightforward:

  • Glass wand and whip: Soak the glass wand, glass mouthpiece, and any glass connector pieces in isopropyl alcohol (90% or higher recommended) to dissolve resin buildup. A pipe cleaner can be used to scrub the interior of the wand. Rinse thoroughly with warm water and allow to dry completely before use.
  • Whip tubing: Silicone or vinyl tubing should be replaced periodically as it accumulates residue over time. Tubing can be rinsed with isopropyl alcohol, but many community members preferred simply replacing it when it became visibly discolored, as it is inexpensive.
  • Heater cover (HC): The ceramic heater cover can be carefully cleaned with a dry brush or cotton swab. Avoid getting liquids inside the heating element housing.
  • Exterior: Wipe down the outer housing with a damp cloth as needed. The unit does not require internal servicing under normal use.
  • ABV (Already Been Vaped) material: Empty the wand after each session to prevent residue from hardening and becoming difficult to clean. Many community members saved their ABV for use in edibles.

Frequency: Glass components benefit from cleaning every 1–2 weeks with regular use. Tubing replacement was commonly done every few weeks to every few months depending on usage frequency and user preference.

Common Issues & Fixes

Slow or No Response from 7th Floor Customer Service (Email)

This was one of the most frequently reported frustrations within the community. Multiple users reported that emails sent to 7th Floor's customer service addresses (including their customercare email) went unanswered for extended periods or were never responded to at all.

Solution: The community strongly and consistently recommended calling 7th Floor by phone rather than emailing. Phone support was reported to be responsive and helpful. Users were reminded that 7th Floor operates on Pacific Time, so calls should be timed accordingly.

Shipping Delays

Some users, particularly international customers, reported delays between order confirmation and shipment, sometimes with no tracking information provided.

Solution: Again, calling was the recommended approach to confirm order status and obtain tracking information. Community members reassured that despite communication issues, orders were ultimately fulfilled — reports of orders never arriving were essentially nonexistent.

Combustion

New users occasionally reported accidentally combusting their herb.

Solution: Start with a lower dial setting and increase gradually. Pay attention to draw speed — too slow a draw at high temperatures can overheat the herb. If you taste or smell combustion, immediately reduce the dial setting.

Inefficient Extraction with Stock Wand

Some users felt the stock wand positioned the herb too far from the heating element, leading to less efficient extraction and requiring larger loads.

Solution: The DDave Mod (see below) was the most widely recommended fix, bringing the herb closer to the heat source for dramatically improved efficiency and vapor density.

Accessories & Modifications

DDave Mod

The DDave Mod was one of the most significant and widely praised aftermarket modifications available for the Da Buddha. Created by a community member known as DDave, this modification kit (typically priced around $15 at the time of community discussion) replaced the stock glass wand with a shorter, more efficiently designed glass piece that positioned the herb closer to the ceramic heating element.

The community widely agreed that the DDave Mod transformed the DBV's performance, delivering noticeably thicker hits with less herb. One community member described it as delivering "big fat hits" for just $15 and recommended it as the first purchase any DBV owner should make. The mod was also compatible with the SSV and LSV (Life Saber Vaporizer), another 7th Floor product. DDave Mod kits were sold through eBay and discussed extensively in a dedicated community thread.

7th Floor Vapor Tamers

7th Floor produced a line of Vapor Tamer accessories designed to cool vapor. Available in three sizes — Original, Fatty, and Saturn — these glass cooling devices could be used with the DBV and other vaporizers.

Community testing with the DBV revealed the following:

  • The Original Vapor Tamer restricted airflow somewhat when used with the DBV's standard heater cover and tubing setup.
  • The Saturn offered virtually no noticeable airflow restriction.
  • The Fatty was reported to have even less restriction than the Saturn.
  • A common observation was that while the Vapor Tamers provided effective cooling, they did not retain their cold temperature for extended sessions. Some users found the need to re-chill them frequently to be inconvenient.

Water Pipe Adapters (WPA)

The DBV could be connected to water pipes/bubblers for enhanced vapor cooling. The community discussed various configurations using 14mm and 18mm (18.8mm) ground glass adapters. This was a popular modification for users seeking smoother, cooler hits, particularly at higher temperatures.

Standard 7th Floor Accessories

  • Aroma Top: A cap that could be placed over the heater to use the DBV as an aromatherapy diffuser. Reportedly available for around $10 at retail.
  • Heat Cover: A protective cover for the heating element housing, available for approximately $15.
  • SSV Heater Covers (HC): The standard SSV heater cover was cross-compatible with the DBV, and some users preferred using SSV glass components with their DBV.
  • VB Mini Wand: An alternative wand option used by some community members with their DBV setup.

Comparisons

Da Buddha vs. Silver Surfer Vaporizer (SSV)

The SSV, also manufactured by 7th Floor, was the most natural comparison point for the DBV. Both vaporizers share the same core heating technology and ceramic element. Key differences discussed by the community included:

  • The SSV features a different (typically angled or spherical) heater cover design and more premium aesthetics.
  • The SSV offered a ground glass joint connection that some users felt provided a slightly better seal.
  • The SSV was priced higher than the DBV.
  • The community frequently noted that with the DDave Mod installed, the performance gap between the two narrowed considerably, making the DBV an exceptional value.

When one user asked why they would choose the DBV over the SSV, the implied answer from the community was typically price — the DBV delivered very similar core performance for less money.

Da Buddha vs. Vapolution

In direct comparison threads, community members weighed the DBV against the Vapolution, an all-glass desktop vaporizer. Key discussion points included:

  • The Vapolution's all-glass design appealed to users prioritizing a completely clean vapor path.
  • However, the community noted that the DBV's temperature control was considered more reliable than the Vapolution's throughout its production history.
  • A knowledgeable community member pointed out that 7th Floor's decision to partially assemble the DBV in China did not make it inferior to the SSV or to vaporizers marketed as fully US-made, and that the DBV's vapor path remained clean regardless of assembly location.

Da Buddha vs. Log Vaporizers

Community members who owned both the DBV and log vaporizers (such as the Underdog, E-Nano, or HI) often kept both in rotation. Log vaporizers were generally considered more efficient for micro-dosing and small loads due to their tiny bowl size, while the DBV was favored for larger sessions and its ability to produce thicker clouds. Some users who sought an upgrade path from the DBV were pointed toward log vaporizers or the LSV with the DDave Mod as complementary additions rather than strict replacements.

Da Buddha vs. Life Saber Vaporizer (LSV)

The LSV, another 7th Floor product, was sometimes recommended as a lateral upgrade for DBV owners. One community member suggested that pairing an LSV with the DDave Mod would provide "a fun new toy without breaking the bank" for someone already familiar with the 7th Floor ecosystem. The LSV offered a different form factor (handheld wand-style rather than tabletop unit) while using a similar heating approach.
