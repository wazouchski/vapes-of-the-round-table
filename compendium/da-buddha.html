# Da Buddha (DBV)

Overview

The Da Buddha (commonly abbreviated DBV) is a desktop dry herb vaporizer manufactured by 7th Floor, a company based in Colorado Springs, Colorado. The Da Buddha is a whip-style convection vaporizer that uses a ceramic heating element to heat air drawn across ground herb packed into a glass wand. It has been in production since the mid-to-late 2000s and was widely regarded within the vaporizer community as a reliable, no-frills entry point into desktop vaporization.

The DBV features a simple, cylindrical design with an analog dial for temperature control. Air is drawn through a glass heating element cover (heater cover or "HC"), across the herb loaded in a glass wand, and delivered to the user via silicone or vinyl tubing (the "whip"). The vapor path is primarily glass and is considered clean by the community, with no electronics in the air path.

Heating Method: Convection

Power Source: Desktop (AC mains power)

Manufacturer: 7th Floor (Colorado Springs, CO)

Community Reception

The Da Buddha was widely regarded within the community as a dependable workhorse desktop vaporizer, especially valued for its simplicity, affordability, and consistent performance over years of daily use. Multiple community members reported using the DBV "several times a week" over multi-year periods without significant issues.

Pros

  • Reliability: The DBV was frequently cited as a durable, long-lasting unit. Members reported years of regular use with no mechanical failures.
  • Value: At its price point (commonly around $150–$190 retail, though some users reported paying up to $200 at local headshops), it was considered an excellent entry into quality desktop vaporization.
  • Clean Vapor Path: The glass-on-glass vapor path was a major selling point. Community members concerned about material safety appreciated that the air path was primarily glass, with no plastics or metals in the heated airflow.
  • Simplicity: The analog dial and straightforward whip delivery system appealed to users who preferred no-fuss operation.
  • Interchangeability: The DBV shares its heater cover with the Silver Surfer Vaporizer (SSV), allowing for parts interchangeability and the use of SSV standard heater covers and tubing.
  • Temperature Control Reliability: One experienced community member noted that the DBV's temperature control "throughout its production history, has easily been more reliable" than some competing units.

Cons

  • Manufacturing Shift: Some community members expressed concern that later models were "mostly assembled in China," though knowledgeable members argued this did not result in inferior quality compared to earlier US-made units or the more expensive SSV.
  • Analog Temperature Control: The lack of a digital display meant users had to learn their unit's sweet spots by feel and experience, which could present a learning curve for new users.
  • Efficiency Concerns: Some users with high tolerance reported needing "at least a couple of trips" to the DBV to achieve desired effects, suggesting the stock configuration could leave room for improved extraction efficiency.
  • Customer Support: Community members reported that 7th Floor's email customer service could be slow or unresponsive. The strong consensus was to call the company by phone rather than rely on email for order inquiries or support issues. As one member summarized, their experience with the company's email responsiveness was "terrible," but phone contact typically resolved issues promptly. The company operated on Pacific Time.

Tips & Techniques

Loading the Wand

  • Grind herb to a medium-fine consistency for optimal convection airflow.
  • Pack the wand lightly — overpacking restricts airflow and can lead to uneven heating or conduction-dominant heating rather than convection.
  • The mini wand (such as the VaporBrothers mini wand, which is compatible) can be used for smaller loads and may improve efficiency for solo sessions.

Draw Technique

  • Use a slow, steady draw. The DBV is a convection-dominant device, and faster draws cool the air before it fully heats the herb. A controlled, moderate draw speed maximizes vapor production and flavor.
  • Stirring the herb in the wand between draws improves even extraction and can extend the session.

Efficiency Improvement

  • The community strongly recommended the DDave Mod (discussed in detail below) as the single best upgrade for improving the DBV's extraction efficiency. One member described it as delivering "big fat hits" for only ~$15.
  • Using the SSV standard heater cover with the DBV was a common modification, as the parts are interchangeable.

Session Approach

  • The DBV is a session-style vaporizer. Users typically draw from it multiple times over the course of a session, stirring as needed, until the herb is evenly extracted.
  • The unit can be left on for extended periods due to its desktop nature, though users should be mindful of leaving it unattended.

Temperature Guide

Because the Da Buddha uses an analog dial without a digital temperature display, community guidance on temperature settings is typically expressed in terms of dial position rather than exact degrees. Users were encouraged to experiment and learn their individual unit's characteristics.

Dial Position / EffectApproximate TemperatureNotes
Low (flavor-focused)~170–185°C / ~338–365°FWispy, flavorful vapor; lighter effects. Good for tasting terpenes.
Medium (balanced)~185–200°C / ~365–392°FGood balance of flavor, visible vapor, and effect. Community-preferred range for most sessions.
High (extraction/clouds)~200–220°C / ~392–428°FThicker clouds, stronger extraction, but diminished flavor. Risk of combustion increases at higher settings.

Community Sweet Spot: Most experienced users settled into a mid-range dial position — roughly "noon" or slightly past on the dial — for a balance of flavor and vapor density. Users seeking maximum extraction (especially with the DDave Mod) could push temperatures higher while still avoiding combustion.

Note: Exact temperatures vary between units due to the analog nature of the dial. New users were advised to start low and increase gradually.

Maintenance & Cleaning

Glass Components

  • The glass wand and heater cover should be cleaned regularly to maintain airflow and flavor quality.
  • Isopropyl alcohol (ISO) — 90% or higher — is the community-standard cleaning agent. Soak glass parts in ISO, then rinse thoroughly with warm water and allow to dry before use.
  • Built-up residue ("honey" or reclaim) inside the wand can be reclaimed by soaking the wand in ISO and then evaporating the alcohol, or by gently heating the wand to loosen the resin.

Whip Tubing

  • Replace whip tubing periodically, as it can accumulate residue and develop odors over time.
  • Tubing can be cleaned by running ISO through it, but replacement is inexpensive and preferable for maintaining clean vapor.

Heater Cover and Element

  • The ceramic heater element generally does not require cleaning beyond occasional inspection.
  • Avoid getting liquid directly on the heating element.

Screens

  • Replace or clean screens in the wand regularly to prevent clogging. A clogged screen significantly impacts airflow and performance.

Common Issues & Fixes

Delayed Shipping / No Tracking Information

  • Multiple community members reported delays in receiving shipping confirmation or tracking numbers from 7th Floor, particularly for international orders. The consistent advice was to call 7th Floor directly by phone rather than rely on email, which was widely considered unreliable for timely responses. The company was noted to be on Pacific Time.
  • International shipping (e.g., to France) was reported to take longer, and communication about overseas orders could be sparse. Despite this, the community noted that orders were ultimately fulfilled — members could not recall instances of orders simply never arriving.

Airflow Restriction

  • If airflow feels restricted, check the screen in the wand for clogging and ensure herb is not packed too tightly.
  • Using a larger heater cover (such as the SSV standard HC) or aftermarket modifications can improve airflow.

Temperature Dial Inconsistency

  • Because the dial is analog, the same position may yield slightly different temperatures over the unit's lifetime or between different units. Users were advised to learn their specific unit rather than relying on others' dial position recommendations.

Theft Prevention

  • In one notable community discussion, a user reported their DBV being stolen during a house party. The community advised keeping vaporizers out of sight and secured when hosting gatherings, noting that while the DBV's size makes it harder to conceal than portable devices, it can still be taken if left unattended.

Accessories & Modifications

DDave Mod

The DDave Mod was the most widely recommended aftermarket modification for the DBV within the community. Available for approximately $15 at the time of discussion, it consisted of a replacement glass piece that reduced the distance between the herb and the heating element, resulting in significantly improved extraction efficiency and denser vapor production. Community members described it as transformative — one recommended it as "the first thing you need to do" before even considering a full vaporizer upgrade. The DDave Mod was also compatible with the Silver Surfer (SSV) and Life Saber (LSV).

7th Floor Vapor Tamers

7th Floor produced a line of Vapor Tamer cooling accessories in three sizes: Original, Fatty, and Saturn. These glass pieces were designed to cool vapor before inhalation.

  • The Original was reported to restrict airflow somewhat when used with the DBV's standard HC and tubing setup.
  • The Saturn offered little to no noticeable airflow restriction.
  • The Fatty was described as having even less restriction than the Saturn.
  • Community feedback noted that while all Vapor Tamers provided noticeable cooling, they did not retain their coldness for extended sessions. One member expressed concern about needing to "put the thing in the freezer every 20 minutes."
  • With log vaporizers using smaller-bore stems (e.g., 1/4" stems), even the Original Vapor Tamer performed well due to the inherently lower airflow volume, though it would not hold cold as long as the larger models.

SSV Heater Cover Compatibility

The DBV accepts the standard SSV heater cover, allowing users to interchange parts between the two 7th Floor units. This was commonly done by DBV owners to experiment with different airflow characteristics.

VaporBrothers Mini Wand

The VaporBrothers (VB) mini wand was used by some DBV owners as an alternative to the stock wand, offering smaller bowl size for efficiency-focused sessions.

Water Pipe Adapters (WPA)

While not extensively discussed in the available thread data, the DBV's whip system is compatible with water pipe filtration through adapters, and the DDave Mod kit included options for water pipe use in some configurations.

Aroma Top

An optional aroma top accessory (~$10) allowed the DBV to function as an aromatherapy diffuser when not in use for vaporization.

Heat Cover

An optional heat cover (~$15) was available from 7th Floor to protect the exposed heater area.

Comparisons

Da Buddha vs. Silver Surfer Vaporizer (SSV)

The SSV, also made by 7th Floor, was the DBV's more expensive sibling. The two shared the same ceramic heating element and heater cover, with the primary differences being:

  • Design orientation: The SSV's heater is angled differently and uses a hands-free connection system, while the DBV requires the user to hold or prop the wand.
  • Build quality/aesthetics: The SSV offered more customization options and was manufactured entirely in the USA, whereas later DBV production was largely moved to China.
  • Price: The SSV commanded a premium (~$50–$100+ more than the DBV). Community members occasionally asked "why the DBV over the SSV?" suggesting the SSV was considered the superior unit when budget allowed.
  • Performance: When equipped with the DDave Mod, the performance gap between the DBV and SSV was substantially narrowed according to community consensus.

A used SSV was sometimes recommended as a replacement or upgrade option for DBV owners, with used units appearing in the community marketplace for around $175.

Da Buddha vs. Life Saber Vaporizer (LSV)

The LSV, another 7th Floor product, was suggested as an upgrade path for DBV owners who wanted to stay within the same ecosystem. The LSV offered a different form factor (handheld heating element applied to a bowl) and was described as "a fun new toy without breaking the bank." The DDave Mod was also applicable to the LSV.

Da Buddha vs. Vapolution

In a direct comparison thread, a user weighed the DBV against the Vapolution, an all-glass desktop vaporizer. Key differentiators included:

  • The Vapolution's fully glass design appealed to users prioritizing an absolutely clean vapor path, though the DBV's glass air path was already considered very clean.
  • The DBV's temperature control was considered more reliable than the Vapolution's.
  • Community members noted that concerns about Chinese assembly in the DBV were somewhat overblown, as the quality remained consistent.

Da Buddha vs. Log Vaporizers

The DBV was occasionally compared to log vaporizers (e.g., Underdog, E-Nano) in discussions of efficiency and form factor. Log vaporizers were generally considered more efficient for small loads due to their smaller bowls and on-demand heating characteristics, while the DBV excelled as a session vaporizer for longer, more relaxed use. Some users owned both types and used them for different purposes — the DBV for extended sessions and logs for quick, efficient hits.
