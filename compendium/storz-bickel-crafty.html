# Storz & Bickel Crafty

Overview

The Storz & Bickel Crafty is a compact portable dry herb vaporizer manufactured by the German company Storz & Bickel, makers of the iconic Volcano desktop vaporizer. Released in late 2014 alongside its larger sibling, the Mighty, the Crafty quickly established itself as one of the most discussed and debated portable vaporizers in the community. It uses a hybrid heating system combining conduction and convection elements, powered by a single internal lithium-ion battery. The device was later succeeded by the Crafty+ (Crafty Plus), which addressed several of the original's shortcomings.

The Crafty was widely regarded within the vaporizer community as a top-tier portable in terms of vapor quality and extraction efficiency, though it was equally noted for significant concerns around battery life and long-term reliability. As one community member succinctly put it, describing Storz & Bickel's position in the portable market: "S&B is now the king of the portable vaporizer market. The Crafty or the Mighty is a very potent vaporizer — about 45 second heatup times, but there is very minimal drag, allowing you to blow huge clouds thanks to its convection heater."

Note on Temperature Control: The original Crafty featured a minimalist single-button physical interface. All precise temperature adjustments were handled through the companion smartphone app via Bluetooth. The physical button cycled between off, standard temperature, and a single boost temperature. Some community members expressed a desire for multiple boost levels, with one member suggesting this "could be achieved very easily by a firmware update" — proposing a sequence of button presses for multiple boost stages.

Community Reception

The Crafty occupied a unique and somewhat polarizing position within the vaporizer community. It was simultaneously praised as one of the best-performing portable vaporizers available and criticized for reliability and battery life shortcomings that many felt were unacceptable at its premium price point.

Strengths

  • Vapor Quality: The community widely regarded the Crafty as producing exceptional vapor — thick, flavorful, and potent. It was frequently described as "the current king in portable cloud generators" during its peak years. The hybrid heating system delivered a combination of flavor and density that few portables could match.
  • Ease of Draw: The Crafty's very open, free-flowing airpath was consistently highlighted as a major advantage. One community member recommended the Crafty specifically for a medical patient with ALS, citing "the free flowing ease of draw on the Crafty or Mighty" as an excellent feature for users with limited lung capacity.
  • Extraction Efficiency: The device was noted for thorough, even extraction. Multiple community members described it as delivering powerful effects, with one experienced user calling it the "hardest hitting flower portable I have ever owned."
  • Compact Size: Compared to the Mighty, the Crafty was meaningfully smaller and more pocketable, making it a genuine on-the-go portable.
  • Short Draw Performance: The Crafty was noted for producing visible vapor even on shorter draws, making it approachable for users transitioning from combustion who did not want to take long, slow sips.

Weaknesses

  • Battery Life: This was the single most criticized aspect of the Crafty. The single-cell battery provided only a handful of sessions per charge, and the unit could not be used while charging (pass-through charging was a Mighty-exclusive feature). One community member noted: "I wasn't talking only about the malfunctions some (if not most) Crafties eventually have but the very short battery life as well… It could suit you though if you don't vape with company and you don't go through many loads per day."
  • Reliability Concerns: Long-term reliability was a significant area of community concern. Reports of units failing — particularly after the warranty period — were common enough that one experienced member stated plainly: "I wouldn't call the Crafty reliable." Common failure modes included battery degradation, error codes, and housing cracking near the cooling unit connection point.
  • Non-Replaceable Battery: The sealed, non-user-replaceable battery design was a source of ongoing frustration, especially as batteries degraded over time. Storz & Bickel's out-of-warranty service policies were considered expensive by many community members.
  • Build Quality Concerns (Cooling Unit Connection): Early adopters identified the plastic area where the cooling unit connects to the main body as a structural weak point. One community member reported: "My Crafty case broke where it connects to the cooling unit. This area could be a little beefier." Another noted concern that "turning the cooling unit a bit too far over and over in time may lead to breakage." Storz & Bickel did cover such failures under warranty.
  • App Dependency: The reliance on a smartphone app for precise temperature control was seen as both a feature and a limitation, particularly as mobile operating system updates sometimes caused compatibility issues.
  • Efficiency vs. Material Use: While the Crafty extracted well, some users found it consumed material faster than they preferred for daily driving. One Crafty owner noted: "I love my Crafty but it's a little too inefficient (burns through my stash too quick) to be used as a daily driver," though this was partly attributed to the bowl size encouraging larger loads.
  • Session-Style Waste: As a session vaporizer, the Crafty continued to apply heat to material between draws, leading to some vapor loss when users were not continuously inhaling. Community members confirmed this waste was real: vapor produced while not actively drawing was "wasted" and did not recondense back into the material.

Overall Consensus

Despite its flaws, the Crafty was consistently recommended as a top-tier portable, particularly for users who prioritized vapor quality and potency above all else. The community often framed the purchase decision as a trade-off: exceptional performance balanced against limited battery life and questionable long-term durability. For users who primarily vaped at home or had access to frequent charging, the Crafty was considered an outstanding performer. For heavy users, travelers, or those who prioritized reliability, the community often steered buyers toward the Mighty or alternative devices.

Tips & Techniques

Loading

  • Grind Quality: A medium-fine grind was generally recommended for the Crafty to maximize extraction efficiency and airflow through the hybrid heating system.
  • Pack Density: The community found that a moderately packed, full chamber produced the best results. The material should be lightly tamped but not compressed so tightly as to restrict airflow.
  • Dosing Capsules: The stainless steel dosing capsules were popular for keeping the chamber clean and for pre-loading material for sessions on the go. They slightly reduced the effective chamber size but made reloading and cleanup significantly easier.
  • Liquid Pad for Smaller Loads: For users who wanted to conserve material, placing the included liquid pad on top of a smaller load helped fill the chamber space and maintain even heat distribution.

Drawing Technique

  • Slow, Steady Draws: While the Crafty could produce vapor on shorter pulls, slow and steady draws of moderate length (5–10 seconds) were widely considered optimal for balancing flavor and cloud production.
  • Aggressive Drawing Caution: Some users reported throat irritation and even bleeding gums from excessively aggressive drawing, particularly when using the Crafty with a water piece at higher temperatures. One community member described deeply inhaling continuously until the temperature light changed, which caused discomfort. A more measured pace with adequate hydration was recommended.

Session Management

  • Turn Off Between Draws: To minimize waste inherent to session vaporizers, experienced users recommended turning the Crafty off between groups of draws and taking additional hits as the unit cooled through the vaping temperature range. One community member described this as "standard procedure."
  • Auto Shut-Off Awareness: The Crafty's auto shut-off timer was relatively aggressive. Users learned to keep drawing or cycle the button to prevent premature shutdown during longer sessions.

Battery Conservation

  • Charge Before Every Session: Given the limited battery capacity, community members recommended keeping the Crafty topped off whenever possible.
  • Lower Temperature Sessions: Vaping at the default temperature rather than boosted temperatures helped extend battery life per charge.

Temperature Guide

The Crafty's temperature was primarily controlled through the companion smartphone app, with the physical button providing access to a default temperature and a single boost level.

SettingTemperatureEffects & Notes
Low Range170–175°C (338–347°F)Maximum flavor, light wispy vapor, strong terpene profile. Best for flavor-chasing sessions.
Default180°C (356°F)Factory default setting. Good balance of flavor and vapor production. Widely used starting point.
Medium185–190°C (365–374°F)Fuller vapor, increased body effects. This was a commonly used temperature range for daily sessions. One user specifically mentioned vaping at 185°C.
Boost / High195–210°C (383–410°F)Dense clouds, maximum extraction, stronger sedative effects. Boost mode (approximately 195°C by default) was commonly engaged after the initial flavorful draws at the base temperature.

Community Notes:

  • Many users followed a "step-up" approach: starting at the default or a lower custom temperature for flavor, then engaging the boost to finish extraction.
  • Vaping at the highest temperatures (above 200°C / 392°F) was noted to increase throat irritation. Staying well-hydrated and using water filtration was recommended at these settings.
  • The community noted that some users found the Mighty "very harsh" at 410°F (210°C); similar cautions applied to the Crafty at equivalent temperatures.

Maintenance & Cleaning

Cooling Unit

The cooling unit was the primary maintenance focal point. Resin accumulated in the cooling unit's internal channels and screens, and regular cleaning was essential for maintaining airflow and flavor.

  • Disassemble the cooling unit into its component parts (top cap, o-rings, screen, inner body).
  • Soak in isopropyl alcohol (ISO) — 90% or higher concentration recommended. Soaking for 15–30 minutes loosened most resin buildup.
  • Rinse thoroughly with warm water after soaking and allow to dry completely before reassembling.
  • Replace screens when they became clogged beyond effective cleaning.
  • O-ring maintenance: Inspect o-rings during cleaning and replace if they showed signs of degradation. Applying a small amount of food-grade lubricant could extend their life.

Chamber

  • Brush the chamber after each session using the included cleaning brush to remove spent material (ABV).
  • Periodic ISO wipe: Use an ISO-soaked cotton swab to clean the chamber walls and the screen at the bottom of the chamber.

Cooling Unit Connection Area

  • Handle with care. As noted in the common issues section, the plastic area where the cooling unit attached was identified as a structural weak point. Avoid over-rotating the cooling unit or applying excessive force when attaching or detaching it.

Cleaning Frequency

The community generally recommended:

  • Brush out the chamber after every session
  • Clean the cooling unit every 5–10 sessions (depending on usage intensity)
  • Deep clean with ISO weekly for heavy users

Common Issues & Fixes

Battery Degradation

Problem: After extended use (typically 1–3 years), battery capacity noticeably declined, leading to fewer sessions per charge and eventually inability to complete a single session.

Fixes:

  • Custom battery packs: A community member known as JCat became well-known for assembling and selling custom replacement battery packs for the Crafty (and later the Mighty, which required two packs). These were offered at significantly lower cost than manufacturer replacement services.
  • DIY battery replacement: Some users successfully replaced the internal battery cell themselves. The Sony VTC6 was attempted as a replacement cell, though compatibility issues were reported — one user found that after installing a VTC6, the device showed a full charge but would not power on, blinking blue 7 times (the empty battery indicator). This suggested that not all 18650 cells were fully compatible with the Crafty's battery management system without proper configuration.
  • Manufacturer service: Storz & Bickel offered battery replacement as a paid service, though community members found this expensive, and some reported that S&B declined to service units beyond a certain age. One UK-based Mighty owner reported being offered only a replacement unit for €248 plus return of the old device.

Housing Crack at Cooling Unit Connection

Problem: The plastic housing where the cooling unit twisted into place could crack or break, particularly with repeated attaching/detaching or if the cooling unit was accidentally rotated too far or installed backwards.

Fix: Storz & Bickel covered this under warranty for units within the warranty period. Community advice was to be gentle when attaching the cooling unit and to avoid over-rotation.

Error Codes

Problem: Various error codes displayed via the LED indicator or app, including Err 04 and other startup errors.

Fix: One community member reported that an Err 04 on first use was resolved by holding the power button to switch off, waiting a few minutes, and restarting. Persistent error codes generally required manufacturer service.

Battery Temperature Sensor Failure

Problem: The battery temperature sensor (a 10k thermistor with a JST connector) could fail or break during repair attempts. Symptoms included blue-red flashing LEDs and app messages stating "battery too low, warm up vaporizer" even when the device was warm.

Fixes:

  • DIY repair: One community member successfully repaired a broken sensor wire using solid breadboard jumper wire inserted into the original connector crimp, then secured with epoxy as a permanent fix.
  • Salvage from donor unit: Obtaining a working sensor from a dead Crafty or Mighty unit.
  • Replacement sourcing: The connector pins and JST connector could be sourced from electronics distributors like DigiKey, but the extremely small wire gauge made crimping very difficult even with specialized tools. One experienced repair member reported only 1 out of 10 attempts yielding a working sensor.

Bluetooth/App Connectivity Issues

Problem: The Crafty's reliance on the smartphone app for temperature adjustment meant that Bluetooth pairing failures or app compatibility issues with newer phone operating systems could limit functionality.

Fix: The community generally recommended ensuring the app and firmware were updated, and some users kept an older compatible device specifically for Crafty management.

Sore Throat and Gum Irritation

Problem: Some users reported sore throats, swollen glands, and in one case bleeding gums when using the Crafty, particularly at higher temperatures or with aggressive drawing technique.

Fixes:

  • Stay hydrated: Drinking water before and after sessions was frequently cited as the most effective mitigation.
  • Use water filtration: Pairing the Crafty with a bubbler or water pipe adapter significantly cooled and moistened the vapor.
  • Lower temperatures: Reducing the vaping temperature decreased throat irritation.
  • Moderate draw pace: Avoiding continuous aggressive drawing and allowing brief pauses between hits.
  • Whip attachment: Using a longer vapor path via a whip was suggested to further cool vapor before inhalation.

Accessories & Modifications

Water Pipe Adapters (WPA)

The Crafty could be connected to water pieces using aftermarket adapters. Dedicated WPA adapters were available from third-party accessory makers that replaced the standard mouthpiece assembly and allowed the Crafty to interface with standard 14mm or 18mm glass joints. One community member referenced the "Crafty/Mighty Easy Flow Water Tool Adapter" from Vapefiend as a solution, noting that it could also serve as a glass mouthpiece when not connected to water.

However, the community noted that the Crafty did not pair with water filtration as seamlessly as some other portables. As one member observed, the Mighty and Crafty "don't pair with water as easily as the Grasshopper or Air," though dedicated adapters largely resolved this limitation.

Dosing Capsules

The stainless steel dosing capsules and capsule magazine were among the most recommended accessories. They allowed users to pre-load multiple sessions worth of material, kept the chamber cleaner, and made reloading quick and mess-free.

Custom Battery Packs

As detailed in the Common Issues section, aftermarket battery packs assembled by community repair specialists (notably JCat) provided a significantly more affordable alternative to manufacturer battery replacement. These packs were designed as direct replacements for the original battery, requiring the user to open the device but offering substantial cost savings.

Liquid Pad

The included liquid pad, originally intended for concentrate use, was commonly repurposed as a chamber reducer for smaller dry herb loads, helping maintain consistent heat distribution with less material.

This was by far the most common comparison in the community, as both devices shared the same heating element and were manufactured by the same company.

The community frequently described the Crafty as the Mighty's "baby brother." One member noted: "Crafty is his baby brother and it meets most of your requirements at a lower price." For users who carried a backpack or primarily vaped at home, the Mighty was generally recommended over the Crafty due to superior battery life and pass-through charging. The Crafty was preferred when pocket portability was a priority.

One user asked a key question that summarized the community's perspective on this comparison: "They both use the same heating element. The only difference is the battery size as far as I know. Mighty has a display and Crafty uses the app. Heating time and cloud output is the same, true?" The community largely confirmed this — the performance difference between the two was negligible; the distinction was primarily in battery capacity, physical controls, and form factor.

Crafty vs. Arizer Solo/Air

The Arizer products were frequently mentioned alongside the Crafty in purchase advice threads. The community generally agreed that:

  • The Crafty produced denser, more potent vapor and hit harder per draw.
  • The Arizer Solo and Air were more "sippers" — delivering good flavor over longer, slower sessions.
  • Arizer stems held less material than the Crafty's chamber, requiring more frequent repacking for high-tolerance users.
  • Arizer devices offered replaceable batteries (Air) and were considered more reliable long-term.
  • The Arizer products were significantly less expensive.
  • One user compared the two philosophies: "Argo is a sipper while the Mighty is a ripper" — a distinction that applied equally to the Crafty vs. Air/Solo comparison.

Crafty vs. DaVinci Ascent

The community generally considered the Crafty superior in vapor quality, with one member noting the Crafty could produce "bigger heavier hits." The Ascent offered a larger chamber, which appealed to some users who preferred longer sessions with more material. However, the Crafty's extraction efficiency was considered meaningfully better.

Crafty vs. Pax

Both were popular pocket portables, but the community generally rated the Crafty's vapor quality significantly higher. The Pax was noted for being more discreet and requiring more maintenance. One user observed that "the Pax gets you more medicated faster (no water tools involved)" compared to the Arizer Air, but the Crafty was seen as surpassing both in raw vapor quality and potency per hit.

Crafty vs. On-Demand Vaporizers (MFLB, Firefly, Grasshopper)

For users who took only occasional hits throughout the day rather than full sessions, the community often recommended on-demand vaporizers over the Crafty. The Crafty's session-based heating meant vapor was wasted between draws, while on-demand devices like the MFLB, Firefly, and Grasshopper only heated material when actively in use. One community member noted they could pack a single MFLB trench and take a hit or two every other day for "about half a month" with virtually no waste — a use pattern poorly suited to the Crafty.

Crafty vs. Boundless CFX

When comparing the Crafty to the significantly cheaper Boundless CFX, the community generally acknowledged the CFX as a respectable budget alternative but considered the Crafty's vapor quality to be in a higher class. For users transitioning from combustion who were price-sensitive, some community members suggested starting with a less expensive option before investing in premium devices.

Crafty vs. Desktop Vaporizers

Several community members who owned both the Crafty and desktop units noted that while the Crafty was impressive for a portable, it could not match dedicated desktop vaporizers (such as log vapes, the Cloud Evo, or the LSV) for raw extraction power, efficiency with micro-loads, or the ability to connect seamlessly to glass. The Crafty was sometimes described as "too inefficient to be used as a daily driver" by users accustomed to the efficiency of desktop units, particularly log vaporizers that could deliver complete extraction from as little as 0.05g of material.
