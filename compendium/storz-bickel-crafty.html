# Storz & Bickel Crafty

Overview

The Storz & Bickel Crafty is a portable dry herb vaporizer manufactured by Storz & Bickel, a German company widely regarded as one of the premier manufacturers in the vaporizer industry. The Crafty was released in 2014 as a smaller, more portable companion to the Storz & Bickel Mighty. It uses a hybrid heating method combining conduction and convection to vaporize dry herb material. The device is powered by an internal rechargeable lithium-ion battery and is controlled via a smartphone application, making it one of the first mainstream portable vaporizers to rely on app-based temperature control rather than an extensive onboard interface.

The Crafty received a hardware revision during its production life, with a notable update around 2018 that included a battery with approximately 20% more capacity than the original model. Storz & Bickel later released the Crafty+ as a successor, but the original Crafty remained a highly discussed and recommended device throughout its production run.

Community Reception

The vaporizer community held the Crafty in high regard, frequently recommending it as one of the top-tier portable vaporizers available. It was widely considered to deliver vapor quality on par with or near its larger sibling, the Mighty, in a more compact form factor. One community member described the Crafty as the Mighty's "baby brother," noting that "it meets most of your requirements at a lower price."

Pros commonly cited by the community:

  • Vapor quality: Consistently praised for producing smooth, flavorful, and potent vapor, largely attributed to Storz & Bickel's proven hybrid heating system.
  • Ease of use: The single-button interface combined with app control made the Crafty approachable for newcomers and convenient for experienced users.
  • Compact size: Significantly smaller and more pocketable than the Mighty, making it a preferred choice for true portability.
  • Dosing capsule system: The Storz & Bickel dosing capsules (also referred to as "dosing pods") were highly valued for convenient loading, cleanliness, and pre-packing sessions.
  • Effectiveness for transitioning smokers: The Crafty was frequently recommended to users switching from combustion, as its vapor production was robust enough to satisfy former smokers.

Cons commonly cited by the community:

  • Battery life: This was the most persistent criticism. The original Crafty's battery was widely considered insufficient for heavy users, often lasting only a handful of sessions before requiring a recharge. While the later revision improved this by approximately 20%, battery life remained a common concern compared to the Mighty or vaporizers with replaceable batteries.
  • Non-replaceable battery: Unlike competitors such as the Arizer Air or ArGo, the Crafty's sealed battery could not be swapped by the user, meaning battery degradation over time effectively limited the device's lifespan.
  • Durability and reliability concerns: The community had mixed experiences with long-term reliability, with some users reporting issues after extended use. Storz & Bickel's warranty service was generally praised, though the need to use it was noted as a drawback.
  • Price: The Crafty commanded a premium price point, and community members sometimes debated whether the performance difference over more affordable options like the Arizer Air or Boundless CFX justified the cost.
  • Cleaning: Some community members found the Crafty's cooling unit somewhat tedious to maintain compared to the simplicity of glass stem-based systems.

Despite these drawbacks, the Crafty consistently appeared in recommendation threads as a top choice for portable vaporization, with the community generally agreeing that its vapor quality was difficult to match at its size.

Tips & Techniques

Loading the Chamber

  • Full pack: The Crafty's chamber holds approximately 0.25g of finely ground material. A medium grind with a consistent texture was generally recommended for optimal airflow and extraction.
  • Smaller loads: For microdosing or efficiency-minded users, the Storz & Bickel dosing capsules were the preferred method. These capsules hold a smaller amount of material and keep the chamber clean, allowing quick swaps between sessions.
  • Dosing capsule pre-loading: Many community members pre-packed several dosing capsules at once, carrying them for on-the-go convenience. Dosing capsules were in enough demand that community members actively sought to buy or trade for them, as evidenced by marketplace requests.
  • Liquid pad trick: For very small loads without dosing capsules, placing the included liquid pad on top of a partial pack helped keep the material in contact with the heater and improved extraction consistency.

Drawing Technique

  • Slow, steady draws: The Crafty responded well to slow, controlled inhalation. Quick, aggressive draws tended to cool the heater and reduce vapor production.
  • Session pacing: As a session-style vaporizer, the Crafty was designed to be sipped over several minutes. Users typically started at a lower temperature for flavor and gradually increased using the boost function or app for thicker clouds.
  • Single-hit use: While the Crafty was designed as a session vaporizer, some community members noted it could serve adequately for single-hit use, though it was not specifically optimized as an on-demand device. For users seeking powerful single hits with water filtration, on-demand options were sometimes suggested as more efficient alternatives.

Battery Management

  • Charge before sessions: Given the limited battery life, experienced users recommended fully charging the device before each outing.
  • Pass-through charging: Some users utilized the device while connected to a USB power source to extend session capability, though this was more of a workaround for home use.

Temperature Guide

The Crafty's temperature was controlled primarily through the Storz & Bickel smartphone app, with the device itself offering a default temperature and a boost mode.

Community sweet spots:

  • 180°C (356°F) as a starting temperature was the most commonly referenced baseline, being the factory default and a well-regarded all-around setting.
  • Stepping up in increments of 5–10°C throughout a session was a widely recommended technique to maximize both flavor and extraction from a single bowl.
  • The boost function (activated by the device's single button) raised the temperature to approximately 210°C (410°F), which community members used for finishing a bowl or achieving maximum cloud production.

Maintenance & Cleaning

Cooling Unit

The cooling unit was the component most frequently cited as requiring regular maintenance. It consists of multiple parts that accumulate residue over time.

  • Frequency: Regular users recommended cleaning the cooling unit at least once per week, or more frequently with heavy use.
  • Method: Disassemble the cooling unit and soak all parts in isopropyl alcohol (90%+ concentration recommended). Allow parts to soak for 15–30 minutes, then rinse thoroughly with warm water and allow to dry completely before reassembling.
  • O-rings: Remove rubber O-rings before soaking in alcohol, as prolonged exposure can degrade them over time. Some users reported replacing O-rings periodically as part of routine maintenance using the wear & tear set.

Chamber

  • Post-session: Empty the ABV (already been vaped material) while the device is still warm for easiest cleaning. A small brush (included with the device) was recommended for sweeping out remaining particles.
  • Screen maintenance: The chamber screen should be checked periodically for clogs. Brushing after each session and occasional soaking in isopropyl alcohol kept airflow optimal.

General

  • Some community members noted that the Crafty was "somewhat of a pain in the ass to clean" compared to simpler designs like the Arizer series with removable glass stems. The use of dosing capsules significantly reduced chamber cleaning frequency and was widely recommended as the best strategy for keeping the device clean.

Common Issues & Fixes

Battery Life Degradation

  • Issue: The most widely discussed long-term concern. The sealed lithium-ion battery naturally degraded with charge cycles, leading to diminished session counts over the device's life.
  • Fix: No user-serviceable solution existed. Storz & Bickel's warranty covered defects within the warranty period. The 2018 revision with the improved battery partially addressed this, but did not solve the fundamental limitation of a non-replaceable cell.

Bluetooth/App Connectivity

  • Issue: Some users reported intermittent Bluetooth connectivity with the companion app, particularly on certain phone models or after OS updates.
  • Fix: Restarting the app or re-pairing the device typically resolved connectivity issues. Since the device could function at its default temperature and boost mode without the app, this was not a session-stopping problem.

Cooling Unit Clogging

  • Issue: Resin buildup in the cooling unit could restrict airflow over time.
  • Fix: Regular cleaning as described in the Maintenance section. Keeping a spare cooling unit was a strategy employed by some dedicated users to ensure uninterrupted use while cleaning.

Accessories & Modifications

Dosing Capsules

The Storz & Bickel dosing capsules (sometimes called "dosing pods") were among the most popular accessories for the Crafty. They allowed pre-loading of material, kept the chamber clean, and made on-the-go use more convenient. A capsule caddy was available for carrying multiple pre-packed capsules. These capsules were interchangeable between the Crafty and Mighty, and demand for them was high enough that community members actively sought them through marketplace channels.

Water Pipe Adapters (WPA)

Water filtration was a popular modification for the Crafty. While the device did not natively pair with water pieces as easily as some competitors (such as the Arizer Air with a GonG adapter or the Grasshopper), aftermarket solutions were available:

  • Crafty/Mighty Easy Flow Water Tool Adapter: An adapter that replaced the standard mouthpiece and allowed the Crafty to connect to standard water pieces or hydratubes. One community member referenced an adapter from VapeFiend, noting that "once fitted it can also be used as a glass mouthpiece."
  • Pinnacle Water Tool (PNWT): The Pinnacle Water Tool and its various clones could be paired with the Crafty using the appropriate adapter, providing a compact, portable water filtration solution.

Community members acknowledged that while the Crafty could be adapted for water filtration, other portables (notably the Arizer Air and Grasshopper) offered more straightforward WPA compatibility. As one member noted, "you can't get better than [the Grasshopper or Air] unless you step up to a Mighty/Crafty (which don't pair with water as easily)."

Wear & Tear Set

Storz & Bickel offered an official wear & tear set containing replacement screens, O-rings, and cooling unit components. This was widely recommended as an essential purchase for long-term Crafty owners.

Comparisons

Crafty vs. Mighty

The Mighty was the Crafty's most direct comparison point, being made by the same manufacturer with a very similar heating system. The community consensus was clear:

  • The Mighty offered significantly better battery life (dual batteries vs. single), a built-in temperature display, and greater session endurance.
  • The Crafty was considerably more compact and pocketable, making it the better choice for true portability.
  • Vapor quality between the two was considered very similar, with some users detecting marginal differences.
  • One community member summarized: the Crafty is the Mighty's "baby brother" that "meets most of your requirements at a lower price."
  • For group use, the Mighty was generally preferred due to its larger battery and uninterrupted session capability.

Crafty vs. Arizer Air / ArGo

This comparison arose frequently, as the Arizer products occupied a similar portable niche at a lower price point:

  • The Arizer Air and ArGo featured user-replaceable 18650 batteries, giving them a significant advantage in longevity and field-repairability.
  • One community member stated: "I just got an Air II and can honestly say I'd take it over a Mighty," citing ease of loading, efficiency, cleaning simplicity, and replaceable batteries.
  • The Arizer products were described as "sippers" compared to the Crafty/Mighty's more powerful vapor production (described as "rippers").
  • For water pipe use, the Arizer Air with a 14mm GonG adapter was considered a more straightforward pairing than the Crafty with its aftermarket WPA adapters.
  • The ArGo was noted as not being an ideal group vaporizer compared to the Crafty or Mighty.

Crafty vs. Boundless CFX

The Boundless CFX was frequently mentioned as a budget-friendly alternative:

  • The CFX offered similar features at a substantially lower price, with a larger battery and faster charging.
  • Community members transitioning from combustion were sometimes steered toward the CFX for its value, though the Crafty was generally acknowledged as producing superior vapor quality.
  • Whether the Crafty's premium price was justified over the CFX was a matter of ongoing community debate, with the answer largely depending on individual priorities and budget.

Crafty vs. DynaVap

An interesting comparison emerged in community discussions, particularly for users transitioning from combustion:

  • The DynaVap M was recommended as an efficient, low-cost entry point for former smokers due to its ritual similarity to pipe smoking.
  • One community member even noted selling an unused Crafty because they "discovered Dynavap vapes" around the same time and never put the Crafty to use, illustrating how different use cases could steer users toward very different devices.
  • The two devices served fundamentally different niches: the DynaVap as a butane-powered, on-demand, ritual-oriented device, and the Crafty as an electronic, session-style, convenience-oriented portable.
