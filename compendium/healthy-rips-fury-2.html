# Healthy Rips Fury 2

Overview

The Healthy Rips Fury 2 is a compact, battery-powered portable dry herb vaporizer manufactured by Healthy Rips, released in 2017 as a successor to the original Fury. It employs a hybrid conduction/convection heating system and quickly gained recognition within the vaporizer community as one of the best ultra-portable units in its price class. The device is notable for its remarkably small form factor, straightforward operation, and competitive price point, making it a popular recommendation for users seeking a discreet, pocketable vaporizer for on-the-go use.

The Fury 2 was widely discussed across a popular community forum, where it was frequently recommended to newcomers and experienced users alike as a strong entry-level or dedicated portable option.

Community Reception

The Healthy Rips Fury 2 was broadly regarded within the community as an excellent ultra-portable vaporizer, especially for its price point. Community members consistently praised its compact design, fast heat-up time, and ease of use. One community member described it as their "absolute favorite out and about device," emphasizing its strength as a portable unit. Another member simply stated that the "Fury 2 is excellent."

Pros identified by the community:

  • Ultra-compact form factor: The small size was frequently cited as a primary draw, making it one of the most pocketable vaporizers available at the time.
  • Affordable price: The Fury 2 was notably less expensive than competing devices such as the Arizer ArGo and Pax 3, making it an attractive value proposition.
  • Fast heat-up: Users appreciated the quick readiness time for on-the-go sessions.
  • Quality vapor at lower temperatures: Many found the flavor production satisfying, particularly during the early portions of a session.
  • Dosing capsule system: The availability of dosing capsules was valued for convenience and keeping the chamber clean.

Cons identified by the community:

  • Battery life: This was the most commonly cited drawback. The relatively small internal battery limited session count between charges, and the non-replaceable battery was seen as a significant disadvantage compared to units with swappable cells. One member noted that the "lite battery life...kills it for me."
  • Vapor harshness at higher temperatures: Some users reported that the vapor could become harsh, particularly at elevated temperature settings. One community member mentioned that the "harsh vapor kills it for me," though this was not universally agreed upon and may have been mitigated by using the glass mouthpiece or a WPA.
  • Limited home-use appeal: Several members noted they would not choose the Fury 2 as a primary at-home device, preferring more powerful units for that context. The community consensus leaned toward the Fury 2 being best suited as a dedicated portable rather than an all-around daily driver.
  • Non-replaceable battery: Unlike competitors such as the Arizer ArGo, the Fury 2's sealed battery meant users could not carry spares, which limited extended outdoor use.

Overall, the community widely agreed that the Fury 2 punched well above its weight class for the price, but was best understood as a specialized portable device rather than a do-everything vaporizer.

Tips & Techniques

While the available community discussion focused more on general impressions than granular technique, several practical insights emerged:

  • Fine grind recommended: As with most hybrid-heating portables, a fine to medium-fine grind was generally recommended to maximize extraction and vapor production from the small chamber.
  • Dosing capsules for convenience: The stainless steel dosing capsules offered by Healthy Rips were valued by the community for pre-loading material and keeping the chamber clean. Members noted that these capsules could be pre-packed and carried for quick reloads on the go. European users reported difficulty sourcing dosing capsules due to high shipping costs and customs fees from Healthy Rips directly, and sought alternative sources within Europe.
  • Use as an on-the-go device: Experienced community members suggested treating the Fury 2 as a dedicated portable rather than a primary daily driver. Pairing it with a more powerful unit at home was a commonly recommended approach.
  • Glass mouthpiece for smoother vapor: The optional glass mouthpiece was suggested by members as an improvement over the standard plastic mouthpiece for smoother, cooler vapor—particularly at higher temperatures where harshness could be an issue.
  • Short session management: Given the auto-shutoff timer and limited battery, users learned to be efficient with their sessions, starting at lower temperatures and stepping up within a single session cycle.

The Fury 2 offers a temperature range of 300°F–430°F (149°C–221°C). While the available threads did not include highly specific temperature recommendations unique to this device, the following general guidance aligns with community discussion:

Maintenance & Cleaning

While detailed cleaning protocols were not extensively discussed in the available threads, general community guidance for the Fury 2 included:

  • Chamber cleaning: Brush out the chamber with the included cleaning brush after each session or every few sessions to prevent residue buildup. A cotton swab dipped in isopropyl alcohol (ISO) can be used for deeper cleaning of the chamber.
  • Mouthpiece maintenance: Both the standard and glass mouthpieces should be periodically soaked in isopropyl alcohol to remove resin buildup. The glass mouthpiece was easier to clean and inspect visually.
  • Dosing capsules: Using dosing capsules significantly reduces the need for chamber cleaning, as most residue stays on the capsule. Capsules themselves can be soaked in ISO.
  • Screen attention: The small screen in the chamber or mouthpiece can become clogged over time. Regular inspection and replacement or cleaning with ISO was recommended.
  • Rubber cover/cap: The rubber cover that protects the mouthpiece area was available as a replacement part. Keeping it clean ensured a good seal and maintained the device's pocket-friendly profile.

Common Issues & Fixes

Based on community discussion, the following issues were noted:

  • Short battery life: The most frequently mentioned limitation. There is no fix for the battery capacity itself, but users mitigated this by carrying a portable USB battery pack for recharging between sessions, or by using the Fury 2 alongside a second device.
  • Harsh vapor at high temperatures: Addressed by using the glass mouthpiece, attaching a WPA for water filtration, or simply lowering the temperature setting.
  • Non-replaceable battery degradation: Over time, the sealed lithium-ion battery would lose capacity. This was a known limitation with no user-serviceable remedy, and was a factor that led some users to consider devices with replaceable batteries for long-term ownership.
  • Accessory availability in Europe: European users reported difficulty obtaining dosing capsules and accessories directly from Healthy Rips due to customs fees and high international shipping costs. Community members sought workarounds such as purchasing from other members or European-based retailers.

Accessories & Modifications

The Fury 2 supported a modest but well-regarded accessory ecosystem:

  • Dosing Capsules: Stainless steel capsules that fit inside the chamber, allowing pre-loading of material and significantly reducing cleaning requirements. Highly recommended by the community for on-the-go use.
  • Glass Mouthpiece: An optional upgrade from the standard plastic mouthpiece, providing smoother and cooler vapor. Community members noted improved flavor and reduced harshness.
  • Water Pipe Adapter (WPA): A dedicated WPA was available from Healthy Rips, allowing the Fury 2 to be used with a water piece for filtered, cooled vapor. This was a popular modification for home use to address vapor harshness at higher temperatures.
  • Rubber Cover/Cap: A protective rubber cap that covered the mouthpiece area, improving pocketability and discretion. Replacement covers were available as separate accessories.
  • Aftermarket accessories: The community also explored third-party mouthpiece and stem options, though specific compatibility details varied.

Fury 2 vs. Arizer ArGo

This was the most directly discussed comparison in the community. Both devices occupy the ultra-portable niche, but differ in meaningful ways:

The community generally viewed the ArGo as the superior long-term investment due to its replaceable battery and smoother vapor path, while the Fury 2 was seen as the better value and the more compact option. One community member who owned both a Fury and an Arizer Solo 2 (which shares the ArGo's heating approach) preferred the Arizer line overall but still valued the Fury as an on-the-go option. The interchangeable battery of the ArGo was repeatedly cited as "a huge advantage over the Fury."

Both devices were described as "solid choices" by the community, with the best pick depending on individual priorities: budget and maximum portability favored the Fury 2, while vapor quality, battery flexibility, and long-term durability favored the ArGo.

Fury 2 vs. Pax 3

Though less extensively compared in the available discussions, the Fury 2 was frequently mentioned as an alternative when users were considering a Pax 3. The Fury 2 was generally seen as offering comparable or better vapor quality at a significantly lower price, though the Pax 3 offered additional features such as app control and concentrate compatibility. Several users who were initially considering the Pax 3 were redirected toward the Fury 2 by community recommendations.
