# Cera Vaporizer

Overview

The Cera is a portable dry herb and essential oil vaporizer distinguished by its ceramic body and modular heating core design. Manufactured as a premium, artisan-style device, the Cera was available in both stainless steel and titanium body variants. It is powered by a single removable 18650 lithium-ion battery and utilizes interchangeable heating cores for loose leaf material and essential oils. The Cera was positioned as a connoisseur-grade portable vaporizer, reflecting a design philosophy centered on material purity — particularly its ceramic construction and use of FDA-approved o-rings.

The device appears to have been commercially available by at least early 2013, with community marketplace listings appearing from April 2013 onward. One community member noted that a classified listing from that period was believed to be "the FIRST Cera in the classifieds," suggesting the device was still relatively new to the broader enthusiast community at that time.

The Cera was sold in several configurations. A "Complete Kit" typically included both the loose leaf and essential oil heating cores, multiple mouthpieces, a thermal cap sleeve, an 18650 charger, and batteries. A "Connoisseur's Kit" bundled the unit with two loose leaf heating cores, one essential oil core, two thermal cap sleeves, and a large supply of batteries. These kits reflected the community's practice of maintaining multiple heating cores and rotating batteries for optimal performance and longevity.

Community Reception

The Cera occupied a niche position within the vaporizer community as a premium, handcrafted portable device. Its ceramic vapor path and material-conscious design (FDA-approved o-rings, ceramic body, stainless steel or titanium shell) appealed to users who prioritized flavor purity and build quality.

Pros as noted by the community:

  • High-quality, pure materials throughout the vapor path
  • Modular heating core system allowing easy switching between loose leaf and essential oil use
  • Removable 18650 battery enabling extended use through battery rotation
  • Available in both stainless steel and titanium body options
  • The titanium model was regarded as particularly desirable

Cons and concerns:

  • The device experienced a known issue with the internal grounding strap, which required manufacturer repair or update (see Common Issues & Fixes below)
  • Reliability was a concern for some owners, though at least one community member reported that "with latest fixes they are plenty reliable"
  • Limited availability and higher price point placed it outside the mainstream
  • The need to keep backup units was mentioned by at least one owner, suggesting early reliability concerns were not trivial

Despite these issues, the Cera generated strong demand within the community. When the first known classified listing appeared, it sold almost immediately — the seller noted "That was quick, everything on hold until payment is received tonight. I think I already have a 2nd in line as well," and another member expressed eager interest in case the sale fell through.

Resale pricing in the community marketplace ranged from approximately $200 for a stainless steel loose leaf unit with batteries to $245–$255 for full connoisseur kits with multiple heating cores and large battery collections.

Tips & Techniques

Based on available community discussion, the following practices were common among Cera owners:

  • Battery rotation: Experienced users maintained large collections of 18650 batteries (listings showed owners keeping 12–13 cells) and rotated them to extend overall battery lifespan. One seller described all batteries as "young because of rotation," indicating this was considered best practice for maintaining consistent performance.
  • Button-top vs. flat-top batteries: Both button-top (e.g., Orbtronic 2900mAh Panasonic PD2900) and flat-top (e.g., NCR18650PD) 18650 cells were used. Users should confirm compatibility with their specific unit.
  • Thermal Cap Sleeve: The included thermal cap sleeve was considered an essential accessory, likely serving to manage external heat and protect the user during sessions.
  • Safety pin: Included in kits, the safety pin was likely used for clearing or maintaining the airpath and heating core area.
  • Dedicated mouthpieces: The essential oil heating core used a different mouthpiece than the loose leaf core. Owners were advised to keep these organized and use the correct mouthpiece for each material type.

Maintenance & Cleaning

While detailed cleaning protocols were not extensively documented in the reviewed threads, several maintenance-related details emerged:

  • O-rings: The Cera uses FDA-approved o-rings, and replacement o-ring kits were included with sales, suggesting periodic o-ring replacement was expected maintenance. Keeping spare o-rings on hand was standard practice.
  • Heating cores: The modular, interchangeable heating core design likely facilitated cleaning, as cores could be removed from the body for individual maintenance.
  • Separate cores for separate materials: By maintaining dedicated heating cores for loose leaf and essential oils, users could avoid cross-contamination of flavors and residues.

Common Issues & Fixes

Internal Grounding Strap Failure

The most widely documented issue with the Cera involved the internal grounding strap. Early units experienced failures related to this component, which was significant enough that the manufacturer implemented a revised design.

  • The fix: Later units and repaired units featured a laser-welded copper strap, which was considered the reliable solution to the problem. One community member selling a stainless steel unit confirmed it had "the latest updates to address a known issue with the internal grounding strap."
  • Reliability after fix: At least one owner who had both stainless steel and titanium models reported that "with latest fixes they are plenty reliable," to the point where maintaining a backup unit was no longer considered necessary.
  • Users purchasing secondhand Cera units were advised to confirm whether the grounding strap had been updated to the laser-welded copper version.
