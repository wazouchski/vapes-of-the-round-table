# Mighty+ (Storz & Bickel)

Overview

The Mighty+ (also written as Mighty Plus or Mighty+) is a portable dry herb vaporizer manufactured by Storz & Bickel, the German company also known for producing the Volcano desktop vaporizer. Released in 2021 as an update to the original Mighty (which launched in 2014), the Mighty+ is a hybrid conduction/convection vaporizer powered by an internal lithium-ion battery. It is widely considered one of the most recognizable and frequently recommended portable vaporizers in the enthusiast community, though opinions on its merits relative to newer competitors became increasingly nuanced over time.

Storz & Bickel's own marketing materials contained a notable contradiction early on, describing the heating system as both "Full Convection Hot Air" and "a full hot air convection heating combined with conduction," a discrepancy observed by community members. The community consensus settled on describing the Mighty and Mighty+ as hybrid heaters — combining conduction heating from the oven walls with convection airflow through the material.

The Mighty+ refined the original Mighty formula with USB-C charging, a ceramic-coated oven for easier cleaning, and faster heat-up time, while retaining the same general form factor, cooling unit design, and session-oriented usage philosophy.

Community Reception

The Mighty+ occupied a distinctive position within the vaporizer community: it was simultaneously one of the most frequently recommended portable vaporizers — especially for newcomers — and one of the most frequently critiqued by experienced users seeking higher performance.

Strengths

  • Consistency and reliability of vapor production: The Mighty+ was widely praised for delivering consistent results session after session with minimal technique required. Multiple community members highlighted it as among the easiest vaporizers to hand to a friend or family member with no vaping experience.
  • Even extraction without stirring: The hybrid heating system was noted for extracting material evenly without requiring users to stir the bowl mid-session, a significant convenience advantage over pure convection devices.
  • Safety from combustion: Several community members specifically noted that the Mighty+ was essentially impossible to combust with, making it an excellent recommendation for users with medical needs or those transitioning from smoking. One member specifically recommended it for a blind user on the basis that it was "totally safe in not combusting."
  • Dosing capsule system: The pre-loadable dosing capsules were praised for outdoor and travel use, with one community member stating that "nothing beats the mighty+ caps" for camping situations.
  • Session-friendly design: For users who preferred a slower, relaxed session style, the Mighty+ delivered a reliable 10–15 minute experience.

Criticisms

  • Non-replaceable battery: This was perhaps the most frequently cited concern. Community members noted that after the 2–3 year warranty period, the sealed battery could fail, leaving users with a potentially expensive paperweight or requiring a fee and shipping for battery replacement service. This was described as a "pretty big deal" by users considering the purchase.
  • Plastic construction: The plastic housing was a recurring point of criticism, particularly among users comparing it to devices built from metal, wood, or other premium materials. One community member contrasted it unfavorably with the Angus halogen vape's build quality.
  • Cooling unit cleaning burden: The CU — a labyrinthine plastic assembly through which vapor passes before reaching the mouthpiece — was nearly universally described as a pain to clean. One community member suggested stocking up on multiple cooling units and cycling through them so that "someone could clean them in one go," noting that "with three or four cooling units you should be able to have a full month without cleaning."
  • Heater power limitations: At approximately 42W, the Mighty+'s heater was considered relatively weak by enthusiast standards. Community members noted it required slow draws to perform well, with one member explicitly stating: "weak heater, slow draw is needed." This contrasted sharply with devices like the Tinymight, which drew up to 70W.
  • Vapor quality: While adequate for many users, experienced community members described the Mighty+'s vapor as "flimsy" compared to devices with superior heat retention or more powerful convection heaters.
  • Portability limitations: Despite being classified as a portable, multiple community members noted that the Mighty+ was not truly pocketable due to its size. One user specifically stated, "My mighty ain't pocketable."
  • Price relative to competition: As newer devices emerged, community members increasingly described the Mighty+ as "outdated, overpriced and overhyped," though this was a minority opinion and others continued to reach for it as their primary device.
  • Unclenable internal air path: Some users expressed concern that portions of the air path within the device body (between the heater and the cooling unit) could not be accessed for cleaning, with one member reporting an "awful taste of plastic" when a unit failed during use.

Overall Standing

The Mighty+ remained a community staple — a reliable workhorse that was easy to recommend, especially to new users or medical patients. However, experienced users with access to more powerful on-demand convection devices increasingly viewed it as a compromise. As one community member summarized, it "does some things very well which has helped it remain a favorite," but newer competitors exposed its limitations. The community widely agreed it was a solid session vaporizer but not the performance leader it once was.

Tips & Techniques

Loading

  • Full oven: Grind herb to medium consistency and fill the oven without packing too tightly. The Mighty+ performs best with a relatively loose fill that allows airflow through the material.
  • Dosing capsules: Load approximately 0.1–0.15g per capsule. These are highly recommended for on-the-go use, keeping the oven clean and making reloading fast. The ceramic-coated oven on the Mighty+ stays cleaner than the original Mighty even without capsules, leading some users to skip capsules entirely — one community member noted the "ceramic coated Crafty" (which shares the same oven coating as the Mighty+) stayed "so clean" that capsules were unnecessary.
  • Micro-dosing: For smaller loads without capsules, place a small amount of herb in the oven and compress it under the liquid/concentrate pad to hold it in place. One community member reported success with "less than 0.1g, compressed under the oil pad" at 180–185°C.

Draw Technique

  • Slow, steady draws: The community widely agreed that the Mighty+ responds best to slow, controlled inhales. Fast draws cool the heater faster than it can recover at 42W, resulting in thinner vapor. This was emphasized repeatedly, with multiple members specifically advising "slow draws" for best results.
  • Session pacing: Allow the device to recover temperature between draws. Rushing hits back-to-back can result in diminished vapor production as the heater struggles to maintain temperature.

General Tips

  • Pre-load capsules for outings: For camping, hiking, or any outdoor activity, pre-loading a caddy of dosing capsules was the most commonly recommended approach.
  • Use with a water pipe adapter (WPA): Many community members preferred using the Mighty+ with a WPA and bubbler rather than the stock cooling unit, finding it provided smoother vapor and was easier to maintain.
  • Glass stems with basket screens: Some community members reported abandoning the stock cooling unit and mouthpiece entirely in favor of aftermarket glass stems with basket screens, which allowed variable load sizes and simplified cleaning.

Community Notes on Temperature

  • The community debated whether temperatures above 200°C (392°F) risked partial combustion, but the consensus held that the Mighty+'s hybrid heating system and airflow design prevented actual combustion even at maximum temperature.
  • Convection-dominant devices at the same set temperature were generally observed to produce denser vapor than the Mighty+, which community members attributed to heater power differences rather than temperature accuracy — a 70W convection heater delivers more thermal energy to the material per unit time than the Mighty+'s 42W hybrid system at nominally identical temperatures.

Maintenance & Cleaning

Cooling Unit (CU)

The cooling unit was by far the most maintenance-intensive component and the most discussed cleaning topic related to the Mighty+.

  • Disassemble the CU completely: Remove the locking ring, separate the top and bottom halves, and remove the internal screen and o-rings.
  • Soak in isopropyl alcohol (ISO): Place all CU components in a bag or container of 90%+ isopropyl alcohol. Soak for at least 30 minutes for routine cleaning; longer soaks for heavily used units.
  • Rinse thoroughly: After soaking, rinse all components with warm water and allow to dry completely before reassembly.
  • Frequency: The community recommended cleaning the CU every 5–10 sessions for optimal flavor and airflow, though some users pushed this further by owning multiple CUs and rotating through them. One member suggested three to four CUs could provide a full month of use between cleaning sessions.
  • Reclaiming honey oil: The residue that accumulates in the CU is rich in cannabinoids. Some users collected this "honey" by soaking the CU in warm milk or coconut oil for edible use, or by scraping it for concentrate.

Oven

  • Brush out AVB (Already Vaped Bud) after each session using the included brush or a small stiff-bristle brush.
  • The ceramic coating on the Mighty+ oven significantly reduces sticky buildup compared to the original Mighty's uncoated oven. Users of the ceramic-coated version reported needing to clean the oven much less frequently.
  • Occasional ISO wipe: Dip a cotton swab in isopropyl alcohol and wipe the oven walls when residue accumulates.

Internal Air Path

  • The internal air path between the heater assembly and the cooling unit is not user-serviceable. This was a noted concern among community members who prioritized clean vapor paths. Some users ran empty heating cycles at maximum temperature as a "burn-off" to reduce buildup, though the effectiveness of this approach was debated.

Common Issues & Fixes

Battery Degradation

  • Issue: Battery life diminishing over time, with the non-replaceable internal battery eventually failing.
  • Community Advice: Register the product immediately upon purchase to extend the warranty to 3 years. Some users reported successfully having units replaced under warranty for battery issues. After warranty expiration, Storz & Bickel offered paid battery replacement service, though this required shipping the unit.
  • Prevention: Avoid leaving the unit on the charger continuously after reaching full charge. Use pass-through charging sparingly.

Cooling Unit Airflow Restriction

  • Issue: Draw resistance increasing noticeably over time.
  • Cause: Resin buildup in the CU restricting airflow.
  • Fix: Clean the CU as described in the maintenance section. Regular cleaning prevents this from becoming a chronic issue.

Slow Charging

  • Issue: The Mighty+ charges relatively slowly compared to devices with swappable 18650 batteries.
  • Community Advice: The USB-C charging on the Mighty+ was an improvement over the original Mighty's charging speed, but users who needed extended use purchased portable power banks for travel. Pass-through charging allowed use while plugged in, which partially mitigated this concern.

Plastic Taste on New Units

  • Issue: Some users reported a faint plastic taste when first using a new Mighty+.
  • Fix: Run 2–3 empty heating cycles at maximum temperature (210°C) before first use to off-gas any manufacturing residues.

Unit Failure / Burning Smell

  • Issue: One community member reported a unit dying during use with an "awful taste of plastic," which they attributed to an internal fuse or component failure.
  • Advice: Contact Storz & Bickel immediately for warranty replacement. Do not continue using a unit that produces unusual smells or tastes.

Accessories & Modifications

Dosing Capsules

  • Storz & Bickel's proprietary dosing capsules (also compatible with the Crafty, Crafty+, and Volcano Hybrid with adapter) were among the most popular accessories. Pre-loading capsules was standard practice for portable use.
  • Capsule caddies (including aftermarket XL versions holding 8 capsules) were widely used for travel and outdoor sessions.

Water Pipe Adapters (WPA)

  • Multiple WPA options were available, allowing the Mighty+ to connect to 14mm and 18mm glass pieces. Using the Mighty+ with a bubbler was a popular configuration, especially for home use, as it cooled the vapor significantly and reduced throat irritation.
  • Some community members used the Mighty+ exclusively with a WPA, bypassing the stock cooling unit entirely.

Aftermarket Mouthpieces and Stems

  • Titanium mouthpiece: A popular aftermarket upgrade from retailers like Cream City Vapes (CCV), offering improved durability over the plastic stock mouthpiece.
  • Glass stems with basket screens: Some users replaced the entire CU assembly with aftermarket glass stems, simplifying cleaning and allowing more flexible load sizes.
  • Aftermarket cooling unit alternatives: Third-party CU designs offering improved airflow or easier cleaning were discussed, though the stock CU remained the most common configuration.

Liquid/Concentrate Pad

  • The included liquid pad could be used for concentrates placed on top of herb, or inverted on top of a small load to hold material in place for micro-dosing.

Comparisons

Mighty+ vs. Original Mighty

The Mighty+ was an incremental upgrade: USB-C charging (faster than the original's barrel charger), ceramic-coated oven (easier to clean), and slightly faster heat-up time (~60–90 seconds vs. ~2 minutes). The fundamental vaping experience remained largely the same. Community members who already owned an original Mighty generally considered the upgrade non-essential unless their original unit was failing, though the ceramic oven coating was appreciated. Existing Mighty accessories (dosing capsules, CUs, WPAs) were cross-compatible.

Mighty+ vs. Tinymight / Tinymight 2

This was one of the most frequently discussed comparisons in the community. The Tinymight offered 70W of convection heating power compared to the Mighty+'s 42W hybrid system, enabling dramatically faster extraction — clearing a bowl in 1–2 draws versus the Mighty+'s 10–15 minute session. Community members who preferred convection heating and on-demand use consistently rated the Tinymight's vapor quality higher, describing the Mighty+'s vapor as comparatively "flimsy." The Tinymight also featured a user-replaceable 18650 battery.

However, the Mighty+ held advantages in ease of use, consistency without technique, and the dosing capsule ecosystem. The community widely agreed the two devices served different use cases: the Mighty+ for effortless sessions, and the Tinymight for on-demand power. One member summarized: "If you like convection, TM2 is a much better choice than the Mighty."

Mighty+ vs. Crafty+

The Crafty+ shared the same heating technology and oven design but with a smaller single battery, resulting in significantly shorter battery life. Users who found the Crafty+'s battery insufficient frequently upgraded to the Mighty+ specifically for the larger battery. The vapor experience was considered essentially identical between the two devices.

Mighty+ vs. Arizer Solo II

The Solo II was mentioned as an alternative offering "huge battery, consistent results, big clouds, almost zero maintenance." Its glass stems were considered far easier to clean than the Mighty+'s cooling unit, with replacement stems available cheaply on eBay. However, the Solo II was a primarily conduction device with longer session times and was considered less hard-hitting than the Mighty+.

Mighty+ vs. Angus (Halogen Vape)

The Angus drew direct comparisons as a potential Mighty replacement. Community members who used both described the Angus as having "considerably better heat retention," superior flavor handling at both high and low temperatures, and better build materials. One member stated that the Angus "shows it for the outdated, overpriced and overhyped device it is." However, the Angus was a newer, less proven product with concerns about battery heat and long-term durability, while the Mighty+ had years of established reliability data.

Mighty+ vs. Tafée Bowle

The Tafée Bowle was discussed as a portable convection alternative that produced noticeably cooler vapor than the Mighty+ — described by one community member as providing cooler hits than any combination of stems and cooling beads on other devices. Some Mighty+ owners considered the Bowle as a home-use replacement while retaining the Mighty+ for true portable scenarios. Battery life on the Bowle was noted as a concern.

Mighty+ vs. Desktop Vaporizers

Community members transitioning from the Mighty+ to desktop devices like the Flowerpot, Herborizer, or VapeXhale EVO consistently described the desktop experience as a significant upgrade in extraction power, vapor quality, and session speed. The Mighty+ was generally not recommended as a substitute for a desktop setup, but rather as a complement for portable use. One member who owned both a Volcano and Mighty+ described using the Mighty+ specifically for situations where the desktop was impractical.

Mighty+ vs. DynaVap

The DynaVap's small bowl (~0.05–0.1g) and on-demand butane heating were functionally very different from the Mighty+'s session approach. Community members noted that direct comparisons of "potency" between the two were misleading due to the significant difference in bowl size — the Mighty+ held 2–3x more material. Users who preferred the ritual of a slower session gravitated toward the Mighty+, while those seeking efficiency and quick extraction preferred the DynaVap. Both occupied respected but distinct roles in most users' collections.
