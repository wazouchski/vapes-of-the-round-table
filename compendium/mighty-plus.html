# Mighty+ (Storz & Bickel)

Overview

The Mighty+ is a portable dry herb vaporizer manufactured by Storz & Bickel, the German company also known for the Volcano desktop vaporizer. Released in 2021 as an updated version of the original Mighty (introduced in 2014), the Mighty+ features a hybrid heating system that combines conduction and convection heating. It is powered by an internal rechargeable battery (non-replaceable) and features USB-C charging, an improvement over the original Mighty's barrel-style charger.

Storz & Bickel's own marketing initially described the Mighty as "Full Convection Hot Air," though further in their own product copy they clarified it operates "on the basis of a full hot air convection heating combined with conduction." The community widely recognized the device as a hybrid vaporizer, with the conduction element playing a significant role in its session-oriented heating behavior. The Mighty+ draws approximately 42 watts from its heater, a specification that became relevant in community discussions comparing it to higher-powered convection portables.

The Mighty+ remained one of the most widely recommended portable vaporizers in the community, particularly for beginners and those seeking a reliable, consistent session experience. However, it was also one of the most critically discussed devices among experienced users, who frequently debated its limitations relative to newer portable designs.

Community Reception

The Mighty+ occupied a distinctive position in the community: it was simultaneously one of the most recommended vaporizers for newcomers and one of the most criticized by experienced users. The community regarded it as a benchmark — the device against which other portables were frequently measured, for better or worse.

Strengths

  • Consistency and reliability of sessions: The Mighty+ was widely praised for delivering consistent, repeatable results with minimal technique required. Community members frequently recommended it as a "set it and forget it" device.
  • Ease of use: The simple two-button interface and clear display made it accessible to all users. One community member specifically recommended it as a vaporizer that was "totally safe in not combusting," making it suitable for users who needed reliability above all else.
  • Dosing capsule system: The pre-loadable capsules were highly valued for outdoor use, camping, and situations requiring discretion and convenience. One member noted that "nothing beats the Mighty+ caps" for camping and on-the-go situations.
  • Even extraction without stirring: Due to its hybrid heating, the Mighty+ required no stirring of the bowl during a session, a significant convenience advantage over pure convection devices.
  • Vapor quality for a session vape: While not the coolest or tastiest in the portable market, the CU's labyrinth cooling path delivered smoother vapor than many competitors.

Criticisms

  • Non-replaceable battery: This was consistently cited as one of the device's most significant drawbacks. Community members expressed concern that after the warranty period (2–3 years), the unit could become unusable with no option for user-serviceable battery replacement. This concern was especially prominent in purchase advice threads.
  • Plastic construction: The extensive use of plastic in the body and CU drew criticism, particularly from users who valued premium build materials. One community member contrasted it with alternatives that used metal, wood, or glass construction.
  • Cleaning difficulty: The Cooling Unit was universally acknowledged as a pain to clean. Its multi-part labyrinth design collected resin effectively but required regular disassembly and soaking. One member described the CU cleaning as a barrier to accessibility, noting that it was "anything but barrier-free."
  • Non-cleanable internal airpath: Multiple community members raised concerns about the heater and internal airpath being encased in plastic and not being user-serviceable. One member who had owned multiple Crafty units (which share the same internal design) reported an "awful taste of plastic" when units failed, raising concerns about long-term airpath cleanliness.
  • Heater power limitations: At 42W, the Mighty+ was considered a "weak hitter" by community members accustomed to higher-powered devices. The community noted that it required slow draws for optimal performance and could not match the extraction speed of 70W+ convection portables.
  • Slow charging: Despite the upgrade to USB-C, charging speed remained a complaint, particularly for heavy users.
  • Price: The Mighty+ was frequently described as "overpriced" by experienced community members, especially as newer portables offered comparable or superior performance at similar or lower price points.
  • Size: Several members noted the Mighty+ was not truly "pocketable," with one stating plainly: "My mighty ain't pocketable."

Overall Sentiment

The community's relationship with the Mighty+ was complex. Many members who moved on to other devices reported periodically "missing" their Mighty, suggesting it filled a unique niche in the session vaping experience. As one member put it, they had owned multiple Mighties and sold them all, "mostly because I dislike the vapor from them but also for the plastic and the slow charging," yet "for some reason, I always end up missing my Mighty/Crafty at some point." Another experienced member characterized it as "outdated, overpriced and overhyped" while acknowledging that "many love Mighty and it does some things very well which has helped it remain a favorite."

Tips & Techniques

Loading

  • Standard loading: Fill the chamber to just below the rim with a medium grind. Do not overpack — a light tamp is sufficient. The hybrid heating works best when air can still flow through the material.
  • Dosing capsules: Load capsules with approximately 0.1–0.15g of ground herb. Capsules add slight draw resistance but dramatically simplify loading, unloading, and cleaning. For outdoor and camping use, pre-load multiple capsules and carry them in a capsule caddy.
  • Microdosing: For smaller loads, compress material under the liquid pad (concentrate pad placed on top of the herb). One user reported successfully taking 5 separate hits from less than 0.1g using this method at 180°C–185°C.
  • Bowl size flexibility with glass stems: Community members discovered that using aftermarket glass stems with basket screens allowed for a variety of load sizes while bypassing the stock CU entirely.

Draw Technique

  • Slow, steady draws are essential: Due to the 42W heater, the Mighty+ performs best with slow to moderate draw speed. Fast draws will outpace the heater's ability to maintain temperature, resulting in thinner vapor.
  • Session pacing: The Mighty+ is designed as a session vaporizer. A typical session lasts 10–20 minutes. Take steady sips rather than trying to rip it hard.
  • Medical use: For users with lung sensitivity, the Mighty+ works well at lower temperatures with slow, gentle draws. One member recommended it specifically for a cancer patient transitioning from Volcano bags, though they noted the draw technique differs significantly from a balloon system.

Bypassing the Cooling Unit

Experienced community members discovered that using the Mighty+ with a WPA or aftermarket glass stems instead of the stock CU improved the experience significantly. One knowledgeable member stated: "I don't even use the cooling unit anymore or the stock stem, there are so many other options now with it... Best is still with WPA and other glass pieces like hooks though, even without water."

Community Notes on Temperature

  • The Mighty+ maxes out at 210°C (410°F), which approaches but generally does not cause combustion due to the hybrid heating design and the fact that convection airflow helps regulate actual herb temperature.
  • Community members debated whether higher-powered convection devices achieved more efficient extraction at the same nominal temperature. The consensus was that the Mighty+'s 42W heater meant that actual herb temperature could lag behind the display reading during active draws, whereas more powerful heaters (e.g., 70W in the Tinymight) maintained temperature more effectively under airflow. This explained why the Mighty+ required slower draws for optimal performance.
  • One member noted that at 210°C on the Solo, herb tasted more combusted than at 210°C on the Mighty, attributing this to the differences in convection vs. conduction heating ratios between the two devices.

Maintenance & Cleaning

Cooling Unit (CU) Cleaning

The CU was the primary maintenance challenge and the most discussed cleaning topic. Community members developed various approaches:

  • Disassembly: The CU separates into several parts including the cap, the seal ring, the labyrinth disc, and the mouthpiece. Complete disassembly is necessary for thorough cleaning.
  • Isopropyl alcohol (ISO) soak: Soak all CU components in 90%+ isopropyl alcohol for 15–30 minutes. Rinse thoroughly with warm water and allow to fully dry before reassembly.
  • Multiple CU rotation: A commonly recommended strategy was to purchase 3–4 spare Cooling Units and rotate them, allowing for extended use between cleaning sessions. One community member suggested this approach could allow "a full month without cleaning."
  • Reclaiming honey oil: The resin that accumulates in the CU is concentrated cannabinoid-rich material. Some users saved their ISO-soaked reclaim for later use.

Chamber Cleaning

  • Use a brush (included with the device) to clean the chamber after each session or every few sessions.
  • Dosing capsules dramatically reduce chamber cleaning requirements, as herb never directly contacts the chamber walls.

Internal Airpath Concerns

The most persistent maintenance concern in the community was the non-serviceable internal airpath. The heater and internal vapor path are sealed within the plastic body and cannot be accessed for cleaning. Multiple members expressed concern about long-term resin buildup in areas that could not be reached. One long-term Crafty owner (same internal design) reported that failed units emitted an "awful taste of plastic," raising questions about whether internal contamination contributed to this. This was a significant factor driving some experienced users toward vaporizers with fully cleanable or disassemblable airpaths.

Common Issues & Fixes

Battery Degradation

  • Issue: Non-replaceable batteries degrade over time, eventually leading to reduced session count per charge and ultimately device failure.
  • Community perspective: This was widely considered the Mighty+'s biggest design flaw. After the warranty period expires, battery failure effectively renders the device disposable. While some technically inclined users attempted battery replacement, this was not an officially supported procedure.
  • Mitigation: Register the product for the extended 3-year warranty. Some users treated the Mighty+ as a device with a defined lifespan and budgeted accordingly.

CU Clogging

  • Issue: Resin buildup restricts airflow through the Cooling Unit.
  • Fix: Regular cleaning on a weekly or bi-weekly basis depending on usage. Rotating multiple CUs ensures a clean one is always available.

Slow Draw Requirement

  • Issue: Users accustomed to bongs, joints, or more powerful vaporizers found the Mighty+ underwhelming when drawing quickly.
  • Fix: Adjust technique to slow, steady draws. The heater cannot maintain temperature under fast airflow. This is a fundamental design characteristic, not a defect.

Unit Failure

  • Issue: Some units failed during use, occasionally producing an unpleasant plastic-like smell (likely related to internal fuse or component failure).
  • Fix: Contact Storz & Bickel for warranty replacement. Multiple community members reported going through several warranty replacements over years of use.

Accessories & Modifications

Stock Accessories

  • Dosing Capsules: Aluminum capsules that pre-load herb, simplifying loading/unloading and keeping the chamber clean. Available in magazines (capsule caddies) for carrying multiple pre-loaded capsules.
  • Cooling Unit (CU): The stock vapor cooling system. Purchasing extras for rotation was a widely recommended practice.
  • Liquid/Concentrate Pad: A steel mesh pad placed on top of herb for microdosing (to keep small loads compressed) or used with concentrates.

Aftermarket Accessories

  • Water Pipe Adapters (WPA): Various WPA options allow connecting the Mighty+ to glass water pieces. Community members widely agreed this was one of the best ways to use the device, significantly improving vapor quality and cooling.
  • CCV (Cream City Vapes) Titanium Mouthpiece: A popular aftermarket mouthpiece upgrade replacing the plastic stock CU top. Offered improved durability and heat resistance.
  • CCV Sidecar Bubbler: A compact glass bubbler attachment specifically designed for the Mighty+, adding water filtration in a portable form factor.
  • Aftermarket Glass Stems with Basket Screens: Allowed bypassing the CU entirely, offering variable load sizes and easier cleaning. One experienced community member enthusiastically endorsed this approach.
  • XL Capsule Caddies: Larger capsule carriers for users who pre-loaded many dosing capsules for extended outings.

Creative Modifications

The community produced some memorable modifications, including one member who mounted two Mighty units onto a drinking hat for hands-free dual-vaporizer use — a creation that the community celebrated for its absurdity, with one member noting, "While the money management here is pretty questionable, the end result is amazing."

Comparisons

Mighty+ vs. Crafty+

The Crafty+ shares the same heating system and internal design but in a smaller, single-battery form factor. Community members who found the Crafty+'s battery life insufficient were natural candidates for the Mighty+ upgrade. The Mighty+ offered significantly better battery life and the addition of an on-device display (the Crafty relied on a smartphone app for temperature control). However, both shared the same fundamental limitations: non-replaceable batteries, plastic construction, and non-serviceable internal airpaths.

Mighty+ vs. Tinymight / Tinymight 2

This was one of the most frequently discussed comparisons in the community. Key differences:

  • Heating type: Mighty+ is hybrid (conduction + convection session); Tinymight is primarily convection with both on-demand and session modes.
  • Heater power: Mighty+ at ~42W vs. Tinymight at ~70W. The community noted this power difference explained why the Tinymight could extract a bowl in 1–2 draws while the Mighty+ required a full 10–20 minute session.
  • Battery: Mighty+ has a non-replaceable internal battery; Tinymight uses a single replaceable 18650 cell, allowing indefinite battery life through swaps.
  • Build materials: Tinymight uses wood and glass; Mighty+ uses primarily plastic.
  • Ease of use: The Mighty+ was consistently rated easier to use, particularly for beginners. The Tinymight required more technique and was noted as being a better choice for users who explicitly preferred convection extraction.
  • Vapor character: Multiple members described Mighty+ vapor as "flimsy" compared to convection-heavy devices. The Tinymight was regarded as producing tastier, more flavorful vapor, especially at lower temperatures.
  • Cleaning: The Tinymight's glass stem and minimal airpath were simpler to clean, though its internal heater area was debated. The Mighty+'s CU was more complex to clean but its hybrid design meant no stirring was required.
  • One community member summed up the comparison: "If you like convection, TM2 is a much better choice than the Mighty."

Mighty+ vs. Arizer Solo II

  • The Solo II offered significantly longer battery life and extremely simple maintenance (glass stems that require only debowling and occasional ISO soaking).
  • The Mighty+ was generally considered to produce thicker vapor and more efficient extraction.
  • One member noted that at the same temperature setting (210°C), the Solo tasted "more combusted" than the Mighty, likely due to differences in heating approach.
  • The Solo II was a common recommendation for users prioritizing ease of maintenance and battery longevity over extraction power.

Mighty+ vs. Angus (Halogen Vape)

The Angus drew direct comparison as a potential Mighty+ replacement:

  • The Angus offered halogen-based heating, swappable 18650 batteries, and what users described as significantly better heat retention and vapor quality.
  • One community member who had owned both stated that the Angus provided "overall better experience in every aspect" and that it "shows [the Mighty+] for the outdated, overpriced and overhyped device" it was. However, this was a minority opinion from an early adopter, and the Angus was too new for broad community consensus.
  • Concerns about the Angus included reports of hot batteries and questions about long-term durability.

Mighty+ vs. Desktop Vaporizers

Multiple community members noted that the Mighty+ could not compete with dedicated desktop units in terms of extraction power, flavor, or session speed. Devices like the Volcano (from the same manufacturer), Flowerpot, Herborizer, and VapeXhale EVO were cited as providing substantially superior experiences for home use. The Mighty+'s value proposition was specifically its portability and convenience — a trade-off the community generally accepted.

Mighty+ vs. DynaVap

The DynaVap offered a fundamentally different experience: butane-powered, on-demand, tiny bowl (~0.05–0.1g), fully cleanable and repairable. Community members noted that direct comparisons of potency were often flawed because bowl sizes differed significantly. One member pointed out they were loading 0.1–0.15g in Mighty+ dosing capsules versus 0.1g in the DynaVap, making the comparison more equitable than the "DynaVap destroys my Mighty" narratives suggested. The DynaVap was frequently recommended as a complementary device rather than a replacement, particularly for microdosing and fully cleanable airpath priorities.

This article reflects community knowledge and opinions gathered over years of discussion. Individual experiences may vary, and the vaporizer market continues to evolve. The Mighty+ remains a significant reference point in portable vaporizer history, representing both the achievements and limitations of its generation of battery-powered portable designs.
