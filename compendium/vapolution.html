# Vapolution

Overview

The Vapolution is a desktop dry herb vaporizer manufactured by Vapolution, Inc. in the United States. The line includes the original Vapolution and the Vapolution 2 (often referred to as the "EV2" or "Vapolution 2.0"), as well as the torch-powered Vapolution Pocket Vape. The defining characteristic of the Vapolution is its 100% all-glass vapor path, which was a major selling point among health-conscious users in the vaporizer community.

The Vapolution uses nichrome resistance wire as its heating element, which is positioned outside the vapor path, heating borosilicate glass that in turn heats the herb. The heating method is primarily convection, with air being drawn through a heated glass chamber containing the herb. It is an AC-powered (corded) desktop unit.

The Vapolution was recognized by the community as one of the most affordable all-glass vapor path vaporizers available, and it was manufactured in the United States — a point of pride frequently mentioned in discussions.

Community Reception

The Vapolution enjoyed a loyal following within the vaporizer community, though opinions were somewhat divided on the unit overall.

Praised aspects:

  • All-glass vapor path: The Vapolution was consistently cited as the most affordable AC desktop vaporizer with a completely glass vapor path. For users whose primary concern was vapor purity and clean taste, this was a significant advantage. One community member described the all-glass design as "primo," noting it produced the best taste they had experienced.
  • Flavor quality: The restricted bowl size and favorable air-to-vapor ratio were credited with producing thick, flavorful vapor despite the unit's modest price point.
  • Ease of cleaning: Both the heating element area and the bowl piece were noted as easy to clean — a characteristic described as "rare" among vaporizers.
  • Affordability: At approximately $100 shipped (with community discount codes), the Vapolution was positioned as an excellent entry-level option for users seeking an all-glass experience.
  • U.S. manufacturing: The unit was made entirely in the United States, which appealed to users who preferred domestically produced products.

Criticized aspects:

  • Temperature control reliability: A notable and recurring concern was the reliability of the Vapolution's temperature control. One long-standing and well-respected community member stated plainly that the Da Buddha Vaporizer's temperature control had "easily been more reliable than that of the 'made in the US' Vapolution" throughout its production history. This suggested that dial or control issues were a known concern over time.
  • Corded/desktop limitation: As a desktop unit requiring AC power, the Vapolution offered no portability.
  • Fragility: Being constructed primarily of glass, the unit required careful handling.

Overall, the community regarded the Vapolution as a strong niche option — particularly for health-conscious users seeking the purest possible vapor path at a budget-friendly price — but it was not considered a mainstream top-tier desktop in the way that units like the Da Buddha, Silver Surfer, or E-Nano were.

Tips & Techniques

  • Bowl loading: The Vapolution's bowl is intentionally small and restricted. Community members recommended packing lightly and not overfilling, as the restricted bowl size was part of the design's strength — it created a favorable air-to-vapor ratio that produced thick clouds from small amounts of herb.
  • Draw technique: Because the unit is convection-based, a slow, steady draw was generally recommended to allow hot air to pass evenly through the herb.
  • Leave-on capability: Like other desktop log-style and convection vaporizers discussed in the same threads, the Vapolution could be left on and ready for use, allowing nearly instant sessions when the mood struck. However, community discussions did not specifically confirm 24/7 leave-on use for this unit in the way it was endorsed for the E-Nano.
  • Use with the All in Wonder Bowl: Community members recommended purchasing the Vapolution with the "All in Wonder Bowl" accessory, which was described as essential ("clutch") to the overall experience.

Temperature Guide

The Vapolution uses an analog dial for temperature control rather than a digital display, so precise temperature readings were not commonly discussed in community threads. Users generally relied on visual cues (visible vapor production), taste, and the color of their ABV (already been vaped) material to gauge temperature.

General community guidance for analog-dial vaporizers applied:

GoalApproach
Flavor-focused sessionsLower dial settings; light, wispy vapor; pale gold ABV
Balanced sessionsMid-range dial settings; moderate visible vapor; medium brown ABV
Maximum extractionHigher dial settings; thick, dense vapor; dark brown ABV

Because temperature control reliability was noted as a concern with some Vapolution units, users were encouraged to start low and gradually increase the dial setting to find their preferred range, paying close attention to taste changes that might indicate the onset of overheating.

Maintenance & Cleaning

One of the Vapolution's most praised attributes was its ease of maintenance, directly attributable to its all-glass construction.

  • Glass bowl and stem: Soak in isopropyl alcohol (90%+ recommended) to dissolve residue. The all-glass design means there are no screens, gaskets, or porous materials to worry about during cleaning.
  • Heating element housing (glass): The glass surrounding the heating element could also be cleaned with isopropyl alcohol. Community members noted that having both the heating element area and the bowl piece be easily cleanable was a rare advantage.
  • Reclaim: As with all glass-path vaporizers, accumulated resin (honey oil) in the glass could be reclaimed by soaking in isopropyl alcohol and evaporating, or by gently warming the glass.
  • Frequency: Regular cleaning was recommended to maintain the pure flavor that was the Vapolution's hallmark. Allowing heavy buildup would negate the taste advantage of the all-glass path.

Common Issues & Fixes

  • Temperature control inconsistency: This was the most commonly referenced issue. The analog dial controlling temperature was reported by some users to be less reliable or consistent over time compared to competing desktop units. If temperature seems to drift or become unpredictable, contacting Vapolution's customer service was the recommended course of action, as the company was U.S.-based and generally accessible.
  • Glass breakage: As an all-glass vapor path unit, replacement parts were occasionally needed. Users recommended keeping spare bowl pieces on hand.
  • Learning curve with analog dial: Without digital temperature readout, new users sometimes struggled to find consistent settings. The community recommended marking preferred dial positions once found.

Accessories & Modifications

  • All in Wonder Bowl: This was the most frequently recommended accessory, often described as essential to getting the best experience from the Vapolution. It was available as an add-on or bundle at the time of purchase.
  • Water pipe adapter (WPA): The all-glass construction made the Vapolution amenable to use with water filtration, though specific WPA compatibility details were not extensively documented in the reviewed threads.
  • Vapolution Pocket Vape: A separate, torch-powered portable product from the same manufacturer. This unit used external flame heating on a glass chamber, maintaining the all-glass vapor path philosophy in a portable form factor. One community member noted that using a glass vape heated externally by a torch (like the Pocket Vape) eliminated concerns about any heating element material contacting the vapor.

Comparisons

Vapolution vs. Da Buddha Vaporizer (DBV)

This was a directly discussed comparison in the community. Both are corded desktop vaporizers, but they differ significantly in design philosophy:

  • The Da Buddha uses a ceramic heating element and offers a larger bowl, whip-style delivery, and was widely considered to have more reliable temperature control over its production lifespan.
  • The Vapolution offered a 100% all-glass vapor path (the Da Buddha's path includes ceramic and possibly other materials), which was its primary advantage for purity-focused users.
  • The Da Buddha was partially assembled in China (a point some users raised), while the Vapolution was fully U.S.-made.
  • Community consensus suggested the Da Buddha was the more dependable and versatile unit overall, while the Vapolution held the edge in vapor path purity and price.

Vapolution vs. E-Nano

Both were compared as affordable, efficient desktop options suitable for solo use with small bowl sizes:

  • The E-Nano (by EpicVape) is a log-style vaporizer that could be left on 24/7, supported whole-nug loading, and offered very quick extraction in a few draws.
  • The Vapolution was similarly efficient with small loads and was noted by at least one community member as being personally preferred over the E-Nano, though this was acknowledged as a matter of personal taste. Both were described as "awesome."
  • The E-Nano was generally considered easier to use through water pipes and had a larger community following.

Vapolution vs. Arizer Solo

These units occupied very different niches:

  • The Arizer Solo was a portable, battery-powered session vaporizer requiring ground herb and 8–12 minute session times.
  • The Vapolution was a corded desktop unit with quicker extraction potential.
  • For users whose primary requirement was a clean vapor path and who did not need portability, the Vapolution was a compelling alternative at a similar or lower price point.

Vapolution vs. MFLB (Magic-Flight Launch Box)

Both were discussed as efficient, solo-use options at accessible price points:

  • The MFLB offered portability and extreme discretion but had a significant learning curve and was sometimes considered limiting for users who might later want larger hits or to share with others.
  • The Vapolution offered better vapor production and taste via its all-glass path but sacrificed all portability.

For first-time buyers, the community generally recommended the Vapolution over the MFLB when portability was not a requirement, citing superior flavor and easier learning curve.
