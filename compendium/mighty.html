# Mighty+ (Storz & Bickel)

Overview

The Mighty+ (also styled as Mighty+) is a portable dry herb vaporizer manufactured by Storz & Bickel, a German company widely regarded as one of the most established names in the vaporizer industry. The Mighty+ is the updated successor to the original Mighty, which itself was long considered a benchmark portable vaporizer. The Mighty+ was released in late 2021, after years of community speculation and anticipation — as one community member joked, "Since I just bought a Mighty the rumored Mighty+ should be out any day now."

The Mighty+ uses a hybrid heating system combining both conduction and convection elements, powered by an internal rechargeable lithium-ion battery. It features USB-C charging (an upgrade from the original Mighty's barrel-style charger), a faster heat-up time, and a ceramic-coated filling chamber. The device retains the signature form factor of its predecessor — a large, ergonomic handheld unit with an integrated cooling unit (CU), OLED temperature display, and two-button interface.

While the Mighty+ was widely acknowledged in the community as one of the easiest and most reliable portable vaporizers to recommend — particularly to newcomers — it was also the subject of substantive criticism regarding its non-replaceable batteries, plastic construction, cooling unit maintenance, and pricing relative to newer competitors.

Community Reception

The Mighty+ occupied a complicated but central position in the vaporizer community. It was simultaneously one of the most recommended portable vaporizers — particularly for beginners and those seeking a reliable, low-technique session experience — and one of the most frequently critiqued by experienced users who had moved on to more specialized devices.

Strengths

  • Ease of use: The community widely agreed that the Mighty+ was among the most approachable vaporizers available. Its simple two-button interface, clear temperature display, and consistent vapor production required virtually no learning curve or "vape-fu." As one community member noted when recommending it to a friend battling cancer who needed to switch from smoking, the Mighty was suggested as "quite powerful, easy to use" for a first portable.
  • Consistent extraction: The hybrid heating system delivered reliable, even extraction without stirring. Multiple community members praised its ability to fully extract a bowl with minimal user intervention.
  • Vapor potency: Community members noted that "one bowl can usually get several seasoned stoners high," and the device was suggested as a benchmark for testing whether a user's material was effective.
  • Social/sharing use: The session-style operation and forgiving draw technique made it well-suited for sharing with others, including non-vaporizer users.
  • Loading system: The filling tool/magazine system and dosing capsule compatibility were praised for making loading quick and mess-free, which was highlighted as important for social and mobile use.

Criticisms

  • Non-replaceable battery: This was perhaps the single most discussed drawback. Community members expressed frustration that after the 2–3 year warranty period, battery degradation could render the unit unusable, requiring a paid factory repair with associated shipping time and cost. One user described this as "a pretty big deal," noting the unit "can die on you at any point" after warranty expiration.
  • Plastic construction: Several experienced users expressed discomfort with the extensive use of plastic in the vapor path and body. One community member who had owned multiple Mighty units stated they sold them "mostly because I dislike the vapor from them but also for the plastic."
  • Vapor quality relative to competition: While regarded as good, especially for a session portable, more experienced users found the vapor "flimsy" compared to pure convection or halogen-heated alternatives. The vapor was described by some as lacking the flavor depth and density available from newer convection portables.
  • Cooling unit cleaning: The built-in cooling unit, through which vapor passes and cools before reaching the user, was frequently cited as a pain point for maintenance. One community member specifically noted "the pain with the Mighty specifically is that its built-in cooling unit that the vapor winds through can especially be a pain to clean."
  • Size and portability: Multiple users noted that the Mighty+ was not truly pocketable. One owner bluntly stated, "My mighty ain't pocket able," distinguishing it from smaller portables they were seeking.
  • Price: The device was described by some as "overpriced" relative to newer competitors that offered replaceable batteries, better materials, and superior vapor quality.
  • Slow charging: Even with the USB-C upgrade, charging speed was noted as a complaint, with one user listing "slow charging" among their reasons for selling.
  • Restricted airflow: Users seeking open, free-flowing draws found the Mighty+ lacking. One community member who owned a Solo 2, AirVape Legacy Pro, and Mighty described none of them as "really open" enough, seeking convection alternatives with less draw resistance.

Despite these criticisms, the Mighty+ maintained a devoted user base. One owner called it their "#1 grabbed vape" even while acknowledging its shortcomings. The community consensus was that it remained an excellent recommendation for users who valued simplicity, consistency, and reliability over vapor purity, flavor, or cutting-edge performance.

Tips & Techniques

Loading

  • Use the filling tool: The S&B filling tool/magazine allows for quick, clean loading and was one of the features community members praised most for social and on-the-go use.
  • Dosing capsules: Stainless steel dosing capsules keep the chamber cleaner, reduce cleaning frequency, and allow pre-loading multiple sessions. A capsule caddy can hold 8 capsules for extended outings.
  • Glass stem with basket screens: Some community members reported abandoning the stock cooling unit and mouthpiece entirely in favor of glass stems with basket screens, which allowed variable load sizes and dramatically easier loading, unloading, and cleaning.
  • Grind: A medium grind is generally recommended. The hybrid heating benefits from a consistent grind that allows airflow through the chamber without being too fine (which can restrict airflow or pull through screens).

Draw Technique

  • The Mighty+ is a session-style vaporizer that does not require a specific draw technique. Long, slow, steady draws produce the most vapor, though the device is forgiving of varied draw speeds compared to pure convection units.
  • No stirring is required mid-session; the hybrid heating extracts evenly.

Session Management

  • Temperature stepping: Start at a lower temperature for flavor and gradually increase across the session for more thorough extraction and denser clouds (see Temperature Guide below).
  • Pass-through charging: The device can be used while plugged in, effectively converting it to a corded portable/desktop hybrid for extended home sessions.

Temperature Guide

The Mighty+ offers precise temperature control from 40°C to 210°C (104°F to 410°F). The community generally recommended a temperature-stepping approach:

Temperature RangeEffect Profile
170°C–180°C (338°F–356°F)Flavor-focused, lighter vapor, terpenoid-rich hits. Good starting point for a session.
180°C–195°C (356°F–383°F)Balanced flavor and vapor production. The "sweet spot" range for most daily users seeking both taste and effect.
195°C–210°C (383°F–410°F)Dense clouds, maximum extraction, more sedative/body effects. Useful for finishing a bowl or for users with higher tolerance or pain management needs.

For CBD-dominant herb (such as strains with <0.3% THC), one community member who used the device for chronic pain management noted the importance of thorough extraction to capture as many cannabinoids as possible, suggesting sessions that step up to the higher temperature range.

The community noted that the Mighty+'s hybrid heating system was more forgiving at higher temperatures than some pure conduction vapes, producing less harsh vapor at max temp due to the convection component and the integrated cooling unit.

Maintenance & Cleaning

Cooling Unit (CU)

The cooling unit was the most discussed maintenance component. Vapor passes through a winding path inside the CU, where resin and condensed oils accumulate over time. This buildup affects airflow, flavor, and hygiene.

  • Frequency: Regular users reported needing to clean the CU every 1–2 weeks, or more frequently with heavy use.
  • Disassembly: The CU disassembles into several components: the cap/mouthpiece, the top screen, the cooling ribs/fins, the bottom screen, and the seal ring.
  • Cleaning method: Soak all CU components in isopropyl alcohol (90%+ recommended). Use pipe cleaners or cotton swabs for stubborn residue. Rinse thoroughly with warm water and allow to dry completely before reassembly.
  • Reclaim recovery: Some users noted that significant amounts of usable reclaim (condensed cannabinoid oils) accumulate in the CU and can be recovered during cleaning — though one user viewed this as a negative, preferring water filtration over the CU to avoid "losing" active compounds to the cooling unit.

Chamber

  • Debowling: After each session, empty the chamber while warm. The spent material (ABV/AVB — Already Been Vaped) should tap out easily.
  • Screen replacement: The chamber screen should be replaced periodically as it becomes clogged with residue. Brush the screen and chamber with the included cleaning brush after each session.
  • Deep clean: Periodically clean the chamber area with a cotton swab dipped in isopropyl alcohol.

General

  • The community noted that compared to simpler vaporizers (such as those using glass stems that only require debowling), the Mighty+ required noticeably more maintenance effort due to the multi-part cooling unit.

Common Issues & Fixes

Battery Degradation

  • Issue: Non-replaceable internal batteries degrade over time, reducing session count and eventually rendering the unit inoperable.
  • Fix: Within warranty (2–3 years), Storz & Bickel handles replacements. Outside warranty, users face a paid repair requiring shipping the unit to S&B. Some adventurous users have attempted DIY battery replacement, but this voids the warranty and requires soldering skills.
  • Community note: One community member pointed out that warranty claims with S&B (a large corporation) could actually take longer than claims with smaller artisan manufacturers, despite the latter sometimes being located overseas.

Broken Internal Heating Element Connection

  • Issue: At least one community member reported a broken internal connection to the heating element, causing the temperature not to rise properly and the motherboard to overheat.
  • Fix: Community advice was to trim back wire insulation and solder the broken connection. Adhesive/glue was not recommended due to potential off-gassing concerns. This type of repair requires some technical skill and access to the device internals.

Cooling Unit Airflow Restriction

  • Issue: Resin buildup in the cooling unit restricts airflow over time.
  • Fix: Regular cleaning as described above. Some users switched to aftermarket glass stems or WPAs to bypass the CU entirely.

Reduced Vapor Production

  • Issue: Vapor quality or density decreasing over time.
  • Fix: Usually resolved by cleaning the CU and replacing the chamber screen. Check that the CU seal ring is properly seated.

Accessories & Modifications

Water Pipe Adapters (WPA)

The Mighty+ is widely used with water filtration. Several approaches exist:

  • "Muzzmod": A community-developed modification using a 14mm female to 18mm male glass adapter attached to a Mighty/Crafty cooling unit, creating a WPA. One community member described sourcing affordable adapters to produce these at a reasonable price, noting the cost of a cooling unit (~$20) plus the adapter could add up.
  • Aftermarket WPAs: Various third-party WPAs connect the Mighty+ to standard 14mm or 18mm water pieces. These typically replace the mouthpiece portion of the cooling unit.
  • Hydrotubes: Users sought 14mm hydrotubes specifically designed for use with the Mighty+, providing water filtration in a self-contained attachment. American-made glass was preferred by some, though DHgate imports were acknowledged as a budget alternative.

Glass Stems and Aftermarket Mouthpieces

  • Titanium mouthpiece: CCV (Cream City Vapes) produced a titanium mouthpiece that was highlighted as a popular accessory.
  • Glass cooling stems: Some users replaced the stock plastic CU and mouthpiece entirely with glass stems featuring pills or pearls for cooling, dramatically simplifying cleaning and, according to some, improving flavor.
  • Basket screens: Used in conjunction with glass stems for flexible load sizes.

Dosing Capsules and Caddies

  • S&B dosing capsules: Stainless steel capsules that fit inside the chamber, keeping it cleaner and allowing pre-loading.
  • Capsule caddies: Third-party XL caddies holding 8 dosing capsules for extended portable sessions.

Stands

  • Purpose-built stands were available to hold the Mighty+ upright during charging and between sessions.

Creative Modifications

In a particularly memorable community moment, one user created a "drinking hat" (novelty hard hat with dual cup holders) modified to hold two Mighty units simultaneously, drawing widespread amusement. As one commenter noted, "While the money management here is pretty questionable, the end result is amazing."

Comparisons

Mighty+ vs. Crafty+

The Crafty+ is Storz & Bickel's smaller, single-battery portable using the same hybrid heating system and chamber/CU design. The primary difference is battery life — the Crafty+'s smaller battery was frequently cited as insufficient, with one user specifically seeking an upgrade from the Crafty+ to the Mighty+ for this reason. The Mighty+ offers significantly longer battery life and a built-in display (the Crafty+ relies on app/Bluetooth for temperature control), but at the cost of being considerably larger and heavier.

Mighty+ vs. TinyMight / TinyMight 2

This was one of the most discussed comparisons in the community. The TinyMight (and its successor, the TM2) represented a fundamentally different design philosophy: pure on-demand convection, open airflow, replaceable 18650 battery, and wood/metal construction versus the Mighty+'s hybrid session heating, restricted airflow, sealed battery, and plastic body.

  • Vapor quality: Community consensus leaned toward the TinyMight producing superior flavor and denser convection hits. One user stated the TinyMight "was always just overall better" than the Mighty.
  • Airflow: The TinyMight offered "much better airflow than all those portables" including the Mighty, making it the recommendation for users seeking open draws.
  • Ease of use: The Mighty+ was consistently acknowledged as simpler and more forgiving. The TinyMight required more technique, particularly for consistent on-demand extraction.
  • Convection preference: For users who identified as "convection kinda guy," the TM2 was "a much better choice than the Mighty."
  • Build quality: The TinyMight's replaceable battery and non-plastic construction appealed to users concerned about the Mighty+'s sealed battery and plastic vapor path.

Mighty+ vs. Angus

The Angus, a halogen-heated portable, drew direct comparison to the Mighty+ and was positioned by some community members as a potential replacement. One detailed comparison noted:

  • The Angus had "considerably better heat retention" allowing harder rips at high temperatures.
  • Halogen heating "handles flavor at high temps (and low as well) way better" than the Mighty+.
  • The Mighty+'s vapor was described as "flimsy compared to Angus."
  • The Angus used swappable 18650 batteries, eliminating the sealed-battery concern.
  • One user stated that the Angus "shows it for the outdated, overpriced and overhyped device" that the Mighty had become in their view.
  • However, the Mighty+'s simpler two-button-and-display interface was acknowledged as easier than the Angus's LED-based controls.

Mighty+ vs. Arizer Solo 2

The Solo 2 was occasionally mentioned alongside the Mighty+ as a reliable battery-powered session portable. Advantages cited for the Solo 2 included huge battery life, consistent results, near-zero maintenance (glass stems simply need debowling), and cheap replacement stems/WPAs available on secondary markets. However, the Solo 2 shares the Mighty+'s restricted airflow characteristic.

Mighty+ vs. AirVape Legacy Pro

One user directly compared these two for couple/shared use. The Mighty+ was considered more "plug and play" with less fiddling than the AirVape Legacy Pro, which required stirring, had more individual pieces to clean, and featured a glass tube and door latch that could break. However, some community members were wary of "too many moving parts" on the AirVape design.

Mighty+ vs. Boundless CFX

An earlier comparison (predating the Mighty+ but relevant to the Mighty line) noted the CFX's advantages: significantly lower price (~$170 vs. the Mighty's premium pricing), 10-second heat-up time, higher maximum temperature, USB charging, and easy bong compatibility. The user also highlighted concerns about "guaranteed battery failure" with S&B products and the cooling unit capturing reclaim that could otherwise be consumed through water filtration.
