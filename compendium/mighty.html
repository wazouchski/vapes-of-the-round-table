# Mighty+

Overview

The Mighty+ (also stylized as Mighty+ or MIGHTY+) is a portable dry herb vaporizer manufactured by Storz & Bickel, a German company widely regarded as one of the most established names in the vaporizer industry. The Mighty+ is the updated successor to the original Mighty, which itself was long considered a benchmark portable vaporizer. The Mighty+ was released in 2021, introducing improvements such as USB-C charging, a ceramic-coated oven, and faster heat-up times over its predecessor.

The Mighty+ uses a hybrid heating system combining both conduction and convection elements, powered by two internal lithium-ion batteries (non-removable). It features a digital temperature display, precise temperature control via two buttons, and a distinctive ribbed plastic body with a cooling unit (CU) mouthpiece system that serves as its primary vapor path.

Within the vaporizer community, the Mighty+ has occupied a somewhat paradoxical position: widely recommended as the most reliable, consistent, and beginner-friendly battery-powered portable, yet simultaneously critiqued for its plastic construction, non-replaceable batteries, cooling unit maintenance burden, and lack of meaningful innovation relative to its premium price point. One community member summarized this duality well when noting it was their "#1 grabbed vape" despite having complaints about the cooling unit cleaning.

Community Reception

The Mighty+ was widely regarded within the community as the gold standard for session-style portable vaporizers, particularly for users who value consistency, ease of use, and reliability over cutting-edge vapor quality. It was frequently the first recommendation for new users transitioning from combustion, and was considered one of the most "plug and play" portables available.

Strengths

  • Consistency: The Mighty+ was praised for delivering repeatable results session after session with virtually no learning curve. Multiple community members noted it as the device they reached for most often despite owning more exotic or higher-performing units.
  • Ease of Use: Two-button temperature control, a clear digital display, and haptic feedback when reaching set temperature made it highly accessible. The community specifically highlighted its suitability for shared use (e.g., passing between partners or at social gatherings).
  • Vapor Production: Capable of producing dense, satisfying clouds, particularly at higher temperatures. Several members noted its strong performance for users prioritizing potency and thorough extraction.
  • Dosing Capsule System: The S&B dosing capsule ecosystem was appreciated for convenience, keeping the oven clean, and enabling pre-loaded sessions on the go.
  • Haptic Feedback: The vibration alerts were noted as a significant accessibility feature. In a thread discussing vaporizer options for visually impaired users, one community member recommended the Mighty specifically because it vibrates at temperature — though they cautioned that cleaning remained a barrier.

Criticisms

  • Non-Replaceable Batteries: This was the single most frequently cited criticism. Community members expressed frustration that a premium-priced device could effectively become a paperweight after 2–3 years when batteries degraded, requiring a paid return to Storz & Bickel for servicing. This was a dealbreaker for some, particularly those in countries where international shipping for warranty claims was burdensome.
  • Plastic Construction: The all-plastic body was a persistent point of criticism in a market increasingly populated by metal, wood, and glass competitors. Several members noted that for its price point, they expected more premium materials.
  • Cooling Unit Cleaning: The multi-piece cooling unit was universally acknowledged as the most tedious aspect of Mighty+ ownership. The CU's internal labyrinth collects significant reclaim and requires periodic disassembly and soaking. One member described the CU as "a pain to clean" and suggested stocking up on multiple cooling units to rotate between deep cleanings.
  • Size: At roughly the size of a large smartphone, the Mighty+ was consistently described as not pocketable. One experienced member noted flatly: "My mighty ain't pocket able." It was viewed as a portable for use around the house or carried in a bag, rather than a true on-the-go stealth device.
  • Vapor Quality: While vapor production was praised, some experienced users found the vapor character itself "flimsy" compared to pure convection or halogen-heated devices. Members who preferred convection flavor profiles sometimes felt the hybrid heating produced a less nuanced taste, particularly at higher temperatures.
  • Session Timer: The auto-shutoff timer was a significant annoyance for some users. One older community member with reduced lung capacity found it especially problematic, as the timer was too short and resetting it by drawing was unreliable. The community discovered that adjusting the temperature by even 1°C using the +/– buttons would reset the timer — a widely shared workaround.
  • Slow Charging: Despite the upgrade to USB-C, charging time was still cited as slower than ideal by multiple members.
  • Price: The Mighty+ was frequently described as "overpriced" by experienced users who had compared it to artisan and newer-generation devices, though its value was more readily accepted by those prioritizing reliability and simplicity.

Overall Sentiment

The community consensus positioned the Mighty+ as an excellent "first serious vaporizer" and a highly reliable daily driver for session-style use, but not the last word in portable vaporization for experienced enthusiasts. It was the device many members eventually moved beyond — yet also the device they frequently found themselves missing after selling. As one member noted after selling multiple Mighty units: "For some reason, I always end up missing my Mighty/Crafty at some point."

Tips & Techniques

Loading

  • Standard Load: Grind herb to a medium consistency and fill the chamber using the included filling aid (or a dosing capsule magazine). A light tamp is generally recommended — do not pack excessively tight, as the hybrid heating system benefits from some airflow through the material.
  • Dosing Capsules: The S&B dosing capsules (holding approximately 0.1–0.15g) were widely recommended for keeping the oven clean and enabling pre-loading of multiple sessions. Members who prioritized cleanliness often used capsules exclusively. One member with a ceramic-coated Crafty noted that the new coating stayed so clean that capsules became less necessary — this observation applies to the Mighty+ as well.
  • Glass Stem with Basket Screens: An increasingly popular alternative to the stock cooling unit was to use an aftermarket glass stem fitted with basket screens. This allowed variable load sizes, easier loading/unloading, and simpler cleaning. One experienced member noted: "I don't even use the cooling unit anymore or the stock stem, there are so many other options now."

Drawing Technique

  • Slow and Steady: The Mighty+ rewards long, slow, steady draws. This is a session vaporizer — it is not designed for quick, powerful on-demand hits.
  • Anti-Cough Technique: A widely discussed technique for reducing coughing involved taking in additional fresh air at the end of each draw (after removing the mouthpiece or pulling it slightly away) to dilute the dense vapor before it settles in the throat. One community member reported this technique virtually eliminated coughing: "Taking in extra air with no vapor at the end has always made a big difference to me in preventing coughing." Another member cautioned against "cloud chasing" — noting that visible cloud size does not correlate with effectiveness, and that chasing larger clouds was the primary cause of irritation.
  • Timer Reset Trick: To avoid the auto-shutoff timer, press the temperature up or down button by 1°C every hit or two. This resets the timer without meaningfully changing the session temperature. Community members described this as "habit now."

Session Strategy

  • Temperature Stepping: Many users practiced "temp stepping" — starting at a lower temperature for flavor and gradually increasing over the course of a session for maximum extraction. This is detailed further in the Temperature Guide section below.
  • Pass-Through Charging: The Mighty+ can be used while charging, effectively functioning as a corded session vape when battery is depleted.

Temperature Guide

The Mighty+ offers precise 1°C increments across its full range of 40°C–210°C (104°F–410°F). Community members generally agreed on a temp-stepping approach:

Temperature RangeEffectNotes
170–180°C (338–356°F)Flavor-focused, light effectsBest for tasting terpene profiles; wispy vapor; ideal first few draws
180–190°C (356–374°F)Balanced flavor and effectGood starting point for most sessions; moderate vapor density
190–200°C (374–392°F)Strong effects, good vapor productionCommunity "sweet spot" range for most users; reliable extraction
200–210°C (392–410°F)Maximum extraction, dense cloudsEnd-of-session finishing range; some flavor degradation; best for thorough extraction of remaining cannabinoids

CBD-Specific Use

One community member who used the Mighty+ exclusively with CBD flower (below 0.3% THC) for chronic pain management noted that thorough extraction was especially important, as "when it hits me hard I need every cannabinoid I can get." For CBD-focused use, higher temperatures (195–210°C / 383–410°F) were generally recommended to ensure extraction of the full spectrum of cannabinoids and terpenes, including those with higher boiling points.

Community Notes

  • The hybrid heating system means the oven continues to heat material between draws (conduction element), so longer pauses between hits at higher temperatures can lead to some waste. Users who preferred to sip slowly sometimes favored lower temperatures for this reason.
  • Some members noted that the Mighty+'s vapor at high temperatures was less flavorful than pure convection devices at equivalent temperatures, though cloud production was strong.

Maintenance & Cleaning

Cleaning was the most discussed maintenance topic for the Mighty+, with the cooling unit (CU) being the primary focus of attention.

Cooling Unit Cleaning

  • Frequency: The community recommended disassembling and wiping the CU every 4–5 loads while still warm, using dry cotton swabs (Q-tips). This preventive approach was credited by multiple members as dramatically reducing the need for deep cleaning.
  • Deep Cleaning: For thorough cleaning, fully disassemble the CU and soak all parts in isopropyl alcohol (ISO) — 90% or higher concentration preferred. Allow parts to soak for 15–30 minutes, then rinse with warm water and allow to dry completely before reassembly.
  • ISO Alternatives: During periods of ISO scarcity (notably discussed during the COVID-19 pandemic when ISO was difficult to obtain), the community recommended alternatives including:

- Everclear (high-proof grain alcohol) — considered the best alternative

- Vodka — effective for lighter cleaning

- Rum — functional but less ideal

- Members noted that potable alcohols had the advantage of being safe if trace residue remained, unlike some industrial solvents.

  • Multi-CU Strategy: Several members recommended purchasing 3–4 cooling units and rotating through them, allowing batch cleaning at intervals. One member noted: "With three or four cooling units you should be able to have a full month without cleaning."
  • Reclaim Collection: The cooling unit accumulates significant amounts of reclaim (condensed cannabinoid-rich residue). Some users collected this for reuse, though the community was divided on whether CU reclaim was worth the effort.

Oven Maintenance

  • The ceramic-coated oven on the Mighty+ stays significantly cleaner than the original Mighty's oven. A quick brush-out after each session (while still warm) was generally sufficient.
  • Dosing capsules dramatically reduce oven fouling.

Screen Maintenance

  • Replace or clean the oven screen regularly. Soaking in ISO and gentle brushing restores airflow.

Common Issues & Fixes

Short Session Timer / Auto-Shutoff

Issue: The Mighty+ shuts off automatically after a period of inactivity, which many users found too aggressive — particularly older users or those with reduced lung capacity who take longer between draws.

Fix: Press the temperature +/– button by 1°C to reset the timer. This became a universally recommended habit within the community.

Battery Degradation

Issue: The non-replaceable internal batteries degrade over time (typically noticeable after 1.5–3 years of regular use), reducing session count per charge and eventually becoming unable to hold useful charge.

Fix: Storz & Bickel offers a battery replacement service for a fee, requiring shipping the unit back. There is no user-serviceable solution. The community widely regarded this as the Mighty+'s most significant design flaw and a deliberate planned obsolescence choice.

Cooling Unit Airflow Restriction

Issue: The CU becomes increasingly restrictive as resin accumulates.

Fix: Regular cleaning as described above. Some users bypassed the CU entirely in favor of aftermarket glass stems or WPAs.

Broken Heating Element Connection

Issue: In at least one documented case, an internal wire connecting the heating element broke, causing the unit to fail to reach temperature.

Fix: Community members advised that soldering was the appropriate repair method — trimming back wire insulation and re-soldering the connection. Adhesive (glue) was not recommended due to concerns about fumes and inadequate electrical contact. This repair requires opening the sealed unit, voiding the warranty.

Counterfeit Units

Issue: Counterfeit Mighty units were documented in circulation, particularly from unauthorized third-party sellers. Telltale signs included:

  • Red replacement O-rings (genuine units use black)
  • Plastic grinders with marijuana leaf branding included as accessories
  • No 3D "Mighty" sticker on top of unit
  • Screen arriving with no protective film
  • Makeup-style brush instead of proper cleaning brush
  • Strong burning plastic smell when heated
  • Cracked cooling fins out of the box
  • Mouthpiece that constantly detaches

Fix: Purchase exclusively from authorized retailers or directly from Storz & Bickel. One community member who disassembled a counterfeit found it actually contained two batteries and a ceramic heating rod, but used low-temperature plastic near the heating element that off-gassed dangerously.

Coughing / Harsh Vapor

Issue: Dense vapor at higher temperatures can cause coughing, especially for users with sensitive airways.

Fix: Take in additional fresh air at the end of each draw to dilute vapor in the throat and lungs. Reduce temperature. Use water filtration. Avoid "cloud chasing." One member advised: "As long as you can feel the vapor, it doesn't matter what you see."

Accessories & Modifications

Dosing Capsules

The S&B dosing capsule system was one of the Mighty+'s most appreciated features. Capsules hold approximately 0.1–0.15g and slot directly into the oven. The dosing capsule magazine allows pre-loading of 8 capsules for convenient on-the-go use. Aftermarket capsule caddies (such as the CCV XL Capsule Caddy) expanded carrying capacity.

Glass Stems & Alternative Mouthpieces

A significant portion of experienced Mighty+ users eventually moved away from the stock cooling unit in favor of aftermarket options:

  • Glass cooling stems (with pills or pearls for additional cooling)
  • Titanium mouthpieces (e.g., CCV Titanium Mouthpiece) — more durable and easier to clean than plastic CU
  • Basket screens fitted into glass stems allowed variable load sizes

Water Pipe Adapters (WPA)

The Mighty+ was commonly used with water pieces via 14mm or 18mm adapters. Community-recommended water tools included:

  • The Honey water tool — a popular choice described as "amazing" for the Mighty, small enough to grip with one hand while stabilizing the adapter connection. Members cautioned about blowback through the air intake if the unit was not removed between draws.
  • J-hooks from DHGate — a budget-friendly option for dry cooling or connection to water pieces, recommended as a complement to water tools
  • CCV Sidecar Bubbler — a purpose-built bubbler designed for the Mighty+
  • Members who preferred water filtration over the CU noted that the cooling unit's reclaim capture reduced efficiency; water filtration preserved more of the active compounds in the vapor path.

Novelty Modifications

In a memorable community moment, one user created a "drinking hat" (beer helmet style) fitted with two Mighty units for hands-free dual vaporization — an invention the community regarded with a mixture of admiration and amusement. As one member commented: "This guy already living in 2069."

Comparisons

Mighty+ vs. Crafty+

The Crafty+ is Storz & Bickel's smaller, single-battery sibling to the Mighty+. Both share the same heating system and dosing capsule ecosystem. The primary differentiator is battery life — the Mighty+'s dual batteries provide significantly longer sessions. Users who found the Crafty+'s battery life "insufficient" commonly upgraded to the Mighty+, though the Crafty+'s smaller size made it more pocketable. The Crafty+ with ceramic coating was noted to stay clean enough that dosing capsules became less necessary.

Community members who preferred convection generally favored the TinyMight, while those who valued consistency and simplicity stuck with the Mighty+. One member noted the TinyMight was "always just overall better" than the Mighty/Crafty, yet admitted to repeatedly missing the S&B devices after selling them — suggesting the Mighty+ filled a particular niche of reliable, effortless session vaporization that convection devices didn't fully replicate.

For users specifically seeking open airflow and flavor, the TinyMight was consistently recommended over the Mighty+. One member with a Solo 2, AirVape Legacy Pro, and Mighty described wanting more open airflow than any of them provided, and was directed to the TinyMight as having "much better airflow than all those portables."

Mighty+ vs. Angus (Halogen)

The Angus drew direct comparison as a potential Mighty replacement, with enthusiasts noting:

  • Heat Retention: The Angus was described as having "considerably better heat retention" than the Mighty+
  • Vapor Quality: Multiple members described Angus vapor as superior, with one stating "Mighty vapor is flimsy compared to Angus"
  • High-Temp Performance: The Angus's halogen heating was said to handle flavor at high temperatures "way better" than the Mighty+
  • Batteries: The Angus uses swappable 18650 batteries, addressing the Mighty+'s most significant criticism
  • Concerns: Some community members expressed hesitation about the Angus's maturity as a product, citing reports of hot batteries and mixed early reviews
  • One early adopter stated that the Angus "shows [the Mighty] for the outdated, overpriced and overhyped device" it was, though this was a minority opinion and the Angus had far less community track record

Mighty+ vs. Arizer Solo 2

The Solo 2 was frequently mentioned alongside the Mighty+ as a reliable battery-powered session vape. The Solo 2 was praised for its larger battery, near-zero maintenance (glass stems only require occasional cleaning), consistent results, and affordable replacement stems/WPAs. However, the Mighty+ was generally considered harder-hitting with denser vapor. The Solo 2's glass stems were noted as a fragility concern by some users.

Mighty+ vs. AirVape Legacy Pro

This comparison centered on maintenance burden and durability. The Mighty+ was considered more "plug and play" than the AirVape Legacy Pro, which required more stirring, had a fragile glass tube, and a door latch mechanism that some users found concerning. One member worried about the ALP's "moving parts" and preferred the Mighty+'s relative simplicity despite its CU cleaning requirements. However, warranty claim experiences were noted as potentially slower with Storz & Bickel than with smaller manufacturers, despite S&B being a larger corporation.

Mighty+ vs. DynaVap

These devices serve fundamentally different use cases. The DynaVap is an on-demand, butane-heated (or induction-heated) device with a very small bowl (~0.05–0.1g), while the Mighty+ is a session vape with a larger chamber. Community discussions revealed that despite widespread claims that "DynaVap destroys my Mighty," some session-oriented users found the DynaVap's small bowl and manual heating process unsatisfying. Bowl size differences (0.1g DynaVap vs. 0.1–0.15g in Mighty+ dosing capsule, or 0.2–0.25g direct load) made direct potency comparisons misleading. The devices were best understood as complementary rather than competitive.
