# Mighty+ (Storz & Bickel)

Overview

The Mighty+ (also written as Mighty+) is a portable dry herb vaporizer manufactured by Storz & Bickel, a German company widely regarded as one of the most established names in the vaporizer industry (also known for the Volcano desktop vaporizer). The Mighty+ is the updated successor to the original Mighty, which had been a mainstay recommendation within the vaporizer community for years. The Mighty+ was released in late 2021, following extended community speculation and anticipation — as one community member humorously noted, "Since I just bought a Mighty the rumored Mighty+ should be out any day now."

The Mighty+ uses a hybrid heating system combining conduction and convection elements, powered by a built-in rechargeable battery (non-replaceable). It features a digital temperature display, USB-C charging (an upgrade over the original Mighty's charging port), and a distinctive integrated cooling unit that channels vapor through a winding path before reaching the user's lips. The device occupies a unique position in the portable vaporizer market: widely recommended as a reliable, easy-to-use session vaporizer, yet frequently discussed in terms of its trade-offs relative to newer competitors.

Community Reception

The Mighty+ occupied a complex position in community discourse. It was widely regarded as one of the most reliable, user-friendly, and consistent portable session vaporizers available, frequently recommended to newcomers and those transitioning away from smoking. Community members often recommended it as a first portable vaporizer — one member advised, for a cancer patient needing to quit smoking, "If portable, I'd recommend the Mighty as a first... quite powerful, easy to use." Another noted that "one bowl can usually get several seasoned stoners high," speaking to its extraction efficiency.

However, the community was far from uncritical. Several recurring criticisms emerged across years of discussion:

Strengths

  • Consistency and reliability: The device was praised for producing consistent results with minimal user technique required — essentially "plug and play"
  • Ease of use: The simple two-button interface and digital display made it accessible to beginners and shareable in social settings
  • Vapor quality for a session portable: Regarded as producing solid vapor, particularly at its price point among session-style devices
  • Dosing capsule system: Well-liked for convenience, cleanliness, and pre-loading capability
  • Extraction efficiency: Widely regarded as effective at extracting cannabinoids, making it a recommendation for medical users including those using CBD-dominant strains for chronic pain management

Weaknesses

  • Non-replaceable battery: This was the single most frequently cited concern. Community members were troubled that after the 2–3 year warranty period, the internal battery could degrade or fail, requiring the unit to be shipped to Storz & Bickel for a paid repair. One member described this as a "pretty big deal," noting that "the unit after 2 (3 with registration) years can die on you at any point due to non-replaceable batteries and it costing a fee and shipping it out."
  • Insufficient battery life for heavy users: Users transitioning from the Crafty+ to the Mighty+ specifically cited battery life as the motivating factor, and even the Mighty+'s improved capacity was considered insufficient by some heavy users, particularly those using CBD strains medicinally throughout the day.
  • Plastic construction: The plastic body was a point of contention, especially as competitors moved toward metal, wood, and glass construction. Community members who valued build material purity noted this as a drawback.
  • Cooling unit cleaning: The integrated cooling unit, while effective at cooling vapor, was widely considered tedious to clean. One member specifically noted that "the pain with the Mighty specifically is that its built-in cooling unit that the vapor winds through can especially be a pain to clean."
  • Slow charging: Charging speed was cited as a frustration, particularly by users who sold their Mighty units in favor of devices with swappable 18650 batteries.
  • Not truly pocketable: Despite being classified as a portable, one experienced member with a large collection noted, "My Mighty ain't pocketable," distinguishing it from truly pocket-sized portables.
  • Vapor character: Some experienced users found the vapor quality "flimsy" compared to pure convection or higher-end artisan devices, though this was a minority opinion among the broader community.
  • Perceived as overpriced: As the market matured and competitors emerged, some community members began to view the Mighty+ as "outdated, overpriced and overhyped," particularly in comparison to newer artisan and convection devices.

Despite these criticisms, the Mighty+ remained one of the most frequently recommended portables in the community. One member who had moved on to other devices captured a sentiment shared by many: "For some reason, I always end up missing my Mighty/Crafty at some point," suggesting the device had an intuitive, accessible quality that kept users coming back even after exploring ostensibly superior alternatives. Another described it as their "#1 grabbed vape" despite acknowledging its shortcomings.

Tips & Techniques

The community developed several practices for getting the most out of the Mighty+:

Loading

  • Dosing capsules were strongly recommended for cleanliness, convenience, and maintaining a tidy oven. Pre-loading multiple capsules allowed for quick session changes, especially on the go.
  • A fine-to-medium grind was generally preferred for the hybrid heating system, ensuring even heat distribution across the chamber.
  • The included loading tool/filling aid was praised as one of the Mighty+'s practical design touches, making it easy to load the oven cleanly, even for those unfamiliar with vaporizers.
  • For smaller loads, dosing capsules helped maintain consistent performance without the need to fully pack the chamber.

Draw Technique

  • The Mighty+ is a session vaporizer, meaning it is designed for slow, steady draws over a period of several minutes — not quick on-demand hits.
  • Community members noted that the airflow, while adequate, was more restricted than pure convection on-demand devices. Long, slow, steady draws were generally recommended.
  • Users seeking more open airflow were often directed to convection alternatives like the TinyMight, as the Mighty+'s draw resistance was considered moderate.

Glass Stem Usage

  • An increasingly popular technique was to bypass the stock cooling unit and mouthpiece entirely in favor of aftermarket glass stems with basket screens. One experienced community member noted: "I don't even use the cooling unit anymore or the stock stem, there are so many other options now." Glass stems were said to provide cleaner flavor and easier maintenance.
  • WPA (Water Pipe Adapter) usage was widely recommended for home use, with the community noting that the Mighty+ performed best "with WPA and other glass pieces like hooks, even without water."

Temperature Stepping

  • A common technique was temperature stepping: starting a session at a lower temperature for flavor, then gradually increasing the temperature in 5–10°C increments to maximize extraction before finishing the bowl. This technique is detailed further in the Temperature Guide section below.

Temperature Guide

The Mighty+ offers precise digital temperature control from 40°C to 210°C (104°F to 410°F). The community generally organized usage into the following temperature ranges:

Temperature RangeEffect Profile
170–180°C (338–356°F)Flavor-forward, lighter effects. Good starting point for temperature stepping. Produces wispy, terpy vapor with emphasis on lower-boiling-point terpenes and cannabinoids.
180–195°C (356–383°F)Balanced range. Good vapor production with retained flavor. Many users found their "sweet spot" in this range for daily sessions.
195–210°C (383–410°F)Maximum extraction and cloud production. Heavier body effects. Flavor diminishes but efficiency increases. Medical users seeking maximum cannabinoid extraction, particularly for pain management, often operated in this higher range.

Community members using CBD-dominant strains for chronic pain management noted the importance of extracting at higher temperatures to ensure full cannabinoid and terpene extraction, as CBD's boiling point is slightly higher than THC's.

Temperature stepping was the most commonly recommended approach: begin at approximately 175°C (347°F), take several draws, then increase by 5–10°C every few draws until reaching 200–210°C (392–410°F) to fully extract the material.

Maintenance & Cleaning

Cleaning the Mighty+ was one of the most discussed aspects of ownership. The community widely agreed that while no vaporizer is maintenance-free ("Cannabis leaves residue, it's unavoidable"), the Mighty+'s integrated cooling unit made cleaning more involved than many competitors.

Cooling Unit (CU)

  • The CU was the most maintenance-intensive component. It required periodic disassembly for thorough cleaning.
  • Isopropyl alcohol (ISO) soaking was the standard cleaning method. Disassemble the CU fully, soak all components in ISO, then rinse thoroughly with warm water and allow to dry completely before reassembly.
  • Some users collected the resin ("reclaim") that accumulated in the CU, as it contained active cannabinoids. This was sometimes repurposed for edibles or other consumption methods.
  • The frequency of CU cleaning depended on usage volume but was generally recommended every 1–2 weeks for regular users.

Oven

  • After each session, the bowl could be simply "debowled" — emptied of spent material (AVB/ABV). A quick brush-out with the included brush kept the oven clean between deeper cleanings.
  • Periodic ISO wipe-downs of the oven with cotton swabs helped maintain performance and airflow.

Dosing Capsules

  • Using dosing capsules significantly reduced oven maintenance, as the capsule contained most of the residue. The capsules themselves could be batch-cleaned in ISO.

Screens

  • Replacement screens for the CU were an essential maintenance item. Clogged or gunked screens reduced airflow and vapor quality.

Community Perspective

  • One member who loved the Mighty+ as their "#1 grabbed vape" nonetheless cited CU cleaning as a primary complaint. The cleaning burden was a motivating factor for some users considering alternatives like the Tafée Bowle or TinyMight.
  • The glass stem workaround (bypassing the CU entirely) was partly motivated by the desire to simplify cleaning.

Common Issues & Fixes

Battery Degradation

  • Issue: The non-replaceable internal battery degrades over time, eventually reducing session count and requiring replacement through Storz & Bickel's service center.
  • Mitigation: Register the device for the extended 3-year warranty. Avoid leaving the device on the charger indefinitely. Use pass-through charging sparingly if available.
  • Repair: One community thread documented a user whose internal heating element connection broke. The recommended fix was to carefully trim back wire insulation and solder the broken connection, rather than using adhesive (which could produce harmful fumes when heated). This type of repair requires comfort with electronics and should be undertaken with caution.

Cooling Unit Clogging

  • Issue: The CU's winding vapor path accumulates resin, reducing airflow and flavor quality over time.
  • Fix: Regular cleaning as described above. Some users kept a spare CU in rotation to ensure one was always clean and ready.

Temperature Not Rising

  • Issue: In one documented case, the heating element lost physical contact with the oven, preventing the temperature from rising properly.
  • Diagnosis: This may present as the motherboard overheating while the oven stays cool.
  • Fix: Requires opening the unit and re-establishing the connection, typically through soldering. Not recommended for inexperienced users.

Slow Charging

  • Issue: Charging time was considered slow relative to competitors.
  • Mitigation: The USB-C charging on the Mighty+ was an improvement over the original Mighty, but heavy users sometimes kept the device plugged in between sessions to ensure readiness.

Accessories & Modifications

The Mighty+ inspired a robust ecosystem of aftermarket accessories and community-developed modifications:

Water Pipe Adapters (WPA)

  • WPA usage was one of the most recommended modifications. Both 14mm and 18mm adapters were available, allowing the Mighty+ to be used with water pipes, bubblers, and other glass pieces.
  • Water filtration was preferred by some users over the stock cooling unit, with one community member noting they preferred "water filtration" and found the CU's reclaim collection to be a downside.

"Muzzmods" (Glass Mouthpiece Modifications)

  • A community-developed modification involved combining a Mighty/Crafty cooling unit with a 14mm female to 18mm male glass adapter to create a custom glass mouthpiece ("muzzmod"). These were discussed as providing improved flavor and a glass vapor path while retaining the CU's cooling properties.
  • Sourcing affordable 14F/18M glass adapters was a topic of discussion, with Oregon glassblowers and import options both mentioned.

Aftermarket Glass Cooling Stems

  • Third-party glass stems with basket screens became increasingly popular, allowing users to bypass the stock CU and mouthpiece entirely. These provided cleaner flavor and dramatically simplified cleaning.
  • Glass cooling stems with "pills or pearls" (small glass beads or shapes inside the stem that increase surface area for cooling) were specifically mentioned as excellent aftermarket options.

CCV (Cream City Vapes) Accessories

  • The CCV Titanium Mouthpiece was a popular upgrade, offering durability and a different thermal profile compared to the stock plastic mouthpiece.
  • The CCV Sidecar Bubbler provided integrated water filtration in a compact form factor.
  • The CCV XL Capsule Caddy with 8 dosing capsules was a popular accessory for users who pre-loaded sessions.

Hydrotubes

  • 14mm hydrotubes (small, self-contained water filtration attachments that sit atop the vaporizer) were sought after by users wanting water filtration without a separate water pipe. Both American-made glass and more affordable DHgate imports were discussed.

Stands

  • Aftermarket and included stands were used to keep the Mighty+ upright during charging and between sessions, preventing it from tipping over.

The "Drinking Hat" Modification

  • In a memorable community moment, one user created a dual-Mighty drinking hat rig — mounting two Mighty units on a novelty drinking hat with tubes running to the mouth. While acknowledged as impractical from a financial standpoint, the community celebrated it as a creative and humorous expression of enthusiasm. One commenter noted, "While the money management here is pretty questionable, the end result is amazing."

Comparisons

The Mighty+ was one of the most commonly compared portable vaporizers in the community, serving as a benchmark against which other devices were measured.

Mighty+ vs. TinyMight / TinyMight 2

This was perhaps the most frequently discussed comparison in later community threads:

  • Heating method: The TinyMight is a pure convection, on-demand capable device, while the Mighty+ is a hybrid session vaporizer. Users who preferred convection heating consistently preferred the TinyMight's flavor profile and versatility.
  • Airflow: The TinyMight offered significantly more open airflow, described as "much better airflow than all those portables" including the Mighty+. Users who wanted shorter, faster draws were directed toward the TinyMight.
  • Vapor quality: Multiple community members who owned both described the TinyMight as "overall better" in terms of vapor quality. One member who had owned "2 Mighties and a Crafty" sold them all, noting they disliked the vapor and that "the Tinymight was always just overall better."
  • Battery: The TinyMight uses a swappable 18650 battery, giving it a practical advantage for extended use.
  • Build quality: The TinyMight's wood and metal construction was preferred by some over the Mighty+'s plastic body.
  • Ease of use: The Mighty+ was acknowledged as simpler and more intuitive, especially for sharing or for users who preferred a "set it and forget it" experience.
  • Warranty/Service: One community member argued that warranty claims for the Mighty+ through Storz & Bickel (a large corporation) could actually take longer than claims with smaller manufacturers, despite the TinyMight being shipped internationally.

Mighty+ vs. Crafty+

  • The Crafty+ uses the same heating system in a smaller body with a smaller battery. Users upgrading from Crafty+ to Mighty+ were primarily motivated by insufficient battery life on the Crafty+. The Mighty+'s larger battery, physical temperature controls, and on-device display (versus app-dependent on the Crafty+) were cited as advantages.

Mighty+ vs. Angus

  • A dedicated comparison thread explored the Angus as a Mighty+ alternative. The Angus was described as having "considerably better heat retention" and superior flavor handling at high temperatures due to its halogen heating element. One experienced member stated that after their Mighty broke, they "never considered replacing it" because the Angus was preferred in "every aspect."
  • The Angus featured swappable 18650 batteries, which was cited as a significant practical advantage.
  • The Mighty+'s simpler controls (two buttons and a display) were acknowledged as more intuitive than the Angus's LED-based interface, though the Angus controls were described as "also very easy to use/read."

Mighty+ vs. Arizer Solo 2

  • The Solo 2 was recommended as an alternative for users prioritizing battery life and low maintenance. One member described the Solo 2's advantages as: "Huge battery, consistent results, big clouds, almost zero maintenance (cleaning, just debowl the stems), cheap replacement stems/WPA on eBay."
  • Both were considered session-style portables, but the Solo 2's glass stem system was regarded as significantly easier to maintain than the Mighty+'s cooling unit.

Mighty+ vs. AirVape Legacy Pro (AVLP)

  • This comparison centered on maintenance complexity and reliability. The Mighty+ was considered more "plug and play" despite its CU cleaning requirements. The AVLP's glass tube, door latch, and need for stirring were cited as potential points of failure and added complexity.
  • One community member expressed concern about "too many moving parts" on the AirVape-style design.

Mighty+ vs. Boundless CFX

  • An older comparison that surfaced in early community discussions. The CFX was positioned as a significantly more affordable alternative (~$170 vs. the Mighty+'s higher price point) with a faster heat-up time (~10 seconds). The CFX's higher maximum temperature and USB charging were noted as advantages, while the Mighty+ was generally regarded as producing superior vapor quality.
  • Community concerns about "guaranteed battery failure" on Storz & Bickel devices were already being voiced at this time.

Mighty+ vs. Desktop Vaporizers

  • Users with both the Mighty+ and desktop vaporizers (e-Nano, Volcano, FlowerPot, Herborizer, OAB) consistently noted that desktop units provided superior extraction efficiency and vapor quality. One CBD user observed that when using an e-Nano at home, they could "use less weed overall for the same result" compared to the Mighty+/Crafty+.
  • The Mighty+ was valued as a complement to desktop setups rather than a replacement, filling the role of portable/around-the-house convenience.

General Positioning

The community widely agreed that the Mighty+ excelled as a reliable, approachable session portable — arguably the safest recommendation for someone wanting a single, easy-to-use battery-powered vaporizer. However, experienced users who valued convection heating, open airflow, replaceable batteries, non-plastic vapor paths, or on-demand capability often gravitated toward alternatives. The device's enduring popularity was attributed as much to its simplicity and consistency as to its absolute performance, making it a frequent gateway device that users either stayed loyal to or eventually supplemented with more specialized units.
