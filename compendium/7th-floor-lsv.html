# 7th Floor LSV

Overview

The 7th Floor LSV (Life Saber Vaporizer) is a desktop dry herb vaporizer manufactured by 7th Floor, a Colorado-based company also known for producing the Silver Surfer Vaporizer (SSV) and the Da Buddha Vaporizer (DBV). The LSV was introduced around 2011 and represents a unique entry in 7th Floor's lineup as a handheld, wand-style desktop unit. Unlike the company's other products, which use a traditional box-and-wand configuration, the LSV integrates the heating element into a single cylindrical tube that the user holds vertically or connects directly to a water pipe.

The LSV uses a convection-dominant heating method, drawing hot air through the herb material as the user inhales. It is a plug-in desktop unit powered by a standard wall outlet and features 7th Floor's signature ceramic heating element and glass-on-glass (GonG) airpath. The device was available in multiple color options, including silver and blue variants.

Community Reception

The LSV was regarded within the vaporizer community as a solid performer in 7th Floor's desktop lineup, occupying a distinctive niche as a handheld desktop unit that could be used both as a direct-draw vaporizer and as a complement to water pipes. Community members appreciated the all-glass airpath and the device's versatility.

The used market activity observed in the community suggests the LSV held reasonable resale value. One community member listed a silver LSV with accessories at $140 shipped (later opting to keep the unit rather than sell it), while another member noted that the accessories alone in that bundle were worth approximately seventy dollars — indicating the community saw genuine value in the device and its ecosystem. A blue LSV in near-new condition was listed at $125 including shipping, suggesting a healthy secondary market.

One notable observation is that some users who considered selling their LSV ultimately decided to keep it, which may speak to the device's ongoing utility and appeal even among users who owned multiple vaporizers. One member who initially listed their LSV for sale eventually explored trades for a "skinny/twig style log vape" with a variable voltage power supply (VVPS) and GonG adapter, suggesting interest in comparing the LSV's performance against smaller log-style desktop vaporizers.

Pros (as gathered from community discussion):

  • Versatile form factor — usable handheld or with water pipes
  • All-glass airpath
  • Good accessory ecosystem
  • Reasonable resale value on the used market
  • Available in multiple colors

Cons (as gathered from community discussion):

  • Being a plug-in desktop unit, it lacks portability
  • Analog temperature control without precise digital readout

Tips & Techniques

Based on available community discussion, the LSV is designed to be held vertically with the herb chamber facing downward, allowing gravity to keep material in place against the screen. The convection heating means that draw speed directly influences extraction temperature — slower draws produce hotter vapor, while faster draws cool the airpath.

The device's GonG compatibility makes it straightforward to connect to water pipes for filtered hits. Community members commonly used the LSV in this configuration.

Maintenance & Cleaning

The LSV's all-glass airpath is one of its maintenance advantages. Standard cleaning practices for 7th Floor glass components apply:

  • Glass wand/mouthpiece: Soak in isopropyl alcohol (90%+ recommended) to dissolve residue. Rinse thoroughly with warm water before use.
  • Heater cover screens: Replacement screens were included with the unit. Screens can be cleaned with isopropyl alcohol or replaced as needed when airflow becomes restricted.
  • Ceramic heater: The heating element itself should be kept free of debris. A brief heat cycle with no material loaded can help burn off minor residue.
  • ABV (Already Been Vaped) material: Should be emptied after each session to prevent buildup and maintain airflow.

Common Issues & Fixes

Ordering and Shipping Issues

Community members reported several issues when ordering directly from 7th Floor's website:

  • "Shipping method unavailable" message: Multiple users encountered a notice on the 7th Floor website stating that USPS shipping methods were "currently unavailable." The company was using an alternative carrier (described by one user as "US Parcel Service or something"), which did not provide tracking numbers. Community members advised contacting the company directly if this message appeared during checkout.
  • No tracking number provided: Several community members reported never receiving tracking confirmation after placing orders. One member noted that their LSV arrived without any prior shipping notification. The advice from the community was to be patient, as the packages were still being delivered despite the lack of tracking information.
  • Order status stuck on "Processing": At least one community member reported that their order status remained on "Processing" indefinitely, even after the item had shipped. This was attributed to the website's order management system not updating properly. The recommended solution was to email 7th Floor customer service directly (noting that responses were typically limited to business days, Monday through Friday).
  • Shipping cost discrepancies: One user observed that shipping costs for small accessories (such as a wand and whip) were the same as shipping an entire vaporizer unit, which seemed disproportionate for items that could fit in a standard envelope.

General Advice

The community recommended contacting 7th Floor directly by phone or email for any order-related issues, noting that the company was responsive during business hours but not available on weekends.

Accessories & Modifications

The LSV's GonG compatibility made it a natural companion for water pipe use. Commonly discussed accessories and add-ons include:

  • Glass transfer wand/mouthpiece: The standard included accessory for direct-draw use, featuring an integrated screen.
  • Replacement screens: For the heater cover; spares were included with new units.
  • Water pipe adapters (WPA): The LSV's design allowed direct connection to water pipes via GonG fittings. Male-to-male (M-M) adapters (such as those from OGB) were used by community members to facilitate various water pipe connections.
  • Whip/hose: Available as a replacement accessory from 7th Floor for users who preferred whip-style draws.

One community member's listing included an "OGB M-M adapter" as a separate accessory valued independently from the base unit, indicating that aftermarket glass adapters were a common enhancement.

Comparisons

Direct comparison data from the available threads is limited, though some context can be inferred:

  • LSV vs. Log Vaporizers (e.g., "skinny/twig style" units): At least one community member considered trading their LSV for a compact log-style vaporizer with a variable voltage power supply (VVPS) and GonG adapter. This suggests that users saw some functional overlap between the LSV and log vaporizers — both being desktop convection units with glass airpaths — while the log vaporizers offered a smaller footprint and potentially more precise temperature control via VVPS.
  • LSV vs. Other 7th Floor Products: The LSV occupies a distinct position in the 7th Floor lineup. While the Silver Surfer and Da Buddha use a traditional base-station-and-wand configuration, the LSV consolidates the heating element into a single handheld tube, offering a different ergonomic experience and more direct water pipe integration.

More extensive comparison data may exist in other community threads not captured in this article's source material.
