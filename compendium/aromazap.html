# Aromazap

Overview

The Aromazap is a log-style desktop dry herb vaporizer produced by a small artisan manufacturer. It belongs to the family of log vaporizers descended from the original ETerra design, which spawned several variations including the Purple Days and Woodeez. The Aromazap is a convection-dominant vaporizer housed in a handcrafted wooden log enclosure, powered by an external DC power supply (typically 12V/1A). It was known within the vaporizer community as one of the earliest and most enduring artisan log vapes, earning the affectionate nickname "the Gramp of vapes" from at least one community member.

The device was designed and sold by a maker known as Rick, who was recognized in the community for standing behind his product even after warranty periods expired. The Aromazap line eventually expanded to include variants such as the Myrtlezap (featuring myrtle wood construction) and the CeramicRockZap, though the core operating principle remained consistent across models.

Heating method: Convection (ceramic resistor heating element)

Power source: Desktop (external 12V DC power supply)

Manufacturer: Aromazap (artisan/small-batch)

Community Reception

The Aromazap held a respected but niche position within the vaporizer community. It was regarded as a simple, reliable, and stealthy device that prioritized durability and minimalist design over raw power or cloud production.

Pros identified by the community:

  • Extreme simplicity — no buttons, no digital controls, virtually nothing to break
  • Very stealthy and compact for a desktop unit
  • Reliable long-term performance; units remained functional for many years
  • Excellent efficiency with small herb loads
  • Multiple wood options available, including myrtle wood (Myrtlezap variant)
  • Could be purchased as a standalone wood unit without accessories at a reduced cost, making it ideal as a backup log vape
  • The manufacturer (Rick) was praised for customer service and standing behind the product beyond the warranty period
  • Universal power supply compatibility — any standard 12V/1A adapter with the correct plug worked

Cons identified by the community:

  • Significant learning curve for new users
  • Did not produce large or dense vapor clouds compared to more powerful vaporizers
  • Described by some as "too weak" for users who preferred heavier sessions
  • One-year warranty was shorter than some competitors (notably the Purple Days, which offered three years)
  • Required patience and technique development to achieve satisfying results

One community member who listed their Aromazap for resale described it as part of a "collection" and priced it at $175 with accessories, suggesting that even used units retained value among enthusiasts. The device accumulated a dedicated subforum's worth of discussion, indicating sustained community interest over many years.

Tips & Techniques

The Aromazap was widely acknowledged to have a notable learning curve. The community developed several key techniques:

  • Draw speed matters: Since the Aromazap lacks adjustable temperature controls, the primary way to modulate vapor temperature and density is through draw speed. Slower, more controlled draws allow the herb to reach higher temperatures, while faster draws cool the air path and produce thinner vapor.
  • Power supply voltage: The standard 12V/1A power supply provides baseline performance. Community members noted that using a 14V power supply increases the operating temperature, which can help users who find the stock configuration too mild. This was a commonly discussed modification for those who wanted more robust vapor production.
  • Stem packing: How tightly the stem is packed and how finely the herb is ground directly affects airflow resistance and vapor quality. Finding the right balance between grind consistency and pack density was considered essential to mastering the device.
  • Leave it on: Like other log vaporizers, the Aromazap was designed to be left powered on continuously, maintaining operating temperature and allowing for on-demand use throughout the day with minimal energy consumption.
  • Multiple stems: Some users kept multiple pre-loaded stems ready for use. One community member owned nine stems for their Aromazap, suggesting a preference for having several loads prepared in advance.

Temperature Guide

The Aromazap does not feature precise digital temperature control. Instead, temperature is a function of:

Power supply voltage (12V standard; 14V for hotter operation)

Draw speed (slower = hotter; faster = cooler)

Stem packing density (tighter pack = more restricted airflow = hotter)

Because exact temperature readings were not available without external measurement, the community did not establish specific degree-based sweet spots for this device. General log vape temperature guidance from the community applies:

EffectApproach
Flavor-focusedUse 12V power supply, moderate draw speed, loosely packed stem
Balanced sessionUse 12V power supply, slow deliberate draws
Stronger extraction / cloudsUse 14V power supply, slow draws, moderately packed stem

Users seeking precise temperature data were encouraged to experiment with draw technique, as the Aromazap's simplicity meant that the user's technique was effectively the temperature control mechanism.

Maintenance & Cleaning

Maintenance information specific to the Aromazap was limited in the available community discussions, but general log vape maintenance practices apply:

  • Stems: Glass stems should be cleaned regularly by soaking in isopropyl alcohol (ISO). Residue buildup in the stem affects airflow and flavor.
  • Heating core: The ceramic resistor heating element generally requires little maintenance. Avoid inserting anything abrasive into the heater bore.
  • Wood exterior: The wooden log body should be kept dry and away from excessive moisture. Do not submerge the unit.
  • Reclaim: As with other log vapes, the glass stems accumulate honey-colored oil (reclaim) over time, which many users saved for later use by soaking stems in warm milk or evaporating the ISO used for cleaning.

Common Issues & Fixes

Weak Vapor Production

Problem: New users frequently reported that the Aromazap produced weak or thin vapor.

Solution: This was almost always attributable to the learning curve. Community recommendations included: slowing draw speed significantly, trying a 14V power supply for additional heat, and experimenting with grind consistency and stem packing. Patience and practice were emphasized.

Material Safety Concerns

A notable community discussion arose regarding material safety in budget vaporizers, with some members comparing the Aromazap's construction materials to those found in cheaper Chinese-manufactured devices. Community members debated the safety of various metals used in vaporizer construction. Key points from the discussion:

  • The Aromazap's use of stainless steel components was compared favorably to restaurant-grade stainless steel, which is universally used in food preparation and surgical equipment.
  • Community members pointed out that stainless steel's chromium content is not a safety concern at vaporization temperatures, as chromium's melting point exceeds 1900°F (1038°C) — far above any temperature the device reaches.
  • Concerns about lead exposure were raised in the context of budget vaporizers generally, not the Aromazap specifically. Community members emphasized that lead exposure is cumulative and asymptomatic, making material sourcing important.
  • The community consensus was that the Aromazap's artisan construction and known materials provided a meaningful safety advantage over unverified budget alternatives.

Power Supply Compatibility

Problem: Users who lost or needed to replace the power supply.

Solution: Any standard 12V/1A DC adapter with a 5.5×2.5mm barrel plug works. This is the same plug type used by many older external hard drives and is widely available. A 14V adapter can also be used for higher operating temperatures.

Accessories & Modifications

  • Glass stems: The Aromazap ships with glass stems that are cross-compatible with other log vapes in the ETerra family (Purple Days, Woodeez). This interchangeability was confirmed by community testing.
  • Aromatherapy cup: Included with the standard package, allowing the device to be used for essential oil diffusion when not being used for dry herb.
  • 14V power supply upgrade: The most commonly discussed modification — simply swapping the stock 12V adapter for a 14V unit to increase operating temperature.
  • Water pipe adapter (WPA): While not extensively discussed in the available threads, the glass stem design of log vapes generally permits adaptation for use with water pipes using appropriately sized glass-on-glass adapters.
  • Standalone wood unit: The Myrtlezap variant could be purchased as a standalone unit (without transformer, stems, or aromatherapy accessories) at a reduced price, which was ideal for users who already owned compatible accessories from another log vape.
  • Magnetic stem attachment: At least one community member experimented with magnetizing the stainless steel stem tip to create a snap-in stem retention system, though they reported that the grade of stainless steel used did not hold magnetization. This was noted to be a property of lower-numbered stainless steel grades (such as 303 or 304 series austenitic stainless), which are non-magnetic.

Comparisons

A comprehensive comparative review of the three major ETerra-descended log vaporizers was conducted by a community member who owned all three: the Purple Days (PD), the Woodeez (WDZ), and the Aromazap.

Key Similarities (All Three Log Vapes)

  • All three use the same ceramic resistor heating element
  • All three ship with identically sized glass stems (cross-compatible)
  • All three follow the same basic log form factor and operating principle
  • All three are designed to be left on continuously for on-demand use
