# Arizer Extreme Q

Overview

The Arizer Extreme Q (commonly referred to as "the EQ," "the Q," or simply "the Extreme") is a desktop dry herb vaporizer manufactured by Arizer, a Canadian company. First released in the late 2000s, the Extreme Q is a multi-function unit offering both whip (direct draw) and balloon (bag) delivery methods, powered by a built-in fan system. It uses a convection-dominant heating method, drawing heated air through herb material loaded into a glass "cyclone bowl" that sits atop an 18.8mm male glass-on-glass (GonG) heating element. The unit is mains-powered (desktop) and features digital temperature control with an LCD display and a remote control for hands-free operation.

The Extreme Q occupies an important position in the vaporizer market as one of the most affordable desktop units to offer both bag-filling and whip capabilities, placing it in frequent comparison with significantly more expensive competitors such as the Volcano. Over the years it became one of the most widely discussed and recommended entry-level desktop vaporizers within the vaporizer community, earning a reputation as a versatile "jack of all trades" unit at an accessible price point.

A stripped-down sibling model, the Arizer V-Tower, omits the fan and bag-filling capability but retains the same heating element, cyclone bowl, and whip functionality at a lower price.

Community Reception

The Arizer Extreme Q was widely regarded within the community as one of the best value propositions in the desktop vaporizer market. It was frequently the top recommendation for users seeking their first desktop vaporizer, particularly those unsure whether they preferred whip or bag delivery. Community members praised several attributes:

Pros:

  • Versatility: The ability to use whip, bags, or direct connection to water pipes from a single unit was considered its strongest selling point. One community member noted it was ideal "especially if you don't know what you want in delivery methods."
  • Price-to-performance ratio: At roughly $135–$200 (depending on era and retailer), it offered features that rivaled units costing two to three times as much.
  • Digital temperature control: Precise 1-degree adjustments and a clear LCD display were appreciated, especially by users transitioning from analog dial-style vaporizers.
  • Remote control: The included remote was highlighted as a significant convenience feature, particularly for accessibility. Community members specifically recommended the EQ for users with mobility limitations, noting the remote allowed full control of temperature, fan, and lighting without needing to reach the unit.
  • Glass-on-glass compatibility: The standard 18.8mm male joint made it straightforward to connect to water pipes and other glass accessories.
  • Elbow pack technique: A community-developed loading method (discussed in detail below) dramatically improved the unit's efficiency with small amounts of herb, generating "dense, lung busting hits."

Cons:

  • Vapor density compared to dedicated whip vapes: Multiple experienced users noted that while the EQ could produce thick vapor, it could not quite match the density of hits from dedicated whip-only units like the Silver Surfer Vaporizer (SSV) or Da Buddha (DBV). As one experienced user summarized: "The E can get thick hits, just not quite as thick as the SSV."
  • Herb efficiency with stock bowl: When using the standard cyclone bowl with a full pack, users found the unit could be somewhat wasteful compared to log vapes or other efficiency-focused designs. Some users consuming around 1g per day felt this was too much and sought more conservative alternatives.
  • Fragility of glass components: The all-glass air path, while praised for purity, meant that breakage of the cyclone bowl, elbow adapter, or whip components was a recurring concern.
  • Bag-filling speed: While functional, the fan speed for filling bags was noted to be slower than that of the Volcano, with one user observing the EQ's maximum fan speed was roughly comparable to an older Volcano Classic's slower rate.
  • Bowl design limitation: A notable design critique centered on the hole in the bottom of the cyclone bowl being smaller than the screen area, leading to uneven heating of herb material, particularly with smaller loads or when using the fan.

Air Path Concerns:

A persistent topic of discussion involved questions about whether heated air in the EQ passed over internal electronics before reaching the herb. This concern surfaced repeatedly in purchase advice threads, with some prospective buyers citing it as a reason for hesitation. However, knowledgeable community members consistently clarified this was a misconception. One well-known community moderator stated definitively: "This is not correct. The air is not heated until it is inside the glass air path and past all circuitry." Another experienced member noted that the air intake is cold air passing through the unit, and that the heating element only operates within the glass air path itself. The community consensus was that while the concern was understandable, the EQ's air path design was sound.

Tips & Techniques

The Elbow Pack Method

The single most important technique developed by the community for the Extreme Q was the elbow pack method. Rather than loading herb into the cyclone bowl, a small amount of finely ground herb (approximately 0.05–0.15g) is packed into the screen of the glass elbow adapter itself. This places the material at the narrow opening of the elbow where it sits in the concentrated stream of hot air flowing from the heating element through the empty cyclone bowl.

Benefits of the elbow pack:

  • Dramatically improved efficiency for solo users, using a fraction of the herb a full bowl requires
  • More even extraction because all the herb is directly in the hot air stream
  • Denser vapor per amount of material used
  • Particularly effective for whip use and through-water sessions

As one community member explained, it "basically works well for the same reason—forces air through all of the herb."

Full Bowl Technique

For group sessions or when larger amounts are desired, the cyclone bowl can be loaded conventionally. Users recommended:

  • Fine to medium grind for best results
  • Stirring between bags or draws using the included glass stirring tool
  • Creating a small mound of material directly over the center hole for better air flow
  • Packing 0.2–0.5g depending on group size; users reported getting 8–9 bags from 0.4–0.5g with frequent stirring

Fan Use

  • The fan is primarily used for filling bags but can also be used to force air through the whip
  • For bags, starting with the fan at lower speed and adjusting upward was recommended
  • Some users preferred purely manual draw through the whip, finding fan-assisted whip hits less satisfying than passive draw

Preheat Protocol

Community members recommended allowing the unit to heat for a full 10 minutes at the desired temperature (or slightly above) before beginning a session to ensure the glass components and air path are thoroughly warmed. Some users would preheat with the empty cyclone bowl in place to bring the glass up to temperature.

CamelBak Mouthpiece Modification

A widely shared tip from the broader vaporizer community involved using CamelBak hydration pack replacement mouthpieces, which fit snugly over the 10mm glass connections on both the bag mouthpiece (to prevent vapor from escaping between draws) and the whip mouthpiece (to enable hands-free use).

Temperature Guide

Temperature preferences varied based on loading method (cyclone bowl vs. elbow pack), delivery method (whip vs. bag), and desired effects. The community developed general guidelines:

Cyclone Bowl Packing

TemperatureEffect
175°C–190°C (347°F–374°F)Light, flavorful vapor; primarily lower-boiling-point compounds
190°C–220°C (374°F–428°F)Sweet spot for most users; good balance of flavor and vapor density
220°C–250°C (428°F–482°F)Dense vapor, full extraction, diminished flavor

One experienced user reported their general range as "190–220 for cyclone bowl."

Elbow Packing

Because the herb sits further from the heating element in an elbow pack, the community recommended increasing the set temperature by approximately 5–10°C (roughly 10–20°F) compared to cyclone bowl settings.

TemperatureEffect
200°C–230°C (392°F–446°F)Typical range for elbow packing

One community member specified: "200–230 for elbow pack generally."

Bag Filling

Users reported good results filling bags at approximately 355°F (180°C) for light, flavorful bags, with temperatures increasing toward 200°C+ (392°F+) for denser fills. Using 0.2g or less produced 2–3 good bags at moderate temperatures.

Temperature Stepping

A widely practiced technique involved starting at a lower temperature and progressively increasing by 5–10°C increments over the course of a session. One community member described their approach with the Extreme Q as starting at 175°C and "slowly making my way up to 260°C." This practice was intended to extract different compound profiles at each stage—lighter, more flavorful compounds first, followed by heavier compounds at higher temperatures. Community opinion was divided on whether this approach was superior to finding a single "sweet spot" temperature and remaining there.

Maintenance & Cleaning

Glass Components

  • The cyclone bowl, elbow adapter, and whip glass components should be soaked in isopropyl alcohol (90% or higher recommended) periodically to remove accumulated resin
  • The glass stirring tool can be used to gently scrape resin from the inside of the elbow while warm
  • Screens should be replaced or cleaned when airflow becomes noticeably restricted; soaking in isopropyl alcohol and brushing clean is effective

Reclaim

Resin that accumulates inside the glass components, particularly the elbow adapter and whip, was noted by community members as being usable—it is essentially condensed vapor containing active compounds. Some users intentionally allowed this to accumulate before reclaiming it.

Screens

The included basket screens should be checked regularly for clogging. Replacement screens are inexpensive and widely available. Some users added a second, finer screen beneath the standard one to further reduce particulate matter from passing through.

Heating Element

The ceramic heating element itself requires minimal maintenance. Occasionally running the unit at a high temperature with no material loaded can help burn off any residue that may have accumulated.

Common Issues & Fixes

Uneven Extraction in Cyclone Bowl

Problem: Herb on the edges of the cyclone bowl remains green while the center cooks thoroughly, especially with smaller loads. The fan can push material to the sides of the bowl.

Cause: The hole at the bottom of the cyclone bowl is smaller than the screen diameter, concentrating airflow through the center.

Solutions:

  • Use the elbow pack method instead for small loads
  • Stir between draws or bags using the glass stirring tool
  • Create a small mound centered over the hole
  • A community member described widening the hole in a modified bowl using a paperclip as a mandrel, though this was not universally recommended due to concerns about material safety (stainless steel paperclips were considered acceptable)

Decreased Vapor Production

Problem: Previously reliable vapor production drops significantly.

Possible causes and solutions:

  • Different herb strain: Community consensus confirmed that different strains can produce dramatically different amounts of visible vapor. One member with experience across nine strains noted: "Some strains simply vaporize better than others; more vapor is not necessarily an indication of potency, however, they often correlate." Less visible vapor does not necessarily mean fewer active compounds are being extracted—it may simply indicate lower overall resin content.
  • Dirty screens or glass: Clean all glass components and replace screens
  • Insufficient preheat: Ensure at least 10 minutes of preheat time
  • Herb too moist or too dry: Herb that is overly wet requires more energy to vaporize; extremely dry herb may produce wispy vapor. Moderate dryness is optimal.

Throat/Airway Irritation

Problem: Some users reported throat dryness or irritation from extended sessions, particularly when using high temperatures or taking many consecutive draws.

Solutions:

  • Use a water pipe adapter to filter and cool vapor through water
  • Take smaller, more measured draws
  • Stay hydrated during sessions
  • Lower the temperature setting
  • The community strongly recommended consulting a healthcare professional if symptoms like blood in mucus appeared, with multiple members advising: "Don't try to diagnose it yourself, go see a doctor."

Accessories & Modifications

DDave Mod Kit

One of the most frequently recommended aftermarket modifications was the DDave Mod kit, which included a shorter, wider bowl that replaced the stock cyclone bowl. This modification addressed the uneven heating issue of the stock bowl and was particularly praised for micro-dosing capability. Community members specifically recommended it: "Pick up the DDave mod kit for it for micro dosing."

Water Pipe Adapters (WPA)

The 18.8mm male GonG joint on the heating element made the Extreme Q naturally compatible with standard 18.8mm female water pipes. Users commonly connected the unit to water pipes by:

  • Using the elbow adapter and whip connection directly to a 14mm or 18mm GonG adapter
  • Purchasing a dedicated 14mm GonG adapter for smaller water pipes
  • One community member described their setup: "I use it with the elbow whip attached to a 14mm GonG adapter for my bongs. Works pretty fantastic!"

The ability to connect to glass was consistently cited as one of the EQ's greatest strengths, with some users noting that once they discovered vapor bonging, they essentially stopped using the bag and dry whip methods entirely.

Volcano Liquid Pads

Community members discovered that Volcano liquid/concentrate pads could be used in the Extreme Q's cyclone bowl for concentrate use, as the EQ's higher maximum temperature (260°C/500°F) actually exceeded the Volcano's capability for this purpose. One member who recommended selling a Volcano in favor of an EQ specifically advised: "Keep your Volcano liquid pads, they'll come in handy."

Custom Bowl Modifications

Early community members experimented with custom glass bowls featuring a ring mechanism to hold screens in place more securely. These custom bowls used a glass ring between two female GonG pieces to create a fixed screen position, improving upon the stock bowl's tendency to allow screens to shift during use.

Replacement Bags

Standard turkey oven bags were commonly used as replacements for the included bags, offering a cost-effective alternative.

For users who only wanted whip use, the SSV was generally considered superior. For those wanting versatility or who were new to vaporizing, the EQ was recommended. As one experienced member put it: "For a vapor noob, I would recommend the E."

Arizer Extreme Q vs. Da Buddha Vaporizer (DBV)

The DBV, also made by 7th Floor (like the SSV), was frequently compared as a more affordable alternative. It shared the same heating element as the SSV and was considered a better pure whip vape than the EQ, offering "more predictable hits." One community member chose the DBV over the EQ specifically because they heard it was "a better direct draw vape." The EQ was preferred when bag capability or digital temperature control was desired.

Arizer Extreme Q vs. HerbalAire

The HerbalAire was compared favorably for bag efficiency and durability ("built like a tank"), with the added benefit of not requiring grinding. The EQ was preferred for its whip capabilities and water pipe integration. One long-time HerbalAire owner praised its efficiency and noted their original unit lasted over 10 years.

Arizer Extreme Q vs. Log Vapes (Underdog, E-Nano, WoodScents)

For users primarily interested in micro-dosing and maximum herb conservation, community members frequently suggested log vapes as superior alternatives. Log vapes could be left on all day for always-ready single hits and used extremely small amounts of herb. However, they lacked the bag-filling capability and group-friendly features of the EQ. This comparison was particularly relevant for solo users seeking to reduce their daily herb consumption.

Arizer Extreme Q vs. Arizer V-Tower

The V-Tower was recommended as a budget alternative for users who were confident they only wanted whip use and had no interest in bags. As one member suggested: "V-Tower, use the rest of the money for a 14mm GonG adapter and a nice bubbler/bong!"
