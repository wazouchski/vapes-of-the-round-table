# Arizer Extreme Q

Overview

The Arizer Extreme Q (commonly abbreviated as "EQ" or simply "the Q") is a desktop dry herb vaporizer manufactured by Arizer, a Canadian company based in Waterloo, Ontario. First released in the late 2000s, it became one of the most widely discussed and recommended mid-range desktop vaporizers within the vaporizer community. The Extreme Q is notable for its versatility, offering three distinct delivery methods: whip (direct draw), balloon/bag (fan-assisted), and direct connection to water pipes via glass-on-glass adapters.

The Extreme Q uses a convection-dominant heating system with a ceramic heating element. Air is drawn or pushed (via an integrated fan) through a glass "cyclone bowl" containing the herb, and the resulting vapor is delivered through the user's chosen method. It operates on AC mains power (desktop unit) and features a digital temperature display with precise degree-by-degree control, a remote control, and an internal fan with multiple speed settings.

The Extreme Q earned a reputation as one of the best value propositions in desktop vaporization — a "jack of all trades" that could fill bags, serve whip hits, and connect to glass, all at a fraction of the price of competitors like the Volcano. While the community acknowledged it did not necessarily excel at any single delivery method compared to dedicated units, its versatility, affordability, and ease of use made it a perennial recommendation for newcomers and experienced users alike.

Community Reception

The Arizer Extreme Q was one of the most discussed desktop vaporizers in the community, and overall reception was favorable, particularly given its price point. The community widely agreed that the EQ represented exceptional value for money, offering functionality that approached far more expensive units.

Pros commonly cited by the community:

  • Versatility: The ability to switch between whip, bag, and water pipe delivery in a single unit was considered its defining strength. As one community member noted, for someone unsure which delivery method they preferred, the EQ was frequently the default recommendation — especially for newcomers.
  • Price-to-performance ratio: At a retail price significantly below the Volcano and competitive with whip-only units like the Da Buddha (DBV), the EQ was seen as a budget-conscious choice that sacrificed little in core performance.
  • Digital temperature control: Precise, degree-by-degree digital temperature adjustment was valued by users who wanted to target specific cannabinoid and terpene release points.
  • Remote control: The included remote was appreciated for adjusting temperature and fan settings without leaving one's seat. This feature was specifically highlighted as beneficial for users with mobility challenges.
  • Glass air path: The all-glass vapor path (from cyclone bowl through elbow adapter) was considered a strength for flavor purity.
  • Water pipe compatibility: The 18.8mm glass-on-glass connections made it easy to pair with standard water pipes, a feature the community valued highly.
  • Bag capability at a fraction of the Volcano's price: While bags were acknowledged as not quite matching the Volcano's convenience (no valve system, slower fill speed), the vapor quality in bags was considered comparable.
  • Elbow pack method: A community-discovered technique (detailed below) that dramatically improved efficiency and vapor density for whip use.

Cons commonly cited by the community:

  • Whip hits not as dense as dedicated whip vapes: The community generally agreed that the Silver Surfer (SSV) and Da Buddha (DBV) produced thicker, denser whip hits than the EQ. As one experienced member put it, the EQ "can get thick hits, just not quite as thick as the SSV."
  • Herb efficiency concerns: Some users felt the standard cyclone bowl used too much herb for solo sessions, though the elbow pack method largely addressed this concern.
  • Glass fragility: Multiple community members noted that the all-glass components (cyclone bowls, elbow adapters, whip connections) were prone to breakage from accidents.
  • Cyclone bowl design flaw: A notable discussion centered on the observation that the hole at the bottom of the cyclone bowl was smaller than the screen area, causing uneven heating and herb being pushed to the sides of the bowl when using the fan.
  • Bag system less refined than Volcano: The EQ's bag system lacked the Volcano's valve mechanism and filled at roughly half the Volcano's maximum fan speed, making bag use slightly less convenient.
  • Session-style use: The EQ was considered a session vaporizer — not ideal for quick single hits the way log vapes or on-demand units could deliver.

The EQ was very frequently recommended as a first "real" vaporizer. Community consensus held that it was particularly well-suited for users who wanted to explore different delivery methods before investing in a more specialized (and typically more expensive) unit.

Tips & Techniques

The community developed an extensive body of knowledge around optimizing the Extreme Q's performance. Several techniques became widely adopted.

The Elbow Pack Method

The single most important community-discovered technique for the Extreme Q. Rather than loading herb into the cyclone bowl, the user packs a small amount of finely ground herb directly into the screen of the glass elbow adapter (the 90° bend piece that connects the bowl to the whip or bag). This method:

  • Uses significantly less herb (approximately 0.05–0.15g vs. the 0.2–0.5g a full cyclone bowl accommodates)
  • Forces hot air through the entire load evenly, resulting in more complete extraction
  • Produces denser, more flavorful hits through the whip
  • Was considered superior for solo use and micro-dosing

To elbow pack: push the screen slightly into the elbow adapter so it sits securely, then press a small amount of finely ground herb onto the screen. The pack should be firm enough to stay in place when inverted but not so tight as to restrict airflow.

Preheat Routine

The community recommended allowing the EQ to heat up for at least 10–15 minutes before use, even after the digital display indicates the target temperature has been reached. This allows the glass components (cyclone bowl, elbow adapter) to reach thermal equilibrium, resulting in more consistent vapor production from the first draw.

Some users recommended preheating at a higher temperature (e.g., 230°C / 446°F) for 10 minutes, then adjusting down to their target vaping temperature.

Bowl Loading for Fan/Bag Use

When using the cyclone bowl with the fan for filling bags:

  • Use a medium-fine grind
  • Do not overfill — leave space for air circulation
  • Stir between bags using the included glass stirring tool
  • Creating a small mound of herb directly over the center hole of the cyclone bowl was recommended to counteract the undersized hole issue
  • Multiple users reported getting 2–3 bags from a light load (~0.2g at ~355°F / 179°C) and 8–9 bags from a fuller load (~0.4–0.5g) with stirring

Whip Use Optimization

  • Using the elbow pack with the whip attached was the preferred method for direct draw
  • Slow, steady draws were recommended for best vapor density
  • Leaving the fan off during whip use was standard; the user's inhalation provides the airflow
  • Some users connected the whip directly to water pipes using the glass-on-glass joints

Volcano Liquid Pad Compatibility

Community members discovered that the Volcano's stainless steel liquid pads could be used inside the EQ's cyclone bowl for concentrate use. This was frequently recommended for users transitioning from or supplementing a Volcano setup.

CamelBak Mouthpiece Hack

A widely shared tip: CamelBak hydration pack replacement mouthpieces (bite valves) fit over the 10mm glass connections on both the bag mouthpieces and whip ends. On bags, this prevents vapor from escaping between draws. On whips, it enables hands-free use.

Temperature Guide

The Extreme Q's digital temperature control allows precise targeting. However, the community noted an important nuance: the displayed temperature represents the heater element temperature, not the actual temperature at the herb. The herb temperature is lower than the display, with the difference being greater for elbow packs (herb further from heat source) than for cyclone bowl packs.

Cyclone Bowl Packing

Temperature RangeEffect Profile
170°C–185°C (338°F–365°F)Light, flavorful vapor; emphasis on terpenes and lower-boiling-point cannabinoids; more cerebral/uplifting effects
185°C–200°C (365°F–392°F)Moderate vapor density; balanced extraction of both flavor and active compounds
200°C–220°C (392°F–428°F)Dense vapor; fuller-spectrum extraction; more sedative/body effects

Community members commonly reported using a range of 190°C–220°C (374°F–428°F) for cyclone bowl loads.

Elbow Packing

Because the herb is farther from the heater in an elbow pack, higher displayed temperatures are needed to achieve equivalent extraction:

Temperature RangeEffect Profile
190°C–205°C (374°F–401°F)Light to moderate vapor; flavor-forward
205°C–220°C (401°F–428°F)Good vapor density; solid extraction
220°C–230°C (428°F–446°F)Dense vapor; near-complete extraction

One experienced community member summarized: "I usually do 190–230 depending. 190–220 for cyclone bowl, 200–230 for elbow pack generally." Another recommended bumping elbow pack temperatures up approximately 5°C compared to equivalent cyclone bowl settings.

Temperature Stepping Strategy

A popular approach within the community was to start at a lower temperature for the first few draws (prioritizing flavor) and progressively increase temperature to extract remaining compounds:

  • Step 1: Start at 175°C–185°C (347°F–365°F) for initial flavorful draws
  • Step 2: Increase to 200°C–210°C (392°F–410°F) for main session
  • Step 3: Finish at 220°C–260°C (428°F–500°F) for full extraction

One member reported starting at 175°C and "slowly making my way up to 260°C" on the Extreme Q, though this upper extreme was atypical. The community was divided on whether temperature stepping was necessary — some users preferred finding a single "sweet spot" and remaining there, while others found stepping provided a more varied experience and more complete extraction.

Bag Filling

For filling bags, slightly higher temperatures were commonly used since vapor cools during the filling process. A temperature of approximately 200°C–220°C (392°F–428°F) with fan speed 1 or 2 was a common starting point for bag fills.

Maintenance & Cleaning

Glass Components

  • Elbow adapters and cyclone bowls accumulate resin over time, which actually serves as evidence of vapor production but eventually restricts airflow
  • Soak glass pieces in isopropyl alcohol (90%+ concentration recommended) for 30 minutes to several hours
  • Rinse thoroughly with warm water after soaking
  • A small brush or pipe cleaner can help remove stubborn residue from the elbow adapter's interior
  • The accumulated resin ("stem milk" or "reclaim") can be salvaged by simmering glass pieces in milk or another fatty liquid before cleaning, a practice common across multiple Arizer products

Screens

  • Stainless steel screens should be replaced periodically or cleaned by soaking in isopropyl alcohol
  • Screens that become clogged restrict airflow and reduce vapor quality
  • Keeping spare screens on hand was universally recommended

Whip Tubing

  • The silicone whip tubing accumulates residue over time
  • Replace periodically or soak in isopropyl alcohol
  • Some users replaced the stock tubing with medical-grade silicone tubing from third-party sources

Heating Element

  • The ceramic heating element generally requires no user maintenance
  • Keeping the unit on a stable surface to prevent glass components from falling onto the element was recommended

Common Issues & Fixes

Reduced Vapor Production

Symptom: Gradually decreasing vapor output over time.

Possible causes and solutions:

  • Dirty screens: Clean or replace screens. Clogged screens are the most common cause of reduced performance.
  • Dirty elbow adapter: Clean with isopropyl alcohol; resin buildup restricts airflow.
  • Insufficient preheat time: Allow 10–15 minutes of preheat, not just until the display reaches target temperature.
  • Herb quality variation: The community confirmed that different strains produce different amounts of visible vapor. As one member explained, "some strains simply vaporize better than others; more vapor is not necessarily an indication of potency, however, they often correlate." Less visible vapor does not necessarily mean fewer active compounds are being extracted — it may simply indicate lower overall resin content.

Uneven Cooking in the Cyclone Bowl

Symptom: Herb in the cyclone bowl is unevenly browned — center extracted while edges remain green, particularly when using the fan.

Cause: The hole in the bottom of the cyclone bowl is smaller than the screen area, concentrating hot air in the center and pushing herb to the sides.

Solutions:

  • Stir frequently between draws or bags using the glass stirring tool
  • Create a small mound of herb directly over the center hole
  • Use the elbow pack method instead, which forces air through all the herb evenly
  • One community member documented a modification using a paperclip (stainless steel only) to widen the effective airflow path, though this required care to avoid allowing herb through the screen

Throat/Airway Irritation

Symptom: Dry throat, coughing, or irritation after extended sessions.

Solutions:

  • Use with a water pipe for filtration and humidification
  • Take slower, gentler draws
  • Lower the temperature setting
  • Hydrate between sessions
  • The community strongly recommended consulting a medical professional for any persistent symptoms, particularly anything involving blood in mucus

Glass Breakage

Symptom: Broken cyclone bowls, elbow adapters, or whip connections.

Solutions:

  • Keep spare glass components on hand
  • Handle glass pieces carefully, especially when warm
  • Third-party glass replacements and modifications are available (see Accessories section)
  • Some users purchased the DDave mod kit specifically because it included more durable glass options

Accessories & Modifications

DDave Mod Kit

One of the most frequently recommended aftermarket modifications for the Extreme Q. The DDave mod (created by a well-known community member) includes modified glass pieces — most notably a shorter bowl adapter that positions the herb closer to the heating element. This was reported to improve:

  • Vapor density and extraction efficiency
  • Micro-dosing capability (smaller bowl allows effective use of very small loads)
  • Overall performance, narrowing the gap between the EQ and more expensive desktop units

The DDave mod was particularly recommended for users seeking to use small amounts of herb efficiently, and was specifically called out as beneficial for micro-dosing in multiple community discussions.

Water Pipe Adapters (WPA)

The Extreme Q's 18.8mm glass-on-glass connections make water pipe integration straightforward:

  • The elbow adapter can connect directly to 18mm female water pipe joints
  • 14mm GonG (glass-on-glass) adapters were available for pairing with smaller water pipes
  • Using the EQ through water was widely considered to produce the smoothest experience
  • One community member recommended purchasing the V-Tower (fan-less version) and investing the savings in a quality GonG adapter and glass piece, particularly for users who primarily wanted to use the unit with water pipes

Third-Party Glass

  • Custom bowl modifications were discussed, including designs with integrated screen-holding rings for more secure screen placement
  • Replacement cyclone bowls, elbow adapters, and whip assemblies were available from Arizer and third-party suppliers
  • All-glass stems with integrated honeycomb screens (similar to those used in other Arizer products) were explored by some users seeking a purer glass air path

Bags/Balloons

  • Replacement bags can be sourced from Arizer or from standard food-grade oven bags (turkey bags), which were a common community recommendation
  • Various bag sizes were used depending on preference

Comparisons

The Extreme Q was one of the most frequently compared desktop vaporizers in the community, owing to its mid-range positioning and broad feature set.

vs. Volcano (Classic and Digital)

The Volcano was the most common comparison point. Community consensus:

  • Bag quality: Vapor quality in bags was considered comparable between the two units. However, the Volcano's valve system was more convenient (no vapor leakage), and its fan filled bags roughly twice as fast as the EQ at maximum speed.
  • Durability: The Volcano's largely plastic-and-metal construction was considered far more durable than the EQ's glass-heavy design. One member noted being "more concerned about all the glass" in the EQ than about any other design concern.
  • Versatility: The EQ won decisively — the Volcano is a bag-only unit, while the EQ offers whip, bag, and water pipe options.
  • Price: The EQ typically cost one-third to one-quarter the price of a Volcano, making it the clear value pick.
  • Concentrate use: The EQ's higher maximum temperature (260°C / 500°F) gave it an advantage for concentrate use. Volcano liquid pads could be used in the EQ.
  • Air path purity: Some concern was raised about the EQ's air path, with early claims suggesting hot air might pass over internal electronics. However, community members with direct knowledge of the unit's design clarified that "the air is not heated until it is inside the glass air path and past all circuitry," effectively debunking this concern.

Several community members reported selling their Volcanos in favor of the EQ, particularly those who valued water pipe compatibility. Others maintained that the Volcano remained the superior dedicated bag unit.

vs. Silver Surfer (SSV) and Da Buddha (DBV)

The 7th Floor whip vapes were the primary comparison for whip-style performance:

  • Whip hit density: The SSV was generally regarded as producing thicker, denser whip hits than the EQ. The DBV was considered to provide "more predictable hits" with the same heating element as the SSV.
  • Learning curve: The SSV's hands-free design had a steeper learning curve, while the EQ's elbow pack was considered more forgiving.
  • Versatility: The EQ's bag and fan capability gave it significant additional functionality over the whip-only 7th Floor units.
  • Build quality: The SSV and DBV's simpler, more analog designs (potentiometer dial, no digital electronics) were considered more durable long-term by some users, though the EQ's digital temperature control was preferred by others.
  • Recommendation pattern: For users who knew they only wanted whip delivery, the SSV was generally recommended. For users unsure of their preferred delivery method, the EQ was the default suggestion.

vs. HerbalAire

  • The HerbalAire was praised for exceptional efficiency, the ability to work without grinding herb, and extremely durable construction ("built like a tank")
  • It was considered a strong competitor for bag use, with some members rating its bag density as superior
  • The HerbalAire was noted for its versatility (whip, bag, water pipe), similar to the EQ
  • The EQ was generally considered to have a more refined and modern interface

vs. Log Vapes (E-Nano, Underdog, WoodScents)

  • Log vapes were recommended over the EQ for users primarily interested in micro-dosing and maximum herb conservation
  • Log vapes could be left on all day for on-demand use, a significant convenience advantage
  • The EQ's bag-filling capability and higher maximum output were advantages for group sessions
  • Log vapes were often suggested as a complement to or upgrade from the EQ rather than a direct replacement

vs. Arizer V-Tower

The V-Tower shares the same heating element and bowl system as the Extreme Q but lacks the fan and bag capability:

  • Community members often recommended the V-Tower for users who planned to use the unit exclusively through a water pipe, as the fan was unnecessary in that configuration
  • The price savings from choosing the V-Tower could be invested in glass accessories
  • For users wanting any bag functionality, the Extreme Q was the clear choice

vs. VapeXhale Cloud EVO

  • The EVO was considered a significant step up in vapor quality and extraction efficiency, particularly through water
  • The EVO's smaller bowl and more powerful heater produced denser vapor from less material
  • The EVO's higher price point and more specialized design (primarily water pipe use) put it in a different category
  • The EQ was often recommended as an entry point, with the EVO as a potential future upgrade
