# Sticky Brick Junior

Overview

The Sticky Brick Junior (commonly abbreviated as SBJ) is a portable butane-powered dry herb vaporizer manufactured by Sticky Brick Labs, based in the United States. It is a compact member of the broader Sticky Brick lineup, which includes the Sticky Brick OG (the original full-size model), the Sticky Brick Runt, the HydroBrick, and others. The Junior was designed as a more pocket-friendly option while retaining the core convection heating principle that defines the Sticky Brick product line.

The SBJ uses pure convection heating — the user directs a flame from a butane torch lighter into a glass intake tube, and the resulting hot air is drawn through the herb chamber, extracting vapor on demand. This places it in the category of on-demand butane convection vaporizers, a niche that the vaporizer community has long valued for its rapid heat-up time, lack of electronics, and ability to deliver potent hits with minimal wait.

The device is constructed primarily from hardwood with glass air pathways, giving it a distinctive artisanal aesthetic. It features a flip-top lid over the bowl area for convenience and portability.

Community Reception

The Sticky Brick Junior was well-regarded within the vaporizer community as a hard-hitting, efficient on-demand vaporizer in a compact package. Community members appreciated its simplicity — no batteries to charge, no electronics to fail — and its ability to deliver thick, flavorful vapor comparable to much larger devices.

Pros frequently cited by the community:

  • Excellent convection flavor, especially on the first few draws
  • True on-demand use — no session commitment required
  • Extremely fast extraction; a bowl can be finished in just a few hits
  • Durable hardwood construction with a pleasing aesthetic
  • No reliance on batteries or electronics
  • Compact and portable relative to the Sticky Brick OG

Cons noted by the community:

  • Significant learning curve — new users frequently risked combustion before mastering technique
  • Requires a quality butane torch lighter for consistent results
  • Not as discreet in public as electronic portables due to visible torch use
  • The official WPA (U-Joint water piece adapter) was considered expensive by some users, with prices around 50€ or more when factoring in shipping to Europe
  • Manual temperature control demands attention and practice

The community often recommended the SBJ as a strong choice for users seeking powerful, on-demand extraction without electronic components, though newcomers were consistently advised to be patient while developing their technique.

Tips & Techniques

The Sticky Brick Junior's performance is heavily technique-dependent. The community developed and shared numerous best practices over the years:

Torch Technique:

  • Use a single-flame or dual-flame butane torch lighter for the best control. Triple-flame torches deliver more heat faster but increase combustion risk for beginners.
  • Aim the flame into the glass intake at an angle rather than pointing it straight down into the bowl. The goal is to heat the air, not to direct the flame onto the herb.
  • Maintain a consistent distance between the torch tip and the intake — typically a few centimeters. Moving the flame closer increases temperature; moving it farther away decreases it.

Draw Technique:

  • Draw slowly and steadily. A slow draw allows the hot air more contact time with the herb, improving extraction. Pulling too hard cools the airstream and produces wispy hits; pulling too weakly with the torch too close risks combustion.
  • Start with gentle, sipping draws until you see vapor forming, then adjust speed and torch distance to taste.

Loading Tips:

  • A medium grind works well — not too fine (which can restrict airflow and increase combustion risk) and not too coarse (which reduces surface area).
  • Don't overpack the chamber. A loose, moderate load allows air to flow freely through the herb for even convection extraction.
  • Stir the bowl between hits for more even extraction, especially on larger loads.

General Advice:

  • One community member noted that the cheapest and most effective way to connect the SBJ to a water piece is by using a short length of silicone whip tubing fitted over the mouthpiece, connected to a male glass adapter that fits into the water pipe joint. This avoids the cost of the official U-Joint adapter.

Temperature Guide

Because the Sticky Brick Junior uses a butane torch with no electronic temperature readout, temperature control is entirely manual and governed by the interplay of flame size, torch-to-intake distance, and draw speed. The community generally described effective use in qualitative terms rather than precise temperatures:

Desired EffectTechnique Adjustment
Flavor-focused / Low temp (~160–185°C / 320–365°F equivalent)Hold the torch farther from the intake; use a smaller flame; draw at a moderate pace. Expect light, wispy, very flavorful vapor.
Balanced / Medium temp (~185–205°C / 365–400°F equivalent)Moderate torch distance and flame size; steady draw. Visible vapor with good flavor and moderate effects.
Maximum extraction / High temp (~205–220°C / 400–430°F equivalent)Torch closer to the intake; slightly longer heat application before drawing; slow, steady pull. Thick, dense clouds with stronger effects but reduced flavor nuance.
Combustion danger zone (>230°C / >446°F)Torch too close, flame too large, or draw too slow. Characterized by harsh taste and visible smoke. Stir and adjust technique immediately.

Community "sweet spot": Most experienced users converged on a medium-distance torch position with a slow, controlled draw as the ideal starting point, adjusting from there based on personal preference. The sweet spot was described as the point where visible vapor begins to form without any harshness — a skill that typically took several sessions to develop reliably.

Maintenance & Cleaning

The Sticky Brick Junior's maintenance is relatively straightforward due to its simple construction:

Glass Components (Intake and Air Path):

  • Remove the glass pieces and soak them in isopropyl alcohol (90% or higher recommended) periodically, especially when residue buildup becomes visible.
  • Rinse thoroughly with warm water after soaking and allow to dry completely before reassembling.

Wood Body:

  • Wipe down with a dry or slightly damp cloth. Avoid soaking the wood in alcohol or water, as this can damage the finish and warp the grain.
  • Some users applied food-safe wood conditioner or wax occasionally to maintain the wood's appearance and longevity.

Screen/Restrictor Disc:

  • Clean or replace the stainless steel restrictor disc as needed. Soaking in isopropyl alcohol and brushing with a stiff brush removes residue effectively.

Frequency:

  • Glass cleaning every 1–2 weeks with regular use, or whenever airflow restriction is noticed.
  • The wood body requires less frequent attention but benefits from regular inspection.

Accessories & Modifications

Water Pipe Adapters (WPA):

The official Sticky Brick Labs U-Joint water piece adapter is the purpose-built WPA for the Junior, allowing it to connect to standard water pipes. It is available in both 14mm and 18mm joint sizes. Community members noted that the U-Joint adapter was functional but relatively expensive, particularly for users ordering from outside the United States.

One practical concern raised in the community was whether the U-Joint adapter would bring the brick far enough away from the central joint of certain water pipe designs (such as sidecar-style bubblers) to avoid physical interference. Users were advised to consider the geometry of their specific water pipe before purchasing.

Budget WPA Alternative:

A frequently recommended low-cost alternative was to use a short piece of food-safe silicone tubing (whip) fitted over the SBJ's mouthpiece, connected to a male glass adapter of the appropriate joint size (14mm or 18mm) that fits into the water pipe. This approach was cited as the cheapest and most flexible way to use the Junior through water.

Joint Size Considerations:

One experienced community member advised that basing one's glass collection on 18.8mm (18mm) joints is more adaptable overall, as it is easy to use a reducer/adapter to accept 14mm WPAs in an 18mm joint, whereas going the other direction is less practical. This is relevant for users who plan to use multiple vaporizers (such as an E-Nano alongside the SBJ) through the same water piece.

Recommended Water Pipes:

The Mobius Matrix Sidecar (available from Chinese glass vendors such as DHgate) was discussed as a popular pairing for the SBJ, valued for its smooth percolation and compact size. Community members considered this style of bubbler well-suited to vaporizer use due to its relatively low drag and moderate water volume.

Torch Lighters:

While not included with the device, a quality single-flame or dual-flame butane torch lighter was considered essential. The community generally recommended refillable torch lighters from reputable brands and emphasized the importance of using high-quality refined butane to keep the lighter functioning reliably.

Comparisons

Sticky Brick Junior vs. Sticky Brick OG:

The Junior was regarded as the more portable member of the family, trading some bowl size and ergonomic comfort for a more compact form factor. The OG was often preferred for home use due to its larger size and easier torch technique, while the Junior was favored for its portability and on-the-go capability.

Sticky Brick Junior vs. Grasshopper:

This comparison arose within community discussions, as both vaporizers target the on-demand, portable market but take fundamentally different approaches. The Grasshopper is a battery-powered electronic convection vaporizer in a pen-like form factor, offering electronic temperature control and greater discretion. The SBJ, by contrast, requires a butane torch, is less discreet, but has no electronics to fail and no battery to degrade. The Grasshopper was known in the community for significant reliability and warranty issues, which made some users recommend the SBJ as a more dependable long-term option despite its learning curve.

Sticky Brick Junior vs. E-Nano:

While the E-Nano is a desktop log vaporizer (AC-powered) and the SBJ is a portable butane device, both were commonly discussed together because users frequently owned both as complementary units — the E-Nano for consistent home sessions and the SBJ for portable or on-demand use. Both were noted to pair well with water pipes, and users who owned both often sought a single water piece compatible with each, making joint size compatibility (14mm vs. 18mm) a practical consideration.
