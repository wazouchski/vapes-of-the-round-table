# Flowermate V5.0s

Overview

The Flowermate V5.0s (also marketed as the Flowermate Vapormax V5.0S) is a portable, battery-powered dry herb vaporizer that gained popularity in the vaporizer community as an affordable entry-level device. Manufactured by Smiss Technology Co., Ltd., based in Shenzhen, China, the V5.0s was introduced around 2014 and became one of the most widely recommended budget vaporizers of its era. It uses conduction heating via a ceramic-lined chamber (sometimes described by the community as a ceramic tube-shaped oven) and is powered by an internal rechargeable battery. The device features a simple, pocket-friendly form factor and utilizes glass or rubber mouthpiece stems for vapor delivery.

The V5.0s was part of a broader Flowermate product family that included the original V5.0, the V5.0s (the "s" denoting an updated version), the V5.0s Mini, the V5 Pro, and the later V5 Nano. Though the brand eventually expanded its lineup, the V5.0s remained the most discussed and recommended model within the community for several years.

Community Reception

The Flowermate V5.0s was widely regarded within the community as one of the best budget-friendly entry points into dry herb vaporization. Its sub-$100 price point (commonly available between $60–$105 depending on the retailer and era), combined with simple three-button operation and decent build quality, made it a frequent recommendation for newcomers.

Pros identified by the community:

  • Extremely affordable, often available for under $80
  • Simple and intuitive to operate — no learning curve
  • Pocketable and reasonably stealthy
  • Glass mouthpiece stem provided acceptable vapor flavor for the price
  • Decent battery life for a budget portable
  • Ceramic chamber considered a positive material choice for the heating element

Cons identified by the community:

  • As a conduction session vape, efficiency was not on par with higher-end devices
  • Maximum temperature of 415°F was considered low by some users, leading to incomplete extraction (herb remaining "very yellowly" rather than fully spent)
  • Mouthpiece screen clogging was widely cited as the single most frustrating aspect of ownership
  • Vapor production was modest; some users reported better results from even cheaper knockoff devices
  • Concerns were raised about insulation materials used in early production units — one experienced community member later described the insulation as "horrible" in retrospect
  • Durability was a recurring topic, with at least one prolific user reporting going through three units with heavy daily use (3–5 sessions per day)
  • The glass stem could become very hot after sessions, and one user's friend reported burning themselves while removing it

By 2021, the community widely considered the Flowermate line to be "super old tech," and one knowledgeable member described prevailing retail prices as overpriced even on sale. The V5.0s was seen as having been surpassed by newer budget options, though it retained a nostalgic following among users who had started their vaporization journey with it. As one community member fondly noted, it was their "first 'good' vape" and they still loved it.

Material safety concerns: Early in the device's life, community members expressed frustration over the difficulty of obtaining a complete materials list from the manufacturer. Identifying the actual manufacturer (eventually traced to Smiss Technology Co., Ltd.) required independent research by community members. The concern about knowing exactly what materials users were inhaling through was a meaningful barrier for some health-conscious prospective buyers.

Warranty concerns: One community member reported that Flowermate refused to honor a warranty on a unit that had been won as a prize rather than purchased, which generated some negative sentiment. Another member emphasized the importance of checking warranty terms and user-replaceable parts before purchasing, as problems tended to emerge after warranty expiration.

Tips & Techniques

Loading the Chamber

  • Grind consistency: A medium-fine grind was generally recommended for best results in the conduction chamber.
  • Packing density: Community opinion was divided on whether to pack the chamber loosely or tightly. Users were encouraged to experiment, though a moderately firm pack (not overly tight) appeared to be the general preference.
  • Ceramic screen on top of the load: The single most recommended modification was placing an 8mm ceramic screen on top of the loaded herb before inserting the mouthpiece. One community member described this as "the most beneficial thing you can do to your Flowermate." The screen should be placed on top of the load before tamping it down. While some users reported it made vapor thicker, the primary and universally agreed-upon benefit was preventing herb particles from being sucked up into the mouthpiece screen, which dramatically reduced clogging — the device's most common complaint.

Stem Rotation

  • One experienced heavy user maintained a rotation of multiple glass stems soaked in isopropyl alcohol, swapping them out every couple of weeks. This kept the vapor path clean and the flavor fresh without requiring constant maintenance sessions.

Post-Session Herb Management

  • Experienced users recommended not allowing herb to cool down inside the chamber after sessions, as this could lead to the bowl becoming excessively sticky and harder to clean. Emptying the chamber promptly after use was advised.

Maximizing Extraction

  • Because the V5.0s maxed out at approximately 415°F, some users found extraction incomplete. Starting at the lowest (blue) setting for flavor and stepping up through all three temperature stages during a single session was a common technique to maximize extraction.

Temperature Guide

The V5.0s used three preset temperature levels indicated by LED color:

LED ColorTemperatureRecommended Use
Blue~385°F / 196°CFlavor-focused draws; lighter, more terpene-rich vapor; best for beginning a session
Purple~400°F / 204°CBalanced vapor production and flavor; moderate clouds
Red~415°F / 213°CMaximum extraction; thicker clouds; end-of-session finishing

Community notes on temperature:

  • The 415°F maximum was a notable limitation. Some users found that herb remained under-extracted (light yellow ABV) even after a full session at the highest setting, suggesting the actual chamber temperature may not have consistently reached the displayed maximum.
  • Stepping through all three temperatures during a session — starting at blue for flavor, progressing through purple, and finishing at red — was the most commonly recommended approach.
  • The V5 Pro variant addressed the temperature limitation somewhat by offering more granular digital temperature control.

Maintenance & Cleaning

Routine Cleaning

  • Mouthpiece screen: This was the component requiring the most frequent attention. Without a ceramic screen modification (see Tips & Techniques), the mouthpiece screen could clog within just a few sessions, significantly restricting airflow.
  • Glass stems: Soak in isopropyl alcohol (ISO) regularly. Maintaining a rotation of clean stems was recommended by heavy users. The alcohol-soaked stems could also be used to reclaim residue for later use.
  • Chamber: Clean with isopropyl alcohol and cotton swabs (Q-tips) after the chamber has cooled. Avoid allowing residue to build up, as the ceramic lining could become sticky over time.
  • Cleaning brush: The included brush was useful for clearing loose herb from the chamber after each session.

Deep Cleaning

  • Disassemble the mouthpiece components and soak all glass and metal parts in isopropyl alcohol.
  • Use pipe cleaners for the vapor path if airflow becomes restricted.
  • One user selling their unit noted they would clean it thoroughly with ISO and Q-tips before transferring it.

Cautions

  • The internal construction was not designed for easy user disassembly. One community member reported accidentally ripping the power button off the motherboard while attempting to slide the metal housing off to inspect the internals, rendering the device inoperable.

Common Issues & Fixes

Mouthpiece Screen Clogging

Problem: The most widely reported issue. Herb particles would be drawn into the mouthpiece screen during inhalation, progressively restricting airflow and degrading the experience.

Fix: Place an 8mm ceramic screen on top of the loaded herb before inserting the mouthpiece. This was described by the community as essentially eliminating the clogging problem. Regular cleaning of the mouthpiece screen with ISO was also necessary.

Insufficient Vapor Production / Incomplete Extraction

Problem: Some users reported disappointingly thin vapor and herb that remained barely toasted (yellow) after full sessions, with at least one user finding a cheaper knockoff device outperformed their V5.0s.

Fix: Ensure a proper grind and pack density. Step through all three temperature levels during a session. Use the ceramic screen trick to maintain airflow. If extraction remained poor, the unit may have had calibration issues — the community did not identify a user-serviceable fix for this.

Hot Glass Stem

Problem: The glass mouthpiece stem could become uncomfortably hot after extended sessions, and at least one user reported burns from removal.

Fix: Allow the device to cool briefly before removing the stem, or use the rubber mouthpiece as an alternative (though some users noted the rubber stem was less desirable for flavor). Handle with care.

Durability / Unit Failure

Problem: Heavy daily users (3–5 sessions per day) reported going through multiple units over time.

Fix: Keep the device charged (avoid deep discharges if possible), clean regularly to prevent residue buildup that could stress internal components, and consider warranty terms at the time of purchase. One community member advised checking what parts are user-replaceable before buying, as this could extend the device's useful life beyond the warranty period.

Internal Component Damage During Disassembly

Problem: The power button on the motherboard could be physically torn off if the metal housing was slid off carelessly.

Fix: None — this was a terminal issue. The community advised against disassembling the unit unless necessary.

Accessories & Modifications

Ceramic Screens (8mm)

The most universally recommended accessory. An 8mm ceramic screen placed on top of the herb load in the chamber prevented mouthpiece screen clogging and was considered essential by experienced users.

Glass vs. Rubber Mouthpiece Stems

The V5.0s shipped with both glass and rubber stems. The glass stem was preferred by the community for vapor quality and flavor. The rubber stem was considered more durable and practical for portability but inferior in taste. Some users maintained a rotation of multiple glass stems for convenience.

Parts Availability

Community members noted that individual components (mouthpiece with mounting screw, battery, motherboard/chamber assembly) could sometimes be sourced from users parting out damaged units. The LG-branded battery used in the V5.0s Mini was confirmed as a genuine LG cell.

Mouthpiece Thread Compatibility

A community member investigating mouthpiece thread dimensions for a DIY project confirmed that Flowermate V5 series mouthpieces were not compatible with Flowermate Nano mouthpieces, despite both being from the same manufacturer.

Water Pipe Adapter (WPA)

While WPA use was not extensively documented in the available thread data for the V5.0s specifically, the community generally encouraged running session vaporizers through water for cooler, smoother vapor.

Comparisons

Flowermate V5.0s vs. Storz & Bickel Crafty

This was framed as a "Timex vs. Rolex" comparison by the community. Both devices shared some practical similarities — pocket-friendly size, ease of use, and decent battery life — but the Crafty (at approximately 4× the price) was acknowledged as faster, more efficient, and producing higher-quality vapor. One community member noted that both would "get you there," but the Crafty did so "faster, more efficiently, and in higher style." Another member wisely cautioned that the point of diminishing returns varies from person to person, and that the value proposition depended on individual priorities and budget.

Flowermate V5.0s vs. Arizer Solo

In entry-level purchase advice discussions, the community frequently recommended the Arizer Solo as a superior alternative for users willing to spend slightly more. The Solo was praised for its stainless steel heating chamber, glass stems, excellent flavor, good battery life, reliability, and overall build quality. Multiple community members emphatically endorsed the Solo over the V5.0s, with one calling it "kind of all you'll ever need" for a first vaporizer. Used Solos could sometimes be found at prices close to the Flowermate's retail, making it a compelling upgrade.

Flowermate V5.0s vs. Boundless CF

When comparing these two budget portables, the community generally favored the Boundless CF. One member noted that while the Flowermate "was OK," it "didn't ring any bells," and recommended the Boundless as a better value, particularly since it was cheaper than the Pax 2 (which required additional accessories to perform optimally).

Flowermate V5.0s vs. DynaVap VapCap

The VapCap was frequently mentioned as an alternative in threads where the V5.0s was under consideration. As a butane-powered device with no battery concerns, extremely high efficiency, and a very low price point (~$40 for entry models), the VapCap was often recommended alongside or in place of the Flowermate — particularly for users prioritizing efficiency and durability. The main caveat was that the VapCap required a torch lighter, which could be impractical in certain situations (e.g., air travel, areas where quality lighters were hard to source).

Within the Flowermate Family

  • V5.0s vs. V5 Pro: The Pro added digital temperature control for finer adjustment, addressing one of the V5.0s's main limitations (only three preset temperatures).
  • V5.0s vs. V5.0s Mini: Smaller form factor but same basic functionality; used a smaller battery.
  • V5.0s vs. V5 Nano: The Nano was a later design with a user-replaceable 18650 battery, which was considered a significant improvement for longevity. However, mouthpiece parts were not cross-compatible between the V5 series and the Nano.

---

This article is based on community discussions spanning from 2014 to 2021, drawn from approximately 796 posts across 14 discussion threads in a popular vaporizer community forum. The Flowermate V5.0s holds a significant place in the community's history as one of the devices that helped make dry herb vaporization accessible to budget-conscious newcomers during the mid-2010s.
