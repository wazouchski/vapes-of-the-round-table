# Firefly 2

Overview

The Firefly 2 is a portable, on-demand convection dry herb vaporizer manufactured by Firefly Vapor, released in 2016 as the successor to the original Firefly (Firefly 1). The device is battery-powered and employs a dynamic convection heating system that activates when the user touches capacitive sensors on the exterior of the unit. It is designed for both dry herb and concentrate use. The Firefly 2 was widely recognized within the vaporizer community for delivering exceptionally flavorful, cool vapor, though it was also noted for having a significant learning curve and polarizing usability characteristics.

The original Firefly (sometimes referred to as the FF1) was released circa 2013–2014 at a retail price of approximately $300. The Firefly 2 was positioned as a premium portable vaporizer, retailing at roughly $330 at launch, and was available in several color options including black, blue, and dark grey. A later revision, the Firefly 2+, was also released with incremental improvements, though the threads analyzed here primarily concern the Firefly 2.

Community Reception

The Firefly 2 occupied a distinctive and somewhat polarizing position within the vaporizer community. It was widely praised for producing what many users considered the best-tasting vapor available from any portable device. One community member who sold their unit shortly after purchase described it plainly: "If you're after the best tasting vapor that money can buy, this is pretty much it from my experience. The Firefly 2 delivers the highest quality vapor that I've ever had." The vapor was consistently described as smooth, cool, and exceptionally flavorful, particularly on the first few draws of a fresh bowl.

Pros:

  • Flavor quality: Near-universal agreement that the Firefly 2 was among the best-tasting portable vaporizers available, frequently cited as a top recommendation when taste was the primary criterion.
  • On-demand heating: The near-instant heat-up time made it well-suited for single draws or micro-dosing sessions rather than extended session vaping.
  • Concentrate performance: Several community members praised the Firefly 2 highly for concentrate use. One member described the device as "magnificent for concentrates," rating it above the Ghost MV1 and other convection portables for this purpose.
  • Removable battery: The swappable battery design was appreciated, though the battery life itself was not.
  • Material safety: The glass-lined bowl and relatively inert vapor path were valued by health-conscious users.
  • Appeal to transitioning smokers: The Firefly (both versions) was noted as a good device for converting combustion users, with one community member describing the original as "what I would use to session with anybody; combusters love it."

Cons:

  • Learning curve: A persistent theme across discussions. The technique-dependent nature of the device meant that satisfying results required practice with draw speed, lip positioning, and bowl stirring.
  • Battery life: Short battery life was one of the most frequently cited complaints. One user who tracked their usage over months noted having to monitor battery status carefully to avoid mid-session depletion.
  • Cleaning demands: Multiple community members described the Firefly 2 as high-maintenance. One longtime owner of the original Firefly noted it was "always a PITA to keep clean."
  • Price: At approximately $330 retail, the Firefly 2 was considered expensive, particularly given its limitations. Multiple users in purchase advice threads noted the price as a barrier, with some suggesting alternatives that offered better value.
  • Vapor density: Users who preferred thick, lung-filling hits found the Firefly 2 underwhelming. One seller noted they preferred "the hotter, lung-busting hits" from devices like the Grasshopper and Underdog.
  • Stealth: Despite its portable nature, the Firefly 2 was not considered especially stealthy. One community member noted it was "not too stealthy" compared to slimmer devices.
  • Counterfeit risk: The original Firefly was plagued by counterfeit units in the secondhand market. Community members advised verifying authenticity through original receipts and packaging when purchasing used.

The overall community consensus was that the Firefly 2 was an excellent flavor vaporizer and a capable concentrate device, but its short battery life, learning curve, and maintenance requirements limited its appeal as an everyday portable for many users. It was often recommended specifically to flavor-focused users rather than as a general-purpose portable.

Tips & Techniques

The Firefly 2's on-demand convection design requires specific technique to achieve optimal results:

  • Loading: Use a relatively loose, coarse-to-medium grind. The convection airflow needs to pass through the herb effectively. Overpacking reduces performance. Loads of approximately 0.1g were found to be effective for solo use.
  • Draw technique: Slow, steady sipping draws were generally recommended rather than hard lung pulls. The convection heating system responds to airflow, and drawing too hard can cool the heater and reduce vapor production.
  • Bowl stirring: Between draws, stirring or flipping the herb in the bowl was necessary to ensure even extraction. Without stirring, the top layer nearest the heater tends to over-extract while lower material remains underused.
  • Concentrate use: Community members found that using the included stainless steel concentrate pads was effective. One technique involved sandwiching concentrates between two pads for improved convection dabbing.
  • Battery management: Given the limited battery life, users who tracked their sessions carefully found that monitoring pull count and session duration helped prevent unexpected battery depletion mid-session.
  • ABV collection: The Firefly 2 was noted to produce lighter-colored ABV (already been vaped) material compared to conduction devices like the Pax, making it suitable for saving and repurposing ABV for edibles such as infused milk.

Temperature Guide

Temperature control on the Firefly 2 is managed through the companion smartphone app rather than physical controls on the device.

Temperature RangeEffect Profile
160–180°C / 320–356°FMaximum flavor extraction; light wispy vapor; ideal for tasting terpene profiles
180–200°C / 356–392°FBalanced flavor and vapor production; good starting point for most users
200–210°C / 392–410°FIncreased vapor density; suitable for users seeking sedative effects (e.g., sleep use); higher extraction of cannabinoids like CBN
210–240°C / 410–464°FMaximum extraction; dark ABV; significantly reduced flavor; approaching combustion risk at the upper end

Community members noted that the Firefly 2 was capable of reaching temperatures of at least 240°C / 464°F, though one community member cautioned that temperatures above approximately 230°C / 446°F approach the combustion threshold and should be used with care. For users seeking sedative effects (such as sleep aid), temperatures of 200–205°C / 392–401°F were discussed as the range where more sedative compounds are released, though community members emphasized that strain selection (indica-dominant) mattered more than temperature alone.

Maintenance & Cleaning

The Firefly 2 was consistently described as requiring regular and somewhat demanding maintenance:

  • Bowl cleaning: Wipe the borosilicate glass bowl with isopropyl alcohol (ISO) and cotton swabs after sessions. Residue builds up on the glass window and the area around the heater.
  • Lid and mouthpiece: The removable lid/mouthpiece should be cleaned regularly. Resin buildup affects both vapor flavor and the seal quality.
  • Screens: Replace screens periodically. The Firefly 2 comes with extra screens, and replacements were available from the manufacturer.
  • Heater and gaskets: The internal heater and gaskets are serviceable. Community members noted that Firefly's warranty service would replace the glass, heater, and all gaskets when units were sent in for repair — effectively providing refurbished internals.
  • Frequency: For daily users, cleaning after every few sessions was recommended to maintain optimal flavor. The device's reputation for excellent taste is partly contingent on keeping the vapor path clean.

One longtime Firefly 1 owner summarized the cleaning situation bluntly, noting it was "always a PITA to keep clean" — a sentiment that carried over to the Firefly 2.

Common Issues & Fixes

Short Battery Life:

The most commonly reported issue. The 770 mAh battery provides a limited number of sessions per charge. The solution was to purchase spare batteries and keep them charged. Users who tracked their consumption found that monitoring session count was essential for avoiding unexpected battery death.

Learning Curve / Inconsistent Hits:

New users frequently reported difficulty getting satisfying vapor. The fix involved refining draw technique (slower, steady sipping), proper loading (loose, not overpacked), and regular bowl stirring between draws.

Counterfeit Units (Firefly 1):

The original Firefly had a known counterfeit problem. Community members strongly recommended verifying authenticity through original purchase receipts, packaging, and serial numbers when buying secondhand.

Glass Breakage:

The borosilicate glass bowl window could crack or break. The manufacturer offered repair service that included glass replacement along with new heater and gaskets.

Seal Degradation:

Over time, gaskets could wear, affecting the quality of the draw. Replacement gaskets were available and were included when units were serviced by the manufacturer.

Accessories & Modifications

  • Concentrate Pads: Stainless steel pads included with the device allow for concentrate vaporization. Community members found that using two pads sandwiching the concentrate improved the convection dabbing experience.
  • Extra Batteries: 770 mAh replacement batteries were available and considered nearly essential given the device's limited battery life.
  • Extra Mouthpieces: Spare mouthpieces could be purchased. Having a clean spare on hand simplified the rotation for users who cleaned between sessions.
  • Screens: Replacement screens were readily available and recommended as a regular consumable.
  • Water Pipe Adapter (WPA): While the Firefly 2 was discussed in the context of water filtration setups, it was not as commonly paired with water tools as some other devices. Community discussions about portable vaporizers and water filtration tended to recommend the Arizer Air with GonG adapters or the Grasshopper over the Firefly 2 for WPA use. The Firefly 2's rapid heat-up was noted as potentially suitable for on-demand hits through water, but dedicated WPA solutions were not as widely discussed for this device.
  • Carrying Case: A soft carrying case was included with the original retail package.

Comparisons

Firefly 2 vs. Arizer Solo

A frequently discussed comparison, particularly for users prioritizing taste versus battery life. The community consensus was that the Firefly produced better-tasting vapor than the Solo, but the Solo offered approximately six times the battery life. For users on a budget of $100–180, the Solo was overwhelmingly recommended as the better value. The Firefly was recommended only when flavor was the absolute top priority and budget was not a constraint.

Firefly 2 vs. Pax 3

These two devices served different use cases. The Pax 3 was a conduction session vaporizer praised for its stealth factor, solid construction, and superior battery life, but criticized for its large bowl, cleaning demands, and lingering smell. The Firefly 2 offered on-demand convection with superior flavor but worse battery life and stealth. One community member who had owned both the Firefly 2 and Pax 3 expressed a preference for the Pax 3 as an overall daily portable.

Firefly 2 vs. Grasshopper

Both were on-demand portable vaporizers, but the Grasshopper produced denser, hotter vapor while the Firefly 2 excelled in cool, flavorful vapor. Users who preferred intense, "lung-busting hits" gravitated toward the Grasshopper, while flavor-focused users preferred the Firefly 2. The Grasshopper's well-documented reliability issues and its pen-like form factor differentiated it as the stealthier option.

Firefly 2 vs. Ghost MV1

For concentrate use specifically, the Firefly 2 was rated notably higher than the Ghost MV1. One community member who owned both stated plainly that the Ghost did not produce good results with concentrates, while the Firefly 2 was "magnificent" for that purpose. For dry herb, the Ghost MV1 was discussed as a potential alternative offering cool vapor and on-demand heating with a removable crucible system.

Firefly 2 vs. Firewood 4/5

The Firewood series was discussed alongside the Firefly 2 in on-demand portable comparisons. The Firewood 4 offered replaceable 18650 batteries (a significant advantage), while the Firefly 2 used proprietary smaller batteries. In stealth-focused discussions, the Firewood was sometimes preferred, though both devices shared the characteristic of requiring technique to master.

Firefly 1 vs. Firefly 2

The original Firefly was larger and heavier, described as having a "new age pipe style" form factor. It used the same 770 mAh removable battery. Community members who owned the original generally acknowledged the Firefly 2 as an improvement but noted that many of the same strengths (flavor quality) and weaknesses (battery life, cleaning demands) persisted across both generations. The original Firefly was notably plagued by counterfeit units on the secondhand market, which was less of a concern with the Firefly 2. Used Firefly 1 units were considered poor value at prices around $90 by some community members, while others appreciated them as reliable on-demand convection vapes.

Firefly 2 for Hash

The Firefly 2 was occasionally asked about for hash vaporization, but the community provided limited guidance on this specific use case. For traditional non-full-melt hash (particularly European-style pressed hash), the VapCap was more frequently recommended. The MFLB was also mentioned as effective for hash. Community feedback on hash in the Firefly 2 was sparse enough that no definitive consensus emerged.
