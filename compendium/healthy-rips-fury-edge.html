# Healthy Rips Fury Edge

Overview

The Healthy Rips Fury Edge is a compact, battery-powered portable dry herb vaporizer released in mid-2019. It is the direct successor to the popular Fury 2, manufactured by Healthy Rips, a company recognized within the vaporizer community for iterating on and improving its product lineup. The Fury Edge utilizes a hybrid heating system (combining conduction and convection elements) and features a USB-C rechargeable internal battery. It was widely regarded within the community as one of the best entry-level to mid-range portable vaporizers available at its price point, praised for its ease of use, compact form factor, and broad accessory compatibility.

The Edge was announced in the summer of 2019, generating considerable anticipation among existing Fury 2 and POTV One owners. Community discussion noted that Healthy Rips was "constantly improving their products," and the Edge represented a meaningful step forward in battery life and build quality over its predecessor.

Community Reception

The community regarded the Fury Edge as a reliable, approachable, and versatile portable vaporizer — particularly well-suited for newcomers transitioning away from combustion. One community member selling a used unit described it as a "reliable, easy to use vape, great for newly reformed combusters," while another called it their "beloved entry to the vape world" before upgrading to a Storz & Bickel Mighty.

Pros frequently cited by the community:

  • Ease of use: The Edge was consistently highlighted as requiring no specific technique to get satisfying results, making it approachable for users and their partners or friends.
  • Smooth vapor: Community members recommended the Edge to users seeking a vaporizer that is easy on the lungs, noting that it produced smooth vapor, especially when paired with a water pipe adapter or glass stem.
  • Accessory ecosystem: The wide range of compatible accessories — dosing capsules, WPA, bent glass mouthpieces, 3D flow stems, and third-party 3D-printed accessories like loading funnels — was frequently praised.
  • Improved battery life: The upgrade to a 2300 mAh battery from the Fury 2's smaller cell was well-received, though some users still desired even more capacity.
  • Compact and stealthy form factor: Community members described the Edge as one of the best stealthy, on-the-go portables available alongside its siblings (Fury 2, POTV One).
  • Maintenance simplicity: With dosing capsules, the Edge was described as "as close to a maintenance-free vape as I've found."
  • Improved airflow screens: Community discussion highlighted new mesh-style screens on the Edge that offered better airflow compared to the Fury 2 and the POTV Snap, where draw resistance had been a noted concern.

Cons and criticisms:

  • No removable battery: This was the single most frequently cited drawback. One community member noted, "almost perfect but still no removable battery," expressing hope that a future revision would address this. The non-replaceable battery meant the device had a finite lifespan, a significant concern for long-term ownership.
  • Flavor rated as good but not top-tier: While vapor quality was praised as smooth, community members described flavor as "definitely good" but not "great," with one noting it was "in no way BAD, it just isn't top tier flavor." This was a common point of nuance — acceptable for most users, but connoisseurs seeking the very best flavor were sometimes directed toward other options.
  • Temperature drop during hits: One community member who also owned the Healthy Rips Fierce reported temperature drops of up to 30°F during draws on that device, and expressed concern that the Edge might share a similar issue. This was noted as a potential limitation of the Healthy Rips heating platform, though community reports suggested the Edge handled this somewhat better at its smaller chamber size.
  • Session-style only: The auto-shutoff timer and session-oriented heating design meant the Edge was not ideal for on-demand use cases.

Overall, the community widely agreed that the Fury Edge occupied an excellent position in the market for users seeking a compact, easy-to-use, affordable portable vaporizer with broad accessory support.

Tips & Techniques

Community members shared a number of practical tips for getting the most out of the Fury Edge:

  • Use dosing capsules for convenience and cleanliness: Dosing capsules were strongly recommended for keeping the chamber clean and making reloading quick. The capsules are pre-loadable, allowing users to carry several for outings. One community member modified capsules with stainless steel mesh and screens for microdosing, enabling very small loads.
  • Grind medium to fine: A consistent, medium-fine grind was generally recommended for optimal extraction with the hybrid heating system. Overly coarse grinds could lead to uneven heating.
  • Do not over-pack the chamber: A light to moderate pack, without tamping down too firmly, was preferred. This allowed for adequate airflow through the chamber, which is critical for the convection component of the heating system.
  • Use a loading funnel: At least one community member used a 3D-printed loading funnel to fill the small chamber more cleanly and efficiently, suggesting this was a worthwhile accessory.
  • Draw technique: Slow to moderate draws were recommended to maximize the convection heating effect. Fast, hard draws could cool the heater and reduce vapor density.
  • Start low, finish high: Beginning a session at lower temperatures for flavor and gradually increasing toward the end for fuller extraction was a commonly suggested technique.

Community members who were sensitive to harsh vapor were advised to use the lower-to-mid ranges and pair the device with a water pipe adapter or longer glass stem for additional cooling.

Maintenance & Cleaning

The Fury Edge was praised for being easy to maintain, especially when dosing capsules were used regularly. Key maintenance practices discussed in the community:

  • Chamber cleaning: After sessions (or after several sessions with dosing capsules), brush out the chamber with the included cleaning brush while the device is still slightly warm. This makes it easier to remove residue.
  • Screen maintenance: The mesh-style screens should be periodically checked for clogging. They can be soaked in isopropyl alcohol (ISO) and rinsed clean. The improved mesh screens on the Edge were noted to provide better airflow than earlier Fury 2 screens.
  • Mouthpiece and glass stem cleaning: Soak glass mouthpieces, WPAs, and 3D flow stems in isopropyl alcohol. Reclaim from the stems can be saved for edibles or other uses ("ABV" and reclaim harvesting were common practices in the community).
  • Dosing capsules: Soak in ISO, scrub gently, and air dry. Regular cleaning prevents buildup that can increase draw resistance.
  • General body: Wipe down the exterior with a slightly damp cloth. Avoid getting liquid into the charging port or air path openings.
  • Frequency: The community generally recommended a quick brush-out after each session and a more thorough ISO cleaning every one to two weeks depending on usage frequency.

Common Issues & Fixes

IssueDescription & Solution
Draw resistance increasing over timeUsually caused by clogged screens or residue buildup in the air path. Replacing or cleaning screens and soaking the mouthpiece in ISO typically resolved this. The upgraded mesh screens on the Edge were noted to be an improvement in this regard.
Temperature drop during drawsSome users noted the displayed temperature could drop during longer or harder draws. This was a known characteristic of the Healthy Rips heating platform. Slower draw speed and allowing brief pauses between hits helped mitigate this.
Battery degradationBecause the battery is not removable, long-term capacity loss was a concern. The community recommended standard lithium-ion best practices: avoid leaving the device on the charger indefinitely, don't fully deplete the battery before charging when possible, and store in moderate temperatures.
Dosing capsule lids lost or damagedThe small capsule lids were easy to misplace. Community members recommended purchasing extra capsule sets and keeping spares on hand.
Small chamber difficult to loadThe relatively small chamber opening could make loading messy. A 3D-printed loading funnel was mentioned as a practical solution.

Accessories & Modifications

The Fury Edge's accessory ecosystem was one of its most celebrated features. Key accessories discussed in the community:

  • Dosing Capsules: Stainless steel capsules that fit in the chamber for cleaner use and faster reloading. Cross-compatible with the Fury 2 and POTV One. Community members modified capsules with stainless steel mesh for microdosing.
  • Water Pipe Adapter (WPA): A popular accessory that allowed the Edge to be used with standard 14mm or 18mm water pieces. This was highly recommended for users seeking the smoothest possible vapor, especially those sensitive to throat irritation.
  • Glass Bent Mouthpiece: A curved glass stem that replaced the standard silicone mouthpiece, offering improved flavor and a cooler vapor path.
  • 3D Flow Stem: A glass stem with internal "turbine" or flow-disrupting structures designed to cool and condition the vapor. Available in clear glass.
  • Mesh Screens: Improved mesh-style screens sold separately (available from retailers like Planet of the Vapes). These were noted to provide better airflow than the stock screens in older Fury models.
  • 3D-Printed Loading Funnel: A third-party accessory that fit over the chamber opening to simplify and speed up loading.
  • Carrying Cases and Accessories: Various aftermarket cases and storage solutions were used by community members for portability.

Comparisons

Fury Edge vs. Fury 2

The Edge was positioned as a direct upgrade to the Fury 2. The primary improvements were a significantly larger battery (2300 mAh vs. ~1600 mAh), USB-C charging, improved mesh screens for better airflow, and a slightly refined form factor. Dosing capsules and screens were cross-compatible between the two devices. Community members generally agreed the Edge was the better buy for new purchasers, though Fury 2 owners debated whether the upgrade was essential.

Fury Edge vs. POTV One

The Planet of the Vapes One shares the same basic platform as the Fury 2 (both were produced from a common design lineage). One community member noted that the POTV One did not suffer from temperature drops during hits as much as the Healthy Rips Fierce, suggesting possible firmware or heater control differences between the branded variants. The community generally considered the Edge, Fury 2, and POTV One as siblings — the Edge being the most refined of the group, with the best battery life.

Fury Edge vs. Storz & Bickel Crafty+

The Crafty+ was frequently compared to the Edge in purchase advice threads. Users seeking the smoothest vapor and best flavor were often steered toward the Crafty+, particularly when budget was less of a concern. One community member leaning toward the Crafty+ was told the Edge "checks off most boxes" but acknowledged that flavor, while good, was not top-tier. The Crafty+ commanded a significantly higher price, making the Edge the preferred recommendation for budget-conscious users.

Fury Edge vs. Storz & Bickel Mighty

At least one community member described the Edge as their entry vaporizer before upgrading to the Mighty, suggesting the Edge served as an effective stepping stone for users who eventually wanted a larger, higher-performing portable. The Mighty offered longer battery life, a larger chamber, and was generally considered to produce superior vapor quality, but was also substantially larger and more expensive.

Fury Edge vs. Pax 3

While direct head-to-head comparisons were limited in the available discussion, the two devices appeared together in classified listings, suggesting community members viewed them as occupying a similar market segment. The community generally favored the Edge over the Pax 3 for vapor quality and value, while the Pax 3 was acknowledged for its sleek design and app integration.

Fury Edge vs. 510 Vapes (Splinter Z, etc.)

For home use, some community members suggested that 510 thread vaporizers (such as the Splinter Z) could offer superior flavor and on-demand performance compared to the Edge. However, the Edge was preferred for its simplicity and portability, with 510 vapes recommended more for users comfortable with box mod settings and wattage tuning.
