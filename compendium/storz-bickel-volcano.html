# Storz & Bickel Volcano

Overview

The Storz & Bickel Volcano is a desktop balloon-style (bag) vaporizer widely considered one of the most iconic and enduring devices in the dry herb vaporizer market. Manufactured by the German company Storz & Bickel, the Volcano was first introduced in 2000 with the Volcano Classic, followed by the Volcano Digit (also known as the Volcano Digital) featuring a digital temperature display. A further update, the Volcano Hybrid, was released in 2019, adding whip functionality and app control alongside the traditional bag system.

The Volcano uses a forced-air convection heating system: an internal fan pushes hot air through a chamber of dry herb, filling a detachable plastic balloon (bag) with vapor. The bag is then removed from the unit and inhaled at the user's leisure. It is a mains-powered (AC plug-in) desktop unit and is not portable.

The Volcano has long been regarded by the vaporizer community as a benchmark device — often the first "real" vaporizer many enthusiasts encountered, and frequently the standard against which other desktop units were measured. As one community member recounted, their first encounter with a vaporizer was "an OG Volcano at a dealer's house in like 2003," describing the experience of watching the fan inflate a bag as something that "taken all the romance out of" traditional smoking — a testament to the device's novelty and impact on the culture.

Community Reception

The Volcano has long been regarded as the gold standard for desktop bag vaporizers within the vaporizer community. Its reputation for reliability and build quality was a consistent theme across years of discussion. One community member noted they had replaced a Volcano after ten years of use, describing the units as "built like tanks" that "work beautifully." Another member described purchasing a used Volcano Classic at a thrift store for a fraction of its retail price, only to find it fully functional — a testament to the device's remarkable longevity.

Strengths

  • Group use excellence: The Volcano was widely praised as one of the best vaporizers for group sessions. As one owner explained, "anytime it's more than just me and the girl, I barely ever use anything else." The ability to fill bags and pass them around made the Volcano the go-to device for social settings. Users maintained both small and large bag setups depending on group size.
  • Durability and longevity: Multi-year — and even decade-long — lifespans were commonly reported. The device was frequently recommended as a long-term investment.
  • Ease of use for groups: The bag-passing system was seen as intuitive and analogous to the familiar "puff puff pass" culture, making it accessible even to people unfamiliar with vaporizers.
  • Conversion power: Multiple community accounts describe the Volcano as the device that converted skeptics. One user reported that a partner who "preferred bongs because the vapes she's tried were too weak" became a convert after experiencing bag vapor from a Volcano-style setup.

Weaknesses

  • Solo use inefficiency: A recurring theme in community discussion was that the Volcano was not the most efficient choice for solo sessions. One owner stated plainly, "As a Volcano owner, I don't use it for just me unless I'm trying to medicate a lot in a short span of time." The chamber size and bag system were often considered excessive for single users.
  • Herb consumption: Some users felt the Volcano used more herb per session compared to other desktop units. One owner who also had an Arizer Extreme Q noted that the Volcano "has been pretty much gathering dust" because the Extreme Q used less material and offered more flexibility (e.g., glass compatibility).
  • Lack of glass connectivity: Unlike whip-style desktops, the Classic and Digit models did not natively connect to water pipes or glass pieces, which became an increasingly important feature as the community's preferences evolved. Users who valued glass connectivity often gravitated toward alternatives.
  • Cost: At retail prices typically ranging from $400–$600 USD (depending on model and era), the Volcano represented a significant investment. This made thrift-store finds — sometimes for as little as $7–$10 — notable community events.
  • Vulnerability to physical damage: While the unit itself is robust, the power cable was identified as a weak point. At least one community member reported their Volcano broke after "tripping on the cable," requiring it to be sent back to Storz & Bickel for repair.

Tips & Techniques

Loading the Chamber

  • Grind herb finely for optimal convection extraction. A consistent, medium-fine grind allows the forced air to pass evenly through the material.
  • Don't over-pack: While the chamber can hold a substantial amount of herb, the community found that moderate loads extracted more evenly. Overpacking can restrict airflow and lead to uneven heating.
  • Use the liquid pad as a chamber reducer: For solo sessions with smaller amounts of herb, placing the included Storz & Bickel liquid pad on top of a smaller load can fill dead space in the chamber and improve extraction efficiency.

Bag Technique

  • Multiple bag fills per chamber: A single chamber load can produce several bags of vapor. Start at lower temperatures and increase incrementally between bags to extract the full spectrum of active compounds.
  • Don't let filled bags sit too long: Vapor in a bag begins to condense and lose potency within minutes. The community generally recommended inhaling a filled bag promptly after filling.
  • Adjust bag size to group size: Owners commonly maintained multiple bag lengths. Shorter bags for solo or paired sessions reduce waste; longer bags (or even the maximum turkey-bag-sized lengths) were preferred for groups.

Solid Valve vs. Easy Valve

Community discussion revealed nuanced opinions on this choice:

  • The Easy Valve was designed for maximum simplicity (pre-made, disposable bags) but was noted by experienced users to be "far more prone to vapor leakage." One member described its construction as "just a tube that pushes through a rubber membrane with 4 slits that open up allowing the mouth tube to go into the bag area."
  • The Solid Valve offered more customization (adjustable bag lengths, reusable components, and a tighter seal) and was not considered complicated despite its name. One owner specifically cautioned newcomers: "Don't discredit the Solid Valve as complicated... it's not."

Temperature Guide

The Volcano's convection heating and large air volume mean that temperature selection has a pronounced effect on vapor character. While exact preferences were personal, the community generally followed these guidelines:

A common technique discussed in the community was temperature stepping: starting a fresh load at around 170°C (338°F) for a flavorful first bag, then increasing by 5–10°C with each subsequent bag fill to fully extract the material.

One community member who acquired a Volcano Digital noted they were still working on dialing in technique to achieve "bags that thick," suggesting that mastering temperature selection and packing technique is an ongoing learning process even for experienced users.

Maintenance & Cleaning

Regular Maintenance

  • Screens: The chamber screens should be cleaned or replaced regularly. Clogged screens restrict airflow and reduce vapor quality.
  • Chamber/filling chamber: Wipe down the chamber area with isopropyl alcohol (90%+ recommended) and allow to fully dry before use.
  • Valve components: Both Easy and Solid Valve parts accumulate resin over time. Soak removable components in isopropyl alcohol periodically.
  • Bags: Replace bags when they become visibly discolored or stale-tasting. Easy Valve bags are designed for periodic replacement; Solid Valve users can cut new bags from oven-safe bag material.

Deep Cleaning

  • Disassemble valve components fully and soak in isopropyl alcohol for 20–30 minutes.
  • Use pipe cleaners or cotton swabs for hard-to-reach areas.
  • Ensure all components are completely dry and free of alcohol residue before reassembly.

Common Issues & Fixes

Power Cable Vulnerability

The Volcano's power cable connects at the rear of the unit, and tripping over it can damage the connection or the unit. Community members advised placing the Volcano in a stable, out-of-the-way location and managing cable routing carefully.

Vapor Leakage (Easy Valve)

As noted by community members, the Easy Valve system can allow vapor to escape due to its rubber membrane design. Users who experienced this issue were often advised to switch to the Solid Valve system or to ensure the mouthpiece was fully and firmly seated.

Reduced Vapor Production Over Time

Usually attributable to dirty screens or a chamber in need of cleaning. Regular maintenance resolves this in most cases.

Unit Sent for Repair

Storz & Bickel offers repair services for damaged units. At least one community member reported sending a broken Volcano to S&B and waiting for repair. The community generally viewed S&B's warranty and service favorably, though repair turnaround time could be lengthy, especially for international customers.

Accessories & Modifications

Valve Systems

  • Easy Valve Starter Set: Includes pre-made bags, simpler to set up but less customizable.
  • Solid Valve: Allows custom bag sizes and offers a tighter seal. Community consensus leaned toward the Solid Valve for experienced users.

Aftermarket Screens and Parts

There was community interest in aftermarket screen alternatives — particularly cheaper and more durable options than official Storz & Bickel replacement parts. While most discussion of aftermarket screens centered on the VapeXhale Cloud ELB and Arizer Extreme Q dome screens, some users explored whether Extreme Q screens could be modified for use with Volcano accessories. Official S&B parts were generally considered high quality but expensive.

CamelBak Mouthpieces

A widely shared community tip involved using CamelBak replacement mouthpieces, which fit over the 10mm glass connection on bags. These served dual purposes: preventing vapor from escaping from a filled bag and enabling hands-free use when connected to whip-style setups.

Obsidian Water Filter (Hybrid)

For the later Volcano Hybrid model, the Obsidian water-filtration attachment became a popular accessory, adding water cooling directly atop the chamber before the bag.

Comparisons

Volcano vs. Arizer Extreme Q

This was one of the most frequently discussed comparisons in the community. Users who owned both devices often noted:

  • The Extreme Q was significantly cheaper and offered greater versatility (bags, whip, and direct glass connectivity). One user reported their Volcano "gathering dust" after acquiring an Extreme Q due to the Q's ability to connect to glass pieces.
  • The Volcano was considered superior for bag-filling performance, build quality, and group sessions.
  • The Extreme Q was considered more herb-efficient for solo use, while the Volcano excelled in shared sessions.
  • Users seeking to reduce daily herb consumption (e.g., from ~1g/day) often explored alternatives to the Volcano for their solo sessions while keeping the Volcano for social use.

Volcano vs. VapeXhale Cloud EVO

The VapeXhale Cloud EVO was recommended by community members seeking a desktop unit that used "tiny amounts" of herb with "high flavor" and integrated directly with glass. The Cloud EVO's all-glass vapor path and ELB (Essential Load Bowl) system offered a very different experience from the Volcano's bag system. Some users pre-ordered the Cloud EVO specifically as a complement to their Volcano.

Volcano vs. Portable Vaporizers

The Volcano was rarely compared directly to portables, as it occupied a fundamentally different use case. However, the community acknowledged that modern high-performance portables (such as the Storz & Bickel Mighty) had narrowed the gap for solo use, making the Volcano's primary remaining advantage its unmatched group session performance. One user who experienced the loss of both a Mighty and a Volcano within a short period highlighted how the two devices served complementary roles in a vaporizer collection.
