# Cloud

Overview

The Cloud is a desktop dry herb vaporizer manufactured by VapeXhale (sometimes humorously referred to as "FakeXhale" by some community members when discussing reliability issues). The Cloud was the original flagship product from VapeXhale, preceding the more widely known Cloud EVO. It is a convection-based desktop unit that heats herb by passing hot air through a bamboo heating chamber. The vaporizer is powered by mains electricity and features a PID (Proportional-Integral-Derivative) temperature controller with an analog dial for temperature adjustment. The Cloud was designed to deliver dense, flavorful vapor and was compatible with VapeXhale's proprietary glass accessories, including the HydraCirc water filtration attachment.

The Cloud represented an early attempt at a high-performance all-convection desktop vaporizer and attracted a dedicated following within the vaporizer community, though it also developed a reputation for certain reliability concerns over time.

Community Reception

The community regarded the Cloud as a capable convection desktop vaporizer that could produce dense, flavorful vapor — qualities that earned it a loyal user base. Members who owned the Cloud often praised its hit quality, with one user noting it was part of their regular rotation alongside other high-end desktop units such as the EVO and log vaporizers.

However, the Cloud also attracted criticism for its reliability and build quality. The bamboo heating section was known to crack, and the internal thermocouple wiring was prone to failure, leading to error indicators. The non-transferable warranty was a notable drawback flagged by community members, making secondhand purchases riskier. One experienced community member explicitly warned prospective buyers in a classifieds thread that "the warranty is not transferrable," suggesting this was an important consideration.

The Cloud was ultimately succeeded by the Cloud EVO, which addressed many of the original's shortcomings and became VapeXhale's more recognized product.

Pros (as discussed by the community):

  • Excellent convection vapor quality and flavor
  • Dense, lung-filling hits
  • Compatible with HydraCirc water filtration glass
  • Capable of producing visible vapor clouds

Cons (as discussed by the community):

  • Reliability issues (cracked bamboo, thermocouple wire failure)
  • Non-transferable warranty
  • Internal repairs required soldering skill
  • Replacement parts not readily available from manufacturer

Tips & Techniques

Community members shared several usage techniques for the Cloud:

  • Screen cups were the standard loading method. The Cloud used proprietary screen cups to hold herb in the airpath. Keeping spare screen cups on hand was recommended — users sometimes had six or more extras available.
  • Dial position: One community member who performed a heater repair found that keeping the temperature dial at approximately the noon (12 o'clock) position produced "nice dark straw" colored ABV (Already Been Vaped material), indicating efficient extraction without combustion. However, this specific guidance was in the context of a modified unit and may not apply identically to stock Clouds.
  • Draw technique: As a convection vaporizer, the Cloud required the user to draw air through the herb to generate heat transfer. Slow, steady draws were generally recommended for convection units of this era to allow sufficient heat exchange.
  • Tolerance management: One experienced community member noted that the Cloud (and its successor, the EVO) could "completely destroy" a user's tolerance due to the efficiency and density of the hits. Some users found benefit in rotating between the Cloud and smaller vaporizers (such as log vapes) to manage their consumption.

Temperature Guide

The Cloud used an analog dial rather than a digital temperature display, so precise temperature recommendations were not commonly shared in the same manner as with digitally controlled units. Community guidance was expressed in terms of dial position rather than degrees:

Dial PositionEffectNotes

Around noon (12 o'clock)Balanced extractionReported to produce "dark straw" colored ABV; good balance of flavor and potency

Note on modified units: One community member who replaced the stock ceramic heater with an aftermarket coil-style heater reported that the dial sensitivity was "almost non-existent" after the modification, with usable temperatures concentrated in a narrow range. This indicates the PID controller was tuned for the specific characteristics of the original heater.

Maintenance & Cleaning

  • Screen cups: Should be cleaned regularly. Visible resin buildup on the glass joint and around the bamboo section was observed even after moderate use. One community member expressed skepticism that a unit with heavy resin buildup around the joint had been used only "4-5 sessions," suggesting that resin accumulation was a reliable indicator of usage.
  • Glass joint area: The joint where the glass accessories (such as the HydraCirc) connect to the unit is prone to resin accumulation and should be cleaned periodically with isopropyl alcohol.
  • Internal components: The thermocouple wires beneath the white ceramic heater should be inspected periodically, as they are prone to coming loose from the circuit board over time. This is a more advanced maintenance task that requires opening the unit.

Common Issues & Fixes

Flashing Red Error Light

Symptom: The Cloud's LED indicator flashes red and the unit does not heat properly.

Cause: The most commonly reported cause was the thermocouple wires becoming disconnected from the circuit board. As one knowledgeable community member explained: "You want to make sure the thermocouple wires are still soldered to the board — if they come loose it will flash red."

Fix: Re-solder the thermocouple wires to the circuit board. However, community members noted that "solder doesn't like to stick to thermocouple wires," making this a somewhat tricky repair. Proper flux and technique are essential.

Cracked Bamboo

Symptom: The bamboo heating section develops a visible crack, potentially affecting heat retention and airpath integrity.

Cause: Thermal cycling and the natural properties of bamboo as a material.

Fix: This was a known failure point. Community discussion indicated that this was not always the cause of unit failure — members were careful to distinguish between bamboo cracks and electronic/thermocouple issues when diagnosing problems.

Disassembly Tips

Community members shared the following guidance for opening the Cloud for repair:

Remove the temperature dial knob — it pulls or unscrews off.

Unthread the nut behind the knob to free the front panel.

Desolder either a wire or the switch (community members could not consistently recall which) to separate the two halves of the housing, as the power cord connects to one side of the housing while also connecting to the circuit board on the other side.

On reassembly: Use a rubber band to hold the two main housing halves together during reassembly for easier alignment.

Internal Components Overview

The Cloud's internal wiring consists of:

  • Two wires connecting the thermocouple (located under the white ceramic heater) to the circuit board — these monitor temperature
  • Two wires connecting the heater element itself to the circuit board — these deliver power

Aftermarket Heater Replacement

One resourceful community member successfully replaced a dead stock ceramic heater with an aftermarket coil-style heater. The results were documented as follows:

  • The replacement worked and produced comparable flavor and vapor density.
  • Hits were hotter than with the stock heater.
  • The entire body became warm during demanding use (versus only the top section with the stock heater).
  • The dial sensitivity was greatly reduced, with most usable temperatures clustered around the noon position.
  • The PID controller was originally tuned for the specific resistance (ohmage) of the Cloud's custom heater, and community members expressed concern that a mismatched heater could cause suboptimal PID behavior.

Accessories & Modifications

HydraCirc

The HydraCirc was VapeXhale's proprietary water filtration glass attachment designed specifically for the Cloud (and later the EVO). It sat atop the Cloud and provided water filtration and cooling for the vapor path. The HydraCirc was considered a signature accessory and was frequently bundled with the Cloud in sales listings.

Screen Cups

Proprietary screen cups served as the herb loading mechanism for the Cloud. These were consumable accessories, and users commonly kept spares on hand. They were available from VapeXhale as replacement parts.

Aftermarket Heater Modifications

As documented above, at least one community member successfully adapted an aftermarket coil-style heater element to replace the stock ceramic heater. This was considered an advanced modification undertaken when the original heater failed and manufacturer repair was not available. Community members noted that PID tuning differences between the stock and replacement heaters could affect temperature control behavior.

Comparisons

Cloud vs. Cloud EVO

The Cloud EVO was the successor to the original Cloud and addressed many of its predecessor's shortcomings. Community members who owned both generally migrated to the EVO as their primary unit. The EVO was noted for its ability to produce exceptionally dense, lung-filling hits that one community member described as capable of "completely destroying" a user's tolerance due to extraction efficiency.

Cloud vs. Log Vapes (E-Nano, Underdog)

Community discussions comparing the Cloud to log vapes highlighted fundamental differences in use philosophy:

  • The Cloud and EVO were regarded as producing larger, denser hits than log vaporizers.
  • Log vapes like the E-Nano were considered better for tolerance management, as their smaller bowl sizes encouraged more moderate consumption.
  • One experienced community member recommended that the E-Nano through a water piece "can provide big hits — not up to EVO size IMO, but not far off."
  • The same member observed that "there's a big plus to using something like the nano and NOT always trying for the biggest hits possible. Reasonably sized log vape hits help keep your tolerance down."

Cloud vs. LSV (Life Saber Vaporizer)

The LSV was compared favorably to the Cloud in some respects, with one community member describing the LSV as "just a weaker version of the EVO." Users who enjoyed the Cloud's hit quality tended to also appreciate the LSV, though the EVO was generally considered superior to the LSV in the community's assessment.
