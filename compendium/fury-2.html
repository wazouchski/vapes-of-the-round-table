# Fury 2

Overview

The Fury 2 is an ultra-compact portable dry herb vaporizer manufactured by Healthy Rips. Released as a successor to the original Fury, the Fury 2 quickly became one of the most widely discussed budget-friendly portables within the vaporizer community. It uses a hybrid heating method combining conduction and convection, powered by an internal rechargeable lithium-ion battery. The device is known for its remarkably small form factor — small enough to disappear in the palm of a hand — fast heat-up time, and surprisingly capable vapor production for its size. It served as the foundational platform for Healthy Rips' subsequent product line, including the Fury Edge and Fierce.

Community Reception

The Fury 2 was widely regarded within the community as one of the best value propositions in the portable vaporizer market during its era. Community members praised it as an "easy, no fuss, no muss vaporizer at a great price," and it frequently appeared in recommendation threads for users seeking a compact, discreet, and affordable portable.

Praised attributes:

  • Stealth and portability: Community members consistently highlighted the Fury 2's ability to disappear in the palm of a hand. One member noted it was far more discreet than the Arizer Air, and others described it as plausibly passing as an e-cigarette in social situations.
  • Fast heat-up time: The rapid heat-up was frequently cited as a major advantage over competitors like the Arizer Air and ArGo, which were considered too slow by comparison.
  • Low draw resistance: Multiple owners noted the Fury 2 had noticeably less draw resistance than the Arizer Air, making it approachable for newcomers and former smokers.
  • Ease of use: The device required no technique — draw speed and method didn't matter, making it ideal for sharing and for converting smokers to vaporizing. One member called it "the best vape I can think of for sharing and passing."
  • Vapor quality: With glass accessories, the vapor was described as flavorful, smooth, and surprisingly dense for such a small device.
  • Efficiency: Particularly when used with a glass stem or WPA, the community considered the Fury 2 highly efficient.
  • Price: At approximately $125, it was positioned well below competitors like the Mighty or Arizer ArGo.

Criticized attributes:

  • Battery life: The most consistently noted drawback. With only 4–6 bowls per charge, it could struggle to sustain group sessions or heavy daily use.
  • Non-replaceable battery: The internal battery was a point of contention. Several community members expressed a strong preference for user-replaceable batteries, and the Fury 2's battery was known to degrade over time. Multiple threads featured users whose Fury 2 units stopped holding a charge after approximately 2–3 years of regular use.
  • Session timer: The auto-shutoff at approximately 4 minutes was considered short by some users (the later Fury Edge added an extra minute).
  • Standard mouthpiece materials: Some users were uncomfortable with the silicone mouthpiece and preferred to use only glass accessories to achieve an all-glass vapor pathway.
  • Not a true on-demand vape: While it heated quickly, the Fury 2 was fundamentally a session vape. Community members seeking true on-demand performance were directed toward devices like the Firewood 5 or Milaana.

Overall, the community treated the Fury 2 as an excellent entry-level or backup portable, though not a long-term daily driver due to the battery limitations. As one community member put it, the Fury 2 was a "backup or tide you over vape" rather than a multi-year workhorse.

Tips & Techniques

Loading

  • Grind: A medium to fine grind was generally recommended. The chamber performed best when loaded at least half full; underpacking without accessories could result in underwhelming vapor production.
  • Packing density: A moderate pack — not too tight, not too loose — was the community consensus. The hybrid heating benefits from some airflow through the material.

Microdosing

  • Dosing capsules: The Fury 2 dosing capsules were highly recommended for microdosing. They reduce the effective chamber size and work with the standard mouthpiece without requiring any additional adapter. Community members noted that they produced lighter vapor than a full chamber pack, which was seen as a feature rather than a bug for users with low tolerance.
  • WPA for microdosing: Some medical users reported packing only a small amount of flower into the glass WPA rather than the full chamber, finding this sufficient for their needs. One medical user reported using the Fury 2 exclusively with the WPA for three years for microdose-level consumption.
  • Session pausing: The Fury 2 can be turned off after a couple of draws and turned back on later to finish the bowl, making it viable for spreading small loads across multiple brief sessions.

Draw Technique

  • The Fury 2 was noted as being very forgiving of draw speed and technique. Unlike convection-dominant vapes, users did not need to master a specific draw style, making it highly accessible.

Glass Accessories for Better Flavor

  • Community members who were sensitive to the taste of the silicone mouthpiece strongly recommended using the bent glass stem or WPA for an all-glass vapor pathway. One owner noted that receiving the bent stem and WPA "greatly improves taste" and brought the Fury 2 closer to Arizer-level flavor quality.

Temperature Guide

The community did not converge on a single widely cited temperature guide specific to the Fury 2, but general usage patterns emerged from discussions:

Community members noted that the Fury 2 produced smooth hits even at high temperatures, which was considered a notable strength of the device. Temperature stepping — starting low and gradually increasing across a session — was a common approach.

Maintenance & Cleaning

General Cleaning

  • Chamber: Regular brushing of the chamber after each session (or at minimum, daily) was recommended to prevent buildup. The included brush or a small paintbrush works well.
  • Mouthpiece and screen: The screen in the standard mouthpiece should be cleaned regularly. Soaking in isopropyl alcohol was the standard method.
  • Glass accessories: The bent glass stem, WPA, and bubbler attachment can be soaked in isopropyl alcohol for thorough cleaning.

Frequency

  • Users who exclusively used dosing capsules or glass accessories reported that the oven itself stayed remarkably clean, with one member noting the oven "has not been discolored at all" after multiple charges of use exclusively with capsules and glass stems.

Odor

  • The original Fury had issues with smelly silicone, but community members reported the Fury 2 had significantly less odor, with only a slight smell when first opened that dissipated quickly. The Pax 3, by comparison, was noted to develop significantly more smell over time.

Common Issues & Fixes

Battery Degradation

Problem: The most commonly reported long-term issue. After approximately 2–3 years of regular use, the internal battery may no longer hold a charge.

Fix: Unfortunately, the non-replaceable battery means this is effectively end-of-life for the unit. Community members in this situation generally upgraded to the Fury Edge (which has a larger battery) or switched to entirely different platforms. Some users recommended carrying a USB power bank as a field solution for shorter-term battery anxiety.

Short Session Timer

Problem: The approximately 4-minute auto-shutoff was considered too short by some session-style users.

Fix: The device can be restarted immediately for another session cycle. The successor Fury Edge extended this to approximately 5 minutes.

Silicone Taste from Standard Mouthpiece

Problem: Some users detected an off-taste from the standard silicone mouthpiece.

Fix: Use the bent glass stem or WPA for an all-glass vapor pathway. Multiple community members reported this as a transformative improvement.

Weak Vapor with Small Loads

Problem: Without glass accessories reducing chamber volume, the Fury 2 needed at least a half-full chamber to produce satisfying vapor.

Fix: Use dosing capsules for small loads, or insert a bent glass stem or WPA to reduce effective chamber size and improve extraction of smaller amounts.

Accessories & Modifications

The Fury 2's accessory ecosystem was considered one of its major strengths and was frequently cited as a reason to choose it over competitors.

  • Water Pipe Adapter (WPA): Available in 14mm and 18mm sizes, the WPA was among the most popular accessories. It provides an all-glass vapor pathway and allows the Fury 2 to be used with water pipes and bubblers for cooled, filtered vapor. Many community members considered the WPA essential.
  • Bent Glass Stem: Improves flavor significantly over the standard silicone mouthpiece by providing a glass vapor path. One of the most recommended accessories in the community.
  • Glass Bubbler Attachment: A self-contained water filtration accessory that attaches directly to the Fury 2 without a separate water pipe.
  • Dosing Capsules: Stainless steel capsules that hold a smaller, pre-measured amount of herb. Praised for microdosing convenience, keeping the chamber clean, and enabling quick bowl swaps. Available in packs (typically 5 or 10). European users noted difficulty sourcing these due to shipping costs and customs from Healthy Rips (US-based). They are compatible with the standard mouthpiece.
  • POTV One Accessories: Some community members noted cross-compatibility between the Planet of the Vapes ONE accessories (mouthpiece, bent stem) and the Fury 2, as the POTV One is built on the same platform.
  • Waterproof Carrying Case: Some users invested in protective cases given the device's pocket-friendly size and active lifestyle use.

Comparisons

Fury 2 vs. Fury Edge

The community considered these to be fundamentally the same vaporizer with incremental improvements on the Edge. Direct comparison testing by community members confirmed they performed "VERY similarly" in efficiency, clouds, and flavor. Key differences favoring the Edge include a longer session timer (approximately 5 minutes vs. 4), larger battery, haptic vibration feedback, and an improved mouthpiece attachment system. The community consensus was: if buying new, get the Edge; if budget is the primary concern and battery life isn't critical, the Fury 2 at a lower price remains an excellent choice. One experienced member who owned both summarized: "if you use sparingly and battery life is no issue get a Fury2 for cheaper."

Fury 2 vs. Arizer ArGo

Both are ultra-portable vapes, but they serve somewhat different user profiles. The Fury 2 was preferred for its faster heat-up time, lower draw resistance, greater stealth, smaller form factor, and lower price. The ArGo's main advantage was its user-replaceable 18650 battery, which was a decisive factor for users who prioritized long-term durability and unlimited field use. One member noted the Fury 2 had "more convection going on" than the ArGo. The ArGo appealed to users who preferred a longer, slower session style.

Fury 2 vs. Arizer Air (Original)

Community members who owned both generally favored the Fury 2 for portability and stealth, while the Air was sometimes preferred for flavor (though this gap closed significantly when using glass accessories on the Fury 2). The Fury 2 was noted to have a more powerful heater and considerably less draw resistance. The Air's replaceable battery was its key structural advantage.

Fury 2 vs. Pax 3

An interesting comparison given the Pax 3's mainstream popularity. The Fury 2 was generally seen as superior in vapor quality, efficiency, and ease of cleaning, while the Pax 3 was acknowledged as having better battery life, a more robust build, and arguably greater mainstream stealth (often mistaken for a generic battery device). One community member who owned both the Fury 2, Firewood 5, and Arizer Solo 2 still preferred the Pax 3 overall for its battery life and discretion. However, the Fury 2 was considered significantly more efficient and less wasteful with herb.

Fury 2 vs. Firewood 5

The Firewood 5 was considered a "vastly superior vape" by some for its convection heating and on-demand capability, but required patience and technique to learn. The Fury 2 was more accessible, requiring no technique, and was more affordable. One experienced member characterized the Fury 2 as a good backup or transitional device, while the Firewood 5 was more of a long-term daily driver.

Fury 2 vs. Fierce

The Fierce was described as "the same vape but bigger with removable battery." It offered the battery replaceability that many Fury 2 owners wished for but sacrificed the compact stealth factor. Some community members noted early production issues with the Fierce, including reports of off-gassing or smell that led to returns.

Fury 2 vs. DynaVap

When a user asked whether the Fury 2 was a suitable replacement for a Grasshopper, the community was divided between recommending the Fury 2 and DynaVap models. The Fury 2 was favored for its ease of use on the go — requiring no technique and no torch — while DynaVap models offered the advantage of no battery concerns and no plastics in the vapor path. One member noted the Fury 2 was "more dependable" and that carrying a power bank was simpler than carrying proprietary batteries.

Fury 2 for Microdosing

While capable of microdosing (particularly with dosing capsules or the WPA technique), the community did not consider the Fury 2 a specialist microdosing device. For dedicated microdosing, devices like the Vapman, DynaVap, and Tinymight were more frequently recommended. The Fury 2's strength was its versatility across a range of use cases rather than optimization for any single one.
