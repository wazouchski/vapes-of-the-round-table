# DaVinci IQ

Overview

The DaVinci IQ is a portable dry herb vaporizer manufactured by DaVinci (also stylized as "DAVINCI"), released in late 2016. It is a conduction-dominant vaporizer powered by a single user-replaceable 18650 lithium-ion battery. The IQ was positioned as a premium, feature-rich portable vaporizer and represented a significant step forward in DaVinci's product lineup from their earlier models (the original DaVinci and the DaVinci Ascent). The device features a sleek, compact design with a brushed metallic or ceramic zirconia body, smartphone app integration, and precise temperature control. The IQ was marketed as a direct competitor to devices like the PAX 3 and attracted significant community attention upon release, though discussions also raised notable safety concerns regarding its thermal management of the 18650 battery cell.

Community Reception

The DaVinci IQ generated considerable interest within the vaporizer community upon its release, arriving as one of the more technologically advanced portable vaporizers of its era. Community members appreciated the removable 18650 battery — a feature that distinguished it from sealed-battery competitors — as well as its compact form factor, precision temperature control, and app integration.

However, community reception was decidedly mixed. While the device was acknowledged as a step up from earlier DaVinci models in terms of build quality and features, several significant concerns emerged:

Pros identified by the community:

  • Replaceable 18650 battery, allowing users to carry spares and use preferred cells
  • Compact and relatively discreet design; one community member noted the device could pass for a large box mod e-cigarette
  • Precise temperature control with single-degree adjustments
  • Smart Path temperature profiles via app
  • Ceramic zirconia vapor path for clean flavor

Cons and concerns raised by the community:

  • Battery thermal management was a major point of contention (see Common Issues & Fixes)
  • Quality assurance was questioned; one community member launched a survey specifically to determine whether reports of faulty IQ units represented widespread manufacturing issues or were "blown out of proportion by the few who are unlucky enough to have a unit that fell through the QA cracks"
  • Another community member noted hesitation about recommending the device to newer vaporists due to reliability uncertainty
  • The community debated whether the device's internal charging was adequate compared to dedicated external 18650 chargers

Overall, the community regarded the IQ as an ambitious device with genuine innovations but one that carried notable caveats — particularly around battery safety — that potential buyers needed to weigh carefully.

Tips & Techniques

Community-sourced tips for getting the best performance from the DaVinci IQ:

  • Use high-quality, high-drain 18650 batteries. Community members consistently recommended reputable cells from manufacturers such as Sony, Samsung, and Sanyo. The Sanyo NCR18650GA (3500mAh, 10A) was specifically mentioned as a popular choice among IQ owners.
  • Avoid extended back-to-back sessions. One experienced community member measured battery temperatures reaching 190°F (approximately 88°C) after running the device continuously for around 22 minutes across multiple consecutive sessions. This is well above the safe operating temperature threshold for most lithium-ion cells. Allowing the device to cool between sessions is strongly recommended.
  • Use an external charger for the 18650 battery rather than relying solely on the internal USB charging. Community members recommended chargers such as the Xtar VP2 and Nitecore D2. Charging at lower amperage settings (0.25A or 0.5A) was preferred to reduce heat buildup and extend overall battery lifespan, though most 18650 cells can safely handle 0.5A charging.
  • DynaVap heating experiment: One community member tested whether the IQ's chamber could heat a DynaVap tip, noting the DynaVap fits "perfectly" in the IQ's oven. However, the experiment was unsuccessful — the IQ simply does not produce enough rapid, concentrated heat to bring a DynaVap cap to its click temperature. Heat dissipation through the DynaVap stem further reduces effectiveness, making this approach impractical.

Maintenance & Cleaning

While detailed cleaning protocols specific to the IQ were not extensively covered in the available community threads, general best practices for ceramic zirconia chamber vaporizers apply:

  • Clean the ceramic oven regularly with isopropyl alcohol (90% or higher) and cotton swabs to prevent residue buildup that degrades flavor.
  • Inspect the 18650 battery contacts periodically for debris or residue.
  • Clean the mouthpiece and vapor path regularly, as conduction vaporizers tend to accumulate reclaim along the air path.
  • Battery wrapper inspection: Community members with 18650 experience emphasized checking battery wraps for tears or damage before each use, as compromised wraps can lead to dangerous short circuits.

Common Issues & Fixes

Battery Overheating / Thermal Safety Concerns

The most extensively discussed issue with the DaVinci IQ within the community centered on 18650 battery safety and heat management. This topic generated enough concern that it was spun off into its own dedicated discussion thread.

The issue: During extended or back-to-back sessions, heat from the conduction oven transfers to the 18650 battery compartment. One community member with experience in e-cigarette battery safety tested battery temperatures with a thermal gun after approximately 22 minutes of continuous use and recorded the battery at 190°F (~88°C) — significantly above the safe operating temperature for lithium-ion cells (which most manufacturers rate for a maximum of around 140°F / 60°C).

Compounding the concern: Community members observed that the IQ appears to lack vent holes on the battery side of the device. In the e-cigarette community, vent holes are considered a critical safety feature that allows gases to escape safely in the event of a battery thermal runaway event. Without venting, a battery failure could potentially create a dangerous pressurized situation. One community member with direct experience venting 18650 cells in e-cigarette devices described the IQ's design as potentially creating "a pressurized pipe bomb so to speak" in a worst-case scenario.

Community-recommended precautions:

  • Do not run the device for extended periods without cooldown breaks
  • Use only high-quality, name-brand 18650 cells (Sony, Samsung, Sanyo)
  • Inspect battery wraps for damage before each use
  • Do not leave the device unattended while heating

If a battery vents (emergency guidance from the community):

Put the device down immediately

Do not attempt to open the battery door or remove the battery

Turn off the device as quickly as possible

Place it in a sink, metal container, or other non-flammable surface

Leave the area and allow the venting process to complete before inspecting

Do not attempt to "save" the device during an active venting event

This issue was significant enough that at least one community member considered canceling their pre-order pending a response from DaVinci.

Quality Assurance / Defective Units

Community discussion indicated uncertainty about the rate of defective IQ units reaching consumers. A community member organized a formal survey to determine whether QA problems were widespread or represented isolated cases amplified by selection bias (users with problems being more likely to post than satisfied owners). The results of this survey were not conclusively documented in the available threads, but the mere existence of the survey indicates the concern was notable within the community.

Charging Inconsistencies

One community member reported significantly different charge times between the two slots of a Nitecore D2 external charger when charging Sanyo NCR18650GA cells used in their IQ — 3.5 hours in one slot versus over 6.5 hours in the other. The battery retailer (IMR) indicated this was normal but did not elaborate. This appears to be a charger-specific issue rather than an IQ-specific one, but it highlights the importance of understanding external charging equipment when using the IQ.

Accessories & Modifications

  • Replacement 18650 batteries: The removable battery is one of the IQ's key features. Popular community choices included the Sanyo NCR18650GA (3500mAh, 10A). Users emphasized purchasing only from reputable battery vendors to avoid counterfeit cells.
  • External 18650 chargers: Recommended by the community over internal USB charging. Specific models mentioned include:

- Xtar VP2 — highly regarded for its 0.25A low-current charging option

- Nitecore D2 — popular and widely available

- Xtar SV2 Rocket and Xtar Dragon VP4 Plus were also recommended

  • DaVinci smartphone app: While technically not a physical accessory, the Bluetooth-connected app was considered an integral part of the IQ experience, enabling Smart Path temperature profiles and device diagnostics.

Specific water pipe adapter (WPA) compatibility and other physical modifications were not extensively detailed in the available community threads.

Comparisons

DaVinci IQ vs. PAX (Original / PAX 2)

The DaVinci product line was frequently positioned as an alternative to the PAX in purchase advice threads. Community members noted several distinctions:

  • The original DaVinci offered better temperature control and a more unrestricted airflow compared to butane-powered alternatives like the Iolite, and was considered a "step up" from that category
  • The PAX was described by at least one trusted community member as "fussy" compared to the more straightforward DaVinci operation
  • The PAX was noted as using a relatively large amount of material per session, though conservation techniques were possible
  • The DaVinci (original model) was favorably compared for its ease of cleaning and use — one community member stated they would happily return to an Iolite if the DaVinci were discontinued, noting both for their straightforward operation
  • Price was a factor: the original DaVinci was available for as low as $155 on Amazon during the period discussed

DaVinci IQ vs. Grasshopper

Both the IQ and the Grasshopper were subjects of a reliability-focused community survey. Both devices appeared to have notable rates of reported issues, though the community debated whether this reflected actual product defects or selection bias in online reporting. The Grasshopper was described as having "a high infant mortality/DOA rate" and "ambiguous and confusing failure symptoms," while the IQ's concerns centered more on thermal management and QA consistency.

DaVinci IQ vs. Arizer Solo

The Arizer Solo was mentioned alongside the IQ in purchase advice contexts. The Solo was "highly recommended" by community members for its vapor quality, though its draw was acknowledged as notably restrictive compared to the DaVinci's unrestricted airflow. Both devices use 18650 batteries, making them compatible with the same external chargers and battery ecosystem — a point of practical overlap discussed in charging-related threads.
