# Cloud Evo

Overview

The Cloud Evo (often referred to simply as "the Evo") is a desktop convection vaporizer manufactured by VapeXhale, an American company. The device was released around 2013–2014 and quickly established itself as one of the premier desktop vaporizers in the enthusiast community. The Evo is an AC-powered desktop unit designed primarily for use with water filtration through proprietary glass attachments called "HydraTubes," though it can also be used with a dry glass mouthpiece. It is compatible with both dry herb and concentrates.

The Cloud Evo was designed from the ground up as a "heavy hitter" — a vaporizer optimized for dense, fast vapor delivery through water. Its all-glass vapor path and vertical design made it a standout in its category. The device uses a bamboo-shaped cylindrical body with a heat element in the core, into which herb-loaded baskets (called ELBs, or Easy Load Bowls) are inserted from the top, with a HydraTube sitting above the basket to filter and cool the vapor.

Community Reception

The Cloud Evo was widely regarded as one of the best desktop vaporizers available for water-filtered vapor delivery. Within the vaporizer community, it was frequently the first recommendation for users seeking a "heavy hitter" — a device capable of delivering dense, fast, and powerful draws through glass.

Pros:

  • Vapor density and delivery speed: The Evo was consistently praised for its ability to produce thick, potent vapor quickly. One community member, when a user expressed a desire for cooler vapor and faster delivery, stated that the Evo "nails your high priority items; actually, it was made for them." This sentiment was broadly shared.
  • Water filtration integration: The proprietary HydraTube system was a key selling point. The device was purpose-built to work with water pieces, and users found this integration seamless.
  • All-glass vapor path: Flavor purity was frequently noted as a strength.
  • Concentrate versatility: The VXNail accessory expanded the Evo's usefulness beyond dry herb.
  • Build quality: Users who sold or traded their units often noted they were in excellent condition even after extended use, suggesting good durability.

Cons:

  • Price: The Evo was consistently described as expensive. One user, upon first seeing it recommended, responded: "Wow, that looks sweet. Super expensive though." This was a common refrain.
  • Not universally loved: At least one community member flatly stated, "I just don't like the Evo," indicating that while it was highly regarded overall, it was not for everyone. Personal preference in draw resistance, form factor, or session style may have contributed to divided opinions for some users.
  • Desktop-only form factor: As a mains-powered unit, it lacks any portability.

The Evo was desirable enough to command attention on the secondhand market, with users trading custom glass and other high-value items for the unit. It was also noted that some owners eventually moved on to newer devices — one user selling their Evo mentioned that "VAS is kicking in with the TinyMight," suggesting that while the Evo was beloved, the constant evolution of the vaporizer market meant some users were drawn to newer innovations.

Tips & Techniques

Loading the ELB

The ELB (Easy Load Bowl) is a small stainless steel mesh basket designed for quick loading and unloading. Community members found that:

  • A moderate, not overly tight, pack works best to allow hot air to pass through the material via convection.
  • Multiple ELBs can be pre-loaded and stored with ELB caps for convenient session-to-session use. Bundles commonly included 4–6 ELBs and corresponding caps.
  • The ELB holder accessory keeps loaded baskets organized and ready to go.

Using E-Juice with Herb

Some community members experimented with adding e-juice to herb bowls in the Evo. One experienced user offered the following guidance:

  • Add only a single drop of e-juice to a loaded bowl of herb.
  • Use pure VG (vegetable glycerin) watered down rather than standard e-juice containing PG (propylene glycol), as PG was reported to be harsh on the throat at typical herb vaporization temperatures.
  • Avoid excess liquid, as too much moisture can cause problems with vapor production and device performance.

Session Style

The Evo is well-suited to an on-demand or short-session style of use. Its design allows users to take a few powerful draws and then remove the ELB, rather than requiring extended session-style usage.

Temperature Guide

The Cloud Evo uses an analog dial rather than a digital display, so precise temperature readouts were not a standard part of community discussions in the available data. General community guidance suggests:

  • Lower dial settings: Better for flavor-forward, lighter draws. Useful for tasting terpenes early in a session.
  • Mid-range settings: A balance of flavor and vapor density; suitable for most daily sessions.
  • Higher dial settings: Maximum extraction and dense clouds. The Evo was particularly praised for its performance at higher temperatures when used with a HydraTube, as the water filtration smooths and cools the vapor significantly.

Community members are encouraged to experiment with dial positions and find their personal sweet spot, as the analog nature of the control means precise temperature calibration can vary between units.

Maintenance & Cleaning

Glass Components

  • HydraTubes and dry glass mouthpieces should be cleaned regularly to maintain flavor purity and airflow. Isopropyl alcohol (ISO) soaks are the standard method.
  • Reclaim from the glass can be collected, as is common with all-glass-path vaporizers used through water.

ELBs

  • ELBs can be soaked in isopropyl alcohol to remove residue.
  • Screens in the ELBs may eventually need replacement after extended use.
  • Keeping multiple clean ELBs on hand allows for uninterrupted sessions while others are soaking.

Body

  • The exterior of the Evo can be wiped down; the neoprene sleeve (included with some packages) helps protect the body and provides insulation during use.

Common Issues & Fixes

The available community data did not surface widespread reports of recurring defects or failures. The most commonly discussed "issue" was the high price point, which is a purchasing consideration rather than a functional problem.

Users who found the Evo did not suit their preferences generally cited personal taste in session style or draw characteristics rather than hardware failures.

Accessories & Modifications

HydraTubes

The signature accessory for the Cloud Evo. HydraTubes are proprietary glass water filtration attachments that sit atop the unit. Multiple models were produced with varying percolation styles:

  • Swagger HydraTube: Featured four large horizontal slits; one community member described it as offering "really perfect diffusion for this vape."
  • Various other HydraTube models with different diffusion patterns were available.

VXNail

A concentrate insert that allows the Evo to be used as an electronic nail (e-nail) for wax and other concentrates. This was a popular accessory and frequently included in bundles.

Nebula Kit

An accessory kit described by one seller as enabling "more relaxed sessions," suggesting a different draw style or diffusion characteristic compared to the standard HydraTube setup.

Dry Glass Mouthpiece

For users who prefer vapor without water filtration, a dry glass mouthpiece was available as an alternative to the HydraTube.

ELB Accessories

  • ELB Caps: Snap-on caps for pre-loaded ELBs, allowing portable storage of prepared bowls.
  • ELB Holder: An organizer for keeping multiple loaded ELBs accessible.

Neoprene Sleeve

A protective and insulating cover for the Evo's body.

Comparisons

Cloud Evo vs. E-Nano

The E-Nano (by Epicvape) was frequently mentioned alongside the Evo as a more affordable alternative. One community member noted that the E-Nano would cost "a lot less" and that "many would say it's similar to Cloud Evo." However, the comparison came with caveats — the E-Nano is a log vaporizer with a different form factor, smaller bowl size, and a more sip-style draw technique. One user who had owned both described having to adjust their lung capacity expectations when switching from the E-Nano's sipping style to the Evo's larger, denser hits. The two devices served overlapping but distinct niches: the E-Nano for efficiency and affordability, the Evo for maximum vapor density and water filtration integration.

Cloud Evo vs. Mighty (with WPA)

In discussions about the best vaporizers for use through water pipe adapters, the Mighty by Storz & Bickel was mentioned as a strong alternative. The Mighty was praised for "packing a punch" and offering "very good flavor," though it required a third-party 18mm adapter or a DIY modification for water pipe use. The key distinction is that the Mighty is a portable device being adapted for water use, while the Evo was purpose-built for it.

Cloud Evo vs. Other Desktop Water Vaporizers

Users considering the Evo also looked at the Herborizer, De Verdamper, AroMed, and ROOR Vapor as alternatives in the desktop water vaporizer category. While detailed head-to-head comparisons were limited in the available data, the Evo was typically the first recommendation when a user asked for a desktop unit optimized for water filtration and heavy-hitting performance.

Cloud Evo vs. miniVAP

The miniVAP was mentioned as an alternative offering "big punch and max max flavor" with the added versatility of portable use (battery) and desktop use (with an optional AC power adapter). The trade-off was a 6–7 minute warm-up time, significantly longer than the Evo's.
