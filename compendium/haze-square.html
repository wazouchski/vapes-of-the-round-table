# Haze Square

Overview

The Haze Square is a portable dry herb vaporizer manufactured by Haze Vaporizers, announced in late 2017 and made available for preorder in early 2018 with initial shipments beginning in February–April 2018. The device is notable for its distinctive square form factor and its signature feature: a rotating four-bowl system that allows users to pre-load multiple chambers and switch between them on the fly. The Haze Square uses a hybrid heating method with replaceable coil-based heaters and is powered by an internal rechargeable battery. The device was designed to handle both dry herb and concentrates. It was available in at least two variants — a standard model and a "Pro" version, the latter featuring USB-C charging for faster recharging.

Haze Vaporizers had an established reputation in the vaporizer community prior to the Square's release, having previously produced the Haze V3 dual-bowl portable. The Square represented a significant design departure and was initially announced exclusively through a popular community forum before a broader public launch.

Community Reception

The Haze Square generated significant enthusiasm in the vaporizer community upon its announcement. Haze Vaporizers chose to announce the device exclusively on a popular community forum, intending to offer preorders to community members first. However, this strategy backfired somewhat — information spread to other forums and sites, and the manufacturer received more than three times the anticipated number of preorders within the first three days, straining their limited initial inventory.

Pros identified by the community:

  • Four-bowl system — The ability to pre-load four chambers and rotate between them was considered a standout feature, particularly for group sessions or extended solo use without needing to reload.
  • Fast heat-up and on-demand capability — After the initial heat-up, the device offered on-demand heating, which was praised as responsive and efficient.
  • Competitive pricing — The price point was seen as a strong advantage, particularly when compared to devices like the Arizer ArGo.
  • Large bowl size — The individual chambers were noted as being generous in capacity.
  • Solid build quality — Beta testers described the construction as robust.
  • Effective app — The companion app was well-received for its temperature control and customization options.
  • Big vapor production — Community members noted the device was capable of producing "big old hits."

Cons identified by the community:

  • Size and weight — The Square was noticeably larger and heavier than competing portables. This was acknowledged as the device's primary weakness for on-the-go use.
  • Battery life — Battery endurance was cited as a concern, particularly compared to devices with replaceable 18650 cells like the Arizer ArGo. The Pro version with USB-C quick charging mitigated this to some extent.
  • Lack of on-board controls — The device relied on its app for full control, unlike competitors that offered physical button or screen-based temperature adjustment.
  • Maintenance — Community members anticipated that the multi-bowl design and coil-based heating system would require more involved maintenance compared to simpler single-bowl designs.
  • Long-term support concerns — By 2022, community members were expressing concern about the availability of replacement parts, particularly coils and batteries, as Haze Vaporizers appeared to have reduced or ceased active support. One community member described the effort to find third-party replacements as an attempt at "reviving the beast."

The preorder and shipping process generated some frustration, with community members noting a lack of clear shipping patterns and questioning whether the promised timelines for community-exclusive early shipping were being met. Haze Vaporizers responded publicly, explaining that orders were being shipped strictly in the order they were received with no preferential treatment.

Tips & Techniques

Community discussion on specific usage techniques for the Haze Square was limited in the available threads, but several general best practices emerged:

  • Pre-load all four bowls — For sessions away from home, the four-bowl system is best utilized by loading all chambers in advance. This eliminates the need for on-the-go packing and is one of the device's key advantages.
  • Use the app to dial in temperature — The app was recommended as the primary means of finding ideal temperature settings. One community member described the workflow as "set it and forget it," suggesting that once a preferred profile is established, the app allows consistent sessions without further adjustment.
  • Get the Pro version if battery life matters — Community members recommended the Pro model for its USB-C quick charging, especially given the device's relatively high power consumption.
  • Take advantage of on-demand heating — After initial heat-up, the Square could be used in an on-demand fashion, meaning users could take a draw, set it down, and return without continuous heating wasting material.

Temperature Guide

Specific temperature recommendations for the Haze Square were not extensively documented in the available community discussions. The device's app-based control system allowed for precise temperature adjustment, and community members generally recommended using the app to experiment and find personal preferences. Standard dry herb vaporization temperature principles would apply:

  • Low range (~160–180°C / 320–356°F): Flavor-focused sessions with lighter vapor
  • Medium range (~180–200°C / 356–392°F): Balanced flavor and vapor production
  • High range (~200–220°C / 392–428°F): Maximum extraction and cloud production

No specific community-agreed "sweet spots" were documented in the available thread data.

Maintenance & Cleaning

Maintenance was identified as one of the Haze Square's relative weaknesses compared to simpler designs like the Arizer ArGo. The multi-bowl carousel system and coil-based heater elements both require attention:

  • Coils — The heating coils are a consumable component that will eventually need replacement. These are small, compact elements that sit within the heating assembly. Photos shared by community members show them to be relatively simple in construction, raising the possibility of DIY reproduction (see Common Issues & Fixes).
  • Bowls — With four chambers, regular cleaning of all bowls is important to maintain airflow and flavor. Standard isopropyl alcohol soaking and brushing practices apply.
  • General upkeep — As with most portable vaporizers, keeping the air path and mouthpiece area clean is essential for consistent performance.

Common Issues & Fixes

Replacement Parts Availability

The most significant long-term issue discussed by the community was the availability of replacement coils and batteries after Haze Vaporizers appeared to wind down active support. By late 2022, community members were actively seeking third-party alternatives:

  • Battery replacement — One community member shared photos of the battery with its specifications, noting that while batteries with similar specs could be sourced, finding ones with the matching connector wiring (described as having red, black, and yellow wires) proved difficult. Users interested in replacement are advised to match the voltage, capacity, and physical dimensions exactly, and to pay close attention to the connector type and pin configuration.
  • DIY coils — Community members began investigating whether the heating coils could be fabricated using readily available wire and materials. Photos of the stock coils were shared to assist in reverse-engineering efforts. As of the last available discussions, no confirmed third-party or DIY coil solution had been widely validated.

Preorder and Shipping Delays

During the initial launch period, shipping delays were a common source of frustration. Haze Vaporizers attributed this to unexpectedly high demand after information about the community-exclusive preorder spread to other forums, resulting in over three times the anticipated order volume against limited initial inventory.

Accessories & Modifications

  • Replacement batteries — Haze Vaporizers sold replacement batteries as an accessory, allowing users to swap out depleted batteries. The Pro model's USB-C quick charging was positioned as an alternative to carrying spare batteries.
  • Concentrate compatibility — The device was designed to work with concentrates in addition to dry herb, though specific concentrate accessory details were not extensively discussed in the available threads.
  • DIY repair efforts — As noted above, the community began exploring third-party battery and coil solutions after official support waned, though these remained works in progress as of late 2022.

No specific water pipe adapter (WPA) or other aftermarket accessory discussions were documented in the available thread data.

The community member who owned both described the Square as "a no-brainer" primarily due to its lower price point and four-bowl system, but acknowledged the ArGo's advantages in portability, battery life, and ease of maintenance. The recommendation was to get the Pro version of the Square to offset the battery life disadvantage with quick charging.

Haze Square vs. Boundless Tera

A limited comparison was discussed when a user sought advice between the two for concentrate use. Community members generally indicated that for concentrate-focused use, the Boundless Tera (not "Sera," as originally misspelled in the inquiry) had more documented community feedback and was the safer choice. The Haze Square's concentrate capabilities were less thoroughly reviewed at the time of discussion. One community member humorously claimed the Square was "miles better in any aspect" before immediately revealing it was a joke, suggesting the community did not view the Square as definitively superior for concentrate use.
