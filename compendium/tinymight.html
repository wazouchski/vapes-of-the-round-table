# Tinymight

Overview

The Tinymight (often abbreviated as "TM") is a portable dry herb vaporizer designed and manufactured by a small Finnish operation. The original Tinymight (sometimes retroactively called the Tinymight 1 or TM1) emerged around 2019–2020, with incremental hardware revisions following (including a v1.5 designation noted on some units). A successor, the Tinymight 2 (TM2), was released subsequently. The device uses a primarily convection heating method powered by a single removable 18650 battery, and features both on-demand and session operating modes selectable via an analog temperature dial on the body. The Tinymight earned a devoted following within the vaporizer community for its powerful on-demand extraction, exceptional flavor, micro-dosing capability, and artisanal build quality.

The device is loaded via removable glass stems—a short stem and a longer "cooling stem"—that serve as both the herb chamber and mouthpiece. The Tinymight ships directly from the manufacturer and, particularly in its early production life, was subject to pre-order wait times that could extend for weeks or longer.

Community Reception

The Tinymight was widely regarded within the vaporizer community as one of the best portable convection vaporizers available. It earned high praise across numerous discussion threads for several key attributes:

Pros:

  • Outstanding flavor: Frequently described as among the best flavor producers in its class. One community member noted it was "the closest to my logs as far as taste, ease of use, and very easy to keep clean." The ability to temp-step using the dial was highlighted as "the best way to enjoy flavorful terp hits early."
  • Excellent micro-dosing capability: The community widely agreed that the Tinymight excelled with very small loads. Multiple members recommended it as a top-tier micro-doser, noting it could efficiently extract from tiny amounts of herb. One member stated it was "very efficient" and "better for one hitter type bowls" than comparable devices, allowing "extremely fast extraction."
  • On-demand convenience: The near-instant heat-up and on-demand mode were praised for allowing users to take a single hit and return to the bowl later without wasting material. One experienced member noted: "I find it more enjoyable to take a hit from my TM and come back to it later."
  • Versatile temperature control: The analog dial, while lacking numeric precision, was praised for being "super handy" and enabling smooth temp-stepping through a session.
  • Replaceable battery: The use of a standard 18650 cell was seen as a significant advantage for longevity and convenience, especially compared to devices with sealed internal batteries. The device can also charge via USB while in use.
  • Ease of cleaning: Repeatedly mentioned as simple to maintain.

Cons:

  • Durability concerns: At least one community member reported that their Tinymight broke "after one minor fall," suggesting the wood-and-glass construction may be fragile compared to more rugged devices.
  • Uneven extraction near the heater: A known tendency for the herb closest to the heater to cook more than the rest of the load was noted by multiple users. This prompted experimentation with stem positioning and packing techniques (see Tips & Techniques).
  • Stirring debate: While some users felt stirring between hits was necessary and found it annoying, others firmly stated that stirring was not required. One member clarified: "There will sometimes be some green left at the wall of the bowl, but that's going to be maybe 1% of your flower." Another stated they had "never stirred" either their TM1 or TM2. Community opinion was somewhat divided on this point, but the consensus leaned toward stirring being optional.
  • Price and availability: The Tinymight was considered a premium-priced device, and pre-order wait times were a recurring frustration. One prospective buyer noted "it's a little more than I want to spend."
  • Learning curve for the analog dial: Without a digital readout, users must learn their preferred dial positions through trial and error.

Tips & Techniques

Loading and Stem Positioning

  • Micro-dosing: The Tinymight handles micro-doses exceptionally well. Users can load as little as a small pinch—community members reported using it with loads in the 0.02–0.05 g range with excellent results.
  • Positioning herb away from the heater: A widely shared technique involved positioning the herb load further up the stem, away from the bottom (heater end). One member reported that "a gently tamped load is at least a quarter inch, maybe 3/8s, from the bottom of the stem and that seems to help a bit and not cook so much initially." Using the long stem allows even greater distance from the heater.
  • Fresh nug technique: One user described breaking off a small nug and squeezing it directly into a 5-inch glass stem without grinding, finding this worked well with fresh herb.
  • Capsule use: The short stem can be used with included capsules for convenience and easier loading/unloading.

Draw Technique

  • Sipping style: The Tinymight was described as accommodating a "sipping" draw technique, particularly when used with a bubbler and whip, making it somewhat suitable for users transitioning from smoking who prefer a mouth-to-lung style.
  • On-demand single hits: For micro-dosing, the recommended approach is to press the button, take one deliberate draw, and set the device down. The convection heating means the herb stops cooking almost immediately when airflow ceases.
  • Temp-stepping: Start with the dial at a lower setting for the first few draws to extract maximum flavor from terpenes, then gradually increase the temperature setting for subsequent hits to fully extract the bowl. This was described as "the best way to enjoy flavorful terp hits early."

Stirring

  • The community generally agreed that stirring is not required for a satisfactory experience. A thin ring of green material may remain along the bowl walls, but this represents a negligible amount of herb. Users who were initially compelled to stir often abandoned the practice after realizing the minimal return.

Temperature Guide

The Tinymight uses an analog dial without numeric temperature markings, so precise temperature values are not available in the same way as with digitally controlled vaporizers. Community guidance is based on dial position and observed effects:

Dial Position / GoalEffect
Low settingsBest for flavor exploration; light, terpy vapor with minimal visible cloud. Ideal for the first draws of a fresh bowl.
Low-to-mid settingsBalanced flavor and moderate vapor production. Community-preferred range for "milking" slow, low-temp draws over multiple sessions.
Mid-to-high settingsFuller extraction, denser clouds, more sedating/body effects. Used for finishing a bowl or for users who prefer efficiency over flavor.
High settingsMaximum extraction; darker ABV, thicker clouds. Suitable for those seeking a "stonier" effect. Caution advised to avoid combustion territory.

Community sweet spot: Many users reported the most enjoyable experience came from starting at the lower end of the dial and stepping up over 3–4 draws. This approach maximizes both flavor and total extraction.

Note: Because the dial is analog, users are encouraged to experiment and mark their preferred positions. Environmental factors such as ambient temperature and draw speed also influence effective heating temperature.

Maintenance & Cleaning

The Tinymight was repeatedly praised for being "very easy to keep clean." Key maintenance points include:

  • Glass stems: The primary cleaning targets. Stems can be soaked in isopropyl alcohol (ISO) to dissolve resin buildup. Reclaim from the stems can be saved if desired.
  • Screens: Replacement screens are included with the device. Screens should be inspected periodically and replaced when clogged or damaged. Screens for the short stem and long stem are different; the long stem uses its own replacement screens.
  • O-rings: Spare o-rings are included. These should be replaced if they become worn, cracked, or lose their seal, as a proper seal is important for consistent convection performance.
  • Heater area: Light brushing to remove any debris. Avoid inserting anything that could damage the heater element.
  • Frequency: Clean stems every few sessions or when flavor begins to degrade. Heavier users may need to clean more frequently.

Common Issues & Fixes

Uneven Cooking / Herb Closest to Heater Overcooking

  • Cause: The convection airflow heats herb nearest the heater more intensely.
  • Fix: Position the herb load further up in the stem, leaving at least a quarter inch gap between the bottom of the stem and the herb. Use a gentle tamp rather than a tight pack. The long cooling stem provides more room for this adjustment.

Green Ring Remaining on Bowl Walls

  • Cause: Convection airflow passes primarily through the center of the load, leaving a thin outer ring less extracted.
  • Fix: This is largely cosmetic and represents ~1% of the herb. Stirring is one option but generally unnecessary. Simply accept it or give the stem a light shake.

Device Fragility / Breakage from Drops

  • Cause: Wood and glass construction.
  • Fix: Use a protective carrying case (see Accessories). Handle with care, especially when the glass stem is inserted.

Defective Units / Warranty Replacements

  • Some users reported receiving replacement units directly from the manufacturer for defective devices. Replacement units were noted to arrive with serial numbers already engraved. Community members generally found the manufacturer responsive, though communication timelines could vary.

Accessories & Modifications

Stems

  • Short glass stem: The default everyday option; compact and pocketable. Compatible with included capsules.
  • Extra-long glass cooling stem: Provides additional vapor cooling and the ability to position herb further from the heater. Community-favorite for home use.
  • Custom glass stems: One community member reported using 5-inch custom glass stems from OregonGlassBlowers, praising the extra cooling length.

Water Pipe Adapters (WPA)

  • The Tinymight is compatible with water pipe adapters, and using it with a WPA through a bubbler was a popular configuration. One community member specifically recommended the Tinymight paired with a WPA as a "fancy/high class" micro-dosing setup. The WPA was also recommended for medical users seeking smooth, cool vapor.

J-Hook

  • Using the Tinymight with a j-hook was a favored home-use configuration mentioned by multiple community members. The j-hook provides additional cooling and a comfortable draw angle without the need for water filtration.

Bubblers and Whips

  • Pairing with a small bubbler and/or whip was recommended for users seeking a sipping or mouth-to-lung-like experience. One former smoker described using "a tinymight in combination with a bubbler and whip" and finding it comfortable for sipping-style draws.

Carrying Cases

  • The Storz & Bickel Crafty+ carrying case was confirmed by community members to be a near-perfect fit for the Tinymight. One member called it "the perfect fit."
  • A compact Amazon hard case (CASEMATIX brand) was also recommended, reportedly fitting the TM2, a stem, and a Mad Heater Reload accessory.
  • A Pelican 1030 Micro Case was reported as a long-term favorite, fitting extra stems or a doob tube and spare batteries while remaining very compact.

Capsules

  • Dosing capsules for the short stem are included and allow for quick loading, unloading, and on-the-go convenience.

Activated Carbon Filter (Not Recommended)

  • One community member experimented with the idea of placing activated carbon above the heater screen to filter incoming air and reduce uneven herb cooking. The community quickly cautioned against this, with members warning that carbon dust in the lungs is dangerous and that heating charcoal is unsuitable in this context. This modification is not recommended.

Comparisons

Community consensus was that either device was excellent, but the Tinymight held a slight edge for on-demand single-hit micro-dosing and flavor chasing, while the FW7 was preferred for its durability and more straightforward operation.

Tinymight vs. Mighty / Mighty+

The Tinymight was frequently suggested as an alternative to the Storz & Bickel Mighty for users seeking more efficiency with small loads. The Mighty's session-style heating was noted to waste herb during heat-up, while the Tinymight's on-demand mode avoids this entirely. For micro-dosing users, the Tinymight was the clear community recommendation over the Mighty.

Tinymight vs. Splinter Z / RBT Devices

Users of 510-powered RBT devices (Splinter Z, etc.) found the Tinymight to be the closest portable equivalent in terms of convection flavor and fast extraction. One experienced member noted that "all my RBT stuff is more sparingly used as a result of me getting the TM." The Tinymight was seen as offering a similar convection experience without requiring a separate box mod.

Tinymight vs. Dynavap

For users who enjoyed the Dynavap's on-demand, small-bowl approach but disliked the butane torch ritual, the Tinymight was frequently recommended as a battery-powered upgrade that preserved the on-demand ethos while adding electronic temperature control and eliminating the need for an external heat source.

Tinymight vs. Log Vapes (E-Nano, Underdog, Heat Island)

One community member described the Tinymight as "the closest to my logs as far as taste" among portable vaporizers—high praise given the community's deep respect for log vape flavor quality. The Tinymight was recommended alongside log vapes as complementary devices: logs for stationary home use, Tinymight for portable and around-the-house use.

Tinymight vs. Xmax V3 Pro

For budget-conscious micro-dosers, the Xmax V3 Pro was suggested as a more affordable alternative to the Tinymight. However, one community member noted that the V3 Pro was not particularly efficient with very small loads (e.g., 0.02 g), requiring "too much huffing and puffing." The Tinymight's convection-forward design was considered significantly more capable for true micro-dose use cases.

Tinymight vs. Vapman

Both devices were praised as "excellent microdosers" and kept in regular rotation by experienced users. The Tinymight's advantage was convenience—"you pretty much just press a button and go"—while the Vapman offered a more ritualistic, torch-powered experience with outstanding flavor. The Tinymight was preferred for quick, casual micro-dose adjustments throughout the day, while the Vapman appealed to users who enjoyed the process.
