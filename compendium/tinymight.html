# Tinymight

Overview

The Tinymight (often abbreviated as "TM") is a portable dry herb vaporizer designed and manufactured by a small Finnish operation. The original Tinymight launched around 2019–2020, with an updated version—the Tinymight 2 (TM2)—following later. The device uses a predominantly convection heating method, delivering hot air through a packed herb chamber on demand or in session mode. It is powered by a single removable 18650 battery and features a manual temperature dial for analog temperature adjustment.

The Tinymight quickly developed a devoted following within the vaporizer community for its exceptional flavor, on-demand capability, fast heat-up time, and versatile use with both short and long glass stems. Its design philosophy—a wood-bodied, artisan-style portable with convection heating and replaceable batteries—placed it in a category alongside other enthusiast-grade portables, though its particular combination of speed, flavor, and simplicity set it apart.

Serial numbers observed in the community suggest ongoing production runs, with units in the 106xx range noted as v1.5 revisions. The manufacturer was known to handle warranty replacements directly, shipping replacement units from Finland.

Community Reception

The Tinymight was widely regarded within the vaporizer community as one of the top-tier portable convection vaporizers available. Community members frequently praised the device for several key attributes:

Pros:

  • Exceptional flavor. The convection heating was repeatedly highlighted as delivering some of the best terpene-forward, flavorful hits available from a portable. One community member described it as "the closest to my logs as far as taste."
  • On-demand versatility. The ability to take a single hit, set the device down, and return to the same bowl later was considered a major advantage, particularly for micro-dosing and medical users. Multiple community members noted the TM was "better for one hitter type bowls" and praised the ability to "take a hit and come back to it later."
  • Fast extraction. Users could achieve "extremely fast extraction" when desired, but also had the option to "milk the low temps to stretch it out a little longer."
  • Micro-dosing excellence. The community widely agreed the Tinymight was among the best battery-powered options for micro-dosing, frequently recommended alongside the Vapman for this use case. One member described both as "excellent microdosers" that "can be used with a tiny amount of flower."
  • Temp stepping with the dial. The analog dial was considered "super handy" for temp stepping, described as "the best way to enjoy flavorful terp hits early."
  • Replaceable battery. The swappable 18650 cell and ability to charge via USB were cited as significant practical advantages.
  • Easy to keep clean. Glass stems contributed to straightforward maintenance.

Cons:

  • Durability concerns. At least one community member reported their Tinymight broke "after one minor fall," suggesting the wood and glass construction may be fragile compared to more rugged portables.
  • Stirring perception. Some prospective buyers believed stirring was required between hits for even extraction. The community was somewhat divided on this point, though experienced users generally concluded that stirring was not required. One member noted, "There will sometimes be some green left at the wall of the bowl, but that's going to be maybe 1% of your flower." Another reported they had "never stirred my TM (1 and 2)."
  • Uneven herb cooking near the heater. Several users noted that herb closest to the heater screen could cook more than the rest of the load, particularly when the load was packed too close to the bottom of the stem.
  • Price. At approximately €300 with shipping and pre-order wait times, the Tinymight was positioned as a premium device. Some users found it "a little more than I want to spend."
  • Pre-order wait times. Due to the small-scale Finnish manufacturing operation, ordering a new unit sometimes required patience.
  • Size and discretion. While portable, the Tinymight with its glass stem protruding was not considered the most discreet option compared to smaller, more pen-like devices.

Overall, the community regarded the Tinymight as a "fancy/high class" option for battery-powered portables, frequently recommended as a first choice for users seeking on-demand convection performance, flavor, and micro-dosing capability.

Tips & Techniques

Loading

  • Pack position matters. The community discovered that positioning the herb load further up the stem—away from the heater screen at the bottom—significantly reduced uneven cooking. One experienced user recommended "a gently tamped load is at least a quarter inch, maybe 3/8s, from the bottom of the stem" to prevent the herb closest to the heater from browning too quickly.
  • Gentle tamp. A light tamp was preferred. Fresh, slightly sticky herb could be broken off as a small nug and squeezed directly into the stem without grinding, according to one community member who used 5-inch glass stems.
  • Micro-dosing loads. The TM handled very small loads well. For micro-dosing, users reported loading minimal amounts and extracting efficiently in one or two hits.
  • Dosing capsules. Capsules compatible with the short stem were available and used by some for convenience, though many users preferred loading directly into the glass stem.

Draw Technique

  • Temp stepping. The community's favored technique was to start with the temperature dial set low, take several flavorful draws to enjoy terpenes, then gradually increase the dial setting across the session. This was described as "the best way to enjoy flavorful terp hits."
  • Slow sipping at low temps. Multiple experienced users preferred to "milk the low temps" for extended flavor and effect, rather than cranking the dial for fast extraction.
  • MTL-style sipping. For users accustomed to mouth-to-lung vaping (common among former smokers), the Tinymight with a bubbler and whip allowed a sipping style that one community member described as "not quite MTL but still feels quite similar."
  • One-hit use. For on-demand mode, users could take a single hit, release the button, and set the device aside. The convection heating meant minimal continued cooking when not drawing.

Stems

  • Long stems for cooler vapor. Users found that the extra-long glass stem provided noticeably cooler vapor. Custom 5-inch stems from glassblowers such as OregonGlassBlowers were popular.
  • Short stem for portability. The included short stem with cooling unit was the most common choice for on-the-go use.
  • J-hook for home use. Using the Tinymight with a glass j-hook at home was a frequently recommended configuration, particularly for enhanced flavor and cooling.

Temperature Guide

The Tinymight uses an analog dial rather than a digital temperature display, so precise temperature readings are not available on the device itself. Community guidance is therefore described in terms of dial position and qualitative effects:

Dial PositionApproximate Temp RangeEffect
Low (dial near minimum)~160–180°C / ~320–356°FMaximum flavor, light wispy vapor, best terpene expression, mild effects
Low-Medium~180–195°C / ~356–383°FRich flavor with increasing vapor density, good balance of taste and effect
Medium~195–210°C / ~383–410°FFull extraction, thicker vapor, stronger effects
Medium-High to High~210–230°C / ~410–446°FDense clouds, maximum extraction, more sedative/body effects

Community-agreed sweet spot: Most experienced users started low and stepped up, spending the most enjoyable draws in the low-to-medium range for flavor, then finishing at medium-high for full extraction. The temp-stepping approach was considered essential to getting the most from the device.

Note: These temperature estimates are approximate and based on community experience rather than manufacturer-published data. The analog dial does not display exact temperatures.

Maintenance & Cleaning

The Tinymight's glass stems were considered among the easiest components to clean in the portable vaporizer category:

  • Glass stems: Soak in isopropyl alcohol (ISO) periodically, or push a pipe cleaner through the stem between sessions. The glass construction means residue is easily visible and removable.
  • Screens: Replacement screens were included with the device. Screens should be checked regularly and replaced when clogged or damaged. The screen above the heater can accumulate residue over time.
  • Cooling unit: The short stem cooling unit should be disassembled and soaked in ISO as needed.
  • O-rings: Spare o-rings were included. Replace when they lose elasticity or show wear, as they affect the seal between stem and body.
  • Body: The wood body should be wiped clean but not soaked. Avoid getting the electronics wet.

The community generally found the Tinymight low-maintenance compared to many other portables, attributing this to the simple glass air path and convection heating design.

Common Issues & Fixes

Uneven Herb Cooking

Problem: Herb closest to the heater screen browns or darkens faster than the rest of the load.

Fix: Position the herb further up the stem. A gap of at least 1/4 inch (6mm) between the bottom of the stem and the herb load was recommended. Using the longer stem provides more flexibility for positioning.

Thin Ring of Green at Bowl Walls

Problem: After a session, a thin ring of green herb may remain along the inner wall of the glass stem, leading some users to believe stirring is necessary.

Fix: The community consensus was that this represents approximately 1% of the total load and is not worth stirring for. Most experienced users simply ignored it. One member noted they "started off stirring because that thin ring of green bugged me, but you're honestly not going to extract much of anything from it."

Fragility/Breakage from Drops

Problem: At least one user reported the device broke after "one minor fall."

Fix: Use a protective carrying case (see Accessories section). Handle with care, particularly the glass stems.

Pre-Order Wait Times

Problem: New units sometimes required pre-ordering and waiting.

Fix: The secondary market (community classifieds) occasionally had unused or lightly used units available, sometimes at or below retail price. The manufacturer shipped replacement units directly for warranty claims.

Activated Carbon Filter Idea (Not Recommended)

One community member proposed placing activated carbon above the heater screen to filter incoming air and reduce uneven cooking. The community firmly advised against this, citing concerns about carbon dust inhalation. One member warned, "Carbon dust in the lungs is No Bueno!" The recommended alternative was simply repositioning the herb load further up the stem.

Accessories & Modifications

Glass Stems

  • Short stem with cooling unit — Included standard; best for portability.
  • Extra-long glass stem — Available from the manufacturer; provides cooler vapor and more room to position herb away from the heater. Comes with its own set of replacement screens.
  • Custom glass stems — Community members commissioned custom stems from artisan glassblowers (e.g., OregonGlassBlowers). 5-inch stems were popular for home use.

Water Pipe Adapters (WPA)

The Tinymight was frequently used with a WPA for water filtration, particularly by medical users and those seeking smoother hits. The combination of the TM with a small bubbler was a common home-use configuration.

J-Hook

A glass j-hook was one of the most recommended home-use accessories, offering enhanced cooling and flavor without water. Multiple experienced community members specifically mentioned using the TM with a j-hook as their preferred at-home setup.

Whip + Bubbler

For users preferring a mouth-to-lung style draw, the combination of a whip and small bubbler was recommended. This configuration allowed a sipping draw style reminiscent of smoking.

Carrying Cases

Several carrying cases were confirmed compatible by community members:

  • Storz & Bickel Crafty+ carrying case — Confirmed as "the perfect fit" for the Tinymight.
  • Small hard-shell cases (various Amazon options) — One member recommended a case that "perfectly fits the TM2, a stem, and my Mad Heater reload."
  • Chums Surfshorts Wallet — Used by one community member "for years," fitting the TM plus extra stems or a doob tube and spare batteries in a compact form factor.

Dosing Capsules

Capsules compatible with the short stem were available, allowing pre-loading and cleaner operation.

Mad Heater Reload

Mentioned as a companion accessory carried alongside the TM2, suggesting it was used for convenient herb loading on the go.

Community consensus: For micro-dosing, on-demand one-hit use, and flavor exploration through temp stepping, the Tinymight was generally preferred. The FW7 was favored for its superior durability, simpler operation, and slightly more portable form factor. One experienced community member summarized: "You really can't go wrong with either."

Tinymight vs. Mighty/Mighty+

The Tinymight was frequently compared with the Storz & Bickel Mighty as users debated between the two. The TM was recommended over the Mighty for users who valued on-demand hits, micro-dosing, and flavor from temp stepping. The Mighty was acknowledged as the easier session vaporizer for set-and-forget use but was seen as less efficient for small loads due to its heat-up time and session-style operation that could waste herb for lightweights.

Tinymight vs. Splinter Z / RBT Devices

Community members who owned RBT (Rastabuddhateepee) devices like the Splinter Z found the Tinymight to be a worthy successor. One member stated that "all my RBT stuff is more sparingly used as a result of me getting the TM." The TM was seen as offering similar convection flavor and fast extraction without requiring a separate box mod.

Tinymight vs. Dynavap

For users looking to move beyond butane torch-heated devices like the Dynavap, the Tinymight was recommended as a natural upgrade path—offering similar on-demand, small-bowl capability but with push-button electronic convenience. The TM's larger bowl capacity and lack of torch requirement were seen as advantages, while the Dynavap maintained an edge in price, simplicity, and durability.

Tinymight vs. Log Vaporizers

One member who had "always had some sort of log" for home use adopted the Tinymight as their portable and found it "the closest to my logs as far as taste, ease of use." This comparison highlighted the TM's convection flavor quality as approaching desktop-class performance in a portable form factor.

Tinymight vs. Xmax V3 Pro

For micro-dosing specifically, the Tinymight was positioned as the premium option versus the budget-friendly Xmax V3 Pro. One community member described the pairing as "(fancy/high class) Tinymight + WPA, (budget but still good) Xmax V3 Pro + WPA." Another noted the V3 Pro was not particularly efficient with true micro-dose loads (0.02g), requiring excessive draw effort for minimal output.

Tinymight vs. Vapman

Both were regarded as excellent micro-dosers with outstanding flavor. The Tinymight's advantages were convenience ("you pretty much just press a button and go") and portability, while the Vapman offered a more ritualistic, torch-heated experience with exceptional flavor. The community saw them as complementary rather than competitive, with several users keeping both in rotation.
