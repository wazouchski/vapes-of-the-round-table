# Splinter Z

Overview

The Splinter Z is a 510-threaded dry herb vaporizer designed and manufactured by RBT (Ryan's Bespoke Tinkers), a small-batch artisan vaporizer maker. It is the successor and refinement of the original Splinter, featuring an upgraded heater design. The Splinter Z utilizes a predominantly convection heating method, drawing hot air through ground herb loaded into a glass stem. As a 510-threaded atomizer, it requires a separate box mod (e-cigarette battery/controller) to operate, relying on external removable batteries (typically 18650 cells) housed in the mod.

The "Z" designation refers to the Zion-style heater used in the device, an evolution of the heating element design that RBT had refined across earlier products. The body of the Splinter Z is crafted from wood, giving each unit a handmade, artisanal character. RBT's production was limited in scale, and by the early 2020s the Splinter Z became increasingly difficult to obtain new, though units continued to circulate on the secondhand market.

Community Reception

The Splinter Z was very highly regarded within the vaporizer community. Users who owned one frequently described it as a near-perfect daily driver. One community member from Australia, who had used the Splinter Z for several years, praised it effusively, noting that it "basically seems perfect," highlighting its fast heat-up time in temperature control mode, amazing taste, efficient extraction in 3–4 draws, and excellent battery life. This user reported reaching for the Splinter Z over their DynaVap collection the majority of the time.

The community widely acknowledged that the Splinter Z delivered an outstanding flavor experience, placing it in the upper tier of portable convection vaporizers. Several members noted it was difficult to find a portable vaporizer that surpassed it—one community member bluntly advised a Splinter Z owner seeking an upgrade: "You're probably not going to find a portable you like anymore than the Z."

Pros frequently cited by the community:

  • Exceptional flavor quality
  • Fast heat-up when used in temperature control mode
  • Efficient extraction (small number of draws per bowl)
  • Long battery life relative to other portables
  • Beautiful artisan wood construction
  • Versatile — compatible with various stems and water pipe adapters

Cons and concerns:

  • Availability — RBT's limited production meant new units were difficult to acquire; by 2020–2022, the community noted they were primarily available used or through remaining stock at select retailers (PlanetVape, VapeFiend, and others were mentioned as still carrying some units)
  • Portability — Like most 510 dry herb atomizers, the combined height of the box mod, Splinter Z, and glass stem made the total assembly quite tall and not pocket-friendly. One community member noted this was their "biggest problem" with most 510-connected devices
  • Irreplaceability — As production ceased, owners became increasingly protective of their units, with one member noting the importance of protecting the device since replacement was essentially impossible ("good luck finding a replacement")
  • Learning curve — While the device itself was straightforward, choosing an appropriate box mod and configuring TC or wattage settings added a layer of complexity, particularly for beginners

Tips & Techniques

Loading

The Splinter Z uses a glass stem that accepts ground herb. Standard dome screens or basket screens can be used to contain the material. A medium-fine grind was generally preferred for convection efficiency.

Operating Modes

  • Temperature Control (TC) Mode: The preferred mode for most experienced users. When properly configured with the correct TCR value on the box mod, TC mode allows the device to heat up in seconds and maintain a consistent temperature, virtually eliminating the risk of combustion. A TCR setting of approximately 185 was recommended by the maker for Z-style heater builds.
  • Wattage (Watts) Mode: Also viable, though it requires more user attention and draw technique to avoid overheating the herb. One community member reported successfully using the Splinter Z in watts mode with an XL8R stem, noting they never combusted due to lack of feedback. However, the community generally treated watts mode as requiring more caution.

Draw Technique

A full bowl can be extracted in approximately 3–4 draws when using temperature control mode, making the Splinter Z function in a semi-on-demand fashion. Users should draw slowly and steadily to allow convection airflow through the herb.

Cooling Stems and Feedback

A topic of community discussion was whether cooling stems (such as the XL8R) reduced thermal feedback enough to risk combustion in unregulated or watts-mode use. One member reported that the XL8R stem used with the Splinter Z in watts mode still provided adequate feedback to avoid combustion. For those particularly concerned, the XL8R Jr (a shorter version) was suggested as a compromise — it cools vapor noticeably while remaining short enough to preserve more thermal feedback in the mouth.

Temperature Guide

Because the Splinter Z relies on an external box mod for temperature control, exact temperature settings depend on the mod's firmware, TCR configuration, and the specific coil resistance of the unit. Community members generally used standard dry herb vaporization temperature ranges:

EffectTemperature Range
Flavor-focused / light effects170–185°C (338–365°F)
Balanced flavor and vapor production185–200°C (365–392°F)
Full extraction / dense clouds200–220°C (392–428°F)

The community noted that in TC mode, the Splinter Z's convection heating provided excellent flavor at lower temperatures, which was cited as one of its strongest attributes. Users were advised to step up temperature through the session to fully extract the bowl.

TCR Sweet Spot: A TCR value of approximately 185 was recommended by the maker (referenced in a community listing of a custom Z-heater build). Users should verify and fine-tune this value on their specific mod, as TCR accuracy varies between mod firmware implementations.

Maintenance & Cleaning

The Splinter Z's glass stems are the primary components requiring regular cleaning. As with most convection vaporizers using glass pathways:

  • Glass stems: Soak in isopropyl alcohol (ISO) periodically to remove resin buildup. Stems can also be pushed through with a pipe cleaner. The community generally noted that convection-dominant vaporizers like the Splinter Z stayed cleaner than conduction units.
  • Screens: Basket screens and dome screens should be replaced or cleaned as they become clogged. Soaking in ISO and rinsing is standard practice.
  • Heater chamber: Occasional dry burn (firing the heater briefly without a stem or herb loaded) can help clear any residue from the heater area. Care should be taken not to overheat the wood body.
  • Wood body: Avoid submerging in any liquids. The wood can be wiped with a lightly damp cloth if needed.

Common Issues & Fixes

Mod Overheating

One community member who used the Splinter Z as a daily driver reported that heat from the atomizer could transfer down into the box mod, warming the mod and batteries. Their solution was to build a 510 extension cable base — a fixed extension cable that physically separated the Splinter Z from the mod. This was reported to completely eliminate heat transfer to the mod and batteries, with the added benefits of improving the device's footprint/stability and ergonomics.

Availability / Replacement Parts

By the early 2020s, the Splinter Z was no longer in regular production. The community advised that used units appeared for sale periodically. Select retailers (including VGoodiez, PlanetVape, and VapeFiend) were noted as still carrying limited stock at various points. The community consensus was to treat a Splinter Z as irreplaceable and take steps to protect it — including the use of 510 extension cables to reduce physical and thermal stress on the 510 connection.

TC Mode Configuration

Users new to 510 dry herb vaporizers sometimes struggled with configuring temperature control correctly on their box mods. The community emphasized the importance of using a TC-capable mod with reliable firmware (such as mods running Arctic Fox or similar custom firmware) and correctly setting the TCR value.

Accessories & Modifications

Stems

  • RBT XL8R Stem: A glass cooling stem with internal ridges that cool vapor. Highly recommended by the community for the Splinter Z and other RBT products. Provides noticeably cooler vapor while still allowing sufficient feedback in watts mode.
  • RBT XL8R Jr Stem: A shorter version of the XL8R, recommended for users who want some cooling without excessive length, or who are concerned about maintaining adequate thermal feedback.
  • Standard glass stems: Basic straight glass stems work well and are the most compact option.
  • Custom glass stems: Some community members used custom-made stems from glassblowers.

510 Extension Cable Base

One community member developed a DIY 510 extension cable base specifically for the Splinter Z. This modification:

  • Physically separated the atomizer from the box mod
  • Eliminated heat transfer to the mod and batteries
  • Improved the overall stability and footprint of the device
  • Created a more ergonomic form factor
  • Allowed mixing and matching of mods and atomizers more easily

The member went through multiple iterations of the design and considered it an important longevity measure for protecting an increasingly irreplaceable device.

Box Mods

The Splinter Z requires a 510-compatible box mod. Community members used various mods, including:

  • Joyetech VTC Dual: Mentioned in at least one community listing as a paired mod
  • TC-capable mods with reliable firmware were strongly preferred
  • Dual-battery mods were common for extended battery life

Water Pipe Adapters (WPA)

As a stem-based device with a standard 19mm glass interface, the Splinter Z is inherently compatible with water pipe adapters, allowing use through bubblers and water pipes for additional cooling.

Custom Builds

Community member unklmark was noted for performing custom rebuilds of Splinter bodies with Z-style heaters, including using alternative wood species like Blackwood. These custom builds maintained the Z heater design in different enclosures, sometimes using original Splinter v1 bodies.

One community member summarized the Stempod's value proposition: "Stempod Si is about $60, a box mod set you back $30–$50. Can't beat it at all." However, the Splinter Z was generally considered the more refined and higher-performing device.

Splinter Z vs. Tinymight

When Splinter Z owners sought an upgrade or alternative, the Tinymight was the most frequently recommended device. Multiple community members, including prominent contributors, endorsed this recommendation:

  • One long-time community member stated that "all my RBT stuff is more sparingly used as a result of me getting the TM [Tinymight]."
  • Another member described the Tinymight as "the closest to my logs as far as taste, ease of use, and very easy to keep clean," using it as a portable alternative.
  • The Tinymight was noted as a standalone device (no box mod required), improving portability significantly.

The community consensus was that the Tinymight was the closest self-contained portable to matching the Splinter Z's convection flavor quality, and for many users, it became the natural successor.

Splinter Z vs. Other 510 Devices

The broader landscape of 510 dry herb atomizers discussed alongside the Splinter Z included:

  • Imp / Impcognito: Recommended by community members as alternatives, with the Impcognito noted as the most portable option due to compatibility with side-by-side mods
  • iHeat: Mentioned as similar to the Imp and worth considering
  • Dreamwood Glow: Available from Germany (or through US retailer VGoodiez), noted as another quality 510 option though international shipping could be slow
  • RBT Milana 3: Another RBT product (not 510-based), available directly from RBT's website when the Splinter Z was not

All 510 devices shared the common drawback of overall assembly length when mounted on a top-fire box mod with a stem attached, making true pocket portability challenging.
