# Tinymight 2

Overview

The Tinymight 2 (commonly abbreviated as TM2) is a portable dry herb vaporizer manufactured by Tinymight, a small Finnish artisan company. Released in 2022, it is considered the third iteration of the Tinymight design lineage, following the original Tinymight and an intermediate version sometimes referred to as the Tinymight 1.5. As one community member noted, "The TinyMight 2 is the third iteration of the TM, because there was a 1.5 version. The TM team have been very good at implementing feedback from customers so I don't think it's an entirely untested product."

The TM2 is primarily a convection vaporizer, powered by a single user-replaceable 18650 battery. It supports both on-demand and session heating modes, making it one of the more versatile portable units available. The device features a tube-style form factor and is known for its powerful extraction, flavor quality, and the flexibility afforded by its replaceable battery system.

Community Reception

The vaporizer community regarded the Tinymight 2 highly, particularly among users who favored convection heating. It was frequently recommended as one of the best portable convection vaporizers available, with experienced community members expressing strong enthusiasm for its performance.

Pros commonly cited:

  • Exceptional convection extraction — Community members consistently praised the TM2's ability to produce flavorful, efficient vapor. One experienced member described the difference from mid-range portables as "huge," stating the TM2 would "replace both" a Solo 2 and POTV One, offering "much more power and efficiency" and the ability to "get you higher with the extraction with less material."
  • Replaceable 18650 battery — This was considered a significant advantage over competitors with sealed batteries (such as the Mighty+), as it extends the device's lifespan indefinitely and allows users to carry spare batteries.
  • Versatile on-demand and session modes — The on-demand capability with a full temperature range was highlighted as offering far more versatility than many competing devices.
  • No stirring required — Multiple community members confirmed that stirring is unnecessary during use. One noted: "I've never stirred my TM (1 and 2). It's a great vape." Another elaborated that while "there will sometimes be some green left at the wall of the bowl," this represents "maybe 1% of your flower" and attempting to extract from it yields negligible returns.
  • Build quality and design heritage — As an artisan product with iterative design improvements informed by customer feedback, the TM2 was seen as a mature product despite its relatively recent release.

Cons and concerns noted:

  • Draw resistance with dosing capsules — Users reported noticeably increased draw resistance when using dosing capsules, even without tamping the material. The device was described as "pretty good with the capsules" but "way better without them."
  • Price — At $349 USD (and significantly more in Canada with taxes), the TM2 represented a substantial investment. Some users found this difficult to justify, particularly when upgrading from functional mid-range devices.
  • Learning curve and technique sensitivity — While not extensively documented in the available threads, the convection on-demand nature of the device implies draw technique matters more than with simpler session conduction vaporizers.
  • Concerns about newer product reliability — Some prospective buyers expressed caution about purchasing newer vaporizer models, though experienced community members countered that the TM2 benefited from years of iterative development across the Tinymight platform.

Tips & Techniques

Loading Methods

  • Glass stem with basket screen — The preferred method for most experienced users. The TM2 ships with at least one rimless basket screen. Material can be loaded directly into the stem, with the basket screen retaining the herb. Both fine and coarser grinds work, though technique may vary with grind consistency.
  • Whole nug or chunky grind — Some accessories, particularly the 14mm and multi 3-in-1 WPA with glass honeycomb screens, were noted to work best with whole nugs or very chunky grinds. One experienced member mentioned they "only enjoy [the honeycomb screens] with a whole nug or super chunky grind."
  • Dosing capsules — Convenient for on-the-go use and keeping the device clean. Community members noted that capsules add "a bit of conduction" to the otherwise convection-dominant extraction. However, draw resistance increases when using capsules, even without tamping the material. Capsules were described as convenient but "not a feature of the Tinymight" — meaning the device was not primarily designed around capsule use.

Draw Technique

  • The on-demand mode allows users to take hits at their own pace without the device continuously heating. Using the full temperature range means extraction does not have to be fast — users have significant control over vapor density and flavor by adjusting temperature and draw speed.
  • No stirring is required between hits. Community members who initially stirred due to a thin ring of green remaining at the bowl walls eventually stopped, finding that the remaining unextracted material was negligible.

All-Glass Airpath Configuration

One well-known community member described their preferred setup as using "all glass pathways (except for steel mesh basket screen inside the stem or WPA connected to a hook or other dry glass)," noting that while the stock setup works well, the all-glass configuration was their favorite approach.

Temperature Guide

Specific temperature settings in degrees were not extensively documented in the available community discussions. However, the following general guidance was reflected:

  • Lower temperatures — Prioritize flavor and lighter effects. The convection heating allows excellent terp preservation at lower settings.
  • Mid-range temperatures — Balance of flavor and extraction efficiency.
  • Higher temperatures — Maximize extraction and cloud production.

The TM2's adjustable temperature dial provides a full temperature range, and its on-demand heating mode means users can effectively control extraction intensity through a combination of temperature setting and draw technique. The community widely agreed that the device's versatility across its temperature range was one of its strongest attributes.

The wiki would benefit from additional community data on specific temperature sweet spots for the TM2.

Maintenance & Cleaning

General community consensus held that all vaporizers require regular cleaning, as cannabis inevitably leaves residue. The TM2's relatively simple airpath — primarily glass stems and stainless steel screens — was seen as straightforward to maintain compared to devices with more complex vapor paths.

  • Glass stems — Can be soaked in isopropyl alcohol for easy cleaning. Reclaim (condensed oil) from the stems can be collected and used.
  • Basket screens — Should be periodically cleaned or replaced. Spare screens are available from the manufacturer and third-party accessory makers.
  • Dosing capsules — When used, capsules help keep the oven cleaner by containing the herb material. The ceramic-coated ovens found in some competing devices (like the updated Crafty+) were noted to stay cleaner without capsules, but the TM2's capsule system provides a similar convenience benefit.

Community members contrasted the TM2's maintenance favorably with the Mighty+, whose "built-in cooling unit that the vapor winds through can especially be a pain to clean."

Common Issues & Fixes

Draw Resistance with Dosing Capsules

Issue: Noticeably increased draw resistance when using dosing capsules, even without tamping.

Fix: Use the device without capsules for the best airflow. If capsule convenience is desired, avoid tamping material and ensure a loose pack. Some users accepted the trade-off for the convenience and cleanliness benefits of capsules.

Thin Ring of Green at Bowl Walls

Issue: After a session, a thin ring of green (unextracted) herb may remain at the very edge of the bowl wall.

Fix: No action needed. Community members confirmed this represents approximately 1% of the loaded material and does not meaningfully affect efficiency. Stirring to address it was found to be unnecessary.

Battery Charger Selection

Issue: As many users' first 18650-powered vaporizer, charger selection was a common question.

Fix: The community recommended the following chargers:

  • XTAR VC2 — A well-regarded 2-bay charger with display screen
  • Nitecore models (various 2-slot and 4-slot options) — The Nitecore UMS4 was specifically recommended for users wanting four charging slots

Investing in a quality charger and spare 18650 batteries was considered essential for getting the most out of the TM2's replaceable battery system.

Accessories & Modifications

Glass Stems

The TM2 uses glass stems as its primary delivery method, and a vibrant aftermarket ecosystem exists. Community-recommended stem makers include:

  • Good Vibes Boro (GVB) — Highly regarded custom stems. Noted to accept flat screens as well as standard basket screens. Described by one enthusiast as a personal favorite.
  • Rogue Waxworks — Another popular artisan stem maker. Their stems typically ship with screens and spare screens are available.
  • Official Tinymight stems — Available directly from the manufacturer in various configurations.

Water Pipe Adapters (WPA)

Multiple WPA options are available:

  • Official 18mm TM WPA — A plain adapter described as working well. Recommended for use with basket screens.
  • Official 14mm WPA — Features a glass honeycomb screen; best suited for whole nugs or very chunky grinds.
  • Multi 3-in-1 WPA — Also features a glass honeycomb screen with similar grind requirements to the 14mm version.

Dosing Capsules

Compatible dosing capsules are available and offer convenience for preloading sessions and keeping the device clean. They add a slight conduction element to the heating profile.

Screens

  • Rimless basket screens — At least one is included with the device. Additional screens can be purchased from the manufacturer or third-party stem makers.
  • Flat screens — Compatible with certain aftermarket stems (notably Good Vibes Boro stems).
  • Glass honeycomb screens — Found in the 14mm and multi 3-in-1 WPA attachments.

Comparisons

Tinymight 2 vs. Mighty+ (Storz & Bickel)

This was the most frequently discussed comparison in the community. Key differentiators:

Heating MethodPrimarily convectionHybrid (conduction + convection)
BatteryReplaceable 18650Sealed (non-replaceable)
Operating ModeOn-demand + sessionSession only

Dosing CapsulesCompatible but not optimized; adds draw resistanceWell-integrated system

Community consensus strongly favored the TM2 for convection enthusiasts, with one member stating simply: "If you like convection, TM2 is a much better choice than the Mighty." The Mighty+ was acknowledged as a reliable, well-established device, but its sealed battery was seen as a significant drawback — described by one prospective buyer as a scenario where "the unit after 2 (3 with registration) years can die on you at any point." Multiple community members advised that users leaning toward the TM2 should go with their instinct, noting that "the Mighty will always be around" as a future purchase if desired.

Tinymight 2 vs. Solo 2 (Arizer)

The TM2 was described as a significant upgrade from the Solo 2, offering "much more power and efficiency." Both devices share a tube-style form factor with glass stems, which was noted as an appealing similarity for Solo 2 users considering the upgrade. The TM2's on-demand capability and faster extraction were highlighted as major advantages, though the Solo 2's lower price point and simplicity remain attractive for users with modest needs.

Tinymight 2 vs. POTV One

The TM2 was considered superior in extraction quality and efficiency but significantly more expensive. One community member noted that taste from the POTV One was "not as great as Solo 2," implying the TM2 would offer further improvement in flavor.

Tinymight 2 vs. Roffu

Both devices share the replaceable 18650 battery feature. The Roffu was recommended as a more affordable alternative for users prioritizing dosing capsule use, compactness, and ease of battery swapping, while the TM2 was generally considered the superior performer overall.

Tinymight 2 vs. Firewood 8

The Firewood 8 was mentioned as an alternative worth investigating specifically for users who prioritize dosing capsule use, though direct comparative analysis was limited in the available discussions.
