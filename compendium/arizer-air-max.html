# Arizer Air MAX

Overview

The Arizer Air MAX is a portable dry herb vaporizer manufactured by Arizer, a Canadian company with a long-established presence in the vaporizer market. Released in early 2022, the Air MAX represents an evolution of Arizer's popular Air line, building upon the designs of the original Air and the Air II. It utilizes a hybrid heating method consistent with Arizer's established approach, featuring the company's signature glass stem/mouthpiece system where herb is packed into a borosilicate glass tube that inserts into the heating chamber.

The Air MAX is powered by a user-replaceable 26650 lithium-ion battery, marking a significant departure from the 18650 cells used in earlier Air models. This battery choice was one of the most discussed aspects of the device within the vaporizer community.

Community Reception

Community reception of the Arizer Air MAX was mixed, with much of the discussion centering on Arizer's decision to use the 26650 battery format rather than the device's vapor quality or performance characteristics.

Pros

  • Continuation of Arizer's well-regarded glass stem airpath system
  • Replaceable battery design, allowing for extended portable sessions
  • USB-C charging (an upgrade from earlier models)
  • Compatibility with the extensive Arizer glass accessory ecosystem
  • Strong hits reported at higher temperature settings (around 220°C / 428°F)

Cons

  • The 26650 battery format was widely criticized within the community. Multiple members expressed that 21700 batteries would have been a far better choice, noting that the 26650 format had fallen out of favor in the wider battery-powered device market by 2018. One community member with extensive experience in nicotine vaping noted the difficulty in sourcing quality 26650 cells that are not "rewraps" (batteries relabeled by third parties with inflated specifications).
  • Concerns about airpath cleanliness were raised, with one member noting that earlier Arizer Solo II models drew air through the charger port and past internal electronics, and expressing hope that the Air MAX had addressed this issue.
  • The larger physical size necessitated by the 26650 battery was viewed as a drawback by some who might have otherwise purchased the device.

Tips & Techniques

Community discussion around the Air MAX was relatively limited compared to other Arizer models, but general Arizer-line best practices apply:

  • Loading the stem: Pack the glass aroma tube gently with a medium grind. Arizer stems work best when not overpacked, allowing airflow through the material.
  • Draw technique: Slow, steady draws are recommended to maximize convection heating through the glass stem.
  • High-temperature finishing: Community members referenced using 220°C (428°F) for strong hits, consistent with Arizer's general usage patterns.
  • Pre-heat: Allow the device to reach set temperature before beginning to draw for best results.

Temperature Guide

While specific Air MAX temperature recommendations were sparse in the available community data, the following general Arizer guidance applies:

One community member specifically referenced 220°C as a setting for strong hits on Arizer devices.

Maintenance & Cleaning

The Arizer Air MAX benefits from the same straightforward cleaning regimen as other Arizer glass-stem devices:

  • Glass stems: Soak in isopropyl alcohol (90%+ recommended). Stems can also be simmered in milk or a fat-containing liquid to reclaim active compounds from the reclaim buildup.
  • Heating chamber: Use a dry cotton swab or one lightly dampened with isopropyl alcohol to clean the oven. Never submerge the unit.
  • Stem O-rings: Inspect periodically and replace if they become loose or cracked, as a proper seal is essential for vapor quality.
  • ABV (Already Been Vaped) removal: Tap out spent material while the stem is still warm for easiest cleaning.

Common Issues & Fixes

26650 Battery Sourcing

The most widely discussed issue was finding quality replacement 26650 batteries. Community advice included:

  • Stick with Arizer's own batteries if possible, despite the higher cost, to avoid counterfeit or overrated cells.
  • Avoid rewraps: One experienced community member strongly cautioned against trusting rewrapped 26650 batteries, noting that many on the market have inflated amperage and capacity ratings. Reputable 26650 cells from known manufacturers are difficult to find.
  • Batteries discussed by the community included the Vapcell 26650 32A 4200mAh, Vapcell G53 26650 20A 5300mAh, MXJO 26650 3500mAh 25A, and Hohm Tech Grown 26650 4244mAh. No definitive community consensus emerged on a "best" third-party option.

Using 18650 Batteries with Adapter Sleeves

One community member inquired about using 18650 batteries (such as the Sony VTC6 or Molicel P26A) with plastic adapter sleeves designed to convert 18650 cells to 26650 form factor. Community feedback suggested:

  • Since only the width differs (all are 3.6–3.7V nominal lithium-ion cells), adapters should work in principle as long as good electrical contact is maintained.
  • Battery life will be significantly reduced due to the lower capacity of 18650 cells (~2600–3000mAh) compared to 26650 cells (~4200–5300mAh).
  • High-drain 18650 cells like the Molicel P28A were suggested as having sufficient amperage for the device.
  • This approach was discussed as a workaround rather than an ideal solution, and no long-term reliability data was shared.

Airpath Cleanliness Concerns

A community member raised concerns about whether the Air MAX's airpath was truly isolated from internal electronics, referencing a known issue with the Solo II where intake air passed through the charger port and over the circuit board. It was not definitively confirmed in community discussion whether Arizer had fully addressed this in the Air MAX design.

Accessories & Modifications

The Air MAX is compatible with Arizer's extensive glass accessory ecosystem:

  • Standard and short glass aroma tubes
  • Water pipe adapters (WPA): Arizer's glass WPA stems allow direct connection to 14mm and 18mm water pieces
  • Third-party glass stems from various artisan glassblowers in the community
  • 18650 to 26650 battery adapter sleeves: Plastic adapters (commonly sold for flashlights) were discussed as a way to use more readily available 18650 batteries, though with reduced battery life

Comparisons

Air MAX vs. Arizer Solo 2

A direct comparison was sought by at least one community member choosing between the two devices. Key points raised:

  • The Solo 2 was noted for strong hits at 220°C but was criticized for its airpath not being fully clean—air reportedly enters through the 12V charger port and passes over electronics before reaching the herb.
  • The Air MAX offered the advantage of being Arizer's more current design with a replaceable battery and USB-C charging, but at a higher price point.
  • Community advice leaned toward either getting the Air MAX as the newer device or, alternatively, looking beyond the Arizer line entirely toward devices with confirmed clean airpaths and stronger performance, such as the Tinymight, Tetra P80, FW7/FW8, or Tafee Bowle.

Air MAX vs. Devices Using Other Battery Formats

The Air MAX's use of the 26650 battery was unfavorably compared to devices using 18650 or 21700 cells. Community members noted that 21700 batteries offer a better balance of capacity, size, and availability, with high-quality cells from major manufacturers being readily accessible. The 26650 format was seen as an outdated and inconvenient choice that complicated an otherwise appealing portable design.
