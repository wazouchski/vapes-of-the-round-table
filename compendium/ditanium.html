# Ditanium

Overview

The Ditanium is a desktop whip-style vaporizer manufactured by Ditanium Vaporizers (marketed under the enailvaporizer.com brand). It is designed as an "all-in-one" unit capable of vaporizing dry herb (flower), concentrates, or both simultaneously. The Ditanium uses a hybrid heating method, employing a ceramic heating element that heats both a hand piece/wand for flower and a quartz dish on top of the unit for concentrates. It is an analog (dial-controlled) desktop vaporizer powered by a standard AC outlet.

The Ditanium carved out a niche in the desktop vaporizer market as one of the few units that could elegantly handle both flower and concentrates in a single session. Its distinctive visual design—often compared to a piece of art or decor—and the option to "enhance" flower hits by simultaneously vaporizing concentrates on the quartz dish earned it a dedicated following within the vaporizer community.

Community Reception

The Ditanium received a generally positive but nuanced reception within the vaporizer community. It was praised for its dual-use capability (flower and concentrates), attractive design, and the unique experience of combining flower and concentrate hits. Several community members described it as a "monster desktop" and appreciated its versatility.

Pros:

  • All-in-one flower and concentrate capability, with the option to use both simultaneously
  • Visually appealing design; often cited as one of the better-looking desktop vapes
  • Large bowl suitable for sharing or extended sessions
  • Lifetime warranty
  • Compatible with 7th Floor Vapes accessories and whip sets
  • Works well through water via included WPA adapters

Cons:

  • Higher price point, especially in European markets where it was reported to cost roughly twice what comparable whip vapes (such as the Da Buddha) cost
  • Analog temperature control only—no digital readout, which some users found less precise
  • Flavor was noted to diminish relatively quickly in the large bowl; one community member observed that "flavour dies off quickly leaving you with a bland bowl to finish off," meaning the first few draws were flavorful but later draws became "roasty" and bland
  • When sharing, this flavor drop-off meant that one person would enjoy the best flavor while others received lesser-quality hits
  • In head-to-head comparisons with higher-end desktop units like the Flowerpot, the Ditanium was generally considered less powerful in terms of vapor density and extraction efficiency

One community member who owned the Ditanium alongside a Flowerpot was candid: the Flowerpot produced denser, more powerful vapor and was quicker to reload, somewhat negating the Ditanium's advantage of a larger shared bowl. However, other members valued the Ditanium for its specific strengths, particularly as a casual desktop concentrate setup that could be left out and used throughout an evening.

A member who had been using the Ditanium for several years noted appreciation for it as a casual desktop concentrate station—something attractive enough to leave sitting out on a desk or table. For users prioritizing concentrates over flower, the Ditanium's always-on quartz dish was considered a genuine convenience.

Tips & Techniques

Loading and Draw Technique

  • The Ditanium's bowl is relatively large compared to other desktop vaporizers. For solo use, users found they did not need to fill the bowl completely; using basket screens allowed for microdosing with smaller loads.
  • Custom whip sets from 7th Floor Vapes were noted to be compatible and could be paired with basket screens for microdosing or water pipe use.
  • For shared sessions, the community advised loading fresh bowls for each person rather than packing one large bowl to share, as flavor quality dropped off significantly after the first few draws.

Flower and Concentrate Combination

  • The Ditanium's signature technique involves loading flower in the hand piece and placing a small amount of concentrate on the quartz dish simultaneously. Drawing through the whip pulls vapor from both sources for a combined hit.
  • Users recommended starting with the flower at a moderate temperature and experimenting with the amount of concentrate on the quartz dish to find a comfortable balance.

Hand Piece Versions

  • Community members noted that the Ditanium shipped with different versions of the glass hand piece over time. The earlier "v1" hand piece (without a built-in glass screen) was widely preferred over the later "v2" version (which included a glass screen). One community member selling their unit explicitly described the v1 as "the good one" and the v2 as "the bad one."

Ball Mod

  • At least one community member successfully applied a ball modification (using 3mm Si₃N₄ / silicon nitride balls) to the Ditanium, a technique borrowed from the broader ball-vape modding community. This mod was reported to improve heat retention and vapor production.

Temperature Guide

Because the Ditanium uses an analog dial without a digital temperature display, precise temperature recommendations in degrees are not readily available. The community generally approached temperature settings as follows:

  • Low dial settings: Best for flavor-forward sessions; produces lighter, more terpy vapor. Suitable for solo, low-temperature sipping.
  • Mid dial settings: Balanced flavor and vapor production; the most commonly used range for everyday flower sessions.
  • Higher dial settings: Denser clouds, more complete extraction, but with diminished flavor and increased risk of harsh or "roasty" hits. Necessary for effective concentrate vaporization on the quartz dish.

Users were advised to experiment with dial position gradually, as analog controls can vary slightly between units. No specific community-agreed "sweet spot" dial position was documented in available discussions.

Maintenance & Cleaning

Quartz Dish

  • The quartz concentrate dish should be kept clean for optimal flavor. Users noted that a clean quartz dish was a selling point when listing units for resale, indicating that residue buildup is common with regular concentrate use.

Whip and Tubing

  • Silicone whip tubing should be replaced periodically. Spare whips were included in accessory bundles, and replacement silicone whips were readily available.

Screens

  • Titanium screens were available as replacements and were included in accessory packs. Regular screen replacement or cleaning (via isopropyl alcohol soak) was recommended.

Glass Hand Piece

  • The glass hand piece and wand should be soaked in isopropyl alcohol for cleaning, following standard glass vaporizer accessory maintenance procedures.

Common Issues & Fixes

Flavor Drop-Off in Large Bowls

  • Issue: Flavor diminishes quickly when using the full-size bowl, leaving later draws bland and roasty.
  • Fix: Load smaller amounts, use basket screens for microdosing, or reload fresh bowls more frequently rather than packing one large bowl.

Hand Piece Version Preference

  • Issue: The v2 glass hand piece (with built-in glass screen) was disliked by some users.
  • Fix: Seek out the v1 glass hand piece (without glass screen), which was the community-preferred version. Titanium hand piece bundles were also available as an alternative.

Analog Temperature Control Imprecision

  • Issue: Lack of digital temperature readout makes it difficult to dial in precise temperatures.
  • Fix: Mark preferred dial positions once found; some users noted that this is inherent to the analog design and recommended digital-controlled alternatives (such as the Herborizer Ti digital) for users who strongly preferred precise temperature control.

Accessories & Modifications

Compatible Accessories

  • 7th Floor Vapes whip sets: Custom black whip sets and other 7th Floor accessories were confirmed compatible, allowing users to tailor their whip setup.
  • Basket screens: Enabled microdosing with smaller loads in the hand piece.
  • Titanium hand piece bundle: Included titanium mouthpiece and titanium handset; popular upgrade from stock glass.
  • Whipless glass adapter: Allowed direct connection to a water pipe without a whip, for a more streamlined setup.
  • Universal concentrate adapter: Expanded concentrate compatibility.
  • 14mm and 18mm WPA: Included with standard whip set for water pipe use.

Ball Modification

  • Community members experimented with filling the hand piece with 3mm Si₃N₄ (silicon nitride) balls, following the ball-vape modification trend. This was reported to improve heat distribution and vapor consistency. At least one community member sold a "ball-modded Ditanium" as a distinct configuration.

Comparisons

Ditanium vs. Da Buddha (7th Floor Vapes)

One community member who owned both units ranked the Da Buddha above the Ditanium in pure flower performance, though they noted using all their whip-style desktops regularly. The Da Buddha was significantly cheaper, especially in European markets where the Ditanium was reported at roughly double the price. For users focused solely on flower vaporization without concentrate needs, the Da Buddha represented strong value. However, the Ditanium's concentrate capability and aesthetic appeal gave it an edge for users seeking a dual-purpose unit.

Ditanium vs. Vapor Brothers VB1

The Vapor Brothers VB1 was ranked third in performance behind the Da Buddha and Ditanium by one community member who owned all three, though another experienced user considered the Vapor Brothers (particularly the non-hands-free version) among their all-time favorites. The VB1 was the cheapest of the three and was praised for its visual appeal. For users on a budget who primarily vape flower, the Vapor Brothers was considered a viable and well-loved alternative.

Ditanium vs. Flowerpot

This was the most extensively discussed comparison. Community members who owned both units generally agreed that the Flowerpot was the superior flower vaporizer in terms of vapor density, extraction power, and overall performance. One member was particularly detailed: the Flowerpot produced "so much denser and more powerful" vapor, and its small, quickly reloadable bowls meant that sharing was actually more practical than with the Ditanium's large bowl, since each person could receive a fresh, flavorful bowl. The Flowerpot (particularly with the V-Rod head) was also recommended for users who prioritized concentrates alongside flower.

However, the Ditanium was acknowledged as a more approachable, less intimidating unit—better suited to casual use, aesthetically pleasant enough to leave on a desk or table, and simpler in setup. For users seeking a relaxed "leave it on and take a hit" experience rather than maximum extraction performance, the Ditanium had its place.

Ditanium vs. Herborizer Ti (Digital)

The Herborizer Ti digital was mentioned as a competitor in the high-end desktop space, particularly for users who preferred digital temperature control. Direct head-to-head comparisons between the Ditanium and Herborizer were limited in available discussions, but the Herborizer's digital precision was noted as an advantage for users who found the Ditanium's analog dial insufficient.

Ditanium vs. Arizer Extreme Q

One user was upgrading from an Arizer Extreme Q (with D-Dave mod) to a more capable unit and included the Ditanium among their considerations. The Extreme Q's cyclone bowl was noted for potentially requiring less stirring, but the Ditanium's concentrate capability was the primary differentiator. For users wanting a meaningful step up from the Extreme Q in both flower and concentrate performance, the Ditanium was considered a legitimate option, though the Flowerpot was more frequently recommended in that scenario.
